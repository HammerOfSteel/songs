# tuning the thread

**Labels:** what a Loop Is like
**Created:** 2025-09-04
**Edited:** 2025-09-04

---

un, dau, tri, pedwar… pump, chwech, saith, wyth, naw, deg.
Never was a thread
so bold and so strong
to let the truth behold
of what is right and wrong

Loom of a morning, shuttle of light,
vowen spun oaths through a fingertip night.
Mercury mind and a salt-sweet tongue,
sulfur of song where the gold was young.
Thread over threshold, hush on the sill,
cup in a saucer, a small steady will.
I pinch one filament out of the air,
nurse it to music, and mend by repair.
If silence grows antlers and stands in the door,
I comb its mane with the map of before.
If doubt puts a nail through the noon-blue wood,
I press my ear to the grain, still good.

String the strung, vowen spun, silent salt, a golden sun;
sang a song, surely sung, all in time by everyone.
By her, by him, maybe you, truths I wished you always knew;
if you won’t, and stillness blooms, I tune this thread and name her, beautiful.

un, dau, tri…

Spindle of patience, wheel of a prayer,
orbit of teacup, a planet of care.
Ash into ember, and ember to wine,
I rinse the dark with a lemon of shine.
Needle of listening, ear to the seam,
stitching the rip in the edge of a dream.
A vow is a vowel you never quite say,
a mouthful of bright you keep for the day.
If grief is a kiln and my ribcage its dome,
I lay soft clay where the cracks might roam.
If rage is a wolf with a wintering jaw,
I teach it to sit with a biscuit of awe.

String what’s strung, sing what’s sung, salt on skin and gold on tongue;
round we run, one by one, turning time until it’s won.
By her, by him, maybe you, things I sew in breath and dew;
if you close the knowing’s door, I tune this thread and bless her, beautiful.

un, dau, tri, pedwar, pump…
Fretless the line and the line isn’t lost,
knot after knot is a mercy that costs.
Copper and cotton, a wrist and a ring,
I temper the tremor and teach it to sing.

House made of breath, and a bell made of steam,
crown of a moment that learned how to dream.
Silver of silence, a lantern of bone,
light in the pocket you thought was a stone.
Here is the spindle, and here is the spool,
here is the hand that remembers the rule:
what you can cradle you also release,
grace is a loop that is learning its peace.
I set down the needle, I lift up the cup,
I circle the rim till the halo shows up.
What I can’t carry, I carry in kind,
a thimble of quiet, a saffron of mind.

String the strung, vowen spun, salt that sings and patient sun;
sang a song, surely sung, gathered breath by everyone.
By her, by him, even you, things I longed to usher through;
when you go all still and full, I tune this thread and know her, beautiful.

wyth, naw, deg… hush hums the rung;
Of everything beyond this bond is bilk
cause it’s not just a thread of silk
If night unspools and the day is dull,
I knot one word to the edge, beautiful.
