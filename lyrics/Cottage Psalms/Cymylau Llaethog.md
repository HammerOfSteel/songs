# Cymylau Llaethog

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-05-03
**Edited:** 2025-05-03

---

"She said: ‘Clouds don’t cry, love — they just get heavy,’
‘Same as we do, when we hold too much sky.’
I laughed, but she meant it. And that, I think, was the moment I knew."

Grass itch on the back of my knees,
London wheezin’ like the bus don’t breathe.
She’s got orange on her cheeks and crumbs on her chest,
Half a Greggs, two rings, and a belly at rest.
“I see a walrus,” she says, “wearin’ heels.”
I squint. “Nah, babe — it’s a bloke in a boat with a wheel.”
She rolls on her side, belly first like a wave,
Says, “You’re right — it’s a spell, and the sea misbehaved.”
I point at a cloud with a bit too much curl,
“That one’s a dragon with a teacup swirl.”
She snorts, “Alchemical,” lips round and soft,
“A lion, a moon, and a moth in a loft.”
And I see it, now. The sky's gone strange —
Bit of silverleaf in the blue exchange.
We lie still. She hums. I tap my shoe —
And her thumb draws a circle where our daughter grew.

Cymylau llaethog, floatin’ wide,
Stories soft in a pregnant sky.
Laughter stitched where dreams begin,
Holdin’ her hand where the clouds drift in.

“You reckon she’ll like this place?” she grins.
“Only if she’s weird like us,” I spin.
We chat names like spells in a lunchtime jam,
Pickin’ ones that sing, not just sound grand.
“There’s a kettle up there,” she says, “can you see?”
I nod. “She’s boilin’ tea for the queen bee.”
She closes her eyes like she’s chargin’ light,
And whispers, “This is the world — when it feels right.”
A breeze sneaks past with the scent of bread,
A fox barks twice where the grass meets shed.
She sits up slow, like a tree thinkin’ deep,
Points out a cloud that’s driftin’ like sleep.
“That’s her,” she says. “She’s lookin’ down.”
"Or up,” I say, “we’re upside-down."
And for a moment the city's hushed,
Just gulls and buses and leaves in a rush.

We're not made of stardust — we’re made of tea steam,
of bus seats and belly laughs, and maybe a dream."
"So if she ever asks where she’s from — just point up."

Cymylau llaethog, spinnin’ slow,
Lullaby sky with nowhere to go.
In the shape of a question, soft and blue,
She’s already there. She’s already true.

Clouds like scrolls, skies like shells,
She hums in tongues where memory dwells.
A lion, a leaf, a feather, a ring —
These are the things that make mothers sing.

We pack up slow, no need to rush,
She leans on me like the world ain't much.
Back down the path with the grass in folds,
Hand in my hoodie, the warm that holds.
Bus stop waits with its usual ache,
Teen scribbles and gum and a peeled-off flake.
She rests her head on my arm, half-zoned,
Says, “Tell her this someday, when she’s grown.”
I smile. “We’ll tell her all the weird ones first —
The cow on a bike, and the cloud that burst.”
We sit side by side, just her and me,
A moon in her belly, and tea on the breeze.

"She said: ‘Clouds don’t go — they just become rain.’
‘And you don’t go either — you just become me.’"
