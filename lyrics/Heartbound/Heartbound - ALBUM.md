# Heartbound - ALBUM

**Labels:** Heartbound, Album
**Created:** 2025-08-14
**Edited:** 2025-08-20

---

1. Bread and Salt

Opening scene: a welcome into the space — the kitchen table, the fire lit, the old blessing of bread and salt offered to guests and family alike.

Tone: Warm, inviting, almost hymn-like in its refrain.



---

2. Ashes in the Pan

Clearing the hearth in the morning after — embers fading but still warm, mirroring love or connection that lingers even after change or loss. Remembering how you held her - barely 2 months, early morning walk - the realization, the waking up. Non narrative but rhyming well, choruses almost like mantras, verses like a spell of rhyming prose's

Tone: plucked rhythm and best, mid tempo, zitar plucking, wild but sweet, bittersweet.



---

3. The Blessing Bowl

A quiet act of care: setting out water or bread as a blessing for the unseen, travellers, or spirits. A song of faith in us, in you daughter, in the continuation of us even now on this road, woven into simple ritual.

Tone: fun but devotional tune, sweet like a nighttime hymn for you. Acoustic picked, hand drums, mid tempo. Fathers voice, british accent, tibetian singing bowls



---

4. Psalm of Small Things

Giving thanks for the small miracles of life — the steam from a mug, the cat by the grate, rain on slate, your first cry, first words, all the Adventures, bedtime, your hugs, your mums smile as she holds you, all I miss, all I remember, Almost prayerful in its repetition.

Tone: mantra like flow, beat of acoustic Harmonies of picked zitar and some light accordion, mid-tempo, child's voices in Harmonies.



---

5. Slate and Flame

Connecting the hearth’s glow to the enduring slate hills — both weathered and rooted in the same place. Family and legacy, non narrative more like a spell that rhymes and layered story telling

Tone: Expansive, with welsh harp and layered strings, evoking landscape, almost reel like



---

6. Hearthbound (title track)

The emotional centrepiece — realising home has been carried within you all along, but the hearth’s light made it visible, you were always my home my daughter.

Deeply phonetically rhyming, non narrative, short rhyming prose's and pieces, banter and sweetheart.

Tone: mid-tempo tune like a reel, picked fiddle, some harmonica, like a tune for the road, wild with that Irish feeling. Sung by a father and a child (use [verse - child's voice] etc so show singer)



---

7. Coal Scuttle Hymn

A playful yet affectionate ode to the rhythm of daily life — the sound of coal shovelling, the dance of embers. Non narrative, just words that rhyme well and speaks to the trials in daily life.

Tone: Light, rhythmic folk tune with fiddle and hand percussion, mixed in with modern elements like some flstudio type beat and effects.



---

8. Candle for the Absent

Non narrative, like prose's on remembering when no one else will, the struggle of everyone else's silence, finding a way forward alone for us not for everyone else not for those that never spoke, held or even tried truly, just for us.

Longer verses and some instrumental breaks
Deeply phonetically rhyming 

Tone: acoustic rap real and sweetly jazz undertones, mid-tempo, welsh harp beat rap, reel like jazzy



---

9. If you see me now

Closing track: will I ever see you again? Do you still see me now? What do you think of me now? I just want a sign, anything. Vs. I'm here, I see you, please dad just open your eyes.

Tone: a mid tempo rap style Irish reel type duet father and daughter voices.
Picked fiddle strings and silver flute solo at some point. Banter and beautiful sweet Jon narrative prose's that rhyme deeply and phonetically

Longer lyrics flowing like water.
Choruses like almost a mantra,
A few instrumental breaks.

Intro and outro like some fitting quotes from mabinogion.
