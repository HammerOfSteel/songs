# When the Bell Breaks

**Labels:** chalice and cauldron
**Created:** 2025-04-13
**Edited:** 2025-04-13

---

She packed silk, not questions.
Took flights to forget that she'd never asked why the sky bends blue.
The villa was loud. The sea, a screen.
They drank by fountains carved by kings who never learned to cry.

The court was teeth behind smiles.
And the fire hadn’t started yet.
But it was there—coiled in her, waiting. 
A match remembering it’s born of thunder.
She didn’t know then.

But when the bell breaks—you do.

She left the states with glitter eyes,
Daddy's AmEx, tailored lies.
Villa booked, the coastline soft
white-gold sand and Aperol cough.
Said it was a sabbatical, soul-search light
really just running from suburban night.
Friends all sharp, dressed in heat,
glossed in glamour, perfect deceit.
She laughed too loud, drank too fast, 
threw her name into the sea like glass.
They danced in ruins older than blame, 
chanted names they’d never claim.
But then—a party deep in the olive shade, 
someone passed a little blade.
Tiny tab, no big deal, just some acid to break the seal.

But oh—the vision bled.The sky ripped red.
And the war came down where the dream had fed.
She saw a flag that tore itself,
a rattle snake on liberty’s shelf.
Bald eagle wept, then ripped its wing,
fed it to the bell that would not ring.
And the snake—devoured itself in golden rings.

Three times the bell chimes low,
and none of us know what we know.
She saw the war, she saw the wheel
and woke to find it might be real.
The vision burned but left no mark
just silence echoing in the dark.

She ghosted the crew, let the group chat die,
Walked cobbled alleys without goodbye.
Windows blinked with laundry and dusk,
Old men smoked in corners of musk.
She followed a sparrow through rain-slick stone
To a crumbling cafe half overgrown.
Inside: chipped china, a crooked grin,
A server said, "Sit. We begin."
They wore time like a second skin
Age and youth coiled deep within.

They spoke in riddles, jokes with teeth,
Said, "You saw the lance beneath the wreath.
Not all snakes bite. Some shed instead.
Not all gods wear gold on their head."

She told of the eagle, the vision, the sting
The server just hummed like a cello string.
Talked of trees with roots in stars,
Mirrors shaped like prison bars.
She listened hard, her skin turned flame,
Her name forgot its Western name.
And the heat grew strong, too much to stand
The serpent coiled in her shaking hands.

"The fire you fear is your first light.
Don’t run from it—let it write.
This isn’t madness, child, or fate.
This is the door. Step through the gate."

She burned but didn’t break that day,
In tongues of light and shadow play.
The waitress grinned with many eyes,
And whispered through her future lies:
"You are not sick. You are not shame.
You are becoming. Speak your name."

That night she dropped into endless sleep,
Dream-walked cliffs too sharp to keep.
Nine doors opened, each with a face,
Each her, but twisted, out of place.
One with knives for teeth and blame,
One in lace who whispered shame.
One who laughed at every prayer,
One with silence braided in her hair.

Each witch spoke: "You left us here.
You wore their mask, then disappeared."
She faced them all, in flame and bone,
Held their gaze and walked alone.
And as she did, they shimmered, bright,
Their horror softened into light.
One kissed her brow: 
"You were always more.
You broke the spell. 
You cracked the door."

She woke with fever, salt on skin,
But something true now bloomed within.

She saw her rage. She held her guilt.
She wore the armor she once had built.
But now, no sword, no mask to keep
Just open hands, and truths that weep.

She walked the ridge where dusk grows thin,
And the wind cried old wars on her skin.
The bell tower rang in her heart again,
Echoed the image of headless men.
She saw the platter, the face, the flame,
And realized it bore her brother’s name.
Not in blood, but in bond unseen,
A friend she left, a pact unclean.

He died while she escaped the stream,
While she chased fire, he drowned the dream.
It wasn’t her fault—but she didn’t ask.
Didn’t hold space, didn’t unmask.

She dropped to knees on moss and stone,
Spoke a vow to walk unknown.
No more palaces, no more page,
Just love, and grief, and sacred rage.

She found the grail not in her hand,
But in her breath, her voice, her stand.
The land she healed was not the world
But the one inside, unfurled, unfurled.

She never booked the flight back home.
Sent no postcard, turned off her phone.
She took a train with no set end,
Wrote in journals, made strange friends.
Read alchemy beneath the trees,
Spoke to rivers, knelt at knees.

She taught the fire to sing, not burn,
Gave silence shape, and from it came to learn
The waitress left a note one day:
"You passed the trial. So go your way."

She smiled. She cried. She didn’t know,
That seed within would always grow.
