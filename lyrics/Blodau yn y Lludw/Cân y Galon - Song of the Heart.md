# Cân y Galon - Song of the Heart

**Labels:** Blodau yn y Lludw
**Created:** 2025-01-30
**Edited:** 2025-01-30

---

"I ran so far I forgot the shape of her voice.
But even here, in a city that has never known her,
she still finds me. In the lanterns.
In the hush of the water.
In the light that refuses to go out."

The streets glow gold in paper light,
Lanterns drifting down the tide.
I walk alone, yet never quite,
Kyoto hums with whispers wide.

Stone on stone beneath my feet,
Autumn's hush and burning leaves.
I close my eyes, but still, I see,
Her hand in mine—just memory.

"Okaeri." (Welcome home.)
I shake my head, “But I never belonged.”
"Subete wa, koko ni aru." (Everything is here.)
The river sings, the night grows long.

Does the heart forget if the miles are wide?
Or does love still burn through space and time?
Mae’r fflam yn wyn, ni all farw,
(The flame is white, it cannot die.)
Even in silence, love is alive.

"Kaze wa kotaeru." (The wind will answer.)
"Ai wa nigasanai." (Love does not run away.)
"Koko ni iru dake de, jūbun da."
(Just being here is enough.)

I watch the water pull the night,
Lanterns flicker, slow and bright.
She drifts among them, soft and free,
Still calling, always calling me.

Does the heart forget if the miles are wide?
Or does love still burn through space and time?
Mae’r fflam yn wyn, ni all farw,
(The flame is white, it cannot die.)
Even in silence, love is alive.

"Tima..."
No reply, just water’s hush,
Yet something stirs beneath the dusk.
A hand unseen, a warmth inside,
A voice that whispers, "I'm still light."

No answers here, no grand refrain,
Just steps that lead me home again.
Through city streets, past paper doors,
Still hers, always, evermore.

"Mae’r fflam yn wyn, ni all farw..."
(The flame is white, it cannot die...)
The wind replies, the river sighs,
And love still walks where silence lies.
