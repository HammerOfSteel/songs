# harbor light

**Labels:** gullveig
**Created:** 2025-11-06
**Edited:** 2025-11-10

---

I came out of the north-west
Staggering from the storm
The surgeons had repaired my body
And my mind hung by one hinge
So I headed for the coast of Wales
To assume the healing rhythm of the sea
And breathe the briny air

I followed the smoke where the shoreline bends,
Where the gulls write your name in the wind again.
The waves hum soft in their linen white,
Calling me home to the harbor light.
Three winters I wandered, three storms I braved,
Each time the sea took what I gave,
But I’m still the flame that the dark can’t drown,
Three times I burned, and I came back down.

Harbor light, shine me through,
Gold on the water, still and true.
If ever I’m lost in the turning tide,
Keep my soul by the harbor side.

I waited on the quay with a lantern high,
Ash in my hair, salt in my sigh,
Every ship a promise, every wave a word,
Every spark in the dark a prayer I heard.
I whispered your name to the starlit foam,
“Come what may, you’ll find me home,”
For the sea remembers, the sea forgives,
And every burned heart still somehow lives.

Harbor light, steady and kind,
Guide all the lost ones left behind.
If ever the world should fade from sight,
Keep me near to the harbor light.

I was gold in the fire, I was salt in the deep,
The world turned thrice while I learned to sleep.
The waves gave shape to the ash I became,
Till even the tide began to know my name.

You were flame, you were sea, you were wind set free,
You were all I dreamed the heart could be.
Now I see you rise where the long waves lean,
In every shimmer the night has seen.

Let the fog roll in, let the buoys all toll,
We’ve carried enough for a single soul.
Let the lantern swing and the anchors bite,
We’re home again in the harbor light.

Harbor light, shine me through,
Gold on the water, old and new.
Three times I burned, three times I came bright,
Now I rest in the harbor light.

I've been in many places in my life
And seen wondrous famous sights
All seared into my minds eye
But their memories will last no longer
Than the haunting harbour bell
