# Salt • Sugar • Candle

**Labels:** Pockets Of Light
**Created:** 2025-08-22
**Edited:** 2025-08-22

---

When will I learn to stop teasing a taste
In hopes to expect something sweet
Only to get something incomplete

You sprinkle sugar onto your words
Words made of salt
To burn my open wounds

Run they say, he’ll never be same
Save yourself before the blame

Grab a spoon
And get a taste
Once you experience the sugar and the grace
You will then learn the lie beneath the face

Salt in the tears that sparkle on skin,
sugar in spoons where the mornings begin.
A candle that hums when the daylight grows thin,
holy and homely, the light’s tucked within.

Three little secrets in a cupboard row,
one for the sorrow, one sweet for the glow.

Salt in the laughter that hides in a cheek,
sugar that crumbles when I sneak a peek.
Candle that bends when the shadows all speak,
love in the wax and the wick and the leak.

Salt for the earth, and sugar for play,
a candle that keeps all the dark far away.

Salt • sugar • candle, sing low, sing bright,
turn the dark kitchen to chapels of light.
Salt • sugar • candle, rhyme till we rest,
everyday alchemy, homely and blessed.

Salt for the stew-pot, the memory’s taste,
sugar for teacups we sip without haste.
Candle that whispers, “no moment’s a waste,”
flame writing poems no ink could replace.

Salt in my pocket, sugar on bread,
candle beside me when prayers can’t be said.

Salt • sugar • candle, sing low, sing bright,
turn the dark kitchen to chapels of light.
Salt • sugar • candle, rhyme till we rest,
everyday alchemy, homely and blessed.

Salt is the ocean, sugar the star,
candle’s the lantern that shows where we are.
Salt is the sorrow, sugar the cheer,
candle’s the waiting that warms when you’re near.

Salt in the soil where the garden is sown,
sugar in berries when summer has grown.
Candle in windows that call you back home,
salt • sugar • candle — the trio, the stone.

Salt • sugar • candle, sing low, sing bright,
turn the dark kitchen to chapels of light.
Salt • sugar • candle, rhyme till we rest,
everyday alchemy, homely and blessed.
… everyday alchemy, homely and blessed.

Poems should be eaten and not left
to the dust-bitten corners
of the starving world
and its hollow-eyed tongue.
