# WELCOME - Album Overview

**Status:** In Development  
**Date Started:** 30 maj 2026

---

## Album Concept

**Core Narrative:** "Seasons of Welcome"

An intimate, multilingual album exploring the practice of welcoming - welcoming loss, welcoming mystery, welcoming love, welcoming home. Each song is a different act of opening one's arms to what life brings, finding beauty in the cycle of grief and joy, darkness and light.

The album moves through different "welcomes" - some painful (welcoming a child only in dreams), some mystical (welcoming nature's wild magic), some existential (welcoming life's riddles), some redemptive (welcoming transformative love). Together they create a tapestry of acceptance and wonder.

## Voice & Perspective

**Approach:** "The Multitude (Many Voices)"

Each song has its own distinct narrator/character, creating a tapestry of perspectives united by the theme of "welcoming." No single protagonist - instead, a chorus of human experiences:

- **Welcome:** The grieving father, visiting his daughter in dreams
- **Dandelion:** Blodeuwedd (the flower-woman from Welsh myth), longing to be seen/chosen
- **En sådan gåta:** The philosophical seeker, grappling with life's riddles
- **Livslust:** The grateful lover, saved and transformed by connection

This approach allows:
- Maximum emotional and sonic variety between songs
- Different vocal approaches (adult/child, male/female, accents)
- Freedom to add more "voices" as album develops
- Each song stands alone but contributes to the greater tapestry

## Song Concepts in Development

### 5. Hermes (Working Title)

**Theme:** Welcoming failure, transformation, the alchemical process of turning pain into something new  
**Alchemical Thread:** Virgin earth/prima materia - the raw material before transformation, Hermes as psychopomp and trickster  

**Feel:** Devotional singer-songwriter meets conversational quirk, intimate but playful, philosophical without pretension

**Voice/Character:** The gardener-alchemist, speaking to Hermes (god of boundaries, messages, transformation) while working

**Singer:** British adult voice (gender-neutral, Arcadian Wild devotional quality, but with rap-like rhythmic banter moments)

**Language:** English with possible Latin/alchemical phrases woven naturally

**Instruments:** 
- Fingerpicked acoustic guitar (intricate, math-folk)
- Subtle stomp-box or body percussion
- Harp (sparse, punctuating)
- Theremin (the "mercury" - liquid, transformative)
- Maybe jaw harp or kalimba (earthy, metallic)

**Structure:** 
- Narrative arc following alchemical stages (nigredo/blackening → albedo/whitening → rubedo/reddening)
- But told through modern gardening/composting metaphor
- Verses: devotional prose-poetry (Arcadian Wild style)
- Bridge/transitions: conversational asides, banter, almost talking-to-yourself quality
- Rhyme: sentence-to-sentence throughout, but varying rhythms

**Lyrical Approach:**
- NOT: "I'm turning my pain into gold" (too direct)
- YES: "Hermes, you old liar, what did you bury here? / Something that smells like last year's fear" (conversational, metaphorical)
- Mix philosophical devotion with earthy humor
- Alchemical symbolism hidden in gardening language (separating, dissolving, coagulating)
- Virgin earth = unworked soil = potential before action

**Conceptual Hooks:**
- Hermes as the one who delivers bad news but also carries prayers
- Dirt/earth as both grave and womb
- The trickster energy of plants (weeds that won't die, seeds that shouldn't grow but do)
- Talking to gods while doing mundane work
- "Solve et coagula" (dissolve and coagulate) - taking things apart to put them back together differently

**Placement:** Between Dandelion and Welcome - bridges wild nature magic with grief through practical mysticism

**Mood Reference:** Arcadian Wild's "Silence," Sufjan Stevens' conversational asides, Lars Winnerbäck's storytelling, but with the rhythmic quirk of Watsky or Aesop Rock's quieter moments

### 6. Cannwyll Fer (Brief Candle)

**Title Translation:** Welsh for "Brief Candle" (from Macbeth: "Out, out, brief candle! / Life's but a walking shadow")  
**Personal Connection:** Nicole's nickname in some contexts

**Theme:** Welcoming the unwanted - the shadow, the difficult truth, the visitor you've been avoiding, the brief flame before it goes out

**Feel:** Urgent, angular, rhythmic, Hot Hot Heat indie energy meets Welsh darkness, driving and relentless but intimate

**Voice/Character:** Someone pacing at night, talking fast to themselves/the shadow/the memory, can't slow down the thoughts

**Singer:** British/Welsh adult voice (gender-neutral, talk-sing rap delivery, angular like Steve Bays from Hot Hot Heat, urgent)

**Language:** English with Welsh phrases/the title woven in, possible Welsh chorus

**Instruments:**
- Driving acoustic guitar (percussive, palm-muted, post-punk rhythm)
- Syncopated hand claps or stomp box
- Minimal bass (walking line, insistent)
- Sparse theremin or synth (ghostly, Nicole's presence)
- Maybe glockenspiel or kalimba (flickering like candlelight)
- Snare brush rhythm (urgent but quiet)

**Structure:**
- Rap-influenced verses: fast-paced, internal rhymes, sentence-to-sentence rhyming but dense
- Indie-style chorus: more melodic, Welsh phrases, "Cannwyll fer" repeated
- Talk-sing throughout like Hot Hot Heat's "Bandages" or "Goodnight Goodnight"
- Bridge: breakdown, slower, more vulnerable, then builds back

**Lyrical Approach:**
- Fast delivery, almost breathless (welcoming what you fear before you can change your mind)
- Rhyme scheme: Strict sentence-to-sentence but with internal rhymes (rap influence)
- Hot Hot Heat style: conversational but urgent, intimate confessions at high speed
- NOT slow and bluesy - FAST and angular and can't-stop-thinking
- Example flow: "Three knocks sharp and I know who's there before I stand / Got your shadow in my doorway, got your candle in your hand / Brief and burning, always yearning, never learning how to stay / Cannwyll fer, you flicker here, then flicker away"

**Conceptual Hooks:**
- Candle as metaphor for brief life, brief visit, brief light in darkness
- The unwelcome guest who IS welcome (contradiction, complexity)
- Pacing/racing thoughts - can't sit still with grief
- Shadow-work: facing what you've been running from
- Nicole as the brief candle - intense, bright, gone too soon
- The visitor might be memory, might be ghost, might be fear itself

**Alchemical Thread:** Nigredo (blackening) - facing the shadow, the dark night, necessary confrontation before transformation

**Placement:** Before "Welcome" - sets up the grief with urgency and fear, then Welcome slows down into acceptance

**Mood Reference:** Hot Hot Heat ("Bandages," "Goodnight Goodnight"), The National's faster songs, Frightened Rabbit's urgent moments, but with theremin and Welsh darkness

**Production Notes:** 
- DRY vocals (no reverb, close-mic, in your face)
- Tight, controlled energy (not chaotic - precision in the urgency)
- Rhythm section drives everything
- Space for the words to land despite speed

### 7. Sunday Roast

**Theme:** Welcoming reciprocity, gratitude, the ritual of feeding/being fed, giving back to the world and those who feed us

**Feel:** Innocent, warm, domestic magic, toddler-logic wisdom, simple gratitude made profound, kitchen-table ritual

**Voice/Character:** A very young child (toddler, 3-5 years old) who understands giving and receiving in their pure, uncomplicated way

**Singer:** British child voice (toddler age, 3-5 years, slightly lispy or imperfect pronunciation, completely sincere, could sound like a child actually wrote it)

**Language:** English (British) with Welsh words mixed in phonetically - rhyming based on sound not spelling, how a toddler would hear/repeat Welsh words

**Instruments:**
- Toy piano or small glockenspiel (child-height instruments)
- Wooden spoons on pots (kitchen percussion)
- Humming or kazoo
- Acoustic guitar (gentle, simple fingerpicking)
- Maybe small hand drum or tambourine
- Recorder or penny whistle (simple, earnest)

**Structure:**
- Simple verse structure like a nursery rhyme or children's book
- Each verse an "offering" - food for birds, milk for cat, crumbs for mice, roast for family
- Chorus about sharing and giving back
- Simple AABB rhyme scheme, sentence-to-sentence
- Could have adult voice humming harmony (parent in background)

**Lyrical Approach:**
- Toddler vocabulary and logic: simple words, profound observations
- Welsh words that sound like English or rhyme phonetically (e.g., "bara" (bread) / "para" share, "cath" (cat) / "that")
- Innocent mixing of languages like a bilingual household toddler
- Food as love, sharing as ritual, everyone gets fed
- Example lines: 
  - "Sunday roast, piece of toast / For the birds I love the most"
  - "Milk for cath, down the path / Crumbs for mouse in his house"
  - "Mam says share, that's what's fair / Bara here, bara there"
  - "Diolch diolch (thank you) all around / For the food we found"

**Conceptual Hooks:**
- Sunday roast = British family ritual, abundance, gathering
- Child's perspective: everyone eats (animals, family, earth)
- Welcoming the practice of giving, not hoarding
- Kitchen as altar, meal as offering
- Bilingual household naturalness - code-switching as breathing
- The un-self-conscious generosity of small children
- Gratitude without being told to be grateful

**Alchemical Thread:** Nutritio (nourishment) - feeding and being fed, the great circulation of giving and receiving

**Placement:** Mid-album - a bright, innocent moment between heavier emotional songs. Perfect palate cleanser after "Cannwyll Fer" or before/after "Welcome" to lighten the grief

**Mood Reference:** Simple like Elizabeth Mitchell's children's songs, but with deeper resonance like Sufjan Stevens' "Exploding Whale" or Joanna Newsom's childlike moments, innocent like traditional nursery rhymes but Welsh-British hybrid

**Production Notes:**
- Could actually record a real toddler if available, or adult doing very convincing toddler voice
- Keep it slightly "imperfect" - charm in the innocence
- Kitchen sounds as percussion (natural, domestic)
- Warm but still DRY (no reverb, close and intimate like bedtime story)
- Maybe record in an actual kitchen for natural resonance

## Musical Inspirations & Sonic Landscape

**Core Sound:** "Nordic Melancholy Meets Celtic Mist"

A unique blend of Swedish narrative folk (Lars Winnerbäck's intimacy) with ethereal Celtic traditions (Lisa Hannigan, Kathryn Williams, early Joanna Newsom). The theremin serves as our signature voice - the otherworldly thread connecting cultures and moments.

**Key Artists/References:**
- **Swedish Folk:** Lars Winnerbäck (storytelling intimacy), First Aid Kit (sibling harmonies)
- **Celtic/Irish:** Lisa Hannigan (ethereal vocals), Kathryn Williams (minimalist folk), Kate Rusby
- **Harp/Strings:** Joanna Newsom (early work), Loreena McKennitt
- **Math-Folk Precision:** The Arcadian Wild, Andrew Bird (intricate but emotional)
- **Production Aesthetic:** No-reverb, dry vocals, busker intimacy, close-mic authenticity

**Sound Architecture:**
- Fingerpicked harp as foundation
- Theremin as the "ghost voice" - memory, longing, the liminal
- Mandolin/cello for warmth and texture  
- Child voices as truth-tellers and echoes
- British/Swedish accents preserved (authenticity over polish)
