Verse 1
By the edge of a still, glass pond,
The Seeker sees a form beyond.
A figure shifting, ever new,
A face that changes with each view.
A swan, a stag, a silver doe,
A flowing stream where the waters go.
Eyes that shimmer, soft yet torn,
A voice that carries both dusk and dawn.

Chorus
A fluid form, a soul untamed,
Bound by a curse, yet never named.
Through shapes and shadows, through light and storm,
In every change, a truth is born.
Embrace the flux, accept the strange,
For what is lost, will also change.

Verse 2
It speaks in tones both low and high,
“I was a swan beneath the sky,
A fish that swam in rivers wide,
A leaf that drifted with the tide.
In every form, I’ve lost and found,
A piece of self in sacred ground.
To be a tree, a wolf, a wave,
Is to understand what it means to save.”

Outro
The shapeshifter fades with the dawn's first light,

The Seeker breathes, the path ahead,
Led by the forms where fate has led.

Song Description: "A Fluid Form"
"A Fluid Form" captures the Seeker's encounter with a shapeshifter, a being trapped in an eternal state of transformation, embodying myths from Welsh and Irish traditions. Through this interaction, the shapeshifter, representing the ever-changing nature of existence, shares the wisdom of embracing life's constant flux and finding peace in the unknown. This song is a meditation on the acceptance of one's true self, despite the shifts and changes that life inevitably brings. It speaks to the resilience of the human spirit, the beauty found in transformation, and the endless quest for self-acceptance and understanding.
