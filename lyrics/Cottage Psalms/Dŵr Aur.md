# Dŵr Aur

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-04-23
**Edited:** 2025-04-23

---

We’d stopped walking… just for a bit.  
Golden hour, that kind of light where the world don’t speak unless you ask nice.  
She found a puddle like it was treasure. Said,  
“Dad… this one’s got secrets.”

You crouched down, fingers dipped like a priest  
In the church of roadside rain and rust  
You looked at me, cheek full of toast  
“Bet this puddle’s got the moon in it… and a ghost.”  
I said, “Probably. Best ask nice.”  
You whispered to it like a friend from school  
Then flicked a bit at me, grinned:  
“See, it likes you. You’re the fool.”  
You wiped a circle on its skin  
“Look — this one’s still got Mum in.”  
I didn’t blink. Just nodded slow.  
The sun went gold. The wind let go.

Gold is what stays when the world lets go  
It’s not in the coin, but the puddle’s glow  
It’s the stillness that says: “You’re allowed to be”  
Gold is what you feel when you don’t need to see

You asked, “D’you think clouds ever clock out?”  
I said, “Not if they’ve got unions.” You laughed, “Daaad.”  
We sat in silence near the hill  
There’s a gate creak, crow, the world stood still  
You tapped your flask, I clinked my spoon  
We played that rhythm from your “sheep field tune”  
Then out the blue you go:  
“Do puddles remember who stepped in ‘em first?”  
I said, “I reckon so. They’re petty like that.”  
You said, “Same. Bet they’ve got secret puddle chats.”  
Then whispered to the water low:  
“You’re safe with us. You don’t need to know.”

Gold is what stays when the name fades out  
It’s not in the crown, but the whispered shout  
It’s the hush that sings when you breathe in deep  
Gold is what you feel when the world’s asleep

You lay flat back on a mossy stone  
One shoe off, snack in hand  
Looked at me sideways like you knew something  
Said, “Dad… maybe this is Mum’s favourite bit.”  
And I nearly broke.  
But I said, “Yeah. Me too.”

We walked again, the world resumed  
But slow — like it knew something new  
A bee buzzed lazy past your brow  
You whispered, “He’s late.” I said, “Aren’t we all now?”  
At the bend, the puddle gone  
But gold stayed floating in your tone  
You said, “Let’s not tell the others, right?”  
“Keep it as ours. Gold don’t need light.”

And when we got home…  
Your coat still smelled of old toast and sun.  
You went,  
“Mum saw that puddle too, yeah?”  
I said,  
“Yeah, love. She left it here for you.”
