# all I did

**Labels:** Sunny side
**Created:** 2025-12-16
**Edited:** 2025-12-16

---

I’m still clutching at the ghosts that I’m supposed to let go,
Replaying the footage in a grainy, grey-scale show. 
Everyone’s moved on, they’ve cleared the debris from the street,
While I’m still standing here, staring at my own cold feet.

They tell me, "Forget it, it’s a chapter you’ve already read,"
But the words are tattooed on the inside of my head.

They say the memory is a poison, that it’s rotting my mind,
But if I lose the memory, what else is there left to find?
I’m thinking of the 'should-haves' and the things I didn’t do,
Praying to a God that I never actually knew.
I’m hoping and I’m praying with the strength of a dying sun,
But it doesn’t change the fact that the damage has been done.

They want me to be blank, they want the slate to be clean,
But I’m the only witness to the things that have been seen.
They want me to vanish, to just simply disappear,
But I’m a monument of bone to everything I fear.

They hold onto my shoulders and they look me in the eye,
Telling me the truth is just a well-intentioned lie.
They say, "It wasn’t your fault," like a mantra, like a prayer,
But I can taste the culpability in every breath of air.

They say, "You were a victim," and they say it with a smile,
While I’m the one who’s walking through the fire, mile after mile.
I know the truth of it, I know the weight of the lead,
I know the exact moment that the innocence went dead.

Everyone else has forgotten, the world has turned its back,
While I’m still tracing every single splinter in the crack.
Thinking about the version of the girl I used to be,
Before the history decided to crack the best me.

I’m holding on for safety, I’m holding on for spite,
Because forgetting is the only way to lose the bloody fight.

They changed me, they broke me, they held me to the floor,
And I’m the one who’s haunted by the closing of the door.
I didn’t know the ending, I didn’t know the price,
I was just a person caught inside the turning of the vice.

And I just fought back—that is all I ever did,
Against the things that surfaced and the things that stayed hid.
I just didn’t want it to ever happen again,
So I carry the memory like a sword and a pen.

I’m not a tragedy. I’m a survivor’s ledger.
Every scar is a line of text I refuse to erase.
They want me gone because my face is a reminder,
But I won't disappear to make your dinner parties kinder.

I won't be forgotten.
I won't be a secret.
If the truth is a haunt,
then I’m the one who’s going to keep it.

What happened shall haunt me, and I’ll wear it like a crown,
A heavy gold burden in the center of the town.
Thinking about what should have happened, but knowing what did,
Uncovering the secrets that the silence always hid.

I really didn’t know. I swear I didn’t know.
But I’ll never let the fire or the memory go.
It will never be forgotten.
