[intro]
[gentle vinyl crackle →

[Female voice, British accent – half-sung]

I came home ’round lunchtime, cwtched in my coat,
heard a hum through the hedgerow, some odd little note.
Out back, Peter was there, goggles askew,
said, “Cariad, you’ve got to see what I can do.”

[soft mechanical whirr, synths bloom]
He said, “I built a time machine…
Like one from the silver screen.”

[verse 2]
We rode that flux thing through the sky,
city lights drifted slow, fish swam by.

the sea took London in its dreams.

Boy bands floated, same old tune,
just holograms under a glass-blue moon.
He laughed, said, “Look, they still dance that way.”
I said, “Duw, some things never fade.”
