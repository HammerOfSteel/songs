# curfew

**Labels:** Sunny side
**Created:** 2025-12-21
**Edited:** 2025-12-21

---

You’re standing at the threshold, looking at the clock,
I’m checking if the front door has actually got a lock.

You’ve got the boots on, the ones with all the lace,
You’ve got that “don’t talk to me” look upon your face.

The eyeliner’s winged like you’re ready for a flight,
Into the middle of a London Saturday night.

I say, “You look nice,” and you give a little sigh,
I’m just a silly man with a worry in his eye.

You’re growing up too fast, it’s a tactical retreat,
From the little girl who used to play in the middle of the street.

It’s a curfew, darling, 
t’s a quarter-past-ten,
I don’t want to have to come and fetch you once again.

The world is getting bigger and I’m feeling rather small,
Just the shadow of a father standing in the hall.

You think I’m being bossy, you think I’m being old,
I’m just trying to keep your spirit out of the bitter cold.

So keep your phone charged and keep your wits about,
And try not to make me have a minor heart attack, no doubt.

Is that a boy with a moped?
who's that a lad with a van?

I’ll have to tell him I’m a very, very dangerous man.
I’ll tell him I’ve got skills like Liam Neeson in the films,
While I’m actually just worried ‘bout the denting of the rims.

You roll your eyes so far that they’re looking at your brain,
While I’m checking on the weather and the forecast for the rain.

“Dad, I’ll be fine,” you say,
reaching for the latch,
But in the game of growing up,
but I'm clearly out of the match.

I make a joke about your hair just to hear you make a sound,
To keep my feet from floating and my heart upon the ground.

Aye, I'm a nightmare,
I know I’m being a pest,
But my heart is in your pocket and it never gets a rest.
The world out there is sharp,
the world out there is wide,
And I’m losing all the places where I used to let you hide.

I trust you with my life, yeah,
I trust you to be wise,
It’s just the rest of them I’m viewing with these suspicious eyes.

Go on then, get moving,
go and have your bit of fun,
I’ll be sitting in the kitchen ‘til the evening is all done.

It’s a curfew, darling,
it’s a quarter-past-ten,
I don’t want to have to come and fetch you once again.
The world is getting bigger and I’m feeling rather small,
Just the shadow of a father standing in the hall.
You think I’m being bossy, you think I’m being old,
I’m just trying to keep your spirit out of the bitter cold.
So keep your phone charged and keep your wits about,
And try not to make me have a minor heart attack, no doubt.
