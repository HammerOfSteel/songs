🌧️ Tracklist (Narrative Flow)

---

SIDE A – Morning Light

1. Taddle Tale
She tells you who she was before she came.
→ British slang lyrics by a young girl, British rap sweet homely, Finger-picked guitar, brushed snare, child’s laughter and whispers.
A song of beginnings — stories spun between raindrops and walks.


2. Field Guide to Holding
Writing down all the things that make you you, scribbles and notes of important and not so important things to remember.
→ Plucked fiddle, field ambience, journal-flip sounds.
Learning gentleness through nature — a poetic manual for love: it starts with the discovery of everything, when you were born, when you came - solved for the exponents of my heart, this is simple and expression the proof where by such came to be.


3. Cloudseed Composure
She plants raindrops in the garden, waiting for a rainbow harvest.
→ Bright zitar and ukulele, laughter sampled like sunlight.
A playful track about faith, growth, and the little magic of childhood.


4. Warm Milk Weather
Rain tapping windows (steeldrum sounds), she’s asleep in your lap.
→ Ukulele, kettle hiss, bass hum.
A lullaby for the stillness of ordinary love.


5. Oil, for Snakes
Change hurts but staying hurts more.
→ Zitar drone, fiddle pizzicato, heartbeat percussion.
The shedding song — about transformation and pain as necessity, even buddhistic philosofical undertones about pain as friction like a natural part of the world.
Not narrative based but always rhyming well


6. Lanolin Sky
Sheep in the valley, smell of wool and rain.
Simple lyrics but sweet and warm rap only uk british male accent and voice,
always rhyming well, cheeky and full of love
→ Field recordings, fiddle, soft beat, jazzy undetones
A pastoral moment — grounding again after the inner storm.




---

SIDE B – Evening Glow

7. Soup for Ghosts
Cooking for the ones who visit only in memory.
→ Clinking bowls, wooden spoon rhythm, humming refrain.
The gentle domestic haunt — healing through ritual.


8. The Paper Crown Parade
A birthday that never really ends.
→ Upbeat folk rhythm, toy piano, layered harmonies.
Childhood as an echo — play and absence mingling.


9. Moth Sermon
She teaches you how to stay gentle near flames.
→ Soft zitar, delicate fiddle, slow spoken verse.
A parable of tenderness and resilience.

11. Twelve Kettles Later
A lifetime measured in cups of tea.
→ Spoken word, upright bass, clinking cups.
