# May, May Moon

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-03-27
**Edited:** 2025-03-27

---

The clover of May,
The lily and her sisters,
Like the full river when there is a flood,
Full of praise and renown are the vales:
And thou also camest back healthy,
Dear little swallow, to our moors.

We walked past the hedges still half asleep,
dandelions yawning through broken concrete.
She talked about clouds that looked like sheep,
and I just nodded, counting my heartbeat.
The swing at the park made a creaky song,
one she hummed while dragging her arms along.
I watched her toes draw lines in dirt
On the ground, a thrice blessed heart of the earth.
I wasn’t cold. But I wasn’t there.
Just a shape in a coat, breathing air.
And she said, “Look, Papa! The moon in the sky!”
“It's the flower moon, way up high"

May, May Moon,
you don’t rise right,
daylight bends,
and the stars lose fight.
Spring came wrong,
but we walked through,
you in full bloom,
me just passing through.

The daffodils came late that year,
Peter brings toast, jam and a smudge of fear,
Small words and a heart in stone,
"ofnadwy" how you have grown.
She danced in her wellies , one sock gone,
hair like scribbles, mittens on.
Heart and head, a prayer not quite said
a whisper you fold and place by the bed.
Heard and seen, a mid may queen
Half a heart sown in Eden, half a heart in the north of Sweden.
She asks if the flowers knows their moon,
The utters play in the late afternoon.

May, May Moon,
don’t fool me now,
I’ve got a girl to walk,
a path to plough.
If the season slips,
I’ll hold her still
keep her warm
in a world stale and still.

They say if you're fooled in April,
you’ll stay a fool all year.
But I’d play the fool for her forever
just to keep her near.

We made toast three times, she burned the first,
laughed like sugar, licked jam off her shirt.
I watched the crumbs fall like silent snow,
each one a moment I’d never outgrow.
The news kept talking but we kept it low
let the world shout where she doesn’t go.
We coloured the sky with half-blunt pens,
I drew stars, she turned them to hens.
And I thought, what if this is enough?
These slow mornings, these bowls, this stuff.
Not the answers, an old refrain
Not the song, but what remains.

Dad do you think the trees feel cold?
A tiny voice with eyes of old.
She wrapped her arms around a postbox,
Knitted orange hat, with ears of a fox.
I watched her eyes go wide at the sun,
while my own just blinked and tried to run.
And there she sat on a pile of hay,
I saw the world, saw today, not thinking more of yesterday 
I don’t know if numbness ever ends
but love’s a song that sometimes mends.
I don’t need spring to be in tune,
not when I’ve got her.
And the May, May moon.

Let the month be crooked,
let the light be strange,
let me be the fool
who never asks for change.
If she laughs, it’s enough.
If she sleeps, let it stay.
Let the moon hang wrong
and the stars drift away.

Who, who, at this delightful time,
Shall stay in the towns,
As a wretched man, in a detestable condition,
As a captive between walls;
While the blue-green earth is very cheerful,
And the wood full of joy?
