# Lodestar

**Labels:** solvet et coagula
**Created:** 2025-05-14
**Edited:** 2025-05-14

---

"Late streets… fog rollin’ in like a duvet.
Thought I was on my ones out ‘ere. Turns out… someone clocked me first."
Shake me up
Roll me around
Tromp on me
Stomp on me
Quake me up

Hood up, steps cold on broken slabs,
Fox eyes flashin’ by rusted cabs.
East End mist got me thinkin’ I’m buggin’,
Streetlamps flicker like they’re huffin’ n’ puffin’.
Then there—soft glow hangin’ mid-air,
Not car light, not street glare, rare.
I freeze, heart stumbles in beat,
Shadow in gold just watchin’ discreet.
“Oi… you clockin’ me? What’s your deal?”
No voice, just a shimmer that felt surreal.
One step forward, it floats back slow,
Teasin’ me gentle, sayin’ “Yeah bruv, let’s go.”

Lodestar, not too far,
Silent pull on my radar.
Not a map, not a sign,
But I follow all the same line.

I’m southbound wanderin’, Elephant streets,
Glow dips behind arches and beats.
I follow, yeah, but I’m still chatty:
“C’mon now mate, you ain’t that scatty.”
Past the pillars, past the railway grime,
Under clocktowers that forget time.
Every turn like a test, like a rite,
Fog thick as soup, no wrong or right.
Suddenly stops. I step through haze,
There’s someone standin’ calm in the maze.
Tall coat, hood low, but the shimmer leaks,
Eyes like embers when the silence speaks.
“Been expectin’ ya, took your time lad.”
“Who you, Yoda? You real or mad?”
They chuckle soft, hand points to sky,
“Just a walker helpin’ you try.”

Lodestar, pull me far,
You know who I really are.
Ain’t no end, just a gate,
Walk the spiral, twist the fate.

They walk, I walk, no footsteps fall,
Like driftin’ weightless through midnight sprawl.
Said, “You stuck in trench long enough, yeah?
Whole world whisperin’, you just ain’t aware.”
I nod slow, feel warmth in the gloom,
Like wild thyme scent in a locked-up room.
They speak: “Alchemy’s start is dissolve,
To lose your shell ‘fore you evolve.
You ain’t lost, you fermentin’ bruv,
Cookin’ in dark ‘til you rise above.”
I grin, “So what now? You got a plan?”
They laugh, fade soft: “Walk, my man.”

Instead of a large-screen tv
A few pallets remain low to the earth
Seeped in sorrow over sadness
And mania over mirth.

While many wander in each day
Only a few will find this house a home
For inside, there are many monsters
And that, you must confront alone.
