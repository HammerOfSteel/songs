# dear daffodil

**Labels:** Pockets Of Light
**Created:** 2025-08-27
**Edited:** 2025-08-27

---

I wandered lonely as a cloud
That floats on high o'er vales and hills,
When all at once I saw a crowd,
A host, of golden daffodils;
Beside the lake, beneath the trees,
Fluttering and dancing in the breeze.

Cradle of morning, lullaby thin,
warmth in my arms where the cosmos begins.
Breath like a candle, flicker so small,
dear daffodil, dearest of all.
Milk-light psalm in the hush of your cheek,
whispering rhythms no language can speak.
Hold you here, hear me now, all my heart I vow. Through all I fear, Little daffodil dear.

Little one, little sun, I see you glow.

giggle spill over the floor,
morning gospel, I open the door.
Songs in the corners, tucked under the chair,
I hum them for you, we're right here.
Buttons and ribbons, pockets of thread,
daffodil blossoms where Mam used to tread.
Her arms are the halo, her voice is the flame,
Tad says out eyes are the same.

Dear daffodil, dear daffodil,
time is a river, and still, and still.
Dear daffodil, dear daffodil,
love is the lantern, it burns, it will.

Continuous as the stars that shine
And twinkle on the milky way,
They stretched in never-ending line
Along the margin of a bay:
Ten thousand saw I at a glance,
Tossing their heads in sprightly dance.

Windowlight painted on paper and skin,
I carry her heartbeat beneath and within.
Cradle remembered, soft-folding rhyme,
I hold it, I hum it, I stretch it through time.
Footsteps are lullabies stitched into air,
daffodil glowing in places she cared.
Her absence is present, her presence is near,
I’m sewn into echoes that shimmer right here.
can I know you now, can I still somehow.

Scattered through heavens, I fracture, I fall,
threads of her arms in the seams of it all.
Second by second I gather her flame,
each fragile eternity whispering name.
Dear daffodil, seed of the sun,
love passed down, thread never undone.
I scatter, I shatter, across what’s unknown,
second by second I’m trickling home.

Dear daffodil, dear daffodil,
time is a river, and still, and still.
Dear daffodil, dear daffodil,
love is the lantern, it burns, it will.

For oft, when on my couch I lie
In vacant or in pensive mood,
They flash upon that inward eye
Which is the bliss of solitude;
And then my heart with pleasure fills,
And dances with the daffodils.

Hush, little star.
I’m here in the light.
Dear daffodil… I’ll come home tonight.
