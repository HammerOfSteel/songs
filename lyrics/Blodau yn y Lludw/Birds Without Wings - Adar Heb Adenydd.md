# Birds Without Wings - Adar Heb Adenydd

**Labels:** Blodau yn y Lludw
**Created:** 2025-02-06
**Edited:** 2025-02-06

---

"The wind knows my name, but it speaks it like a whisper.
The hills remember, even if I have tried to forget."

The Ebbw sings beneath my feet,
A restless hum, a steady beat.
The past is buried, yet not gone,
Its echoes call with each new dawn.

Through streets where time has come undone,
Shadows stretch with the setting sun.
Windows flicker, faces fade,
Ghosts in glass, names unsaid.

Footsteps lost where the cobbles crack,
Once I left, but now I’m back.
Each stone, each step, a weight I bear,
Yet here I stand, still gasping air.

Birds without wings, lost in the night,
Bound to the earth but craving flight.
Oh, to soar where the wild winds call,
But fear still holds, and hearts still fall.

Up the hillside, past the trees,
Where bracken dances with the breeze.
The summit waits in dying light,
A fire burns beyond my sight.

The path remembers, though I don’t,
My feet move forward, though I won’t.
Every whisper, every stone,
Feels like I’ve never been alone.

Then through the hush, a whisper near,
A voice I know, soft and clear.

Birds without wings, yet still we try,
Between the earth and endless sky.
A feather falls, the wind still sings,
Perhaps we were never wingless things.

"Little star, is it you?"
"You’ve felt me all along, haven’t you?"
"I walked through years of silence."
"But silence doesn’t mean I wasn’t there."
"I keep climbing, but I never reach you."
"I’m not above you, Dad. I’m beside you."

'The wind hums low, a lullaby,
The stars hang quiet in the sky.
I close my eyes, she takes my hand,
For just one moment, I understand.

The Ebbw glows in morning’s breath,
The hill still stands, untouched by death.
I climb back down, though nothing’s the same,
The wind still whispers, still calls her name.

I reach the streets I used to roam,
Now not a house, but still a home.
Each wall, each road, still carries weight,
But love is stronger than the hate.

Birds without wings, yet here we rise,
With love still burning in our skies.
Not lost, not gone, not out of sight,
Only carried on winds of light.

"Dwi yma o hyd, tad."
