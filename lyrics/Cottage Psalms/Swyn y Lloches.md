# Swyn y Lloches

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-04-23
**Edited:** 2025-04-23

---

Rain again. Of course it is.  
It’s London, innit — rain, roundabout, and regret in a reusable cup.  
Still... it always brings me back.  
To that stop. That shelter. That spell.

There was a place near Risca hill  
Bus stop tin, bent, but magic still  
I sat on your boot, zipped up tight  
Rain all teeth — but your voice, light  
You said, “This roof’s got runes, bach — look”  
Each drip, a secret in a crooked nook  
And when the thunder rattled the bin  
You tapped your coat zip like a violin  

This is for the shelter made of song  
For coats zipped up when the world goes wrong  
For whispers warm in sleet and smoke  
A charm for hearts that never spoke  
Swyn y lloches — spell of stay  
For rainy minds and runaway days

You taught me names for every rain  
“Rhuthro’n arw” when it hits like pain  
“Brwdlaw” soft like a mother’s hum  
“Cilfadon” when it doesn’t quite come  
I counted puddles like spells to cast  
Each step a rite to outwalk the past  
We drew with fingers on steamed glass  
“Charm of Now” — then watched it pass

Back in this city with its neon spills  
I watch the drips chase down café sills  
There’s a bloke outside, coat like mine  
Foot tapping some old coat-zip time  
I smile and stir my tea like lore  
Hold the silence like a well-worn door  
Still feel your rhythm in the rain  
That quiet song that kept me sane

This is for the hands that made a wall  
For every “stay” when you thought they’d fall  
For paper bags turned paper boats  
For names that still keep people afloat  
Swyn y lloches — let them be  
A roof of voice, a coat, a key

No grand cathedral, no woven thread  
Just you and me, the bin, the shed  
And this — a spell the rain repeats  
Each drop a line, each gust a beat  
“You are held, you are heard”  
That’s what the roof always murmured

I pass that stop now and then  
Still got weeds but holds the bend  
I leave a note, just once or twice  
Tucked in the seat, folded nice  
“Charm of shelter, made of talk  
Still holds dry where the soaked ones walk”  
Then back to London, boots all wet  
But I’m the driest I’ve ever been yet

Not all spells need candles.  
Some just need your old coat  
And someone who said:  
“Come here. The rain can wait.”
