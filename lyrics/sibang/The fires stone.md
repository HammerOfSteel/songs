# The fires stone

**Labels:** sibang
**Created:** 2024-10-20
**Edited:** 2024-10-20

---

Verse 1
By the shore where the sea keeps calling,
I sit like a stone, quiet and gray.
Once I stood tall, a guardian wall,
But the years, they wore me away.
Waves whisper my name, but I don’t answer,
I’ve given too much just to stay.

Chorus
I’m a stone grandmother, carved by the tides,
Silent and strong, though the world passed me by.
The ocean might pull, but I won’t drift away—
A heart of rock, and here I’ll stay.


---

Verse 2
I was the shelter when storms came crashing,
The arms that held through every fight.
But time, it chisels—takes what it pleases,
And leaves you weathered in the fading light.
Yet in these cracks, my strength still lingers,
In every scar, a story shines bright.

Chorus
I’m a stone grandmother, carved by the tides,

The ocean might pull, but I won’t drift away—

Bridge
Even when the winds howl my name,
Even when the rain beats me down,
I know the stones still stand together—
Old souls anchored in the ground.

Chorus
I’m a stone grandmother, carved by the tides,
Silent and strong, though the world passed me by.
The ocean might pull, but I won’t drift away—
A heart of rock, and here I’ll stay.


---

Outro
By Yongduam Rock, I find my place,
Where the dragon met the sea’s embrace.
Like a stone grandmother, I won’t fade,
In the rhythm of tides, I’ve found my way.
