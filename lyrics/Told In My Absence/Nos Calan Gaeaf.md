Little sheet ghost, I boast, I’m cosy, see?
Candle in a jar on the windowsill, me.
Mug’s gone cold but I hold it still,
dear daffodil, all the nooks and crannies in me heart you fill.
Night’s like ink, I blink, it hums,
witch-fire breath on the tip of my thumbs.
Mum said “home now, lock that door,”
Gran said “quiet, love, mind the floor.”
But I’m not scared, I swear, just small,
wrap me in a cardigan, that’s all.
Moon sits low like it’s keeping score,
bonfire smoke in the hallway more.
Warm in the ribs where the hush goes round,
soft little drum in a belly-deep sound.
Still, still , distil, all that awake in the wake,
awake I can’t really shake and won’t,
no I won’t, I don’t, want to break.
I keep the flicker, sweeter than ache.

Go in, go in, love, don’t stay,
Last one home as we, close the day.
Last one out, she’ll have your name,
hwch ddu gwta in the flame.
I’m in, I’m in, I’m safe, I’m stored,
blanket, biscuit, old oak door.
Whisper, promise, soft and sure,
I’m kept. I’m good. I’m yours.

Listen: tap-tap claws on stone,
someone out there wants you alone.
Old black sow with her lantern eyes,
can’t tell truth from children’s lies.
Some say leather, man in a skin,
some say spirit that pulls you in.
I say hush now, brew that tea,
I say stay right here by me.
I know winter, cold on the lung,
songs ‘bout loss that ain’t been sung.
I know people vanish fast,
hearth still warm and a cup half-cast.
I know silence, heavy and thick,
sticks to the room like November sick.
But I also know this: you’re held,
hands round you where fear got spelled.
Round and round like chalk on slate,
safe in the circle of “you’re not late.”
Home in the glow where the dark can’t press,
kiss on your hair, little forehead bless.

Go in, go in, love, don’t stay,
Last one here as we, close the day.
Last one straying, last one slow,
tailless shadow says time to go.
I’m in, I’m in, cardigan tight,
breath like mist in the amber light.
Whisper, promise, soft and true,
I’m here. I’m warm. I’m you.

See, I ain’t brave, I’m honest ash,
I’m candle maths, I’m after-flash.
I’m little-but-old, I’m kitchen lore,
I’m broom by the door and salt on the floor.
I’m copper kettle, simmer low,
tell what’s kind and tell you slow.
I’m pumpkin grin in the frosty pane,
I’m tiny spell for a tender brain.
I’m “stay inside,” not ‘cos you’re weak,
but ‘cos the night’s got teeth in its cheek.
The world out there’s not always tame,
and I ain’t letting it learn your name.
I’m pocket-warmth, I’m lamp you clutch,
don’t need much, just gentle touch.
All them hauntings, all them screams?
Half are stories, half are dreams.
Some are warnings ‘cos we care:
“Get home safe,” said like a scare.
Love’ll dress up mean in black,
just to make sure you come back.

Go in, go in, love, don’t stay,
Last one out as we, close the day.
Last one out, she’ll take your pace,
run you home to a softer place.
I’m in, I’m in, lights down low,
curtains drawn where the embers glow.
Whisper, promise, hush and hold,
you’re kept. you’re here. you’re gold.

This ain’t fear, it’s how we live,
warm our hands and learn to give.
Pain’s that bruise you tuck in sleeve,
grief’s that draft on nos Calan eve.
We don’t name all that we lost,
we don’t count the proper cost.
We just lay one bowl, one chair, one light,
make enough safe for the night.
That’s the work, the work we’ve done,
keep the hearth for everyone.

Little sheet makes a ghost, I boast,
I’m fine, I’m mine, I’m buttered toast.
I’m flicker-soft in a tinfoil crown,
socked-up feet and I’m settling down.
All them cracks in my little heart,
you fill ‘em up, you call it art.
Patchwork kid with a witch-girl soul,
I guard what’s warm ‘til the morning’s whole.
Still, still, distil,
all that ache I can’t unspill.
I’ll cuddle it quiet, stroke it tame,
won’t let it spit out your name in flame.
See, I hold tight ‘cos tight holds you,
I hold light ‘cos that’s what we do.
Night can knock and night can moan,
but night can’t have what I call home.

Go in, go in, love, don’t stay,
Last one out at the end of day.
Last one wandering, lost and late,
come inside through the iron gate.
I’m in, I’m in, and I’m allowed,
blanket round me like a cloud.
Whisper, promise, soft and small:
you’re safe. you’re mine. that’s all.

Lantern low, curtains drawn,
hold ‘til morning, be the dawn.
