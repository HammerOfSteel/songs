# Cauldron and Cradle

**Labels:** Cyfaredd
**Created:** 2025-06-01
**Edited:** 2025-06-01

---

Credo fechan, credo lan,
Credo i Dduw ac Ifan,
Rhag y dwfr, rhag y tan,
Rhag y sarffes goch ben-llydan,
Cerddais fynydd, ac o’r fynydd,
Gwelais Mair a rei Gobenydd,
A’i angel, angel ufydd,
A Duw ei hun yn Dedwydd,
A’r gwr llwyd a’i wisg wen,yn llunio llen,
Rhwng pob enaid ac uffern, amen

Rockin' the cradle with bramble and birch, Mum lit a candle, Dad built a church— Not for prayin’, nah, for castin’ in flame, Where the oak knows your soul and the ash knows your name.
Ceridwen’s brew got stirred for a year, Bubblin’ low while she whispered in fear: “Drink just a drop, and you’ll see through time, Drink the rest, and you’ll choke on the rhyme.”
I mop the floor, but I spit like Gwydion, Pendragon bars, and a Tesco obsidian. Taliesin’s ghost in a hoodie and cap, Got bars like a druid, but I talk like a brat.

Cradle to cauldron, star to stone, Lay me in bracken, leave me alone. Stir up the story, light up the spell, If I dream deep enough, I might break the shell.

Samhain night, wore Mum’s old shawl, Burned last year’s hope in a tin-foil ball. Imbolc snow, lit a match for spring, Left cream by the door for whatever it brings.
Modern rites on a busted-up block, Sage in my sock and a verse in my sock. My bruv got spark like a druid in Jordans, Bless his bars every time he’s performin’.
No robes here, just rage and rhythm, Just real talk and ancestral vision. We don’t need stone circles or chants in the mist— Just rhyme like a sigil and a voice that insists.

Turn it thrice, stir the mist, Name your fear, seal it with a kiss…

Cradle to cauldron, ash to flame, Sing me backwards, call my name. When the stars slip low and the fire burns high, I’ll be the shadow under your eye.

Don’t fear the boil, fear the stillness. Don’t fear the dark—just your witness. Every tale’s got teeth, and they bite in sleep. So dream soft, child. But don’t dream deep.

Come and wake the sleepers
Rouse them with a song
Tell them winter’s over
Though it has been long

Sing awake the flowers
Bushes, grass, and trees
Tell them spring is here now
Sun and birds and breeze
