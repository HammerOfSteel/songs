# The deep darkness - Album

The Deep Darkness

Magicant Heartspace
Dream pop meets soft rap. Ness explores his internal world. Gentle piano loops, underwater drums.
“Every enemy I beat still lives here. Every smile too.”

"Boing! In the Steam We Dream"
Playful indie-folk meets bossa nova with toy piano, light percussion (wood blocks, water drops, hand claps), vocal layers — and a dreamy final act with soft harmonies. Think Kero Kero Bonito meets Studio Ghibli's ending themes.
Concept & Structure:
Three friends (maybe teens) escaping from something — not the world, but the weight of it. They reach a weird, tucked-away onsen, run by Mr. Saturns. What happens isn't magical in the fantasy sense... but it becomes a place they'll remember forever. Laughter. Stolen glances. Floating lanterns. Soup. Steam. The kind of night where you're still young, still hurt, but a little more alive.
Structure Outline:
[Spoken Intro – Mr. Saturn Voice]
“boing. steam go up. thoughts go down. welcome. boing!”

Moonside Is... Yes
A distorted disco fever dream. Nonsensical but sharply introspective.
Broken synths, reversed vocals, surrealism.
Could function like a “mirror” track on the album.

Threed Street Shuffle
Theme: Zombies, paranoia, a town half-asleep.
Style: Funky horror-rap with Michael Jackson Thriller vibes, bass guitar groove, spooky theremin.
Concept: A silly yet eerie bop about fighting zombies with frying pans and skateboards.
“Smell that? That’s Tuesday in Threed.”

You Feel Tired but Determined
Theme: Burnout, weariness, the weight of ongoing struggle — but also the refusal to give in.
Narrative: A late-night walk through the suburbs of memory. Ness alone. Streetlights flicker, every mailbox looks familiar. He’s tired, but keeps going because there’s still something left to do.
Lyrics Vibe: Stream-of-consciousness flow. Spoken verse + minimal chorus, like an inner monologue.
Music Style: Sparse piano chords, light percussion like tapping fingers, ambient texture of buzzing power lines and wind.
“I’ve saved the world, but not myself.”
“I keep swinging even when the bat feels hollow.”

PSI: Melancholy
Theme: A meditation on emotion as magic. How grief, love, and memory become power.
Narrative: The song is structured like a PSI move itself. It starts small (like PSI Fire α), then escalates with each emotional beat. Could be Paula or Ness recalling how their sadness becomes strength.
Lyrics Vibe: Poetic, symbolic — with references to spells as metaphors for feelings.
Music Style: Moody lo-fi with gentle string swells, reversed textures, synth pads with echo trails. Builds into a soft crescendo.
“PSI: Cry — α, then β... then nothing but storm.”
“Cast from the space between missing and memory.”

Mister Saturn’s Tiny Anthem
Theme: Joy for no reason at all.
Style: Toy piano, mouth trumpet, silly chants. Ends in a surprisingly beautiful a cappella section.
Concept: Mr. Saturn teaches you how to say “I love you” in a language that makes no sense.
“Boing! Love is fuzzy socks and soup for no reason!”

Apple of Enlightenment.exe
Theme: Glitches in fate. What happens when you know what’s coming — and it still hurts?
Style: Glitch-hop, chopped synth vocals, corrupted data samples.
Concept: A song from the perspective of a character who read their destiny... and didn't like it.
“The Apple said I’d win — it just didn’t say at what cost.”

Onett to the End
Theme: Home, beginnings, endings — the cyclical nature of where you started and what you carry with you.
Narrative: A nostalgic guitar-led ballad reflecting on the town where it all began. Could be Ness visiting as an adult — realizing the place is the same, but he’s not.
Lyrics Vibe: Warm, reflective. Each verse names a place (the hilltop, the arcade, the library) and ties it to a life moment.
Music Style: Indie folk with field recordings (birds, cicadas, passing cars). Gentle fingerpicked guitar, soft harmonies, possibly a harmonica solo.
“From Onett to the end, I walked the map of me.”
“The stars still hide behind that same old tree.”

Don’t Talk to the Photographer
Theme: Breaking the fourth wall. Meta-narrative.
Style: Quirky acoustic swing with sudden polaroid camera sound FX.
Concept: Every time things get emotional, the photographer drops in. It’s both funny and frustrating.
“I blink, you flash — you never ask if I’m okay.”

Monotoli Elevator Jams
Theme: Late capitalism, elevator music, and existential boredom.
Style: Jazz-fusion interlude with live bass and lo-fi vinyl noise.
Concept: A looping instrumental track where the floors never change — or do they?

Blue Blue Night
Theme: Isolation, cult mentality, forced positivity, and reclaiming selfhood.
Narrative: The strange experience of being surrounded by people who all say the same thing — and the courage it takes to say something else. Metaphor for depression disguised as order.
Lyrics Vibe: Quirky, darkly humorous at first, then turns raw and human by the final verse.
Music Style: Lo-fi groove with off-kilter jazz drums, vocoder effects, vintage synths, and sudden musical shifts.
“They told me paint it blue, so I painted my grief.”
“Blue blue night — but I’m seeing red underneath.”

Pokey’s Playlist
Theme: The villain’s jam. Toxic ambition. The kid who wanted more.
Style: Hyperpop + distorted trap + 8-bit punk.
Concept: A satire of ego-driven villainy, but with hints of real loneliness.
“He wanted power. He got a throne made of static.”

PSI Rockin’ (R)emix
Theme: Fighting back — your way.
Style: Bombastic funky rap-rock anthem. Could be the one track where Ness truly unleashes.
Concept: An empowering, high-energy fight song — but still deeply personal.
“This isn’t magic. This is mine.”

Because I Knew You (Ending Song)
Inspired by the final scene. A song about letters not sent and friends who changed you.
Gentle acoustic with soft harmonies, slow build to a quiet refrain.
“You don’t see them again, not because they’re gone — but because you’ve arrived.”

Thank You for Calling, Dad
Theme: Distant parental love, support, and disconnection.
Style: Soft spoken word with telephone FX and ambient guitar.
Concept: A phone call that always ends with “click.”
“You always save my progress, but I don’t know how to talk to you.”
