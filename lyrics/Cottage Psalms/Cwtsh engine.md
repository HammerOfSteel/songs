# Cwtsh engine

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-05-04
**Edited:** 2025-05-04

---

My Uncle Pete’s car don’t run on fuel.
Tad says it runs on snuggles… and sausage rolls.
And maybe magic. But mostly cwtsh.

This car don’t go "vroom" — it goes mmm like toast.
Smells like mint humbugs and jacket-coat ghosts.
Got one sock off, biscuit in my sleeve,
I think she sings when we all breathe.
Mum says, “Stay buckled,” but I’m wrapped in the rug,
And Pete calls it “The Cwtshmobile” with a shrug.
Windows fog up like dragons asleep,
We write our names and draw hearts we keep.

There’s a secret lever for marshmallow boost,
And a pocket where my dreams get loose.
Glovebox got glitter from Mum’s old scarf,
And a pebble that sings when we all laugh.
Uncle Pete’s got one eye on the road,
The other one checks if my bear’s too cold.
Tad taps the dash like it’s part of a tune —
Then I see sheep in the clouds shaped like moons.

It’s the cwtsh engine, runs on love,
Cwtsh engine, hums like doves.
Goes round the bend with a biscuit beat,
Wheels like hugs and a backseat heat.
Risca hills and Cornish skies,
This old car knows lullabies.

If you cry, she slows.
If you giggle, she glows.
If you sing real soft, she steers with her nose.
Got no radio — just our voices in tune,
And a glovebox full of the smell of Mum’s room.

Once she broke down outside Llandeilo,
So I made a spell from my dad’s old biro.
“Fix her heart,” I said with chalk on the tyre,
Then Pete made tea on a pocket fire.
We danced till the stars all clapped real slow,
And she started again — like she just needed to know.
I think she’s got a soul, deep and wide —
Made of toast crumbs and tear-wiped pride.

If you see a car with fogged-up glass,
With jam stains, and laughter that lasts —
That’s us. Still moving fast… and soft.

It’s the cwtsh engine, keeps me warm,
Cwtsh engine, in every storm.
Still rolls forward on love and bread,
Still hums the tunes we never said.
From Aber to Barry, rain to sun,
This car don’t stop — till the cwtsh is done.

Tad says one day I’ll drive her.
But I don’t need to steer.
She already knows where the heart is.
