so cold this fuzzy floor.

but,
like...
why,
aye,

just to get by.

Kettles on the ready,
then a tiny little roar,
pulls me into cheeky little smile,
like a billion times before.

I reach out for the cupboard,
biscuits and a grin,
To let the ritual of the morning,
finally settle in.

It’s the Earl Grey sway,
the steam in my face,
A tiny little anchor in a frantic sort of space.

Watch the bergamot swirl in the chipped ceramic cup,
Wait for the caffeine just to wake the engine up.

The rain is on the skylight,
playing drums upon the glass,
Watching all the clouds and the moment pass.

It’s a comfort in the struggle,
it’s a rhythm in the mess,
A four-minute window just to de-compress.

"Mum, not that jacket!"
staring at the wall.

Ready to go,
she's waiting in the hall.

She’s got that gray plush hippo,
and jam upon her chin,
Giving me a cheeky smile,
to let the sunlight in.

laugh and grab the milk,
try to find my center-point,
As though the morning,
has not already proven it's point.

"dad, are you okay?"
as I stand here in the sway.

"I’m fine, little monster, just wait for the brew,"
we're just standing here waiting for you.

Mash of time,
cookie and crumble
staggering to a halt,
let's go as I fumble.
fumble out the door,
three for three,
my brilliant tiny family.

Sometimes the world is loud
and the frequency is,
just wrong,
And I forget the verses to my favorite song.
But the smell of the tea-leaves,
this hand in my hand,
Is a language that my body is allowed to understand.

It’s a pause in the panic,
it’s a hold on the fright,
A tiny little shape next to me,
in the middle of the night.

Sway with the mug,
feel the heat through the bone,
