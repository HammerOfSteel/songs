# Lands end

**Labels:** chalice and cauldron
**Created:** 2025-04-10
**Edited:** 2025-04-13

---

At first  I became insane, with long intervals of horrible sanity.

Then, looked for God. I went to a temple and I didn't find him there. Then I went to a church and I didn't find her there. I went to a mosque and I didn't find them there. Finally I looked in my heart and there god was.

Momma said grace with cracked hands tight, Whispered to air like it owed her light. I never listened, too full of fire, Thought war was a song and glory was choir. Boots in the mud, dawn dripping red, Brothers with smokes, jokes 'bout the dead. No hymns in the jungle, just rot and steam, Steel in your stomach, blood in the stream. But then— They laughed as they burned a village down, Held a child up and called it a crown. I watched, frozen, breath like stone, Couldn't kill, couldn't pray, just ran alone. Through brush, through fear, through devil's breath, Running not from death—but what was left.

I lost my name in the fire and mud, Faith bled out in a crimson flood. God was a ghost, a book on the shelf, And I was no better than the worst of myself.

Days turned to madness, nights to scream, Each step cracked another dream. I spoke to ghosts. I slept in trees, I begged the rocks for a little peace. And then— On a cliff near the sea, A boy with nets came quietly. Wore no shoes, hands full of fish, Eyes like rivers, voice like wish. He said, "Come sit. You're starving clean." Fed me bread, not judgment or mean. He lived in wood, carved with care, Made spoons and shelves and humble prayer. Talked of faith not with fear, But like it lived in every tear.

He said, “You’ve walked through hell, But grace walks too. Sometimes it just waits, In sandals by the shore, In laughter, not lore.”

He gave me fire without a match, Peace without a sermon patch. Didn’t save my soul with weight, Just taught me love is stronger than fate.

The sky was blue when they came, Three men with knives and taxman names. Said we owed for living free, That every breath had a hidden fee. He stood there calm, said, "Take what’s due," Offered his cloak and sandals too. They mocked his peace, spat in his face, I saw red bloom in sacred space. I stepped between, rifle raised, Fired a shot into the blaze. They ran. But I trembled deep, Afraid I’d shattered what I’d keep. He touched my hand, no anger there, Just a look that said: “You still care.”

I fought for love, not for the flag, Not for orders or bloody brag. I stood for him, and in that fight, Something returned: a flicker of light.

We built a table from driftwood pale, He told me the story of Jonah and whale. Of Lazarus sleeping in tombs too tight, Of broken men turned into light. And slowly, I began to see, God was not a place—but who sat with me. His laugh was psalm, his hands were hymn, His eyes turned my doubts dim. We planted garlic, we made jam, He showed me joy in “I don’t give a damn.” One night, stars all bending wide, He said, “If I’m wrong, at least I tried.” And I said, “If you're wrong, then I still believe— 'Cause what you gave, I can’t un-receive.”

I found my faith in his quiet breath, In love that rose and wept and left. No altar but the open shore, No priest but the man who loved me more.

He didn’t walk on water. But he walked across my war-torn mind Like it was nothing. He didn’t raise the dead. But he raised me.

And We loved with a love that was more than love. I was never really insane except upon occasions when my heart was touched.
