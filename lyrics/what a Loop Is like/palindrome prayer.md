# palindrome prayer

**Labels:** what a Loop Is like
**Created:** 2025-09-03
**Edited:** 2025-09-03

---

Count with me, lights low, mercy to and fro.
Sing it now, soft and slow, then backward let it go.

Mirror me, mercy me, morning in a cup,
kettle breath, little bells, bubbles rising up.
I write it on the window where the fog makes lines,
left to right to left again, the loop still shines.
“May all hearts be held,” and I hum the tone,
“may every small wandering find a way home.”
If sorrow’s sold in silver and in gold so cold,
let kindness be the coin we pass hand-to-hold.
I hopscotch hope, one, two, chalk on the stone,
step on a square that says “never alone.”
If I get lost, I’ll rhyme until I’m found,
forward I whisper, backward I round.

May all beings be free from suffering,
from suffering be free all beings, may.
Live on, no evil.
Let mercy meet me; me meet mercy, let.
Heart to home to heart, we pray,
pray we, heart to home to heart.

Little drum under my ribs keeps time,
handclaps scatter like a flock in a rhyme.
If shame says “shrink,” I answer “grow,”
I plant two feet and let the soft wind blow.
“May all beings be so bold as to age with grace,”
wrinkles like river-maps on a laughing face.
The loop comes back, but I don’t have to break,
I can bow, I can breathe, I can bend not ache.
I ferry my feelings on a paper boat,
upstream, downstream, still keeps afloat.
What I can’t carry, I set in the light,
I turn it around till the edges feel right.

May all beings be free to roam and to rest,
to rest and to roam be free all beings, may.
Never odd or even.
Hold hope; let go, go let; hope hold.
Find their heart, then find their home,
home their find, then heart their find.

Level, civic, kayak, radar, solos in a ring,
word-wheels turning while the bright bells sing.
If I say “forgive,” will “forgiven” say me?
When I open one lock, another finds a key.
Round the room the river runs, hush and hum,
I bless what I’ve been and what I will become.

“May all beings be free from suffering,” I start,
then I tuck the line gently in the pocket of my heart.
If anger bites its tail, I’ll offer it bread,
a kinder meal for a storm-tossed head.
If grief is a mirror that doubles the night,
I’ll carry two candles and share my light.
If fear says “freeze,” I answer “flow,”
like skipping stones that learn to go slow.
“May none be always alone,” I vow,
say it forward, say it backward, now.

May none be always alone,
alone always be none, may.
Live on, no evil.
Let mercy meet us; us meet mercy, let.
May all beings be free from suffering,
from suffering be free all beings, may.

If you lose the words, just breathe and start the row.
We’ll go again, soft and slow, to and fro.
