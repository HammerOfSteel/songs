# Blue Blue Night

**Labels:** The Deep Darkness
**Created:** 2025-04-18
**Edited:** 2025-04-18

---

They said "Blue is peace," so I wore the robe,
Sipped the cola, watched the town reload.
Same old smile on every face,
Paula whispered, “This place’s got no grace.”

Signs say “JOY” but the font feels scared,
Pastel pain in the window glare.
They shake my hand like a script they learned,
While blue torches flicker and the sky don't turn.

One guy said, “Brother, your aura’s pale!”
Offered me paint from a holy pail.
The preacher grinned with his pixel jaw,
Said, “You’ll feel better once you forget what you saw.”

Next day I woke with a mushroom crown,
Thoughts rerouted, upside down.
My name came out in a sing-song jingle,
And fear showed up in a cheery jingle.

Paula walked backward, said “Something's off.”
I nodded — then forgot how to cough.
We smiled like puppets in a shop display,
Bought our groceries in enforced ballet.

“Blue blue bright!” the children screamed,
While painting fences in their dreams.
A mailbox said “Good morning, grief!”
So I mailed it back my disbelief.

Paula cracked first — ripped her robe,
Said “I’ve cried better colors on a bathroom floor.”
She smeared brown dirt on the preacher’s grin,
Said, “This is what truth looks like in skin.”

I took my bat and it bent like foam,
The cult leader laughed from a golden dome.
So I hit the sign that read “NO PAIN,”
And it shattered like fake windowpane.

Someone said, “Blue is the future!”
I said, “Nah. Blue’s a filter.”
Poo’s voice echoed like a bell in rust:
“You cannot control what you dare not trust.”

We burned the maps. The sky blinked red.
The vocoder loop stuttered and bled.
Blue chants failed, became static spit,
And a thousand painted smiles just quit.

We ran uphill ‘til the color dropped,
Until the trees looked wrong — then stopped.
Paula laughed, “The stars are back.”
I said, “Yeah. So am I.”
And we didn’t look back.

They told me paint it blue.
So I painted my grief.
And it peeled off like sky
when the clouds finally spoke.

Blue blue night —
but I’m seeing red underneath.
