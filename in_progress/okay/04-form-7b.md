# Form 7B
**Album:** Okay
**Track:** 4
**Created:** 2026-03-16

---

## The Premise

Someone has arrived at a counter to file a report. They need to register something that happened — something large and permanent and unclassifiable. The clerk is not cruel. The clerk is simply working with the system available, and the system was not built for this.

The song moves between the person at the counter and the clerk's responses — two voices, two registers. The bureaucracy is the joke. The bureaucracy is also the accurate description of how the world receives grief: politely, professionally, and in completely the wrong category.

The grief is never named. It is "the item." It is "the thing in question." It is "the matter you're referring to." The form cannot hold it. This is the whole song.

---

## Style
- Gorillaz: "Feel Good Inc" meets "19/2000" — that slightly cold, funky groove with menace underneath the bounce
- Two voices: the Person (slightly ragged, British, trying) and the Clerk (smooth, professional, utterly inadequate — almost robotic in its helpfulness)
- The Person raps/speaks in an anxious, slightly unravelling cadence; the Clerk sings in a clean, pleasant, bureaucratic register
- Hold music appears as a structural element — literally a few bars of bland hold tone between sections, a recurring motif
- Sentence-to-sentence rhyme throughout the Person's sections; the Clerk's lines have their own internal rhyme — neat, contained, tidy

## Instrumentation
- Feel Good Inc groove: driving drum machine, deep synth bass, clean and slightly cold
- A recurring two-note keyboard motif that evokes hold music — alternates between sections
- Guitar: sparse, slightly angular, enters in verse 2
- The Clerk's vocal sections: slightly over-produced, compressed, almost like a phone line — the contrast with the Person's close, raw vocal is the point
- Bridge: everything drops to just the hold music motif on keys — bare, slightly absurd, then the beat drops back
- Outro: the groove continues, the Person's voice gradually drops out, the Clerk's loop plays over the top until only the hold music remains

## Production Notes
- Person: close-mic'd, British, male — anxious energy, not unhinged; the composure is holding but only just
- Clerk: slightly processed vocal, warm but professional — could be either gender; pleasant in a way that makes it worse
- The hold music bars (4 bars, bland two-note keyboard) should appear three times: after verse 1, after the bridge, and at the very end
- The comedy must be played completely straight — no winking, no nudging; the straighter it's played the harder it lands
- The raw moment in the bridge must feel genuinely unguarded before the beat snaps back
