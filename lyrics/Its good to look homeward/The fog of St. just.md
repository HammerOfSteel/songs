# The fog of St. just

**Labels:** Its good to look homeward
**Created:** 2024-12-02
**Edited:** 2024-12-02

---

The fog rolls in, a curtain drawn,
The cliffs of St. Just wake with dawn.
Whispers rise where seas collide,
A world unseen, where myths abide.

She walks ahead, her steps are light,
Through veils of gray, soft as the night.
He follows close, his heart in tune,
As fog enfolds the ancient dunes.

In the fog of St. Just, we lose and we find,
The echoes of legends, the threads that bind.
Stories linger where the wild winds stray,
In the mist, we’re part of their sway.

She points to shapes in the shrouded air,
A castle high, a maiden fair.
"Do you hear the sea’s lament?" she asks,
"Every wave wears a storyteller’s mask."

They pause to rest by the ocean’s edge,
Among the stones, a secret hedge.
"The fog," she muses, "is more than gray;
It hides what the sun can’t show today."

Her voice grows soft, her gaze turns bright,
She whispers, "A new story stirs tonight."
He stops, surprised, then takes her hand,
In her words, a future planned.

Hand in hand, they walk once more,
The cliffs behind, the sea a roar.
In her eyes, the morning glows,
A future bright the fog bestows.
