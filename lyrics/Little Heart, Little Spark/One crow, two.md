# One crow, two

**Labels:** Little Heart, Little Spark
**Created:** 2025-08-11
**Edited:** 2025-08-11

---

One crow, sorrow… two crows, joy,
three for a girl, four for a boy.
Five for silver, six for gold,
seven’s a secret never told.

Count ‘em on the wire, black coats in a row,
eyes like ink in the frostbite glow.
Shadow on the hedge, it’s a sign, they say,
tip your hat, turn ‘round, keep the bad luck away.

Footstep echo on a narrow lane bend,
whispers in the wind that don’t quite end.
A feather in the mud like a mark you’ve missed,
same as the first time your palm got kissed.

One crow, sorrow… two crows, joy,
three for a girl, four for a boy.
Five for silver, six for gold,
seven’s a secret never told.

Black wing flick like a coin in the sky,
catch it if you can but the luck runs dry.
Boot scuff dirt, draw a ring in chalk,
break it and the dead will come to walk.

Count from the left or count from the right,
see the one-eyed crow and you lose your sight.
Patter like rain on the chapel roof,
tap three times if you need the proof.

Spin in place, don’t let ‘em see,
tie that thread ‘round the ash tree.

One crow, sorrow… two crows, joy,
three for a girl, four for a boy.
Five for silver, six for gold,
seven’s a secret never told.

Breath in mist and the world stands still,
signs on the fence and the top of the hill.
Numbers in the hedge like a code to crack,
turn your coat ‘round, never look back.

Old wives’ tales on a young tongue’s hum,
fate’s just a game till the dark days come.
One black feather in a mason’s wall,
keeps out the curse when the nightbirds call.

Crow on the sill, keep my secrets in flight,
carry them safe through the teeth of night.

One crow, sorrow… two crows, joy,
three for a girl, four for a boy.
Five for silver, six for gold,
seven’s a secret never told.
