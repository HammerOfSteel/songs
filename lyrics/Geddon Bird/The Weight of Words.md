# The Weight of Words

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-26
**Edited:** 2024-10-26

---

(Verse 1)
We sit in the quiet, no words left to say,
Thoughts hanging like smoke at the end of the day.
Lucas taps rhythms on the edge of his chair,
While I trace letters that aren’t really there.

There’s a weight in the air, too heavy to lift,
A silence that widens, a space where we drift.
Unspoken thoughts echo louder inside,
Words we don’t say we still can’t hide.

(Chorus)
The weight of words lies in what’s left unsaid,
In the echoes that linger and turn in your head.
They build up like letters never sent away,
Stories untold that still make us sway.

(Verse 2)
Lucas says, "Words are a mirror, a flame,
A truth you can hold or a shadow to blame."
I laugh, but his gaze pulls me deeper within—
How much of myself do I keep hidden?

I think of old letters tucked under my bed,
Each one a fragment of things I never said.
And in this room, with the silence so near,
The weight of it all feels too heavy to clear.

(Chorus)
The weight of words lies in what’s left unsaid,
In the echoes that linger and turn in your head.
They build up like letters never sent away,
Stories untold that still make us sway.

(Bridge – Spoken Word Section)
Lucas leans closer, his voice low and clear:
"Every word we choose pulls us nearer,
Or it pulls us apart, thread by thread.
Even silence is something said."

I close my eyes, feeling the strain,
Words held inside like drops of rain.
Some truths are too sharp, others too bright—
So we carry them quietly through the night.

(Chorus)
The weight of words lies in what’s left unsaid,
In the echoes that linger and turn in your head.
They build up like letters never sent away,
Stories untold that still make us sway.

(Outro)
In the quiet, we sit, both knowing the cost,
That some things are spoken, and some things are lost.
But through the silence, a thread remains tight—
Words will come, just not tonight.
