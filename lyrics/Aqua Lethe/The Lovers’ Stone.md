# The Lovers’ Stone

**Labels:** Aqua Lethe
**Created:** 2025-05-22
**Edited:** 2025-05-22

---

We weren’t made for gentle ends, love—
we were carved from scream and split.
Two halves flung from the same old storm,
sliced from heat, then forced to fit.
You cracked in summer, I caved in frost,
you kissed to kill, I held to cost.
They say that love’s a softer place—
but it only found us when we lost.

You and me, we broke to bind,
torn and bled then realigned.
You can't become 'til you’ve been thrown—
we found each other…
at the Lovers’ Stone.

You came sharp, heart in bits—
spoke like knives, no half-meant shit.
I weren’t clean, I weren’t gold—
just burnin’ low, and twenty-fold.
They said love mends—nah, it breaks again.
Just a quiet beat hid in the din.
You looked at me like I weren’t wrong,
and that’s when I knew I’d been gone too long.

You and me, we cracked to shine,
dripped in dark then crossed the line.
Can’t fuse the soul till the shape is known—
we bled together…
at the Lovers’ Stone.

We weren’t made for harmony—
not like birds or bells or breeze.
We’re the kind that scream to speak,
but when we quiet,
we don’t need.

You tore fast, I stitched slow,
but both of us were built to go.
Not to last, but to arrive,
not to hold—just to survive.
But love ain’t safe, and love ain’t kind,
it’s salt in wound, it’s flame in rind.
And still I’d take it, still I’d bleed—
if you’d break with me, I’ve got what I need.

You and me, we split to mend,
not the start, but not the end.
Whatever we were, it stands alone—
etched in time,
as the Lovers’ Stone.

Break me again, if it leads me home.
