# The voice in the silent Pines

**Labels:** childrens books - star sailor
**Created:** 2024-10-05
**Edited:** 2024-10-05

---

Intro:

Through the mist of the pines, where the shadows lay deep,
A lone wolf wandered, where memories keep.
His gaze lost in silence, his heart heavy and cold,
Carrying a sadness that’s quiet and old.


---

Verse 1:

The pack watched from a distance, unsure what to say,
Each word felt too small, so they stayed far away.
But silence grew heavy, like a stone on his chest,
And the wolf wandered farther, lost and distressed.

Star Sailor appeared, her glow soft and warm,
“He needs you to reach him, to calm this storm.”
“It’s not about perfect words, just a small sound,”
“To show him you’re here, to bring light around.”


---

Chorus:

“For silence can linger, and deepen the pain,
But a voice in the dark is like gentle rain.”
“Even a whisper, a question, a call,
Can show him you’re near, can shatter the wall.”

“It’s not about knowing or saying what’s right,
But being beside him, and bringing your light.”
“So take one small step, just let your voice show,
That love doesn’t fade, even when we feel low.”


---

Verse 2:

The youngest wolf stepped forward, voice trembling and low,
“I… I miss her too, I wanted you to know.”
The Father Wolf paused, his eyes filled with tears,
He turned to the pack, caught between hope and fears.

The others stepped closer, their voices so faint,
“We were afraid,” whispered one, “of adding to your weight.”
“But silence has hurt us, and kept us apart,”
“So we’re here now, dear brother, to open our hearts.”


---

---

Outro:

The pack gathered closer, no more words left behind,
And Star Sailor smiled, her glow soft and kind.
“It’s okay to feel lost, to not have all the ways,
But silence is lifted when love finds a place.”

The Father Wolf nodded, his eyes clearing bright,
“Thank you,” he whispered, “for being my light.”
And the mist slowly faded, the dawn breaking through,
With the pack standing closer, all their voices like dew.

“Even a whisper, a tear, or a sigh,”
“Can light up the night, can lift up the sky.”
