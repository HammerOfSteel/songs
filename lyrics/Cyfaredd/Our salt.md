# Our salt

**Labels:** Cyfaredd
**Created:** 2025-06-01
**Edited:** 2025-06-01

---

tedium, boredom, boil and bubble,
add a spice, then add it double,
stir it well and let it settle,
in a kettle,
made of metal.

what's your fancy, what's your trouble?
basin clogged with dwarven stubble?

Barely two, wandered out through fern and flame,
Gwent held her breath, never spoke her name.
Crossed the Wye where the whispers wade,
Water like silver, memory decayed.

Birds stilled their song, leaves held their hush,
Feet bare, voice small, heart a hush.
Came to the tower in the dusk-hung blue,
Slept at the step with a moonlit shoe.

Salt in her hair and none to claim,
River stole her given name.
Fog on the banks, stars wouldn't stay,
She forgot the road, and the world looked away.

He found her there in a spellbound yawn,
With crumbs in her palm and dew in the dawn.
Tried three tongues, tried mirror and flame,
But the girl just blinked — she had no name.

Didn’t mean to keep her, didn’t mean to care,
But winter came, and she braided his hair.
Spoke in the old runes by accident once,
Drew glyphs in chalk all in a bunce.

Nightshade sprigs and marrow mist,
Snail-shell ink and serpent twist.
Nine drops blood from the tongue unspoken,
Boil ‘til the silence bends and broken.

Sleep in stone, wake in sky,
Answer not to where or why.
Name the wind, forget your kin,
Breathe in salt. Begin again.

She dreamt it then — a hall of flame,
Owain’s crest, a sister’s name.
Woke with tears, hand clutching coal,
Her past returned, but not her role.

He saw the weight, the gold in her gaze,
Said, “You may go, if you choose the ways.”
But she shook her head like mist in spring:
“You gave me more than remembering.”

give me dragons, give me daggers,
give me jewels with emerald feathers!
give me—“what?
what's this, right now?
of course I know exactly how!”

roots to find, true essence to distill,
adventure?
no, but pays the bills.
