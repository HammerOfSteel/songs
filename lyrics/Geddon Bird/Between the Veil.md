# Between the Veil

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-28
**Edited:** 2024-10-28

---

(Verse 1)
In silence, she comes, soft fur and bone,
A fox with eyes like shadows sown.
She curls by my side, warm as flame,
A quiet nudge, and I know her name.

I breathe her in, this spirit of night,
Her coat like embers, her touch so light.
She jumps to the path, her gaze pulled tight,
And I follow her shape through silver light.

(Chorus)
Between the veil, where shadows wake,
Where love and loss entwine and ache.
A fox beside me, her steps my guide,
Through memories hidden, love left behind.

(Verse 2)
She leads me to a village edge,
Where lanterns flicker beneath the hedge.
Inside, I see them, faces aglow,
Laughing, remembering all they know.

I burst through the door, heart split in two,
“How can you laugh?” but their love feels true.
They lift their glasses to honor her name,
And tears fall like whispers in soft refrain.

(Chorus)
Between the veil, where shadows wake,
Where love and loss entwine and ache.
A fox beside me, her steps my guide,
Through memories hidden, love left behind.

(Bridge)
A toast to the laughter, the joy she gave,
To moments that linger, bright and brave.
Their smiles weave with sorrow’s thread,
As I feel her presence, though words are unsaid.

The fox waits outside, beneath the stars,
Watching the scene through half-open bars.
Her eyes catch mine as my tears flow free,
And I wake alone, but her warmth stays with me.

(Chorus)
Between the veil, where shadows wake,
Where love and loss entwine and ache.
A fox beside me, her steps my guide,
Through memories hidden, love left behind.

(Outro)
I rise to the window, snow coats the ground,
The quiet of dawn, no other sound.
At the yard’s edge, a fox waits near,
She watches, then vanishes—yet I feel her here.
