# Oort Cloud

**Labels:** Fylgja
**Created:** 2025-10-16
**Edited:** 2025-10-16

---

“Tad says the sky’s full of dust like glitter,
sleeping in circles, never bitter.
When it drifts down, I blow it back up,
makes the stars giggle".

"Oh aye, it's a stellar cloud".

Cup gone cold, I stir it again,
steam writes poems on the windowpane.
Same damp lane, same bin-day rain,
same thought crawling through the brain.
Swore I’d break through once I could cope,
but the fog just folded, called me home.
Walked the fields till the hedges spoke,
old crows laughing, “Mate, it’s smoke.”
There’s comfort hid in the muck and grind,
if you stop for a sec, the stars align.
Every orbit hums, small and deep,
what you sow in ache, you’ll harvest in sleep.
So I chat to the dark like a daft old friend,
“Stay for tea, love, there’s no end.”

“Pan ddaw’r nos, dw i’n dal fy nghariad,
fel goleuni bach, yn fy mraich i’n gwahodd.”

Rhymes with the kettle, hisses in time,
hearts don’t heal, they learn to rhyme.

Oort cloud, round me close,
spin me soft where the starlight froze.
No road out, no line drawn through,
just me and the dust and the thought of you.
Oort cloud, crowd me kind,
teach my bones your patient mind.

Ash in the breeze, brass in the throat,
whisper of coal where the prayers once float.
I tried to punch holes clean through the mist,
got my knuckles kissed by the night I missed.
Turned to the hedge, said “cheers, old sage,”
he said, “There’s no door, just a turning page.”
I laughed, I swore, I set my pace,
the mud kept time, the sky kept grace.
Then down by the stile where the moss grows thick,
I found a peace that would not stick.
So I sat till dawn made cider-gold,
and the wind told tales my heart once told.

“Rydyn ni’n cylchdroi, nid i fynd heibio,
ond i ddod adref, law yn llaw â’r haul.”

It rhymes with my breath, comes round again,
you don’t get through, you live within.

Oort cloud, loud and low,
hum in the marrow where old lights glow.
Cariad bach, don’t drift too far,
I’ll see you again, wherever we are.
Oort cloud, shroud me mild,
the dust’s no curse, it’s the air gone wild.

Every orbit’s a hymn half-sung,
ashes in rhythm with a farmer’s lung.
There’s alchemy hid in the breath we take,
you burn, you spin, you bend, you break.
No end, no edge, just ember and dew,
and the cloud hums back, “I’m part of you.”

By the gate where galaxies seep,
I plant my grief, it learns to sleep.
Tea steam curls like Saturn’s ring,
small things teach the biggest thing.
A lamb kicks snow off April clay,
sun-through-mist makes the hurt okay.
Hiraeth’s hymn on a cracked old phone,
voice on repeat: “You’re not alone.”

“Pan ddaw’r dydd, fe ddaw’r goleuni,
ac mae’r llwch yn dawnsio’n dawel inni.”

So I smile at the fog, “aye, cheers,”
we’ve been circlin’ this road for years.
Same slow turn, same tune, new key,
the cloud was never it, it was me.

Oort cloud, crowd me deep,
spin me slow till the heart can sleep.
No getting through, no grand escape,
just stardust learning its humble shape.
Oort cloud, sound so true,
I’m dust, I’m rain, I’m part o’ you.
Round and round, the circle hums,
tea goes cold and the daylight comes.

"I'll be out here,
No need to rush tad"
