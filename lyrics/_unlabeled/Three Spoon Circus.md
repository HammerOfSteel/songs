# Three Spoon Circus

**Created:** 2025-04-10
**Edited:** 2025-04-10

---

Where’s the sodding toaster gone?
She used it to dry her socks again.
Mammy made pancakes!
Did she now?

Three spoons in the porridge, one bowl cracked
Radio Cymru hummin’ in the back
Tiima’s got jam on her tiny fist
Mam does her hair with a sleepy twist

You say “mornin’, love,” in your cereal fog
She sings to the kettle like it’s a dog
I catch her dancing with bare feet on tile
Burnt toast, bright grin, that look, that smile

It’s a three spoon circus, no ring, no tent
Just a flat on fire and the rent half spent
There’s glitter on the kettle, syrup on the floor
But I wouldn’t trade a single drawer

It’s oi and clatter, it’s love and spill
Our tiny byd bach that’s never still
It’s mam and her, it’s you and tea —
My morning mess, my family

Tiima shouts “milo!” with her teddy held high
Mam catches the spoon mid-flight from the sky
She kisses her cheek and wipes off the jam
Then places cawl on the hob and chops some lamb 

You groan at the mess, I laugh like a loon
She says, “This flat’s got more love than room”
The eggs are cold, the tea’s gone grey
But the sun still rises in her face each day

It’s a three spoon circus, no script, no cue
Just toast in the duvet and love that grew
There’s crayons in the cutlery, dolls in the sink
And time moving faster than we all think

It’s cwtsh and crash, it’s spoons and song
Our rhythm might be wonky but it’s never wrong
Mam’s soft hum and Tiima’s roar —
This flat’s a stage we all adore

“She’s fed the radiator some raisins again.”
“Mam said it’s the radiator’s birthday.”
“We should buy it a balloon.”
“We did. Last year.”
“She’s got your smile, you know.”
“I see it every day.”

It’s a three spoon circus, let the kettle scream
Let the toast be charcoal and the dishes dream
Our tent is a kitchen with no clean plate —
But our hearts are full and the door’s not late

It’s dawn and daftness, it’s cwtsh and clang
Our love’s in the jam jar, not the wedding band
You, and her, and mam’s soft laugh —
My chaos, my joy, my photograph

“She’s fallen asleep in the cereal box.”
“Mam’s curled up too.”
“I’ll do the washing up.”
“Nah. Just sit.”
