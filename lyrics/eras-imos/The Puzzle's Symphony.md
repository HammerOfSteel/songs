# The Puzzle's Symphony

**Labels:** eras-imos
**Created:** 2024-11-27
**Edited:** 2024-11-27

---

Golden leaves drift in the sunlight,
Crisp air whispers on the breeze,
Steps crunch softly on the pathway,
A father, a son, and the autumn trees.

The playground waits beyond the clearing,
Slides and swings in quiet glow,
The boy moves close, his hand out reaching,
"Let’s go slow, Dad. Let’s go slow."

It’s not a race; it’s not a climb,
Each step is part of the design,
A world of wonder in his eyes,
The puzzle’s symphony comes alive.

The sandbox hums with endless stories,
Shapes of castles rise and fall,
“Look, a star,” he softly murmurs,
Tracing patterns on the wall.

The father smiles, his heart is glowing,
"That’s a masterpiece you’ve made."
No need for words; the world is showing,
The beauty within every shade.

It’s not a race; it’s not a climb,
Each turn reveals a thread of time,
A quiet magic, a life divine,
The puzzle’s symphony comes alive.

Beneath the swings, the golden branches,
Cast their shadows on the ground,
“Can you see the hidden dances?”
“Dad, the leaves, they make a sound.”

The father watches as the boy moves,
Spinning circles in the sun,
Each moment woven, full of meaning,
A silent story just begun.

The boy looks back and softly whispers,
"Is the sky a piece of this?"
The father kneels, their eyes together,
“Yes, the sky, the stars, and this.”

It’s not a race; it’s not a climb,
Each thread reveals the grand design,
A world of wonder in his mind,
The puzzle’s symphony comes alive.

As shadows stretch, the day is fading,
Autumn’s chill begins to grow,
They wander back through glowing pathways,
"Let’s go slow, Dad. Let’s go slow."

The father feels his heart expanding,
As he lifts his son to see,
The world is bigger, richer, brighter,
Through the lens of his symphony.

Golden leaves drift in the twilight,
Steps crunch softly through the trees,
Every piece they placed together,
The puzzle’s song, their harmony.
