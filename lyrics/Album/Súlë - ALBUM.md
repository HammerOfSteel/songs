✦ Súlë – Album Poem (in Quenya)

(Rhyme-focused, spell-like)

Súlë linta, lúmë canta
Nai arata, nai alanta
Eä calma, náre hlasta
Cé elenya, cé auta-rásta


---

✧ English poetic rendering (not literal):

Breath is swift, time is wide
May it rise, may it slide
There is light, and flame that hears
A star may come, or vanish near

🎼 Album Title: Súlë

Language: Quenya (with occasional root-derived neologisms)
Musical Style:
harp, lyre, flute, monochord, nyckelharpa, frame drum, ambient pads, choral layering, elven trance, reversed vocals, bowed guitar, modular synth textures, subtle modern percussion (breath samples, heartbeats, metallic rhythm)

Central Theme:
The journey of the soul — through the veils of time, memory, the elemental world, and its entanglements with forgotten ages. Each song is like an invocation, a sung spell in Quenya — not telling a story, but evoking a phase, a truth, a ripple.


---

🌬️ Tracklist:


---

1. Súlë (Breath)

Theme: The first breath — not of life, but of existence. The soul's awakening.
Mood: Minimal, one vocal thread repeating syllables like wind over snow. First and last track use the same motif.
→ “Á súlëa, nai elenya…” (May the breath carry us, like a star…)


---

2. Lómequen (Shadow-Dreamer)

Theme: A soul walking between sleep and memory.
Style: Harp with deep ambient synths. Ethereal background voice whispers backward Quenya words.
→ Inspired by the idea that dreams are echoes of the world-before.
Beat: more modern like an acoustic welsh harp almost reel like


---

3. Mírë Mornë (Black Jewel)

Theme: The weight of forgotten sorrow embedded in the soul’s strand.
Mood: imagine Mongolian throat singing taken to the next level, the polyphonic tones of the throat singing not only create those beautiful whistling cutting harmonica but they also transform and lead into beautiful acoustic instruments like zitar plucks and various traditional flutes etc and back and forth that occurs in many layers, then we have a child's voice do the quenya mantra lyrics.
→ A dark yet beautiful invocation of memory as treasure and curse.


---

4. Naiquë (Fading)

Theme: The soul’s passage through decay, loss, and the entropy of the ages.
Style: Lo-fi recording of a bowed monochord dissolving into filtered breaths and static.
→ Lyrics only partially audible; fading like the past itself.


---

5. Hrestale (Awash)

Theme: The soul submerged — not drowning, but resting in the waters of time.
Mood: Lapping water rhythms, ambient tide pulses, elven vocals drifting in and out.
→ No clear melody, just waves of tonality and syllables. Spell-like.


---

6. Sílalyë (You Shine)

Theme: Recognition of another soul — two threads touching across eons.
Style: Duet in layered Quenya vocals, shimmering plucked strings, bell tones.
→ “You shone in me before names, before memory.”


---

7. Núrë Vána (Secret Beauty)

Theme: The unnoticed, sacred undercurrent of the world.
Mood: Subtle rhythmic tapping, echoing ocarina tones, low choral hums.
→ Inspired by the feeling of discovering something ancient, sacred, and alive.


---

8. Fanyarelya (Your Veil of Clouds)

Theme: The soul’s breath caught in the veils of the sky — shifting forms, unknowable truths.
Style: Wind chime samples, reversed instruments, distant thunder, soft choral invocations.
→ Lyrics obscured in layers of atmospheric haze.


---

9. Eäri (Seas)

Theme: The collective breath of all souls — the vastness of what is and was.
Mood: A fuller, orchestral sound. Modulated harp runs, ocean field recordings, layered harmonies.
→ Feels like standing on a shore of all time.


---

10. Súlë (Ennála) (Breath, Again)

Theme: The final breath loops into the first. The soul returns to the source.
Style: Echoes the first track but with more voices, more resonance, more silence.
→ Ends on a single held note that fades into silence.
