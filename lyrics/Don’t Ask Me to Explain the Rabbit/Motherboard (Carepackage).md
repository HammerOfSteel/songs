# Motherboard (Carepackage)

**Labels:** Don’t Ask Me to Explain the Rabbit
**Created:** 2025-04-19
**Edited:** 2025-04-19

---

I’m basically a gangster now.

You’re wearing socks with sandwiches on them.

That’s… streetwear.

Cringe is cool. Always has been.

She’s the port, the pulse, the heat beneath,
the wires hum with her belief.
She knows the voltage, keeps the beat —
Without her laugh, the world’s offbeat.

Carepackage queen, Ethernet dream,
she codes her love into whipped cream.
Mandolin riffs and laundry folded,
a chiptune lullaby — never scolded.

Motherboard, soft and strong,
patching bugs where I went wrong.
She doesn’t flash, she doesn’t brag —
but every hug resets the lag.

She’s the heart inside the case —
held me firm, then gave me space.
Didn’t ask for praise or fame —
just whispered: “You're enough. Now game.”

“I’m a gangster!”

“You’re a donut.”

She danced to 8-bit in the hall,
wearing bunny slippers, standing tall.

She fixed my code without the screen,
made meaning soft and in between.
She said, “Glitching’s part of art,”
then fed me toast shaped like a heart.

Motherboard, cradle of calm,
her voice could turn a storm to balm.
Didn’t upgrade, didn’t flee —
she let me break, then booted me.

Knows when to ping, when to ignore —
knows I’m safe when I close the door.
Love’s not loud, and care ain’t fame —
but without her spark, I lose the game.

She didn’t glow in neon pride,
she just stayed by my bug-eyed side.
She didn’t crash when I rebelled —
she loved, and laughed, and still upheld.

She gave away and asked for none,
lit every fuse and called it fun.
“The act of love,” she softly said,
“is showing up. With jam. With bread.”

“I know one duty — it’s to care.
To stay when no one else is there.”
She’s not a queen, not coded gold —
but love like hers... rewrites the soul.

I once declared I didn’t need,
I rolled my eyes, I craved the speed.
But when I broke, guess who replied?
Her steady hum. She never lied.

She held my silence like a vow,
and somehow still, she hears me now.
Through every beep and bug and ping —
she is the thread in everything.

Motherboard, mother flame,
home in pieces, love the same.
You gave it all, and then some more —
your arms were always the restore.

Now I know what power means —
not control, but patching seams.
You didn’t flex, you didn’t shout —
you just rebooted all my doubt.

Mom… you’re kind of OP.

I nerfed myself so you could level up.

…that’s actually really deep.

That’s love, darling. Now drink water.
