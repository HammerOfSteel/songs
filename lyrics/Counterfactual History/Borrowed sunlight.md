# Borrowed sunlight

**Labels:** Counterfactual History
**Created:** 2025-07-03
**Edited:** 2025-07-03

---

I saw her dancing in the kitchen light…
Laughing like I never broke.
But I’m just fog in the window now,
Borrowed sunlight, that’s all I wrote.

Walking down streets I once called mine
But the faces don’t flinch, I’m outta time
That’s my coat on his back, my boots in stride
But that joy ain’t mine, still, I don't mind
See, she’s older now, curls wild and free
Same smile, but it don’t reach me
She says “Dada” and he lifts her high
And I’m just breath caught in the sky
Not bitter, nah, just soft around the bone
Like I lent him the dream I never got shown

Borrowed sunlight on your hair
And I was never really there
But every shadow’s shaped like love
From the trees, from skies above
I gave my name, gave up my place
For your smile in his embrace

Same lullaby I used to hum
Plays on the radio while she drums
Tiny feet on kitchen tile
I stand in the doorway, cry a while
She draws suns with her left hand
He tapes ‘em up like it’s a master plan
They never know I wrote that rhyme
On the back of the bill by the window blind
And that kite she flies in the garden breeze?
That string? It was tied from me to these…

Every song I left, every truth I bent
Was just a letter I never sent
To say: “Keep her warm, keep her wild
And when it storms, let her be a child.”

Borrowed sunlight in her laugh
And I’m the ghost in every path
Not a hero, not a name
Just the wind that moved the flame
He holds her hand, I hold the night
And vanish in the morning light

She turned seven last week, carved her name
In the tree where I once carved the same
He read her poems I used to know
She giggled, said, “Dada, you’re slow.”
I smiled though it hurt to hear
‘Cause that joy’s worth every year
Worth every crack in this old man’s chest
Every breath I gave, she’s blessed
I fade with grace, not grief this round
Sometimes love means not stickin’ around

You don’t have to see me to know I was real
My footsteps echo in the way you feel
When she says “I’m safe,” when she sings off-key
That’s me, boy. That’s me.

Borrowed sunlight, soft and low
And that’s the way I chose to go
Not as father, not as flame
But as the silence in her name
She lives, she loves, she runs, she flies
And I am only in the skies

Every choice is a door
Not all are meant to stay open.
Some just let the light through,
And close behind you
