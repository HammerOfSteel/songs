# Three Sheets to the Wind

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-04-04
**Edited:** 2025-04-04

---

Salt in the boots, last step on the track,
Peter grins, slaps my back.

“S’pose that’s it, then?”

Suppose it is.
But you don’t stop walking just ‘cause the road gives in.

Boots worn thin, sea in my beard,
Plymouth calls, but we ain't yet steered.
Lanterns swing on the harborside,
gulls play tag with the turning tide.
We walked for miles, we laughed, we swore,
feet kissed cliffs and begged for more.
Peter points to the rising moon, says, 

“Feels like she’s watching.” 

I nod. “Aye, soon.”
Hands in pockets, steps in sync,
two old ghosts in the tavern blink.

Three sheets to the wind,
but my heart stays tight,
feet on the road,
but my soul sails right.
Call it a storm, call it a tune,
but I’m laughing loud
under last night’s moon.

Bar’s warm, full of sea-stained grins,
someone’s playing the violin.
Peter leans in, half a sip down,
says, 

“Remember her laugh?”

I spin my crown.
Aye. Like bells in a hollowed-out tree,
like rain on a roof where we used to be.
I tap my glass, let the cider ride,
toast to the ghosts still sat inside.
And for a moment, the past leans near,
but don’t speak—just lifts its beer.

The tide rolls out, but the shore don’t mind,
every step leaves something behind.
But listen close in the hush and spin—
old songs know how to pull you back in.

Three sheets to the wind,
but my heart sails true,
some storms pass,
but the sky stays blue.
Call it a dream, call it a tide,
but I walk this world
with her by my side.

Streetlights flicker like lighthouse beams,
night leans soft on the city seams.
We pass a window, faces blurred,
past lives pressed in painted curves.
She’s here, in the hush, in the distant glow,
in the pause when the cold wind starts to slow.
Peter hums, some half-named tune,
I know it. I hum it too.

Three sheets to the wind,
but I stand, I stand,
home ain’t a place—
it’s the warmth in your hand.
Call it a wish, call it the past,
but the fire burns bright
where the stars hold fast.

Fire licks driftwood, flames hum low,
waves like whispers, breath like snow.
Peter’s talking, voice half light,
words rolling easy, lost in night.
I nod, but the stars pull me deep,
salt in my lashes, eyes half asleep.
Wind in the dune grass sings the tune,
and I don’t fight it.
Not yet.
Not soon.

Not every anchor’s meant to weigh,
not every wind is meant to stay.
But some songs drift, some songs cling,
some songs come back
like birds in spring.

Head on Peter’s lap, world gone dim,
waves hum soft, horizon swims.
“Get some rest,” 
he says, voice lined in grin.
Aye.
Three sheets to the wind.
