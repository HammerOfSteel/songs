# Sea Glass Gospel

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-03-28
**Edited:** 2025-03-28

---

Se, sköna slingor klänga
på skeppets kala mur
och sira vittrad kalkstensvägg
med grönskande figur.

We’re out before breakfast, boots still wet,
dog ahead, boy behind, sky not set.
Lummelunda mist and tide hush wide,
salt on the tongue, that clean kind of quiet.
He holds a stick like a sword,
Golden curls all lit like old folklore.
And I think I was ten once, this same wind,
Ma holding my sleeve, saying, “Let the sea in.”
Now I watch him, running past boats long gone,
names faded like lullaby songs.
Sea glass in my palm green, cold, true,
shaped by ebb, the flow, the tide it knew.

Sea glass gospel,
Smooth and worn
a soft that came from being torn.
We change to rise
like driftwood does.
Found by chance,
but kept because.

We pass the old flunder stall near the dune,
smoke in the air, fish laid in rune.
The same old smell from years before, 
I swear, stalls must been here since the war.
Whilma sniffs at seaweed piles,
my boy laughs with his whole wild smile.
And I remember Tenby—sand in shoes,
two girls chasing kites in the coastal blues.
She held her coat like a flag, proud, bold,
and I held the leash while the wind turned cold.
Another dog back then, ears to the sky,
we stayed ‘til dusk with no reason why.

Sea glass gospel,
washed but true
not what it was,
Yet shining through.
Doesn't get better,
Doesn't get good
It's like time's misunderstood
But brother my brother, things do change
The ebb and flow of time is strange.

He finds a piece, says, “Look! Like treasure!”
Holds it up with that reckless pleasure.
And I nod, gone in thought
it’s what we lose that teaches what’s not.
Ma would pick shells and not say why,
just pocket them soft and let time lie.
Not all things have to speak to stay,
and I’ve kept that line tucked away.
I see her now in the way he moves,
small skips on wet stone, chasing grooves.
This shore’s no shrine,
but it holds its grace
keeps the shape of every face.

Got no time for times platitudes
Changing with seas brood moods
just rhythm, space and a sunflower seed,
just a pace that lets me breathe.

Even the wind knows us now, I swear,
knows our names, our skin, our hair.
Boy laughs again, and I stay quiet,
let the gospel hum in the hush between the riot.
There’s a rhythm in letting go,
in seaweed knots and the ebb’s slow flow.
Here and there, a lifetime wide,
all stitched soft by the turning tide.
We aren’t what we were,
but we’re still right here
walking the shore
with what stayed near.

No need to return unchanged,
no need to be the same.
Sea glass sings in silence,
and that’s enough to name.

På stenen stävar ett drakskepp fram
över brytande vågor på fjärden.
Högrest hövding i högvälvd stam
länkar ledungafärden.
