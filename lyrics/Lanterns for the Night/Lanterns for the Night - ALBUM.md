# Lanterns for the Night - ALBUM

**Labels:** Lanterns for the Night
**Created:** 2024-12-17
**Edited:** 2024-12-17

---

Album Title
“Lanterns for the Night”

The album title evokes the image of lights—stars, lanterns, memories—that guide the listener through the darkness of grief and loss. It symbolizes the act of carrying the light of love, even when the night feels endless.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Themes and Resonance
The album will revolve around the following emotional and symbolic ideas:

The Stars as Anchors
Stars are symbols of constancy and eternity, representing lost loved ones and their enduring presence in our lives.
Grief becomes celestial and poetic: a journey through the vast “night” of sorrow to find light again.

The Passage of Time
Grief moves through seasons, cycles, and small moments. Each song explores a different stage or place in time—frozen memories, the weight of absence, and the slow movement toward healing.

Love as a Lullaby
Songs often take on the tone of lullabies, soft and intimate, blending conversations with the departed into gentle reassurances.

Navigating Places of Memory
The settings are tangible: forests, cliffs, rivers, small towns, and homes. Each location holds echoes of the past, becoming sacred spaces where memory and presence blur.

The Impossibility of Now
The disconnect between past and present is explored. The world goes on, yet grief feels like a fracture in time. How do you live in the now when a part of you remains in the past?

Life in the Wake of Loss
The focus isn’t just on death but on what comes afterward—building a life, raising children, finding connection, and learning how to live alongside grief rather than fight it.

Tracklist and Song Ideas

“Lleuads light”
The cornerstone of the album: a father’s dialogue with his daughter’s memory on a walk home, under the stars. Grief, love, and hope blend into the night sky.

“Love, light and never ever letting go”
Theme: Letting go and honoring love.
Narrative: A quiet evening where lanterns are floated on a river, carrying wishes and memories. A ritual of release.

“A Garden That Remains”
Theme: Carrying grief through daily life.
Narrative: A story of tending to an overgrown garden—a metaphor for grief—and finding beauty among the weeds.

“Hiraeth, heretofore”
Theme: Homesickness for the past.
Narrative: A day spent in a familiar place, where the wind carries echoes of laughter and footsteps. The protagonist feels the pull of hiraeth but also finds comfort in the memories that remain.

“Mae hi’n bwrw eira”
Theme: Winter as a metaphor for grief and resilience.
Narrative: A father and child walk through frost-covered woods. Each step leaves an imprint, a reminder that they are still moving forward.

“The Lighthouse’s Song”
Theme: Love as a guiding light.
Narrative: A metaphorical lighthouse calls out to lost ships—representing grief pulling at the heart—while its beam offers guidance home.

“The room where silence bloom”
Theme: The weight of absence.
Narrative: A song centered on an empty chair, a quiet room, and the unseen presence of someone gone. Love lingers even in the stillness.

“Shaping clay (here to stay)”
Theme: Childhood innocence and fleeting time.
Narrative: A father helps his daughter fold paper boats and set them afloat on a stream, reflecting on how precious and fragile these moments are.

“Holly, the Hollow”
Theme: Finding sanctuary in nature.
Narrative: A hidden hollow in a tree becomes a safe space for the protagonist to sit, grieve, and feel held by the natural world.

“Midnight Bells”
Theme: Marking the passage of time.
Narrative: On a New Year’s Eve, the protagonist hears distant bells and reflects on the contrast between celebration and private grief. The bells mark a new year, but also carry the weight of memories.

“Elara is a moon of jupiter”
Theme: Honoring the departed.
Narrative: A song written as a tribute to the daughter, using the motif of lanterns as symbols of remembrance and love.

“The long long longing”
Theme: Acceptance and continuation.
Narrative: The protagonist walks a familiar path, retracing steps once taken together, now alone. Though grief remains, love carries them forward.

Sound and Mood
The album will use a mix of folk, acoustic, and Celtic influences to create an intimate, reflective tone. Elements like:

Soft fingerpicked guitar for lullaby-like moments.
Warm piano and strings for emotional depth.
Gentle vocal harmonies to create the sense of connection and memory.
Ambient sounds (footsteps, river water, wind) to make the songs feel immersive and grounded.
Occasional inclusion of Welsh phrases or imagery tied to nature and light for poetic depth.
