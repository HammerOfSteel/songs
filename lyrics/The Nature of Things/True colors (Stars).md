# True colors (Stars)

**Labels:** The Nature of Things, Stars And Seas, Stories, Stars
**Created:** 2025-02-10
**Edited:** 2025-02-10

---

Page 1: The Rain’s Goodbye

The rain had passed.
Tiny droplets still clung to the leaves, whispering secrets as they slid to the earth.
The air smelled fresh, like wet grass and cool stone, and the world stretched wide before them.

Tima stepped out onto the soft, damp ground, barefoot and bright-eyed.
She tilted her head up, watching the sky shift—gray to silver, silver to gold.
And then, just beyond the hills, a shimmer—a ribbon of colors spun through the sky.

“Papa, look!” she gasped, her tiny hands pointing. “The rainbow! It’s in the air!”


---

Page 2: A Promise of Adventure

Papa stepped beside her, his voice warm as the wind after rain.
"Do you see it, love, just over there?"

Tima nodded quickly, her feet shifting with excitement.
"Shall we chase it, Papa? Can we try?"

Papa smiled, kneeling to wipe a raindrop from her cheek.
"Of course, my sun. Let’s see where it lies."

The rainbow stretched far, beyond the river, over the rolling hills, where Twmbarlwm watched in quiet patience.
Tima took Papa’s hand, her fingers tiny but sure, and together, they stepped onto the wet stone path.


---

Page 3: The First Steps

The world was soft with the hush of rain, the path glistening beneath their feet.
They walked past the old stone wall, where ivy and wildflowers weaved their secrets into the cracks.
Drops of water slid from the leaves, landing in small puddles that shimmered like mirrors.

Tima paused, crouching beside one. She touched the water with her fingertip and gasped.

"Papa, look! The rainbow is in the puddle too!"

Papa chuckled, watching the colors dance on the surface.
"It’s hiding everywhere, my love. Even where we least expect it."

Tima stood, her excitement bubbling over.
The rainbow arched above them, calling them forward, and the adventure had begun.

---

Page 4: The Forest’s Whisper

They stepped into the trees, where the forest still whispered of rain.
Leaves dripped silver, shaking off the last of the storm’s touch.
The air smelled of damp earth, fresh moss, and pine.

Tima’s small boots splashed in hidden puddles, her laughter weaving with the sounds of the woods.
Above them, the rainbow shimmered faintly between the swaying branches, flickering with the wind.

"Papa, do you think the rainbow is watching us?" she asked, tilting her head.

Papa smiled. "Maybe, love. Or maybe it’s leading us to a secret place."

Tima’s eyes widened. A secret place!

She grabbed Papa’s hand and pulled him forward, her steps quick with wonder.


---

Page 5: The River of Colors

The path led them to the Ebbw River, its waters still lively from the rain.
It rushed and swirled, tumbling over smooth stones, dancing in the fading sunlight.
The rainbow glowed in the water’s ripples, stretching across the current like a floating ribbon.

Tima knelt at the riverbank, her fingers grazing the water.
A gleaming, round stone caught her eye—pale and smooth as the moon.

She held it up. "Papa, look! It feels like the rainbow left it just for me."

Papa crouched beside her, running his fingers over the stone’s cool surface.
"Maybe it did. Maybe the world leaves us treasures when we look closely enough."

Tima grinned, tucking the stone into her pocket.

The river hummed, reflecting gold and violet, as they followed the trail deeper into the hills.


---

Page 6: A Question for the Fish

The path climbed higher, weaving through ferns and wildflowers beaded with rain.
The rainbow flickered on the water, vanishing, then returning as the river bent.

Tima stopped suddenly, pointing to the ripples below.
"Papa, do fish see rainbows too?"

Papa tilted his head, watching the way the water caught the light.
"They might!" he said with a chuckle. "They swim where the colors grew."

Tima leaned forward, eyes bright with curiosity.

"Do you think they follow it, like we do?"

Papa thought for a moment.
"Perhaps, love. Maybe every creature has its own rainbow to chase."

Tima smiled, watching the fish dart beneath the surface.

Then she turned back to the path, her heart full of quiet wonder.


---

Page 7: The Climb to Twmbarlwm

The trail became steeper, wilder.
Moss-covered stones peeked through the damp, dark soil, leading toward the distant crest.
The sky had deepened to soft lilac, the rainbow now a gentle ghost above them.

Tima’s legs burned a little, but she refused to slow down.
The rainbow was waiting, and so was its secret.

Papa reached out a hand as she scrambled over a fallen tree.
"Almost there, my brave one."

Tima looked up, eyes shining.
She could feel it—they were getting closer.


---

Page 8: The Meadow of Colors

The trail softened beneath their feet, opening into a wide meadow.
Here, the air was sweet—wild thyme, damp earth, and the last kiss of rain.

Tima gasped in delight.
Flowers stretched in every direction, swaying gently as if whispering secrets to the wind.
The rainbow hovered low over the hill, its colors melting into the golden field.

Tima twirled, her laughter as light as the breeze.
"Papa, this is the rainbow’s garden!"

She knelt, fingers grazing the petals, and began weaving a ring of flowers.
"Maybe the rainbow left this here for us!"

Papa chuckled. "Maybe. Or maybe it’s waiting for someone to find it."

Tima’s eyes sparkled with excitement.

"Or maybe... it’s being guarded!"


---

Page 9: A Rustling in the Ferns

Tima turned suddenly.
A sound—a soft rustling deep in the ferns at the edge of the clearing.

She froze, heart thumping.
Could it be...?

"Papa, do you think..." she whispered.
Papa’s eyes twinkled. "I wonder what’s hiding there."

Tima stepped carefully, her tiny hands clutching her flower ring.
The air felt different here—softer, older, as if the land was holding its breath.

Another rustle. A shape among the ferns.

Tima’s fingers tightened around Papa’s hand.

Then—a pair of golden eyes blinked open.


---

Page 10: The Guardian of Stories

Tima gasped.

Nestled in the green, half-hidden by leaves and moss, lay a small, moss-covered dragon.
Its scales glistened like river stones, its breath slow and steady as the turning of the earth.

Tima took a step forward, her voice barely above a whisper.
"It’s real..."

The dragon watched her, its eyes as deep as the sky before a storm.
It was not fierce, nor frightening—only curious, patient.

Papa knelt beside her.
"I don’t think it guards gold, my love."

Tima tilted her head, studying the dragon.
"Then what does it guard?"

The dragon stretched, shaking off raindrops like pearls.

"Not gold. Not jewels."
Its voice was soft, like wind through the trees.

"I collect stories. The tales of those who chase the rainbow."


---

Page 11: A Gift for the Dragon

Tima stared in wonder.
A dragon that collected stories?

She glanced at her hands, at the ring of flowers she had woven.

She stepped forward.
"Then... this is for you."

She placed the flower ring gently before the dragon’s mossy feet.
It blinked, then smiled—a slow, knowing smile.

The dragon lowered its head, letting the flowers rest against its scales.

"Rainbows never leave," it whispered.
"They just find new skies to dance in."

Tima’s heart filled with warmth, as if she had found a secret meant just for her.
The rainbow shimmered one last time, as if nodding in agreement.

---

Page 12: The Climb to the Crest

The dragon’s words lingered in Tima’s heart.

"Rainbows never leave. They just find new skies to dance in."

She squeezed Papa’s hand as they continued the climb, the last stretch before Twmbarlwm’s crest.
The trail narrowed, the air thinned, and the world grew quiet.

With each step, the sky softened into twilight hues—lavender, rose, and deepening blue.

Finally, they reached the top.

The whole valley stretched below, rivers winding like silver ribbons, the last light of the rainbow fading into the horizon.

Tima let out a small breath. "It’s beautiful, Papa."

Papa knelt beside her. "Yes, love. The world is full of colors, even when we can’t always see them."


---

Page 13: Do Rainbows Always Go?

Tima stood tall on the crest, the wind playing with her hair.

She watched as the rainbow melted into the evening sky, its colors fading like a dream.

"Papa..." she whispered. "Do rainbows always go away?"

Papa wrapped an arm around her shoulders, his voice soft as dusk.

"They drift away, my love. But they never truly leave."

Tima frowned slightly. "How do you know?"

Papa smiled.
"Because rainbows live in every sunrise, every stream, every golden leaf in autumn. They live in the world we believe in."

Tima closed her eyes, breathing in the cool air.

She listened to the wind, to the hush of the trees, to the quiet magic that had always been there.

When she opened them again, the rainbow was gone.

But the world still glowed with its memory.


---

Page 14: The Wildflower Rings & the Night’s First Star

Tima looked down at her flower ring, still in her hands.

She turned to Papa with a small smile. "Then I’ll carry the rainbow with me."

She lifted the ring over her head and placed it gently upon her hair.

Papa watched her, his heart warm.

"You already do, my love."

The first stars blinked into the sky, quiet and steady.

Tima hummed a small tune, a song made of rain, rivers, and colors that never fade.

---

Page 15: Homeward Bound

The night stretched soft and peaceful.

Papa took Tima’s hand, and together they began the walk home, lantern light guiding their steps.

The meadow, the river, the dragon—all now part of the quiet world behind them.

Tima looked up at the stars, her eyelids heavy, her heart full.

"Papa, will we find another rainbow soon?"

Papa smiled. "Every storm brings a rainbow’s tune. We’ll chase it again, through rain or shine."

Tima sighed, nestling closer to his side.

As they reached their small cottage by the Ebbw, she curled into bed, her flower ring still in her hair.

And as she drifted into sleep, her dreams painted the sky with the colors of a rainbow.

Because true colors never leave.

They live in the heart, forever bright.
