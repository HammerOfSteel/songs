# Milkstone & Meddyliau

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-05-03
**Edited:** 2025-05-03

---

Fog sat tight like a thought not ready.
Library clock ticking sideways.
She said, “I think my tooth's loose.”
I said, “Aye, so’s the world sometimes.”

We curled near the ‘M’ shelf, her nose in the page,
A book too old, too heavy with age.
She pointed — “Milkstone? Is it for tea?”
I smiled, “More like for mothers, and memory.”
She tugged her scarf, that one with the stars,
Held up her tooth like it’d travelled from Mars.
“I could wear this,” she said, “on a string!”
I nodded, “You’d be the smallest knightling.”

“If this book is a potion, is sadness the glass?”
I paused. “Maybe. Or the bit that won’t pass.”
She traced the script with a biscuit thumb,
Read out loud: “The right and perfectest meanes.”
“What’s that mean?” she asked, chewing slow,
“It means the best way — but we rarely know.”
Her eyes went foggy like the world outside,
I said, “You feel it when something’s died.”

Milkstone hearts and marmalade skies,
We hold what's gone in lullaby guise.
A kiss, a crumb, a spell for ache —
To mend the milk, not let it break.

I told her her mum wore a stone near her chest,
For nursing and nerves, for peace and rest.
“Did she cry?” she asked, “Like me last night?”
I said, “Aye, but her tears made the stars bright.”
She cupped the book like a morning cup,
“Is grief a kind of holding up?”
I said, “It’s lifting the thing you’ll never drop —
Like a thought in your chest you don’t want to stop.”

She looked up — tooth gone, lost in play,
Said, “Maybe grief is just love that’s stayed.”
We wrapped up the book, and her breath slowed too,
She whispered, “Will the milkstone remember you?”
I kissed her crown and held her tight,
Said, “It already does, even at night.”
The fog pressed on but the page stayed bright,
And the tooth? She gave it to morning light.

Soften the flame, don’t douse the fire.
Carry the ache, but never tire.

Milkstone hearts and marmalade skies,
We hold what's gone in lullaby guise.
A kiss, a crumb, a spell for ache —
To mend the milk, not let it break.

Verdant lion, mother’s breath,
Stone in cradle, cradle in depth.
Silver rind, and salt on lace,
The child walks through time,
To carry grace.

“If I forget it... does it still count?”

“Aye, love. That’s why we write it down.”
