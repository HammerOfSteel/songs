# longshore drift

**Labels:** gullveig
**Created:** 2025-11-05
**Edited:** 2025-11-10

---

Tea gone cold on the windowsill,
Seagulls bickering on the hill,
Radio hum and the kettle hiss,
Little things I never thought I’d miss.

Sand in my shoes and salt on my skin,
Door half-open, the tide breathing in,
I hum your tune when the day goes grey,
Forget the words but I hum anyway.

Longshore drift, just letting it be,
The world keeps pulling the ground from me,
Every shell, every sound, every sweet old gift,
Finds its way back on the longshore drift.

Postcards curling in the kitchen drawer,
Your handwriting’s waves along the shore,
Same old joke on the fridge in pen,
Still makes me smile now and then.

Coffee rings like tidal marks,
A record crackles before it starts,
We said forever and maybe we did,
It’s just stretched out, like the sea when it hid.

Longshore drift, easy and kind,
Brings what you lose, leaves what you find,
Nothing stays whole, but nothing’s adrift,
It all rolls home on the longshore drift.

Pebbles turn smooth,
Pain turns song,
Even the wrong things learn to belong.
Time’s just a tide, love’s just a gift,
Worn soft and slow by the longshore drift.

Lamps in the harbour blink goodnight,
Steam on the glass, soft amber light,
I don’t need to know where the waves begin,
Just glad the sea remembers you in.

Longshore drift, gentle and true,
Brings back pieces I thought I outgrew,
Shoreline shifting, hearts still lift,
Everything mends in the longshore drift.
