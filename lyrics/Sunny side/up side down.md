I’ve got lists of things and postcards and these tiny little notes,
Tucked inside the pockets of our oversized winter coats.
Photos and remarks and a flickering glow-in-the-dark spark,
Keeping us together when the city gets a bit too dark.

It’s magic in the mundane,
little tales for the sleepless to sustain,
I’d be insane if I thought that I’d blink and miss the moment in the rain.

See, rainbows are despite,
all of their magic just a bit of air, 
Water vapour and the light,
but it’s only fair to say I don’t care.
It doesn’t take the pretty colors or the mystery away,
Even when we play these games of indecision every day.
Mostly left wishing for a little bit more of the view,
Sitting in this chair by the bed just staring at the back of you.

Could we not just stay exactly as we are right now?
In this little bubble that we’ve built, I don’t know how.
Is this not enough?
Just the simple "you and me"?
Floating in the silence of a domestic mystery.

It’s the magic in the mundane,
it’s the sparkle in the mess,
A vast collection of the things that we’re too lucky to possess.

It’s not the first time I’ve been here,
and it won’t be the last,
Staring at the vast collection of the treasures of a past.
Trinkets and the postcards and the things you keep while you sleep,
Promises and secrets that I know I’m gonna have to keep.

How did it come to be like this?
Was it that one explicable kiss?
I wish I had a clue because I know there’s something I might miss.

I’m trying to find the formula,
the ingredients and measurements precise,
To ensure that every second of this right now will always suffice.

But I haven’t got the chemistry,
I haven’t got the logic or the glue,
Binding up the particles that make up the "us" instead of "you."

I left some air in,
standing by the window pane, 
Thinking I must go,
or maybe I’ve already gone insane.

We nourish each other with these childish little games we play,
Putting silly moments into frames to keep the grey away.

But I’d be leaving out a part of the whole truth if I lied,
Because there’s another part of me I’m trying not to hide.
A part of us you’ve yet to really know or even see,
a little something that is growing in the garden of our history.
It’s the someone that makes the chalice overflow and start to spill,
Our little wonderful, bright yellow, sleepy Daffodil.

It’s the magic in the mundane,
it’s the sparkle in the mess,

I think it’s fair to say...
