# There’s My Love (Down by the Orchard)

**Labels:** Wool and Wonder
**Created:** 2025-05-22
**Edited:** 2025-05-22

---

There’s my love down by the orchard,
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
I’d go now if I could afford it,
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
There’s the tree and the café table,
Saw her laughin’ and readin’ fables—
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
Ffaldi radl didl dal.

Saw her once at the corner shop late,
Duffed my words while I grabbed me grapes.
She said “you goin’ to the bus or the moon?”
And I smiled like a prat, gone too soon.

Years since then, and I still see her—
By the school gate, dog park, heatwave blur.
Shared an umbrella once, pure fate,
Rain in her hair like the sky said wait.

Wrote her name in the fog on glass,
Wiped it off when me nan walked past.
She wore boots with the laces loose,
Laughed like thunder and minty juice.

There’s my love down by the orchard,
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
I’d go now if I could afford it,
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
There’s the dog and the open stable,
Gave her apple pie from my label—
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
Ffaldi radl didl dal.

She once danced by the bins, no shame,
Makin’ magic out the mundane.
Chipped nail polish, boots with the heel gone,
Still the kind to turn sky into song.

We spoke once, real talk, just once,
Outside a gig in the back of the grunts.
I said “you remind me of tales and storms,”
She said “you look like your soul’s half-formed.”

We laughed. We shared a spliff.
She passed it like a hymn, dead swift.
Said “your voice sounds like vinyl crack,”
I said “you laugh like you know the track.”

There’s my love down by the orchard,
Leaves in her coat and smile all scorched.
There’s the oak and the muddy grass,
Dreamed of her twice, both times passed.

Then it came, quiet as breath—
A message, a walk, a Monday left.
She sat with tea, two cups poured,
Said “took the long way, now I’m yours.”

Didn’t kiss, not then, not yet—
But I knew her shadow like a silhouette.
We talked ‘bout foxes, books, bad knees,
About her mum, about galaxies.

Said “you rhyme like you’re never done.”
I said “you glow like the yellow sun.”
She smiled and sipped.
And I knew—
some things are worth the waitin’ through.

There’s my love down by the orchard,
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
Time took years but the heart kept score,
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
There’s the bench and the book and laughter,
Now she’s here, and I’m still after—
Tw rym di ro, radl didl dal,
Ffaldi radl didl dal.
