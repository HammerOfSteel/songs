# The other side of silence

**Labels:** Super Nova
**Created:** 2025-06-10
**Edited:** 2025-06-10

---

I weren’t lookin’ for ghosts, just a kettle to boil.
But there you were — all grin, no spoil.
Hair like dusk and breath like a tune,
dragged me straight back to that room.
House by the sea with the rust-red tiles,
and the way you’d blink like you’d been here awhile.
Ain’t no map for the way back through,
but dreams don’t care — they just bring you.

This chaos, this calamity, this garden once was perfect
Give your immortality to me, I'll set you up against the stars.

I saw her again last night —
barefoot, laughin’ in a hallway full of sea light.
We were young, too young —
our hands didn’t ache, our mouths not yet full of unsaid things.

Some friend of hers was there —
they talked like waves, all half-remembered care.
We explored this house by the ocean,
every room a secret,
a drawer full of shells,
a room full of clocks that ticked like spells.

She turned a corner too fast,
laughin’ like she never bled,
and I chased her through wallpaper skies
like grief forgot its thread.

No fighting. No death. No child.
Just her —
like a first day rewritten wild.

And I swear for a minute I forgot I was someone who wakes up.
Someone who folds socks in silence,
measures joy in packed lunch cups.

Then morning — the silence kicks in
like the central heating hum,
like the dishes waiting dumb.
Like the crust on the counter
and the juice cap gone again.

I put toast on for her.
The living one.
The now.
The spark I’d die for — while dyin’ anyway somehow.

She asks if squirrels can read signs,
and I nod while stirrin’ tea like a prayer with no lines.
I’m tired.
Not just tired — underground.
Smiling like I’m found, while no one hears the sound.

There’s a gap no one clocks.
Where the “we” should be.
Where she should stand.
Even when I’m killin’ it,
even when I’ve got her hand.

The other side of silence
is where I still speak to you.
In dishwater steam and shoelace knots,
in every single thing I do.

We weren’t done, not really.
We’d just stopped havin’ names for the pain.
I wanted to hate you clean,
but you haunt me soft, and I break again.

You lit that laugh like a lantern,
but you left me holdin’ the wax.
All this weight in my arms,
but the past don’t come back.

I carry her — the daughter,
but I carry you too.
In the way she raises eyebrows,
in the things she never knew.

You don’t get to choose who your soul remembers,
even when they wrecked it all in embers.
Even when they vanished into sleep,
left me here to climb the keep.

I do all of it —
the notes, the dinner, the bedtime sea.
I hold her hair when she’s sick.
I build a world for free.

And yet, in the quiet, it hits wrong.
Like a chair missin’ a leg.
Like I’m finishin’ the song
that you were supposed to beg.

You should be here.
Even just to shout.
Even just to storm off
or call me out.

But instead, it’s me.
Holdin’ her with cracked hands.
Givin’ her the stars
while buryin’ our plans.

The other side of silence
is where I still speak to you.
In bedtime light and laundry stacks,
in every single thing I do.
You’re gone — and I’m still here.
But love don’t stop. It just runs clear.

A grin, still like bonfire smoke —
she dreams of stars and giggles at ghosts.

You’re a shadow on the kitchen clock,
still dancin’ ‘round when the tickin’ stops.

We lied, we can't go on
This is the time and this is the place to be alive,
And there is discord in the garden tonight.
