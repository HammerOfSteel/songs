# Giggeling Greggs

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-05-02
**Edited:** 2025-05-02

---

Given up, deluxe in Essex
Cornwall, seaside Fortress
Stonehenge, felt the Vortex
One Vision, one idle Apex

Met him by the bins with a ham roll grin,
shirt half-untucked, bit of sauce on his chin.
Called it “spiritual lunch” — I never did doubt it,
even saints need pastry, no need to shout it.
He’d say, “This one’s for the soul,” lift a steak bake,
punchline already flaking off the flake.
We’d sit on the curb like it were a pew,
dodging chips and seagulls, just me and my crew.

We break our bread where the pigeons beg,
bless the crumbs and the giggling Greggs.
Tea too sweet and stories too long —
but that’s how the good ones carry on.
Laughter’s cheap and so was we,
but we shared our gold in pastry.

He said, “You can’t be sad round sausage rolls.”
I said, “You clearly ain’t watched EastEnders.”
Still, we howled like pups by the Co-op sign,
chucking scraps and talking enders.
One time he cried, said he missed his nan.
I gave him my last jammy doughnut — man.
“Don’t tell the boys,” he muttered low.
But next day he brought me two, with a bow.

He left his coat on that same brick wall.
Still smells like mayo and menthols.
Every Thursday now, I buy two of each.
One for the curb. One for the breach.

We break our bread where the pigeons beg,
bless the crumbs and the giggling Greggs.
Toast the gone, the here, the near —
their laugh’s still hot in the paper, dear.
Laughter’s cheap and so was we,
but we shared our gold in pastry.

A pasty’s a memory folded tight,
A bap’s a hug when you’ve lost the night.
They said we were scruffy — maybe it’s true.
But our chapel had sugar and a view.

Cleanse your Palette, taste must never
Mix, or coagulate, congeal, or Root
Fluidic Fauna, Flower Sauna, Resolute
Cleanse, release, Ashen Soot
Absolute Sanctuary, must enter, Barefoot
