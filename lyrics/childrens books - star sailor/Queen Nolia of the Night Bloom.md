# Queen Nolia of the Night Bloom

**Labels:** childrens books - star sailor
**Created:** 2024-10-20
**Edited:** 2024-10-20

---

Intro:
As the comet Serenwyn glides through the starry night,
Star Sailor drifts gently, her glow soft and bright.
She finds Queen Nolia, waiting still as the air,
Among unopened blooms in a garden so rare.

Verse 1:
Star Sailor whispers, “Why don’t they bloom?”
Queen Nolia smiles, “It’s not yet their moon.”
“Each flower will open when it feels the night,
Some things unfold only out of sight.”

Through Cysgod Llyn (Shadow Lake) they wait and they gaze,
The flowers asleep beneath the comet’s haze.
“Be patient, little one,” Nolia does say,
“For beauty blooms where shadows lay.”

Chorus:
“The brightest blooms rise from the dark,
Like stars that shine when nights are stark.
Be patient, dear one, and you will see,
Every flower finds its time to be free.”

“Under the night’s soft silver veil,
Through dreams that drift on a comet’s tail,
We grow and bloom without a sound,
In quiet moments, life’s gifts are found.”


Verse 2:
Star Sailor sighs, “Will they bloom at all?”
Nolia just smiles, standing steady and tall.
“For growth takes time, just like the stars,
They only shine bright once they know who they are.”

A flower stirs in the garden’s heart,
Its petals unfolding, a radiant start.
In Llys Ysbrydion (The Spirit’s Court), blooms begin to glow,
Showing the light that only night can know.

Bridge:
“Changes come slowly, without a sound,
We see them best when stillness is found.”
“Just like this garden, your heart will grow,
In times unknown, your light will show.”

As they wait under Serenwyn’s gleaming arc,
Flowers awaken from slumber dark.
Star Sailor watches, her heart at ease,
As blooms spread gently on the night’s soft breeze.

Chorus (Reprise):
“The brightest blooms rise from the dark,
Like stars that shine when nights are stark.
Be patient, dear one, and you will see,
Every flower finds its time to be free.”

“Under the night’s soft silver veil,
Through dreams that drift on a comet’s tail,
We grow and bloom without a sound,
In quiet moments, life’s gifts are found.”

Outro:
The comet drifts on, through stars far and wide,
Queen Nolia’s garden, a glow by her side.
Star Sailor waves as she sails away,
Her heart full of light to brighten the day.

Through Cysgod Llyn and the skies unknown,
She carries the wisdom the garden has shown.
For even in darkness, hope starts to bloom,
And life’s greatest gifts find their own room.

Queen Nolia, a regal figure with shimmering robes like moonlit petals, stands gracefully in a celestial garden on the tail of the comet Serenwyn. Around her bloom ethereal flowers glowing softly in blues, silvers, and purples, their petals unfurling under the dark sky. Star Sailor stands nearby in her crescent-moon sailboat, her light blending gently with the blooms.
