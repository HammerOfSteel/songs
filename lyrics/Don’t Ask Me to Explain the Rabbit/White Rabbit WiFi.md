# White Rabbit WiFi

**Labels:** Don’t Ask Me to Explain the Rabbit
**Created:** 2025-04-19
**Edited:** 2025-04-19

---

“White Rabbit WiFi, I’m logged in late —
waiting for a quest and a snack plate.”
“No signal? That’s cool. We built a fort.”
“I didn’t get the update — but I brought juice.”

White Rabbit WiFi, signal’s down,
mapped my heart to the hometown crown.
Today’s boss fight? Socks and soap,
I dodged chores, declared new hope.

I pinged the couch, declared it base,
hid behind Mum’s pillowcase.
The cat’s a mount, the floor’s a void,
XP gained: fun deployed.

White Rabbit WiFi, glitch and go,
quest for snacks and undertow.
Sunbeam armor, juice-stained pride —
you find the magic where you hide.

Handshake missed, still played the game,
typed your name into my frame.
No level cap, no “press to win” —
just laughed so hard we let life in.

Poolside boss fights, cannonball fate,
a noodle sword, splash-attack gate.
Somehow you cast “invisibility,”
hid your grief in swim-streak glee.

I ran with joy and tripped on air,
summoned band-aids, breathed sweet care.
We made the world from chalk and tile —
called it love, then camped a while.

You said, “Adventure’s when it glitches.”
I said, “Same with families, switches.”
You said, “I’m tired.” I said, “Me too.”
Then we respawned. Just me and you.

White Rabbit WiFi, slow reboot,
under blankets, still in pursuit.
Signal weak but meaning clear —
your heartbeat’s loud in atmos-sphere.

No final boss, no stat to track —
you held me close, I held you back.
The call to quest was soft tonight,
a quiet "yes" in pixel light.

Loading...
Loading...
Still here.

No sword, no scroll, no epic plan —
Just knowing you still understand.

We never slept, just dozed and spoke,
made up stars and Dad-joke lore.
I didn’t need the latest patch,
you held me tight — perfect match.

“Not okay” was okay too,
you pinged my world, I pinged back you.
We lost the plot, but not the thread —
still glitched, still loved, still far from dead.

White Rabbit WiFi, wait with me,
no need to charge emotionally.
If the quest is late, we’ll build our own —
a base camp in the comfort zone.

You don’t need maps to love someone,
we play the world where light has run.
Press “Start” again, and this time too —
I choose this player. I choose you.
