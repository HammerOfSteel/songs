# 絶体絶命 (Zettai zetsumei)

**Created:** 2026-02-21

Instrumentation
- Sparse piano, bowed cello, distant field recordings (rain on tin), soft electronic heartbeat. Occasional Japanese shakuhachi or bamboo flute for color.

Focus
- Moment of utter crisis reframed as tiny luminous acts; using Japanese title ("desperate situation") but lyrics treat it as a narrow doorway to small miracles.

Hook
- A whispered bilingual refrain: "絶体絶命 — hold the breath, find the small light" repeated as call-and-response.

Structure
- Haiku intro in Japanese/English (3 lines)
- Verse (one scene of quiet crisis: kettle boiled dry, phone unanswered, child's sleep)
- Chorus (bilingual mantra)
- Bridge (soft spoken Japanese line, then English translation as echo)
- Outro (single shakuhachi note + fading piano)

Lyrical seed

Haiku:
窓の雨粒 —
窓の中に小さな灯り —
we hold the hush.

Verse sketch:
The kettle went to silence, the street stopped in its shoes,
a chair sat like a witness to the weight of all our news.
We patched the tiny leak with a towel and a prayer,
we found a match and struck it in the closet of despair.

Production note
- Use the bilingual hook sparingly; aim for tenderness rather than melodrama.
