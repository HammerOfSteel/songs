# 6AM (She Rises)

**Created:** 2025-04-18
**Edited:** 2025-04-18

---

[Spoken Intro, low and textured]
It’s quiet now.
Just the hum of the old fridge and the buzz of the block light.
She ties her laces like a prayer —
Double knot. Breath in.
[voice in Afrikaans] Ek sal opstaan
She always does.

Verse 1
Up before the drones start scanning,
Eyes half-closed but the will’s still standing.
Toast in her hand, mind on the mission,
Silk wrap tight like coded tradition.
They tell her calm down, don’t be so loud,
Smile more, wear less, stand in the crowd.
But she got a name not meant to be softened,
Whispers in tongues they ain't learned in the office.

Chorus
[voice in Afrikaans] Dis my tyd
(I walk where the morning’s wide)
[voice in Afrikaans] Jy sien my nie
(Still I’m blazing quietly)
[voice in Afrikaans] Ek is nie bang nie
(They don’t hold the map for me)
[voice in Afrikaans] Ek sal opstaan
(6AM, she rises — free.)

Verse 2
Click-clack boots on the block-paved lane,
Every step stitched with her sister’s name.
Round the kettle, stories pour like steam,
Faith not in pews but in soft routine.
A hand on her back, "You alright, my girl?"
She nods — don’t need to say a word.
They braid resistance in curls and care,
Truths untold in the way they stare.

Pre-Chorus
She walks in faith, but it’s not yours to name.
Not robes, nor books, nor digital chains.
She got gods in grime and gospel notes,
In Sunday soups and secondhand coats.
[voice in Afrikaans] Susters hou vas
(We carry each other through the glass.)

Chorus 2
[voice in Afrikaans] Dis my tyd
(I walk where the morning’s wide)
[voice in Afrikaans] Jy sien my nie
(Still I’m blazing quietly)
[voice in Afrikaans] Ek is nie bang nie
(They don’t hold the map for me)
[voice in Afrikaans] Ek sal opstaan
(6AM, she rises — free.)

Bridge
She saw her mother crying into tea,
Soft gospel playing on old CDs.
And thought: I’ll write a verse for every woman they erased,
Spoken loud, hair wild, unashamed grace.

Final Verse
Now she stands, not waits,
Feet on faith, and time don’t dictate.
They call it defiant — she calls it breath.
Alive at dawn and not done yet.
[voice in Afrikaans] Ek sal opstaan
Again, and again, and again.

Outro
Wind in her coat. Keys in hand.
[voice in Afrikaans] Dis my tyd
Lights blink on across the land.
