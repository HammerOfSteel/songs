# the tannoy oracle

**Labels:** The Alchemists Estate
**Created:** 2025-11-18
**Edited:** 2025-11-20

---

“Mind the gap between what you dream and what you do...”

Camden heat, the tiles still sweat from morning crowds,
A busker hums the news beneath the sound.
Your oyster card won’t tap, you swear, then laugh,
they’re herding all the ghosts down the central path.

You sip your tea from a flask with stars,
steam draws halos round the subway scars.
Your cracked screen lights up carriage blue,
tiny constellations all orbiting you.

You say, “this city’s just a deck of cards,
shuffle all the days ‘til they look like ours.”

Posters peel, prophecies curl in glue,
The tannoy coughs, then whispers through

“Next stop: you, love, step lightly, it’s true.”
The tannoy oracle calling me through.
In the echo I hear your name fall clear,
like steam on glass, “we’re nearly here.”

An old man nods, counting wishes in ticket stubs,
mouths some quiet spell to the tracks below us.
Kids trade crisps like secret notes,
someone’s playing drill on a tinny phone.

I find a poem on a seat, “beware of love,”
scribbled in biro over chewing gum.
Then the tannoy glitches, trips on a street name,
twists the vowels ‘til it sounds like your name.

Your hand finds mine, soft, unplanned,
we miss our stop by three whole brands.
But we stay, let the circle line redraw,
every future version of us it saw.

You say, “maybe London’s just alive today,
talking back in riddles ‘cause it knows our names.”
And the tannoy hums again
“Connection made... hold tight, my friend.”

“Next stop: you, step lightly, it’s true.”
The tannoy oracle calling me through.
The static sings in soft gold glue,
a message meant, for me and you.

“End of the line, or is it...?”
