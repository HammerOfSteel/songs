# Half of the half - ALBUM 1/1

**Labels:** Half Of The Half, Album
**Created:** 2025-03-02
**Edited:** 2025-04-18

---

1. Album Concept

Genre & Style: Acoustic folk with a raw, organic sound.
Themes: Mental health, personal struggles, loss, healing, self-acceptance, resilience, loneliness, hope.
Narrative Focus: Individual stories of people facing hardship, with poetic lyrics that explore their emotions and internal battles.
Instrumentation: Acoustic guitar, fingerpicking, minimal percussion, soft strings, subtle harmonies.

---

2. Potential Song Ideas & Themes
3. 낡은 기타 (The Worn-Out Guitar)
Theme: Music as a lifeline in depression.
Narrative: A man with a worn-out guitar, singing quietly to himself in a small room, using music as his last connection to the world.


2. 무게 (The Weight) - DONE
Theme: The burden of expectations and burnout.
Narrative: A woman exhausted by life’s expectations, carrying unseen weights that no one else notices.


3. 벽과 창 (Walls and Windows)
Theme: Isolation vs. connection.
Narrative: Someone who never opens their window but stares through it every night, longing for a world they’re too afraid to step into.


4. 바람에 띄운 편지 (A Letter on the Wind)
Theme: Unspoken words to a lost loved one.
Narrative: A person writing a letter to someone who has passed, knowing they can never send it but still needing to say the words.


5. 그림자 춤 (Dancing Shadows)
Theme: Anxiety and self-doubt.
Narrative: A young woman sees shadows moving around her room at night, whispering every mistake she’s ever made.


6. 먼지 속에서 (In the Dust)
Theme: Abandonment and starting over.
Narrative: A man walking through an empty house filled with memories, deciding whether to stay in the past or step into the unknown.


7. 한 잔의 달빛 (A Glass of Moonlight)
Theme: Alcohol and escapism.
Narrative: A man sitting alone at a bar, drinking moonlight as he tries to forget the weight of his regrets.


8. 바람이 불 때 (When the Wind Blows)
Theme: Panic attacks and survival.
Narrative: A woman describing the sensation of a panic attack as a storm inside her, struggling to breathe but finding calm when the wind finally settles.


9. 길 위의 사람 (The One on the Road)
Theme: Homelessness, lost identity.
Narrative: A man who lives without a name, walking endlessly down empty roads, unsure of where he belongs.


10. 새벽의 이름 (The Name of Dawn)
Theme: Finding hope in darkness.
Narrative: Someone who has lost all hope but waits for the sunrise every morning, believing that light will come again.


11. 너머의 방 (The Room Beyond)
Theme: Hikikomori and digital escapism
Narrative: A teenager in Seoul who hasn’t left their room in months. Their only window to the world is through games and online avatars, but deep down, they dream of a life outside the screen.
Setting details: Glow from a monitor, distant city sounds through the wall, night food deliveries.
“My voice is just keystrokes now / but I still hope for sun between my blinds.”


12. 업데이트 (Patch Notes)
Theme: Life changes and growing pains framed like a software update
Narrative: A young adult reflecting on their own emotional "updates" — heartbreak, healing, new perspectives — as if they’re changes in a changelog.
“Bug fixed: trust issues / Added: courage to speak.”


13. 네트워크 불안정 (Unstable Network)
Theme: Long-distance relationship, connection and disconnection
Narrative: A couple separated by borders and time zones, trying to stay together over unstable Wi-Fi calls and glitchy video.
“Your smile froze again / right before goodbye.”


14. 가짜 웃음 (Fake Smile)
Theme: Workplace pressure and masking pain
Narrative: A young woman working in Gangnam tech scene, smiling through every meeting while hiding panic and depression behind a polished appearance.
“Coffee break confession / but no one heard.”


15. 로그아웃 하기 전에 (Before I Log Out)
Theme: Suicide ideation expressed through metaphor of leaving a digital space
Narrative: Someone writes a goodbye post no one will see, but right before logging out for good, a message comes through — simple, small, but enough.
“You still there? // Yeah. Just... needed to ask.”


16. 엄마의 전화 (My Mother’s Call)
Theme: Generational disconnect
Narrative: A young woman in her 30s living independently in Seoul, repeatedly avoiding her mother’s calls. The guilt, the longing, and the unspoken love build in each missed ring.
“She always calls at dinner / like she knows I haven’t eaten.”


17. 오늘도 괜찮아요 (I'm Okay Again Today)
Theme: Daily depression check-ins
Narrative: Following a person’s quiet ritual every morning: opening the window, brushing hair, texting "I'm okay today" to a group chat. Some days they believe it. Some days they don't.
“It’s not a lie / if I want it to be true.”


18. 버스 470 (Bus 470)
Theme: Transient lives and silent moments
Narrative: The bus ride across Seoul at 2AM, filled with stories — a student asleep, an ajumma holding groceries, a man texting a child he doesn’t see.
“So many lives in this moving box / and none of us say a word.”


19. 우리 가족 시스템 (Our Family System)
Theme: Family roles and unspoken dynamics
Narrative: Told from the perspective of the "peacekeeper" child who holds everything together — afraid that if they stop, the family will fall apart.
“If I’m okay, then they can be too / that’s what I tell myself.”


20. 비밀번호 (Passcode)
Theme: Secrets and emotional walls
Narrative: Someone creates digital folders of memories and passwords that even they forget — a metaphor for hidden grief and pain.
“Four digits / that only I could guess / and I still can’t.”


21. 경계선 (The Borderline)
Theme: Escaping the North, leaving family behind
Narrative: A young woman crosses into the South under the cover of night. Every footstep forward is a betrayal, but she keeps walking, whispering goodbye to her mother in her mind.
“I crossed the river, but it wasn’t water that burned / it was your voice calling me back.”
Sparse instrumentation, slow breathy vocals — could end with a single sustained note, like a held breath never let go.


22. 외국인 등록증 (Alien Registration)
Theme: Being stateless and misplaced
Narrative: A man who escaped the North is now stuck in legal limbo, called “alien” on every form. He wonders if he belongs anywhere anymore.
“Name: erased. / Nationality: unknown. / Home? Still searching.”


23. 두 번째 생일 (Second Birthday)
Theme: Rebirth after defection
Narrative: A woman marks the day she arrived in the South as her second birthday. She celebrates quietly, lighting a candle for the family she can’t call.
“My mother doesn’t know / but I was born again / on a bus heading south.”


24. 구로디지털단지 (Guro Digital Complex)
Theme: The hidden class of migrants and gig workers
Narrative: Night shift at a call center in Guro — Vietnamese, Uzbek, North Korean, and Korean workers sharing noodles, music, and stories between shifts.
“We speak ten tongues, but laughter sounds the same.”


25. 청춘북한 (North of Youth)
Theme: A teen who escaped alone
Narrative: A 17-year-old now lives in Seoul but dreams of Pyongyang’s skyline as it was before it faded. They carry memories of streetlights, breadlines, and stories their grandmother whispered.
“I was a child where youth had no name / now I’m grown, and I still don’t know mine.”


26. 택배의 노래 (The Delivery Song)
Theme: Gig economy, unseen labor
Narrative: A courier rides through Seoul’s night, delivering happiness to others while their own life unravels quietly.
“I bring cake to lovers / shoes to children / and I go home to silence.”


27. 카톡, 안 읽음 (Unread Kakao)
Theme: Emotional distance in modern relationships
Narrative: A girl watches her Kakao message sit on “unread” for days. It was her attempt to explain herself, but silence is louder than a fight.
“You left me on gray / before I could turn blue.”


28. 입양 (Adoption)
Theme: Identity and family
Narrative: A Korean adoptee raised abroad returns to Seoul in their 30s, walking streets they’ve only known in dreams.
“They said this is my motherland / but it feels like an echo / of something I never heard.”


29. 학원 소년 (Hagwon Boy)
Theme: Burnout in youth
Narrative: A middle-schooler lives between home, school, and after-school academies. The only time he feels free is when he sketches in the corner of the PC bang.
“My dreams are made of pencil lead / and they always get erased.”


30. 알고리즘 엄마 (Algorithm Mother)
Theme: Parental burnout and social comparison
Narrative: A mother obsessed with parenting YouTube channels feels like she’s failing, every recommendation pushing her deeper into despair.
“The screen says what I should be / but it doesn’t know her name.”


31. 제로섬 (Zero-Sum)
Theme: Capitalism and survival
Narrative: A woman working three jobs just to stay afloat in Seoul. She's not climbing a ladder — she’s running in place.
“If I win, someone loses / so I stopped playing.”


32. 마스크 뒤에서 (Behind the Mask)
Theme: Pandemic aftermath & emotional repression
Narrative: A nurse who smiled with her eyes for too long, now finds she can’t show her real face even when masks are gone.
“I wore it for safety / now I wear it for silence.”


33. 비혼주의자 (The Unwed)
Theme: Choosing not to marry in a traditional society
Narrative: A woman in her 40s with no partner, no children, and no regrets — except for the loneliness at every family gathering.
“I chose this peace / but I still hear them whisper it’s failure.”


34. 프로필사진 (Profile Picture)
Theme: Online identity vs. real self
Narrative: A person with multiple accounts and personas, wondering which one their real friends know — if any.
“My smile has filters / my silence does not.”


35. 퀴어한 봄 (Queer Spring)
Theme: Queer youth in Korea
Narrative: A soft-spoken high schooler falls in love with her classmate during exam season, hiding their notes and their feelings.
“We studied love / but never learned how to live it.”


36. 가짜 뉴스 (Fake News)
Theme: Misinformation, media, and generational rifts
Narrative: A young man argues with his father every night over TV news, tired of conspiracy theories and cold dinners.
“We sit at the same table / but speak different worlds.”


37. 로또 인생 (Lotto Life)
Theme: The dream of sudden change
Narrative: A taxi driver listens to lotto number predictions between fares, holding onto one scrap of impossible hope.
“I don’t dream of gold / just a Sunday without worry.”


38. 국경 없는 사랑 (Love Without Borders)
Theme: International couples and visa struggles
Narrative: A woman from the Philippines and her Korean wife face bureaucracy, bigotry, and distance, but they keep loving anyway.
“They ask for proof / but don’t see the quiet things.”


39. 실패작 (The Failure)
Theme: Academic pressure and suicide
Narrative: A boy who didn’t get into SKY, watching his peers move forward while he feels stuck.
“Everyone passed / I passed by.”


40. 다음 생에 (In the Next Life)
Theme: Reincarnation, regret, and hope
Narrative: A woman writes letters to herself in another life, hoping she’ll have the courage to live differently.
“Next time, don’t apologize so much / and kiss her back.”


41. 셀카 (Selfie)
Theme: Self-image and performance
Narrative: A teenage girl spends hours perfecting a photo she'll delete anyway.
“This version of me / is always just out of frame.”


42. 군번줄 (Dog Tag)
Theme: Military service and identity
Narrative: A young conscript writes letters he’ll never send, wondering who he’ll be when he returns.
“I sleep with a number / but dream with a name.”


43. 점집에서 (At the Fortune Teller)
Theme: Desperation and superstition
Narrative: A mother visits a fortune teller in a back alley to ask if her son will survive his depression.
“She read his fate / and my silence.”


44. 계절 노동자 (Seasonal Worker)
Theme: Migrant labor and displacement
Narrative: A man in a strawberry field counts days in a language that isn’t his.
“Every berry red / but none for home.”


45. DMZ의 새 (The Bird of the DMZ)
Theme: Life above politics
Narrative: A rare bird nests in the DMZ, free across borders no one else can cross.
“Barbed wire below / sky without sides.”


46. 우리집 냉장고 (Our Fridge)
Theme: Poverty and pride
Narrative: A single mom opens the fridge at night to remind herself there’s food.
“It hums like a lullaby / of what I can give.”


47. 할머니의 된장국 (Grandma’s Doenjang Soup)
Theme: Heritage and memory
Narrative: A woman makes her grandmother’s recipe, realizing it’s not just food, it’s history.
“She passed the spoon / and I stirred the years.”


48. 에어컨 바람 (AC Wind)
Theme: Summer loneliness
Narrative: A student studies alone in a silent room, the AC wind the only thing touching her skin.
“Even the breeze / feels borrowed.”


49. 무연고자 (The Unclaimed)
Theme: Dying alone
Narrative: A song from the point of view of someone who died without family, hoping their name still lives somewhere.
“No one came / but the stars remembered.”


50. 인터넷 바다 (The Internet Sea)
Theme: Digital childhood
Narrative: A kid raised by forums, livestreams, and memes, now grown and unsure how to speak in person.
“My lullaby / was buffering.”
---

4. General Mood & Inspiration
A minimalist, raw, and heartfelt acoustic sound, where silence is just as important as music.
Lyrics that feel deeply personal but universally relatable to anyone who has faced mental health struggles.
A warm, intimate vocal delivery, like someone singing their truth in a quiet room.
