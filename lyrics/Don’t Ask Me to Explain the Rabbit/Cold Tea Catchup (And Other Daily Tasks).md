# Cold Tea Catchup (And Other Daily Tasks)

**Labels:** Don’t Ask Me to Explain the Rabbit
**Created:** 2025-04-19
**Edited:** 2025-04-19

---

You made tea again, didn’t drink it.

That’s how I mark time now.

Like tree rings, but steeped.

Folded socks, unmatched again,
ironed my grief, called it zen.
He said “truth lives past the line,”
I biked too far, missed the sign.

Toaster blinked like it believed,
morning’s charm, slightly deceived.
I read Camus on a shopping list —
philosophy with a buttered twist.

Cold tea catchup, and other daily tasks,
meaning in the mirror when no one asks.
Hope’s in the heater, grief’s in the floor,
I found her smile near the laundry drawer.

She said 
“charm’s a way to bend the sky,”

I said “fine — we’ll burn the pie.”
They left a note in the form of steam,
on the windowpane of a half-held dream.

The fridge hummed truths I couldn’t name,
I alphabetized my hurt and shame.
Camus whispered near the jam:
“You’ll find no answers, but here I am.”

Don’t walk in front of me — I might stop for snacks.

Don’t walk behind — I get weird if I’m tracked.

So what then?

Walk beside me. Let’s miss the bus together.

Cold tea catchup, not much to declare,
except I found your glove on the second stair.
We talk like prophets, shop like fools,
and fold our grief into daily rules.

Morning breaks like cracked eggshells,
still the kettle hums, the story tells.
Charm is silence where questions sat,
truth is wherever we forgot our hat.

He said “I miss her in the drying sheets,”
I nodded, stirring memory’s heat.
She laughed and vanished into the toast,
left behind what I miss the most.

There’s poetry in unpaid bills,
there’s hope in checking windowsills.
Always go too far, then pause —
that’s where I found her, tea and flaws.

Cold tea catchup, the ritual’s the balm,
she walked through dust, but left her calm.
We fold the pain and stack it neat,
a little jazz, a little heat.

Cold tea catchup — and that’s okay.
Some lives are lived in mugs and gray.
If grief is daily, then so is grace —
I found her smile in the pillowcase.

When I look at my life and its secret colours...

…You feel like bursting into tears?

No. Like painting the hallway. Finally.

I’ll bring the rollers.
