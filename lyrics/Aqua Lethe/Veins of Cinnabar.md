# Veins of Cinnabar

**Labels:** Aqua Lethe
**Created:** 2025-05-22
**Edited:** 2025-05-22

---

You said I talk like a haunted pub,
all creaky floorboards and too much hubbub.
I said you love like a lit cig dropped—
all heat and hazard, no plan to stop.
You called me poison, I said “just a splash.”
Then we clinked our wounds like broken glass.
You’re all brass keys and I’m padlocked shame—
but still we meet, and say the name.

Veins of cinnabar, babe—it’s sweet then it stings,
I kiss like a whisper with rust on its wings.
Love ain’t clean, love don’t spar—
It bleeds soft through veins of cinnabar.
Veins of cinnabar, go on then, sip—
We were doomed from the wink, not the slip.

You're a walkin' omen in lace-up boots.

You’re a hymn with half your notes gone mute.

You make tea like you’re brewin’ spells.

And you make hell feel quite like Wells.

You laughed when I said "love's alchemy,"
then slipped me a note said "burn with me."

We're logic’s mess and passion's crime—
and I’d still take you every time.

Veins of cinnabar, babe—it’s sugar and spite,
We’re just stars who forgot how to light.
Love don’t weep, love don’t char—
It floods slow through veins of cinnabar.
Veins of cinnabar, black and pink—
You said don’t fall, but love—I sink.

We’re no cure, no cure at all—
But you fit the wound, and that’s the call.

I’d rather be cracked with you again
than safe with anyone sane.

Veins of cinnabar, always a risk,
But still I drink, and still I wish.
Love don’t wait, love don’t mar—
It slips like light through veins of cinnabar.

Go on then, sip.
It’s already in the blood.
