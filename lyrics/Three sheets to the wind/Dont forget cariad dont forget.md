# Dont forget cariad dont forget

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-03-29
**Edited:** 2025-03-29

---

She used to say it like prayer.
Back door open, wind in the kettle.
Rain hit the slate like time itself,
and I was too young to hold it.
But I hear her now—clearer than ever
and I’m walking back the long way round.

Walked past rusted gates, same paint flaked since ninety-four
Gravel under trainers, ghosts under floorboards
House with the lean where Mamgu kept stories in jars
Pickled onions, old lace, and a shoe box of stars

Used to ride rain down gutterpipes, chase the Ebbw flow
Learned to rhyme from birdsong and my cousin’s echo
Spat verse at the brambles, thorns caught my sleeve
Truth in my mouth like blood on a loose front teeth

Never much money, but rhythm came free
It was Sunday soup steam and a cracked TV
Slate smoke chimneys, colliery air
But love spoke loud in the silence there

I was just a boy with a heart full of sparks
Drawn in the frost on windows, leaving marks
Hiraeth in my chest like a second lung
Been breathing it deep since I first bit my tongue

Cof yn fy nghefn – memory in my spine
I carry that weight in every line
Like golau through mist, it flickers and fades
But I’m still that boy in the garden shade

Seren on my path, whispering home
Words I forgot, but my marrow knows
I walk with the wind, with my name on a thread
And a voice in my head sayin’
“Don’t forget, cariad. Don’t forget.”

Left for the city, tried to scrub off the coal
Thought new words could fill the holes in my soul
But the syllables slipped like rain off glass
I spoke in tongues that forgot my past

Phone full of noise, but no calls home
Just texts from ghosts and a metronome
Tick-tick in my chest where the cof once lived
My mouth got sharp but my roots got thin

I'd dream in English, wake in doubt
Lost in a flat where the light wore out
My voice got louder, my thoughts got cold
Rhyming for fame but my rhyme got old

I stared at screens with a swallowed scream
Trying to pixel my way into someone’s dream
But all I saw was that backdoor swing
And Mamgu sayin’, "Come in, bachgen — bring your wings."

And then it hit — a smell in the rain, a kettle hiss
A crow on the wire with a look I missed
A line in a book I forgot I knew
And a boy in the mirror asking: "Where are you?"

The land don’t forget, just waits real still
Under moss and ash on the old stone sill
I felt it stir like the hour before dawn
That hiraeth hum — I was never gone

Awr yn y gwynt – the hour in the wind
Calls me back where the hills begin
Golau bach through the fog and pine
Leading me home one breath at a time

Seren still shines where the shadows bend
Language returns like an old best friend
And through the silence, I hear her yet:
“Don’t forget, cariad. Don’t forget.”

My father spoke little, but his silence taught
Lessons in patience and the weight of thought
His hands were coal, his words were rare
But when he nodded, I knew he cared

He left before the spring came in
I held his watch, still ticking thin
And in that click I heard the truth —
Time don’t stop, it just loses youth

I etched a line in a birch-tree bark
My name beside his, both faded and dark
The river rose and washed that tree
But something in me stayed rooted, free

I rhyme like the river — old, cold, clear
Spoke with the stones, now they echo in here
Seren yn y dŵr – stars in the tide
Memory don’t drown, it just learns how to hide

From the bridge to the bend, I walk my name
In the hush of the dusk, I still hear the same
“Don’t forget, cariad.” Soft like rain
Whispered in the water, again and again

There’s ghosts in the grain of the chapel pew
And I swear they move when I speak the truth
My bars ain’t bars — they’re blacksmith swing
I forge what I feel and the anvil rings

My mother hummed hymns when the world got tight
Now I hum back in the dead of night
My son don’t know the old tongue yet
But he knows the look when my eyes forget

So I tell him stories with rhythm and light
Wrap hiraeth in verses and kiss him goodnight
The line don't break — it just bends through time
I’m the bridge now. The beat. The rhyme.

I speak like a stone that remembers the fire
Pressed into the earth with a name and a choir
Cof yn y gwaed – memory in blood
I walk with the weight of the ones I loved

No fame can soften what hiraeth knows
It hums in my chest where the cold wind blows
And still she sings when I forget:
“Paid ag anghofio, cariad — not yet.”

Cof yn fy nghefn – memory in my spine
I carry that weight in every line
Like golau through mist, it flickers and fades
But I’m still that boy in the garden shade

Seren on my path, whispering home
Words I forgot, but my marrow knows
I walk with the wind, with my name on a thread
And a voice in my head sayin’
“Don’t forget, cariad. Don’t forget.”
