# Untitled

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-03-31
**Edited:** 2025-03-31

[Spoken]
Den mätta dagen, den är aldrig störst.
Den bästa dagen är en dag av törst.
Nog finns det mål och mening i vår färd
men det är vägen, som är mödan värd.
Det bästa målet är en nattlång rast,
där elden tänds och brödet bryts i hast.
På ställen, där man sover blott en gång,
blir sömnen trygg och drömmen full av sång.
Bryt upp, bryt upp! Den nya dagen gryr.
Oändligt är vårt stora äventyr.

[Verse 1]
North wind hums through pine and pain,
and I’m walking slow through the thaw again
Town still sleeps, frost on the sign,
footstep echoes like a fault in time

Boots in the snow, thoughts too loud
Face in a café glass, lost in crowd
Letters unread, things unsaid,
small cracks spiderwebbing through the head

Life ain’t sharp, just dulled by hours
Melted minutes and lukewarm showers
But you asked me once if winter ends —
I said, “It softens when the light begins”

I don’t know much, but I know this:
There’s still a kind of grace in what we miss
And I felt it today in your little laugh —
just enough light to cut the past

[Chorus 1]
I’m stuck in the stilllight —
half-asleep with my eyes wide
Days move slow, but the years slip fast
And I hold to the warmth that dares to last

Like a mittened hand or a breakfast hum,
a crayon line or a whispered “come”
Snow keeps falling, but I still stand
with your whole world wrapped in my hand

[Verse 2]
Nights stretch long like a father’s doubt
Watching shadows drift through the room and out
I used to count stars to fall asleep
Now I count breaths when the silence keeps

Moonlight spills on a hardwood floor
Half-done laundry, toys by the door
Life ain’t tidy, but you’re still small
And in your mess, I find it all

You climbed on my chest, said “sky too far”
So I traced you the constellations of my scars
Told you, “Every cut becomes a thread —
pull them right, they hold you instead”

I’m no map, but I’ll be near
Through storms, through years, through every tear
The road gets lost, but we don’t run
We light a lamp, we wait for sun

[Pre-Chorus]
And it comes —
not loud, not proud,
but like steam in the cold
or a smile from a stranger
or you

Saying “Papa,”
and it feels like spring
even in February

[Chorus 2]
I’m stuck in the stilllight —
where the quiet holds me just right
Time don’t heal, but it lays things down
like snow on the streets of this small town

You run ahead with your red wool hat
I walk behind with a heart gone slack
But you look back — and you wait, and you smile
And suddenly I’m alright for a while

]Verse 3]
There’s a birch tree path past the frozen creek
Where I used to go when I couldn’t speak
And I swear last week when I walked through slow
I heard the voice of a boy I used to know

He asked me:
“Do you still dream in light?”
I said:
“I do — just mostly at night”

But in your sleep, I see it shine
That golden thread I lost in time
You breathe like you’ve never feared the end
And I learn that lesson again and again

]Verse 4]
Now I make breakfast like a prayer
Eggs in the pan and steam in the air
You hum a tune I don’t quite know
But it’s the most real song I’ve ever been shown
Some folks chase fire, others chase fame
I chased silence, but it never came
Until your voice filled the cracks in me
Like light through the cracks in a winter tree
I still get stuck, I still fall through
But now when I break, I break near you
And you hold me fast with your mittened grip
Like I’m the one just learning to live

]Bridge]
We don’t outrun the cold.
We walk through it.
One step.
One breath.
One small laugh
that echoes louder than regret.
And some nights still sting —
But there’s warmth in your drawing
of the two of us
under a giant yellow sun.

[Musical]

[Spoken]
Hundra år i fickan, men tiden känns tom,
drömmar som damm i en gammal salong.
Vi sparar på skratt som om världen är skör,
går runt i cirklar där ingen oss hör.
Vi skriver med hjärtat, men läser med skärm,
glömmer att känna, vi scrollar oss varm.
Men nånstans i bruset, en röst som består:
"Vi har bara nu — inte hundra år."
