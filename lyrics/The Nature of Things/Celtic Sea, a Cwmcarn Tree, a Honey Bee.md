# Celtic Sea, a Cwmcarn Tree, a Honey Bee

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-11-20
**Edited:** 2024-11-20

---

[Intro]
The kettle hums, the toast turns gold,
The cabin’s warmth wards off the cold.
She shuffles in with sleep-tousled hair,
Climbing up into her favorite chair.


"Good morning, little sun, what’s the plan today?"

"Adventures, Papa, let’s go and play!"
"But first, my love, let’s eat our fill,
Before we wander the woods and hills."


[Verse 1]
Through the Cwmcarn woods, the paths run deep,
Where ferns and leaves in shadows sleep.
She finds an acorn, smooth and round,
Her laughter rings, a joyful sound.

"What’s this treasure, love, you’ve found?"

"An acorn crown for my head so proud!"
"Shall we leave it here for the squirrels to see?"
"No, Papa, it’s a crown for me!"


[Chorus 1]
Celtic Sea, a Cwmcarn tree, a honey bee,
Her favorite things, so wild, so free.
Through Wales and Cornwall, her heart takes flight,
A world of wonder in her sight.

[Verse 2]
At the Machen brook, the waters glide,
Reflecting the trees on either side.
She skips a stone, her aim just right,
It bounces twice, then takes its flight.


"Papa, do fish see the stones we throw?"

"They watch them fall, then let them go.
But they wonder too, just like you,
What treasures the stream might carry through."


[Verse 3]
By the Celtic Sea, the winds run wild,
The waves greet her with a nature’s smile.
She finds a shell, so smooth and white,
Her eyes aglow with soft delight.


"What will you do with the shell, my dear?"

"I’ll keep it close, to hold the sea near.
When the waves are far, and I feel alone,
I’ll listen to this, and it’ll take me home."


[Chorus 2]
Celtic Sea, a Cwmcarn tree, a honey bee,
Her favorite things in all she sees.
Through mountains high and rivers wide,
Her world unfolds, her heart’s her guide.


[Bridge]
Through golden fields and skies of blue,
Her little heart finds something new.
Each hill we climb, each road we roam,
Turns every path into a home.


"What will tomorrow bring, my light?"

"More favorite things and new delights!"


[Instrumental Solo | Strings and Guitar]


[Verse 4]
At Llyn Crafnant, the lake lies still,
A mirror of clouds and the rolling hills.
She tosses a pebble, her giggle bright,
As ripples dance in the fading light.

"Papa, do clouds stay in the lake at night?"

"They drift away when the stars shine bright,
But they leave their mark, just like you,
In every place they pass through."


[Verse 5]
In St. Ives’ lanes, the gulls take flight,
The air smells of salt, the sun feels light.
She holds her cone of chips with pride,
Her laughter rising like the tide.


"Papa, do gulls like chips as much as me?"

"Perhaps they do, but they’d steal them, you see!"
"Then let’s eat fast before they come near,
Our chippies are safe when I’m here!"



[Chorus 3]
Celtic Sea, a Cwmcarn tree, a honey bee,
Her favorite things, her melody.
Through winding trails and endless skies,
Her world’s a wonder in her eyes.


[Outro]
The sun dips low, the day winds down,
The hills grow soft with a golden crown.
Her treasures gathered, her stories told,
Her heart holds memories worth more than gold.


"Celtic Sea, a Cwmcarn tree, a honey bee,
Her favorite things, forever with me."
