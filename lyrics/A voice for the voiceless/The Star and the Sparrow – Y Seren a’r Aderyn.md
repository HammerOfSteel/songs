# The Star and the Sparrow – Y Seren a’r Aderyn

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-02-14
**Edited:** 2025-02-15

---

"Long ago, in the valleys below Twmbarlwm, the cuckoos called, singing in the April breeze. The people of Risca listened, their hearts warmed by the sound, for they knew it meant the days would be long, the fields would be bright, and summer would stay. But summer never stays forever... and neither do the cuckoos."

High above the hills so steep,
Where the shadows softly sleep,
A little bird with trembling wings,
Dreamed of all the sky could bring.

Y seren yn galw, y gwynt yn canu, (The star is calling, the wind is singing,)
Adenydd bach, ond breuddwyd fawr. (Small wings, but a mighty dream.)
Fly, little sparrow, don’t be afraid,
The sky was made for you today.

The cuckoo soared, the cuckoo cried,
The sparrow watched, afraid to try.
“What if I fall? What if I stray?”
The star above just shone and swayed.

Y seren yn sibrwd, adenydd yn ehangu, (The star is whispering, wings are widening,)
Hedfan bellach, does dim ofn. (Fly further now, there is no fear.)
Lift your heart, let go, be free,
The wind will carry, trust in me.

"They tried to build a fence, higher and higher, to keep the cuckoo close, but it was no use—the bird would always rise, always fly. A little sparrow watched, its tiny heart unsure. Could it fly too? Could such small wings carry such a big dream?"

"And high above, the evening star shimmered, watching over the valley, waiting for the sparrow to listen..."

One deep breath, the sparrow leapt,
The valley watched, the mountain wept.
And though the world seemed far below,
The star just smiled and let it go.

Y seren yn dawnsio, y gwynt yn cario, (The star is dancing, the wind is carrying,)
Breuddwyd bach, yn fawr o hyd. (A small dream, still so grand.)
Once afraid but now so high,
The smallest wings still touch the sky.

"And so the sparrow flew, far beyond the fences and fear, into the sky it was always meant to claim. And the star, forever watching, shone a little brighter that night..."
