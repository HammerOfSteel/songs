# The blessing bowl

**Labels:** Heartbound
**Created:** 2025-08-16
**Edited:** 2025-08-17

Shine upon you

On the sill where the night-breeze leans,
we place the gift that hums unseen.
For the traveller passing, tired and worn,
for the spirit that lingers till the break of dawn.
A crust of bread, a drop of wine,
simple offerings, old as time.
In the giving, the circle holds,
in the sharing, the story’s told.

May the long-time sun
Shine upon you
All love surround you
And the pure light
Within you
Guide your way on
Guide your way on

I see your hands, so small, so sure,
laying down care on the threshold floor.
You smile like the moon when the candles gleam,
your laugh the prayer that the silence means.
For in your breath, the old is new,
and faith’s not a book but the way we do.
With every crumb, the bond is sown,
that what we share, we never own.

Blessing bowl, river of song,
take the weary, carry them on.
Bread and water, night made whole,
feed the heart, the blessing bowl.

Stranger, neighbour, ghost, or kin,
all are welcome, all walk in.
The road is heavy, the dark is long,
but here’s a hearth, a hymn, a song.
And when tomorrow lifts its head,
you’ll find the crumbs, the wine, the bread.
What matters most is not what stays,
but how the gift gives back its rays.

Blessing bowl, circle unbroken,
every gift a vow unspoken.
Bread and water, soul to soul,
love abides, the blessing bowl.

May the long-time sun
Shine upon you
All love surround you
And the pure light
Within you
