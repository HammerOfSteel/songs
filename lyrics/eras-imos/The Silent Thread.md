# The Silent Thread

**Labels:** eras-imos
**Created:** 2024-11-27
**Edited:** 2024-11-27

---

The frost has kissed the window’s edge,
The morning pale, the sky half-light,
The coffee steams, the clock keeps ticking,
A quiet start to winter’s bite.

He tugs my sleeve; his coat is waiting,
Shoes lined neatly by the door,
His eyes, they speak what words are silent,
“Let’s go, Dad. What’s there to explore?”

The air bites sharp as breath turns clouds,
The sun barely rises, soft and thin,
He points to sparrows in bare branches,
A fleeting grin, a silent hymn.

The silent thread between our hearts,
Woven through the world apart,
In every glance, in every sign,
A quiet bond, so pure, so fine.

At work, my thoughts begin to wander,
Does he laugh, or is he shy?
Does he play, or stand in stillness?
The hours stretch as minutes fly.

The snow begins its quiet falling,
Dusting rooftops, soft and slow,
My phone buzzes; it’s time to meet him,
And share the world in winter’s glow.

The silent thread between our hearts,
Holds steady as the seasons start,
Through drifting snow, through fleeting light,
It guides us home on winter nights.

His hand slips firmly into mine,
The snowflakes melt upon his cheek,
A world of white, so vast, so quiet,
No words are needed; we both speak.

We take the long way home together,
Through snowy fields where shadows play,
His boots leave prints like tiny echoes,
A path of wonder on display.

He turns to me and lifts a handful,
Snowflakes shimmering in his palm,
He doesn’t speak, but I can hear him,
“Look, Dad, the world is calm.”

The silent thread between our hearts,
A golden weave in frozen art,
Each snowy step, each fleeting sign,
A bond eternal, yours and mine.

The night is deep; the stars are scattered,
We watch the moonlight’s silver glow,
His breath is steady, soft and quiet,
The world has stopped; we let it show.

The days grow short, the nights grow colder,
But through it all, I’ve come to see,
No words can shape the love we carry,
This silent thread, our symphony.

The frost returns to claim the glass,
The morning pale, the sky half-light,
The thread unbroken, ever woven,
Through snow and silence, we hold tight.
