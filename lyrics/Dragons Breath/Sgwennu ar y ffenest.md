# Sgwennu ar y ffenest

**Labels:** Dragons Breath
**Created:** 2025-07-16
**Edited:** 2025-07-16

---

Teddy in the chair, socks on the stair,
Toast with jam, and mum’s old hair.
Dinosaurs sleepin’, the moon lookin’ shy —
I drew a ghost and I don’t know why.

Sgwennu on the window, finger cold tight,
Heart with wings and a star in flight.
Miss you big like toast with no spread,
Miss you even when I’m tucked in bed.
Nan says love don’t end, it just hides,
But I ain’t seen you on the garden slides.
Drawn you twice in the steam today,
One with a hat, and the smile stayed.
Rain’s on the glass like tiny taps,
I talk to you while mum has naps.
Said you’re a bird now, flying far —
But I saw you once in the cookie jar.
Ghost in the kitchen, soft and small,
I blinked — and you weren’t there at all.

Sgwennu ar y ffenest, with my hand,
Draw you near with mist and sand.
One more heart, one more kiss,
All I got’s a shape and this.

I told the dog but he don’t care
He just yawns and sniffs the air.
Said I’d run away with you to town,
Where clouds make tea and don’t wear frowns.
Wrote your name with a steamy swipe,
Put a crown on top and a tiny bike.
I heard your laugh in my left shoe,
It said, “Oi, don’t step in poo!”
It’s silly, innit? Still, I try —
Whisper to the towels when I dry.
Draw our house with the big green tree,
And me and you and our cup of tea.
Don’t need much, just this small square,
A window fogged and a breath of air.

Sgwennu ar y ffenest, let it stay,
Til the sun comes up and takes it away.
One more ghost, one more grin,
It’s just me here — drawin’ you in.

Mam says stories never end,
They loop like trains round every bend.
I made you soup in my toy pan,
With dragon beans and a kiss from Gran.
Left it out by the flower pot,
Hoped you’d visit — but you forgot.
Still okay, I wrote it down,
With clouds and stars and a paper crown.
Sgwennu is magic, see it fade,
But the mist remembers what we made.
Wish I could hug you through the pane,
But all I can do is write your name.

Sgwennu ar y ffenest, here I be,
Drawin’ bits of memory.
One more smile, one more breath,
I miss you lots, but not like death.

Chalk on my sleeve, socks with holes,
Buttons that dance, cereal bowls.
Lanterns blinkin’, snails on the wall —
Wrote “I love you” in letters tall.
