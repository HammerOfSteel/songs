# Through the gardens gate

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-11-26
**Edited:** 2024-11-26

---

The sun sits high as the train rolls near,
Voices rise in youthful cheer.
A map’s unfolded, plans half-made,
"We’ll wing it, we’re here—don’t be afraid!"

"Do you think they’ll let us play on stage?"

"Only if you promise to act your age."

"Then I’m doomed—we’ll never get far,
But maybe I’ll buy you a snack at the bar."

Through the gate where the music swells,
A patchwork of colors, a world that compels.
The scent of food, the buzz of sound,
A festival sprawling, magic unbound.

"Let’s find the stage, the good stuff’s there!"

"I’d rather explore—do you dare?"

"Lead the way, I’ll follow your call,
But if we’re late, it’s your fault, after all."

Through the garden’s gate, where the world feels wide,
Where laughter blooms and dreams collide.
Each sound a spark, each step a thread,
A fleeting joy where hearts are led.

A puppet show where the children cheer,
A mural bright with its colors clear.
They stop by a stall with handmade rings,
"Do you think this suits me?" "You like strange things."

The group gathers close by the cider stand,
Clinking glasses in a makeshift band.

"To the stars, to love, to bad decisions!"

"To maps we lose and blurry visions."

Through the garden’s gate, where life feels new,
Where voices rise and colors bloom.
Each sound a spark, each thread a song,
A fleeting joy that won’t last long.

The band begins as the sun dips low,
A golden hue in the evening’s glow.
She leans on him as the music plays,
"Do you think it’s moments like these that stay?"

"It’s not the big things, not the plans we make,
It’s the quiet seconds, the paths we take."
Their friends are singing, their arms held high,
A melody woven beneath the sky.

The music fades, the stars unfold,
A velvet night in a world of gold.
She turns to him, her voice turns low,
"Do you think we’ll remember this when we’re old?"

"It’s written here in the air, the tune,
In the garden’s gate, beneath the moon."

The last set starts, the crowd sways near,
A soft refrain they both can hear.
Her friends are laughing, their voices bright,
But she feels at peace in the fading light.

"Let’s stay a bit longer, just you and me."

"But don’t you need cider or snacks by the tree?"
"Maybe I do, but not just yet,
This is the part I don’t want to forget."

Through the garden’s gate, where the vines entwine,
A fleeting warmth, a moment divine.
Each sound a spark, each voice a thread,
A story woven where hearts are led.

The lanterns fade as the garden sleeps,
The echoes linger, the night runs deep.
They walk as the laughter softly wanes,
Through the garden’s gate, where love remains.

"Through the garden’s gate, where dreams unfold,
The world feels warm, the night feels whole."
