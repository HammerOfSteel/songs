# Boing! In the Steam We Dream

**Labels:** The Deep Darkness
**Created:** 2025-04-17
**Edited:** 2025-04-17

---

We snuck out past the neon haze,
The vending machines blinked like old arcades.
She said the air felt "too clean to be safe,"
And he laughed, said, "maybe it's flavored like grapes."

Down winding trails past signs that blinked,
A steam cloud rose where the edges kinked.
A voice said, “boing! you look like soup,”
And we looked at each other, too tired to stoop.

We dropped our bags and kicked off our shoes,
Let silence do what words might bruise.
He said, “I think this water remembers us.”
And she said, “If we vanish here, I’d be okay.”

In the steam we dream,
Of nights not stitched at the seams.
Of touch that’s light, and talk that floats,
Of wrinkled skin and half-told hopes.
No answers here, no battles to win —
Just lanterns glowing where the dark’s been thin.

She asked if I’d ever kissed someone scared,
Then looked away like it wasn’t quite dared.
He said, “once, under bleachers — but I think I missed.”
And we laughed so hard it curled like mist.

Mr. Saturn offered “sparkle leaf stew,”
Said, “boing! makes love and lungs feel new!”
We toasted cups, not knowing what was in,
But it burned just right — like a secret grin.

I traced a line on the fogged-up glass,
Wrote “home” then wiped it, let it pass.
She said, “Let’s not fix this. Let’s just float.”
And I said, “Deal. I forgot my coat.”

In the steam we dream,
Where the world stops asking what we mean.
No quests, no maps, no need to try,
Just sighs and stars and soup nearby.
We won’t stay long — but long enough
To remember soft is also tough.

We stayed ‘til dawn made halos of steam,
And walked back changed — but never clean.
She said, “This night’s not real, right?”
And he said, “Then let’s keep it tight.”

I still feel that heat in colder days,
The way laughter curved in Saturn’s maze.
They waved goodbye with spoons held high —
“boing! tell world — onsen is why.”

“boing. your heart is noodle. time is warm water. come back when sky feels loud. we wait, we soup. thank you. zoom.”
