# Fractal

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-11-26
**Edited:** 2024-11-26

---

The train hums low; the skyline bends,
Each tower fades, where the river ends.
The glass reflects a face I know,
But the edges blur where the fractures show.

"Do they see me, the me that’s real?
Or just the one I let them feel?"

"They see the surface, polished bright,
But miss the cracks beneath the light."

The house feels smaller than it did before,
The walls too clean, the air unsure.
They greet with smiles, polite and brief,
Their words a bandage, not relief.

"Am I the daughter they think they raised?
Or just the ghost of their well-worn praise?"

"You look tired, love, are you eating well?"

"It’s not food that keeps me in this shell."

Fractal lines, a mirrored sea,
Each piece reflects what they can’t see.
Infinite shapes in endless streams,
A tangled self, a lattice of dreams.

The tea turns cold, the clock ticks slow,
She texts a thought she can’t let show:
"It’s quiet here, but not in me."
He replies, "You’re safe. Just breathe."

"If it gets too loud, you know I’m near,
Every piece of you is welcome here."

"Does he mean it, the all of me?
Even the parts they’ll never see?"

Fractal lines, a shape unfolds,
A fragile maze where love takes hold.
Each twist, each turn, a part of me,
A shattered whole they’ll never see.

She steps outside; the streets run wide,
The river churns with the evening tide.
Her breath slows down; the lights grow dim,
She finds herself thinking of him.

"Could I be seen, and not retreat?
Could I be loved, and feel complete?"
The fractal spins, its beauty clear,
A map of pain and love and fear.

The train hums back through the evening gray,
She texts again, "I’m on my way."
His reply: "Come home, my star,
Every piece of you is who you are."

The city fades, the skyline bends,
Each shadow breaks, but it mends, it mends.
The fractal turns, its beauty wide,
A world of selves she won’t let hide.

Fractal lines, a mirrored sky,
Each shard reflects the reasons why.
Infinite shapes in endless streams,
A tangled self that dares to dream.

The house glows warm as she steps inside,
The pieces shift, but they coincide.
A text reads softly, "You’ve made it through,"
Each fractal line, a path to you.
