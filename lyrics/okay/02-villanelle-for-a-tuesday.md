# Villanelle for a Tuesday
**Album:** Okay
**Track:** 2
**Created:** 2026-03-16

---

## About the Form

A villanelle has 19 lines: five tercets (3-line stanzas) and a closing quatrain (4-line stanza).
Two lines — called the refrains — repeat throughout and lock together at the very end.

**Refrain A:** *It's just a Tuesday. Nothing happened here.*
**Refrain B:** *The world kept going. I was somewhere near.*

These two lines return again and again whether the speaker wants them to or not.
That is the form. That is the grief.

---

## Style
- Opens with fingerpicked acoustic — The Arcadian Wilds territory: precise, intimate, math-folk clarity, no excess
- When the second verse arrives the UK indie element enters: angular electric guitar, tight drumming, that Hot Hot Heat/early British indie urgency underneath the acoustic
- The tension between the gentle fingerpicked line and the tight indie rhythm *is* the tension of the song — ordinary surface, pressure underneath
- The two refrain lines are sung identically each time, same melody, same weight — the music does not ornament them or rescue them
- Everything strips back for the final quatrain — acoustic fingerpick only, like the beginning, the indie urgency gone; what remains is the voice and the two lines arriving together for the first time

## Instrumentation
- Fingerpicked acoustic guitar (capo'd — bright, close, precise)
- Electric guitar enters verse 2 — clean tone, angular picking pattern, slightly dry
- Brushed snare and kick enter with the electric — restrained, tight
- No bass until the final stanza, where a single bowed bass note holds underneath
- No chorus, no swell — the villanelle form provides all the structure needed
- Final quatrain: acoustic only, bowed bass note, voice

## Production Notes
- Single voice throughout — male, British, close-mic'd, steady
- The refrain lines must be sung identically every time — same pitch, same phrasing, same volume — the repetition is not for emphasis, it is the point
- No vocal reverb on the refrains — dry, present, in the room
- The song should feel like being trapped in a very ordinary room, rather than like suffering dramatically

## Full Lyrics

---

[Verse 1]
[fingerpicked acoustic only, voice close and plain]

The kettle boils. The light comes through the blinds.
I make the tea the way I always make the tea.
It's just a Tuesday. Nothing happened here.

The cup sits on the table. Something winds
through the morning like it always used to be.
The kettle boils. The light comes through the blinds.

---

[Verse 2]
[electric guitar enters, angular and dry; brushed snare underneath]

I read the same line four times. Someone finds
a reason somewhere I can't see to disagree
it's just a Tuesday. Nothing happened here.

A car goes past. The neighbour's radio unwinds
through the wall — a song from years ago, maybe three.
The kettle boils. The light comes through the blinds.

---

[Verse 3]
[fuller now — electric more present, kick drum steady; but not loud, controlled]

The afternoon does what the afternoon reminds
itself to do: it shortens. Gradually.
It's just a Tuesday. Nothing happened here.

I wash the cup. I put it back. What finds
me in the doing is the done — what used to be
the kettle boiling, light through different blinds.

---

[Verse 4]
[slight pull-back — electric quieter, brushed snare only; something thinning]

I check my phone. I don't know what one finds
in checking — maybe just the act, the company
of just a Tuesday. Nothing happened here.

The window holds the last of it. It grinds
toward evening like a day that doesn't need me.
The kettle's cold. The light has left the blinds.

---

[Final Quatrain]
[everything drops — acoustic fingerpick only, single bowed bass note underneath; the two refrain lines arrive together for the first time]

I set the cup out for the morning. Quietly.
The kettle boils. The light comes through the blinds.
It's just a Tuesday. Nothing happened here.
The world kept going. I was somewhere near.
