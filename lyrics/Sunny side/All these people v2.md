# All these people v2

**Labels:** Sunny side
**Created:** 2025-12-27
**Edited:** 2025-12-27

---

[Intro]
[The sound of a heavy sigh,
followed by the clicking of a retractable pen.]

[A steady, acoustic guitar strum begins—warm but melancholic.]

[A mandolin enters, picking a nervous, fluttering melody high in the mix.]

[Spoken/Muttered under breath]
Right. Let’s get this right.

[Verse 1]
[Delivery is half-spoken, half-sung—rhythmic, intimate, and fast-paced]

I’ve been sitting at this kitchen,
table since the sun went down,
Watching the light fade,
from the grey to the black of the town,
And the pile of paper is growing,
a stack of white sheets,
Like a tower of surrender,
or a list of defeats.

I’ve written the stories,
I’ve told them all well,
Curated the ending,
crafted the hell,
And each one is different,
tailored and precise,
A bespoke goodbye,
a final piece of advice.

The first one was easy, 
Maybe hard in a soft sort of way.
Addressed to the one who told me to stay.

I wrote
I have always loved you,
and I always will.
Please don’t look for the logic,
don’t look for the pill.
Don’t expect forgiveness,
for the blood on the floor,
Because it isn’t your fault,
of that I am sure.

I'll tell you I’m sorry,
I absolved you of guilt,

Just..
Don’t burn down the house,
that we carefully built.
There’s so much about me,
that you never knew,
So don’t carry the weight,
of what I decided to do.

The biro is running out of ink,
scratching the page,
Encapsulating indifference,
missing the rage.

[Chorus]
[Sung melodically, harmonies layered to sound like a crowd]

Then I look at the names,
in a line on the desk,
All these people,
put to the test.

Here, I'll always carry this alone,
So I just want..
I want to go home

You don't have to understand,
Or even try to pretend.

Where do they begin?
Where do I end?

All these people I’m leaving behind,
Are the only things keeping me tethered to time.

[Verse 2]

[The beat kicks in slightly harder—a dry, muffled kick drum.]
[The vocal becomes more agitated, faster]

Oh aye,
there’s the second pile,
the ones written in red,
Metaphorically speaking,
things perhaps better unsaid.

To the one who broke me,
I wrote down your name,
And I hope when you read this,
you drown in the shame.

I told you quite clearly,
You are to blame,
For the cracks in the mirror,
For extinguishing the flame."

No,
I can’t forgive you,
I won’t make peace,
My silence today is the only release.

The pen digs through the paper,
tore a hole in the sheet
The anger feels solid,
the anger feels sweet.

But then there was the third one,
the gamble, the bet
To the person I haven’t,
quite given up on yet

Aye,
Maybe you could stop me,
before I go cold,
If you walked through that door,
if you were just bold,
If you confessed that you loved me,
if you made a scene.
You could wipe the slate dirty,
you could wipe the slate clean.

But I doubt you’d,
go to that sort of extent,
So I sealed up this envelope,
heavy with intent.

This hand cramping,
This coffee stone cold,
I’m seventeen years young,
and a thousand years old.

[Musical Section]
[The mandolin takes a solo—frenetic and rapid, sounding like thoughts racing.]
[The music swells, then suddenly drops to just the bass and kick drum.]

[Chorus]
[Vocals are softer now, more hesitant]

So I read back the notes,
line after line,
All these people, intertwined.
Standing here,
hiding behind open doors,
But look at the handwriting,
look at the scores,
Connected to memory,
connected to vein,
Whatever I lose,
is what they sustain.

All there is now,
Is what is long since gone.
Why should the night,
Ever awaken the dawn.

[Verse 3]
[Music strips back to just the acoustic guitar.]
[Very intimate, spoken word, no rhythm, just storytelling]

I looked at the stack.
Must be ten,
maybe twelve letters there.
Holding on to the anger,
The guilt and the fear.

The sheer volume of it.
The decision of leaving,
Where is the grand design,
In these threads they are weaving.

Who are all these people,
There are to live for.
Those instans before.
The ones I love,
The ones I hate,
Is it already too late...
Including myself..
Especially myself...

[Outro]
[Gentle, hopeful guitar picking.]
[The mandolin returns, slow and sweet]

In all my anger,
In all of this hate,
No, it's is not,
Not to late.
The love and the anger, the virtue and sin.
Tore up the letters,
Don't let this win.
This sorrow, rage and regret,
And so I told myself quietly... 
"Not yet.",
"Not yet."

So I picked up a new sheet
clean and white,
In the very first crack of the morning light,
I didn’t write a poem,
I didn’t write a rhyme,
I didn’t try to explain the this time.

I just wrote five words to start the display

"Dad..."
[Pause—voice cracks slightly]

"I think I need help."

[Fade Out]
[The mandolin holds a final, suspended chord. One deep exhale.]
