This little light o’ mine, I’m gon’ let it shine…
…yeah, all day, all night, even when the sky don’t like.

Got a matchstick heart in a jar of storms,
Tiny flame dancin’ where the night gets warm,
Pocket of gold dust, mercury moon,
Mix it in the mortar, watch the shadows swoon.
Street lamps flicker like they tryin’ to speak,
Same as my nana hummin’ hymns in her sleep,
I’m keepin’ my spark where the dark can’t chew,
Alchemy’s truth in a childlike view.
Step through puddles, reflections crack,
But the glow keeps bouncin’ and it don’t look back,
Said, “The stone in your hand might be lead, might be star,
But you’ll never know truth if you don’t walk far.”

This little light of mine, I’m gon’ let it shine,
Even if the wind wanna steal my time,
This little light of mine, through the rain and grime,
Ain’t no shadow gonna bend my line.

Yo, the wick don’t quit when the candle cries,
It just learns new shapes for the flame to rise,
My chest is a lantern, ribs the frame,
Breath the bellows, life the game.
Turn lead into laughter, stone into song,
Every wrong turn still pulls me along,
Pocket watch time with a crack in the glass,
Tickin’ out truth that the years can’t pass.
Said, “Hold your light close, but let it breathe,
Else it fades in the grip like autumn leaves,
The lamp don’t fear the midnight cold,
It was born in the dark to make shadows fold.”

Shine in the kitchen, shine in the street,
Shine where the cobbles and the crossroads meet,
Shine like a promise you made as a kid,
Before the world told you what you couldn’t did.

This little light of mine, I’m gon’ let it shine,
Through the tear-stained nights and the uphill climb,
This little light of mine, keep it pure, keep it fine,
Cause a glow like this rewrites the line.

See, the first thing God did was whisper, “Light.”
Didn’t shout, didn’t roar, just cut the night,
A single spark in an endless sea,
That’s enough, sometimes, for alchemy.
I seen candles in prison, torches in rain,
Hope in a pocket with nothin’ but pain,
I’ve been both the moth and the match that burns,
Both the student and the book that learns.
And I keep that glow where my heartbeat hums,
Even when the streetlight never comes,
It’s a child’s laugh in an old man’s chest,
It’s the gold you find when you’ve lost the rest.

Shine in the corners where the hurt still sleeps,
Shine for the promise that the dark can’t keep,
Shine for the dream you were scared to name,
Shine ‘cause the light and the love’s the same.

This little light of mine, I’m gon’ let it shine,
In the cracks of the world where the stars won’t climb,
This little light of mine, through the grit and grime,
It’s a spell, it’s a song, it’s a gold-red sign.

This little light… in my pocket, in my soul,
Even if I’m small, I still glow whole.
