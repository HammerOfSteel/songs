# Through Fire and Ash

**Labels:** A Spiral Path, Dancing salamanders
**Created:** 2024-09-23
**Edited:** 2024-09-23

---

Style: Folk-rock, with a haunting melody and driving rhythm


Verse 1
I return through fields of fire, where the ash still paints the sky,
The home I left feels cold and dark, though the sun still rises high.
My father’s eyes, they pierce me still, though his words, they never land,
In silence, I can feel the weight, the burden of their hand.

Pre-Chorus
Through fire and ash, I’ve found my path, but is it what they see?
The man I am, the road I’ve walked, will it ever set me free?

Chorus
But their eyes burn like embers, their silence like stone,
I carry their judgment deep in my bones.
Through fire and ash, I seek my own flame,
Yet all they can see is the weight of my name.


Verse 2
My mother turns her gaze away, her heart locked in the past,
She speaks of honor, duty’s call, but never asks what lasts.
They gave me life but hold it tight, a tether to their dreams,
But in my soul, a fire burns, to tear apart these seams.

Pre-Chorus
Through fire and ash, I stand alone, though I’m standing in their light,
The love I seek, the peace I crave, feels just beyond my sight.

Chorus
But their eyes burn like embers, their silence like stone,
I carry their judgment deep in my bones.
Through fire and ash, I seek my own flame,
Yet all they can see is the weight of my name.

Bridge
The chains of blood, they hold me fast, though I yearn to break away,
To walk my path, to find my voice, in the dawn of a new day.
But can I leave the ashes here, the remnants of their dreams?
Or must I carry them with me, through the night, like moonlit beams?

Chorus
For their eyes burn like embers, their silence like stone,
I carry their judgment deep in my bones.
Through fire and ash, I seek my own flame,
But all they can see is the weight of my name.

Outro
Through fire and ash, I walk this line, with family in my veins,
But somewhere in this burning land, I’ll break free from the chains.


---

Style Explanation:
The folk-rock style, with its haunting melody and driving rhythm, reflects the protagonist’s internal struggle between the expectations of his family and his desire to forge his own path. The music carries a sense of urgency, mirroring the tension between family duty and individual freedom, while the folk influence grounds the song in the traditions of Edria, adding depth to the emotional conflict.
