# Jack fell down

**Labels:** Little Heart, Little Spark
**Created:** 2025-08-12
**Edited:** 2025-08-12

---

aye, jack fell down… got back up…
still, that hill’s got teeth, bruv.

Tumble in the thistle, roll in the rain,
bit of mud on the lip, bit o’ grit in the brain.
Bucket in hand like a crown for a king,
trip on a root, still hummin’ that thing.

Little slip, little twist, little tilt of the sky,
laugh ‘til you wheeze, don’t ask me why.
Well keeps callin’ like it’s singin’ my name,
every climb back up is the same old game.

Jack fell down, tumbled ‘round,
rolled his crown on stony ground.
Still I go, still I run,
chasing hills in the morning sun.

Bounce to the beat of the bucket’s clang,
trip on the path where the nettles hang.
Hop to the side, then skid on the moss,
gravity’s cheeky when you’re not the boss.

Wind in the ears and the sweat in the eyes,
feet keep dancin’ on a slope that lies.
Bruise on the knee and a grin in the soul,
you can’t fill a well if you don’t take the stroll.

See, the thing ‘bout falling?
It’s only the ground saying hello again.

Jack fell down, tumbled ‘round,
rolled his crown on stony ground.
Still I go, still I run,
chasing hills in the morning sun.

Every spill’s got a story, every slip’s got a grin,
you lose what you carry, then you go back in.
Well’s still there with its dark glass stare,
dare me again? Yeah, I’ll climb, I swear.

Round and around like the rhyme won’t quit,
bucket in the hand and my feet won’t sit.
Jack fell down, but the sky stayed blue,
you’d fall too if the hill called you.

Can’t keep me off it,
can’t tell me no.
I’ll be up that hill
‘til the water won’t flow.

Jack fell down, tumbled ‘round,
rolled his crown on stony ground.
Still I go, still I run,
chasing hills in the morning sun.
