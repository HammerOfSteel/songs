# Kindeling kin

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-25
**Edited:** 2025-02-03

---

“Fly away, little bird, and drift through the sky,
Close your wings now, let dreams pass by.”
Dad taps the door twice, a rhythm I know,
And leaves with a whisper, soft as snow.

Rain taps lightly on the window’s glass,
And sleep pulls me down as memories pass.
I’m small again, in the garden we stay,
With marigolds bright, and her voice like day.

Through my young eyes, the world felt kind,
Her love a fire, though sparks flicked behind.
But shadows stirred where I couldn’t yet see,
A burden she carried quietly for me.

Her hands in the soil, tracing deep lines,
Eyes drifting to places outside of time.
She hums a song, soft as the rain,
But I feel a sadness she can’t explain.

The sun was high, but her gaze turned low,
And I didn’t yet know the weight she’d tow.
She told me, "Little bird, stay near, stay bright,"
While holding her own storms out of sight.

Through my young eyes, the world felt kind,
Her love a fire, though sparks flicked behind.
But shadows stirred where I couldn’t yet see,
A burden she carried quietly for me.

The petals danced where butterflies flew,
And I thought all skies were endless and blue.
But in her hands, I now understand,
She held both joy and a weight unmanned.

Through my young eyes, the world felt kind,
Her love a fire, though sparks flicked behind.
But shadows stirred where I couldn’t yet see,
A burden she carried quietly for me.

Years have passed, and I see it now—
The weight she carried, the why and how.
But every flame she kindled burned through,
A gift she left in all that I knew.
