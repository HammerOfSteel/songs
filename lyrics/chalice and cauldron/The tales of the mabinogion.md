# The tales of the mabinogion

**Labels:** chalice and cauldron
**Created:** 2025-04-07
**Edited:** 2025-04-17

---

Culhwch and Olwen (c.1100) - "Homecoming"
A wild, comic quest where Culhwch seeks to win the hand of Olwen, daughter of the giant Ysbaddaden, with the help of his cousin King Arthur and a band of fantastical warriors.

The Four Branches of the Mabinogi (c.1190)
A cycle of four interconnected tales exploring magic, betrayal, transformation, and fate in a mythic Welsh world.

Pwyll Prince of Dyfed - "قمر، شمس، نجم"
Pwyll swaps places with Arawn, lord of the Otherworld, earning honor and eventually winning the hand of the powerful Rhiannon through cunning and perseverance.

Branwen Daughter of Llyr - "Starling Cry"
A tragic tale of political marriage and betrayal between Britain and Ireland, ending in war, devastation, and deep sorrow.

Manawydan Son of Llyr - Let there be light
A quieter, stranger tale where Manawydan and his companions face mysterious enchantments and must use wit and patience to restore their land.

Math Son of Mathonwy - Blodwen.exd
A tale of power, punishment, and magical transformation, including the creation of Blodeuwedd, a woman made from flowers who betrays her husband.

Lludd and Llyfelys (c.1200–1250) - "Three things"
King Lludd seeks the aid of his brother Llyfelys to rid Britain of three mysterious plagues, blending folklore and political allegory.

The Three Romances (c.1200–1250)
Welsh retellings of Arthurian legends with distinct local flavor and emphasis on honor, love, and loyalty.

The Lady of the Fountain - Lands end
Owain, a knight of Arthur’s court, discovers a magical fountain, fights its guardian, wins a lady’s love, and must reconcile his wandering heart with his promises.

Peredur Son of Efrawg - When the Bell Breaks
A naive youth sets out to become a knight, encountering strange and symbolic trials, including a mysterious bleeding lance and a hidden prophecy.

Geraint Son of Erbin - OOP (Object Oriented Philosophy)
A tale of love and jealousy, where Geraint proves his worth as a knight while testing the strength of his bond with his beloved Enid.

The Dream of Macsen Wledig (c.1200–1250) - Dream Kernel
The Roman emperor Macsen dreams of a distant land and a beautiful maiden, then journeys to Britain to claim both, blending dream-vision with historical romance.

The Dream of Rhonabwy (c.1300–1350) - Everyone in This Room Is Me
A surreal, satirical dream in which the warrior Rhonabwy sees Arthur’s court in a strange light, revealing tensions between past ideals and present realities.
