# Rhif y Gwlith

A paperclip can be a wondrous thing. 
More times than I can remember, one of these has gotten me out of a tight spot. 

I could fix this if I just had some duct tape.
Pete, you have no taste. 

Bus pass stitched with plasters, pockets full of old disasters, paperclip dreams and hopes on toast, that’s dinner, and still I’d boast.
Scuffed knees and heartstrings frayed, patched ‘em up with the words she made. Little voice sayin’ “Nid yw'n ddim byd”, like: “it’s nothin’, we’ll be alright, see?”
Cwtsh in a storm, brewed strong in a flask, healin’ don’t ask, it just does what it has. Duct tape soul, stitched outta bits, string theory? Nah, more like string and grit.

Rhif y gwlith, too much to name, but we carry it on, all the same. From bendy spoons to busted shoes, we mend the world with whatever we use.
Rhif y gwlith, too much to take, but still I smile for both our sakes. We don’t need gold, don’t need fame, just breath, just love, just call my name.

See, Taid once built a kite from bins, flew like magic on the Llanrumney winds. Told me: “you ain’t broke, bach, just bent,” And cracked his tea grin, wise and spent.

While skipping up and down the sky,
a missing moonbeam mocked my eye,
enough to make a swallow cry,
enough to make a swallow cry

For someone stole a star or two
and something else that fate withdrew,
jewel of joy, my Tima Elara,
my whole wide world, my samsara.

She’d bring me screws and dinosaur plasters, said they fix sad just a bit faster. Bit of ribbon, a jigsaw piece, and somehow the ache would slightly cease.
Ain’t no manual for this grief dance, just slow steps and second chance. Tryin’ to fix the unfixable feels, like gluin’ back waves or stitchin’ up eels.

So what is left, O soul, where does
the prudent soul pay heed?
Become less of a taker
always give to those in need.
Which seeds are we to plant
upon this plot of ground we plow?
Sow seeds of love, be brave, and dwell 
in the eternal now. 

So don’t hand me hope like a tissue for show, I want fire, realness, rain, the low. But even then I gather the scrap, build a raft with my rage and the bubblewrap.
McGyver grief with matchstick tools, paper boat floatin’ through tidal rules. Tears as glue, stubborn as sin, I build from pain, and still I grin.

Crack on, crack up, then crack wide open, that’s the sermon of the soft-spoken. Singin’ in tune with a warbled breath, makin’ somethin’ from nothin’, defyin’ death.

Rhif y gwlith, too much to name, but we carry it on, all the same. From bendy spoons to busted shoes, we mend the world with whatever we use.

MacGyver or conniver,
aye, spidey-sense tingling..
