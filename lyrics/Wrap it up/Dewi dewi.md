# Dewi dewi

**Labels:** Wrap it up
**Created:** 2025-03-16
**Edited:** 2025-03-17

---

"What matters it that all around
Danger and grief and darkness lie,
If but within our bosom’s bound
We hold a bright unsullied sky,
Warm with ten thousand mingled rays
Of suns that know no winter days?"

Little feet running, skipping each stone,
Red wool jumper, daffodil sewn.
Her hands in mine, tight like a vow,
"Tad, will we always celebrate now?"

Steaming cawl, bread on the side,
She laughs so hard, her voice swings wide.
Flags on the high street, voices sing,
She spins like the world is a golden ring.

Mae’r haul yn gwenu, dros y bryn,
(The sun is smiling, over the hill,)
Ei lleisiau’n aros, y galon yn llawn.
(Her voices remain, the heart still full.)

Boots on the canal path, slow on the bend,
Same road, same sky, but not the same end.
Market’s alive, bread’s still warm,
But nothing here carries her form.

Tables set, but no one calls,
Flags still wave, but silence falls.
I order a pint, but I sip too slow,
Watching the doors for a face I know.

"Strange how the past walks beside you.
Even when you’re not looking."

Mae’r haul yn gwenu, dros y bryn,
(The sun is smiling, over the hill,)
Ei lleisiau’n aros, y galon yn llawn.
(Her voices remain, the heart still full.)

Paper dragons, flags held high,
She’d race the wind, arms open wide.
Singing too loud, getting words all wrong,
Didn’t matter, she knew she belonged.

She’d tug my sleeve, point to the sky,
"Look, Tad! Dewi’s watching!"—light in her eye.
And I’d nod, I’d smile, I’d take her hand,
Sand through an hourglass, turned in my hand.

"If I had time,
I'd say so much more,
I'd hug you all the tighter,
Like always before"

Now, I light the candle, place the bread,
Say her name in the words I left unsaid.
The town still sings, the bells still chime,
I walk the long way, counting time.

Footsteps in echoes, daffodils bright,
Red ribbons flutter, caught in light.
Maybe she’s here, maybe she’s near,
Or maybe it’s just St. David’s Day again this year.

Mae’r haul yn gwenu, dros y bryn,
(The sun is smiling, over the hill,)
Ei lleisiau’n aros, y galon yn llawn.
(Her voices remain, the heart still full.)

"With never-failing thankfulness I
welcome thee, benignant power,
Sure solacer of human cares
And brighter hope when hope despairs."
