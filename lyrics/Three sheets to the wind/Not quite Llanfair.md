# Not quite Llanfair

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-03-30
**Edited:** 2025-03-31

---

I never quite made it to Llanfair.
Not the place on the sign, but the one in my mind.
It was always just ahead
past your laugh,
past your first step,
past the echo of her voice in yours.
And still, I walked.

First time I saw you
red-faced storm, lungs full of fight,
your whole body said “Here I am” before you even opened your eyes.
And I was scared,
'cause she wasn’t there.
And I didn’t know how to hold both joy and ache
in the same arms.
But I did.
You curled under my coat on that frost-bitten morning,
canal path silent, swans drifting like prayers.
And I whispered to the water,
“She’s here.
She’s here.
She’s mine.”
And the water didn’t answer,
but you did.
With a blink,
and a yawn,
and a tiny fist against my chest.

Not quite Llanfair,
but close enough to sing.
With your hand in mine,
even winter felt like spring.
Not the ending we dreamed,
but still a path we shared
with your joy like a lantern
in all the places I was scared.

You said “Hold still,” when I scraped my knee,
but I kicked and cried like it swallowed me.
The world was too loud, too sharp, too wide,
but you stayed,
and you held,
and you never left my side.
When they wouldn’t share, I broke inside
and you didn’t fix it,
you just sat in the tide.
You taught me stars were names we borrowed,
that every bruise means there’s a tomorrow.
You held my hair when I was sick,
sang songs that didn’t even rhyme right,
but they worked.
And that made them mine.

wasn’t always calm.
But I was always there.
I wasn’t always wise,
but I never stopped care.

Not quite Llanfair,
but I still knew the road.
With your laugh like a river
and my heart the boat.
Every cry, every skip, every middle-of-the-night
we carved out a home
from the edge of the light.

Do you remember the fair?
You in that yellow scarf,
cotton candy cheeks and the stars like soft glass above us?
I watched you spin beneath the lights,
feet barely touching ground.
And I thought this is it.
This is the place.
And then it passed.
Like so many things.
But I carry it still
that night when the music slowed
and you looked at me like I’d hung the moon
when all I did was pay the tokens.
That’s fatherhood.
That’s magic.
That’s you.

Not quite Llanfair,
but maybe we never had to go.
We had knees to patch,
and tales to sew.
Llanfair’s a song,
and you were the tune
my compass, my candle,
my echo, my June.

I fell asleep on the backseat once

You were humming some tune from school.

You tucked my bear back in my bag

I smiled like I wasn’t the fool.

I asked if stars remember names

I said “Only the ones who sing.”

I never quite believed that
But still, you kept me believing.

We didn’t make it to Llanfair.
But we made it here.
And that’s something.

No maps for this.
No train to take.
Just feet on the path,
and the time we make.
Not quite the end,
not quite the start
just a place between
your hands and my heart.

You once said
we were just passing through
but I think some places live inside you.
And Papa...
I see Llanfair
in you.
