# Two, for two

**Labels:** Counterfactual History
**Created:** 2025-07-29
**Edited:** 2025-07-29

---

We sit among the cushion clouds
to chat and giggle, laugh
High up away from worldly dust
Delightful morsels bites,
Light banter, lovely pleasantries
the gentle winds of night.

Cloud like tea, soft, still warm
Half a smile tucked into a thunderstorm
Buttons undone, coat left hung
I keep speaking in someone else’s tongue
Aye these walks like a song I’ve half-forgot
Knows my laugh, but not the lot
Boots too big, hands like mine
Oak curls remember a warmer time
Not mine. Still mine.
Borrowed breath in another line
I nod to, the man I ain’t
Saint of the quiet, sinner of restraint

I’m not here but I ain’t gone
the hum when the telly’s left on
A tea stain, supple sigh
The page she skipped, the why not, aai.
Two for two, same old skin
parked just slightly in
Soft like rain, little leaf
Still here, in disbelief.

Jumper folded on the chair
Salt on glass, frost in air
Sticky tape, birthday card
I wrote it once. That was hard.
laughing now in someone’s arms
Aye safe. All fine. No alarms.
Book and frame, little flame
Forgot my middle name.
Wish I could hold,
Wish I could not,
Some days fire,
Some days not.
Two wears shoes that fit
Bet he does get this bit.

Sure the half that never fled
The bedtime rhyme still in yah head
A little hush inside the swing
A ghost who doesn't haunt anything.
Two for two , kinder face
He stayed around, I made the space.
Same old story, different tune
Still this crescent moon.

There’s a toothbrush in a jar
That ain't mine, but not that far
I hum her lullaby in trains
To nobody. Still, it rains.
I saw her yesterday — not her
But close enough, blur to blur
Boots too pink, steps too bold
Still I froze, heart gone cold
He was there, hand on back
His was full. Mine, slack.
That’s alright. That’s the deal.
Love don’t end — it just turns real

I’m not him. He’s not me.
But both of us still bend the knee
I’m the breath in between the songs
The part of right that carries wrong
Other me — he gets to stay
I’m the one who walks away
But I’m not lost, and I’m not less
I’m just the hush in her happiness

Summer at last
It's almost already gone past
Flowers still in bloom
Here's a purple one just for you.

I'll place it here just for you
Hoping it calls to you
Will you come visit me soon?
It could even be at noon.

Summer sweet tea
Just the way you like it
I'll pour you a glass
If I can just ask,

Two for two, all for one
Slowly rising, tired old son.
One, two and three,
The part of love you’ll never see
