# Thank You for Calling, Dad

**Labels:** The Deep Darkness
**Created:** 2025-04-18
**Edited:** 2025-04-18

---

“Beep. You have one saved message. Sunday. 7:43 PM.”

Hey, Dad.
Thanks for calling.
You always do — right before it gets too much.
Right before I feel like I might fold in half.
You ask if I want to save.
Yeah. I always do.
But not just the game.
Not just the progress.

Sometimes I wish you’d ask if I was okay.
Not my stats. Just me.
But maybe that’s not how you say things.
Maybe saving is your way of saying
“I love you.”

Click.

You say you’re proud.
I believe you. Mostly.
I hear it under the static.
I think I used to pretend you were just busy —
Not distant.

You sent money.
I spent it on cookies, hotel stays,
and the times I didn’t want my friends to see I was tired.
Thanks.
Really.

Paula said I should talk to you.
But how do you talk to a dial tone
that always sounds like your own voice
waiting to be heard?

I beat the final boss, Dad.
It didn’t feel like winning.
I came home, and it was quiet.
You called again.
Said “Well done.”
I said “Thanks.”
But I wanted to say
“I’m scared.”

I saved the world.
I don’t know if I saved myself.
You always let me save the game,
but you never said I could restart.

Sometimes I pick up the phone,
just to hear it ring.

“Next time…
just stay on the line.
I think I’d like that.”

End of message.

Click.
