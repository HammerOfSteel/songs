# Llyn y Llofrudd

**Labels:** Dragons Breath
**Created:** 2025-07-08
**Edited:** 2025-07-08

---

Take this kiss upon the brow!
And, in parting from you now,
Thus much let me avow --
You are not wrong, who deem
That my days have been a dream;
Yet if hope has flown away
In a night, or in a day,
In a vision, or in none,
Is it therefore the less gone?
All that we see or seem
Is but a dream within a dream.

Went past chapel road on me little red bike,
Bell went ting, sky went night.
Gate was open, fence was broke,
Tree said “shhh” and the wind just spoke.

Got me torch, got jam on toast,
Got one sock on and I seen a ghost.
Didn’t say boo, didn’t float nor moan—
She just whispered, “Best go home.”

Knock twice, turn the key,
Bring a cuppa if you’re comin' with me.
Ghosts don’t like no lemonade,
They want milk, and marmalade.
Three skips, hop the gate—
If it’s Llyn y Llofrudd, don’t be late.

Nana said don’t trust a puddle that stares,
Don’t trust a frog what climbs up stairs.
But I did, and he sang in Welsh,
With a top hat made of nettle mesh.

There’s faces hid in chapel glass,
And names carved under blades o’ grass.
Some say murder, some say mess,
Some say she just said yes.

Knock twice, don’t look back,
Bring a scone and a sugar sack.
Whispers crawl when it’s half-past tea,
And shadows hum in minor key.
Two skips, hold yer breath—
It’s Llyn y Llofrudd, nothin’ left.

I brought a cup, she brought a grin,
She poured the lake right over my chin.
Said: “We don’t bite, not really, pet—
We just forget, and that’s the threat.”

Wrote me name on a fern with chalk,
Left me shoe by the chapel walk.
If it’s gone by mornin’, I’ll know for sure—
That ghost had jam, and asked for more.

Knock once, then knock again,
Lake don't talk but it counts to ten.
Leave a key, take a crumb—
If you see her, don’t you run.
Llyn y Llofrudd, sweet and sour,
Tell the time by the chapel tower.

Goodnight moonlight,
Sweet dreams, moonlight,
I am away now,
Driving under your blanket, your bright stars lighting everywhere dark,
