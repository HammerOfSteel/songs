# Tiny philosopher

**Labels:** Super Nova
**Created:** 2025-06-08
**Edited:** 2025-06-08

---

Set out early,
boots unlaced and sky just bruisin’,
She’s hummin’ to herself, bag full of snacks she’s refusin’,
refusin to share, 
share with me aye little miss, 
don't you dare.

Stick in hand, already a queen of the trail,
says 

“the ants need biscuits”,

Oi, moral logic without fail.

The path dips through moss and stone,
She names every puddle like they’ve always been known.
Trickly tiny clouds, rise up like steam.
watchin’ the world redraw itself in her scheme.

puddles, pond, aye the lake.
Sunlight drips as the days awake.
Swimming, laughing, wild wild child
Clean cut questions, mind's beguiled.

“If you cry in a dream… does the pillow know?”

She says it like it’s nothin’, although.
Although little sun, "Why cry in a dream?"

well well not all is sewn as it's seems,
She just grins wide and gleams.

Tiny philosopher, sun in your teeth,
you question the sky like it owes you belief.
Toss me riddles with crumbs in your hand,
am I to grown to understand.

we hike past nests and nettle stings,
She spots a squirrel and sings: 

“That's the prince of spring.”

light on her feet, but full of ideas —
On the hill we spot some drias
(Aye, that's a deadly carrot in case you weren't aware, no really I swear)

We sit by the ridge where the trees lean slow,
She’s stackin’ stones like dominoes in a row.

“Where do the maybes go when you pick yes?”

Yes, no, maybe my love.
Any of the three or all of the above.

Fire’s lit, marshmallows in orbit,
She’s gigglin’, burnin’ them down just to sort it.
Quiet. Watching. And mid-hotdog

“Do feelings get old, like do they age?”
“Like… what’s the old happy like?
A little teeny tiny sad?

She shrugs, wipes her hands, feeds the fire with a stick
and returns to her marshmallow, unfazed by the trick.

Compiling a thought in me heart, I say no pretending to be smart

"Oh aye, they age in style, storm n brew a long time inside of you"
"They boil and bubbel, trickle double. Ferment forever stick like glue, they've become a part of you"

Like the way my love for you,
always grew and grew and grew.
Grows still with every beat,
Whole warm beat of my heart,
Has no ending nor a start.

Tiny philosopher, face full of ash,
you sit with the stars as they twinkle and flash 
You speak in questions that don’t beg a reply,
We just space out for a while beneath the breath of the sky.

Night crawls gentle. Sleeping bag zipped tight to her chin,
she's wrappin’ her words like kindling within.
The fire’s low — her voice lower still:

“If I say sorry too much… will it stop workin’?”

The silent night outside the tent is lurkin.
"Never love, sorry doesn't break as long as you mean it, the hearts awake"

“Let’s name a star after a sandwich,” she mumbles a bar.

Okay, that's the peanut butter jelly bean star.

“If I don’t look at my shadow, does it get bored?”

Oh that one strikes a chord.
"Well, do you get bored it doesn't see you?"

"Nah, I'm all up in my own init?"
She says sleepy and true

We lie back, her head just under my chin,
blanket of stars, breath synced in.
She yawns, already half-off.

"Sleep tight, tiny philosopher in night"
