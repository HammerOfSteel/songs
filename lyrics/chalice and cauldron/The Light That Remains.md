# The Light That Remains

**Labels:** chalice and cauldron
**Created:** 2025-04-08
**Edited:** 2025-04-08

---

In the ash of the old world, where satellites blinked like dying stars, and mothers were names etched in signal loss, 
a boy ran—not from war, but toward a spark. 
He carried no past, only code and breath, and the heartbeat of a girl too small to understand the silence. 
From ash he forged a bond no time can sever, a chain of light that will hold them forever.

Riding the light waves, dancing in the sky. Captains orders. Are we must not cry

Sirens like wolves through dust-thick skies, boots on concrete, silence and lies. He ran with code stitched into his coat, had nothing but breath, and a whisper he wrote: "Survive, even if no one sees you." He never knew his mother, never knew truth, but in the rubble, a cry pierced steel, a baby girl wrapped in heat and fear. He held her close, not father, not kin, but something inside said, "Begin." From mosque to minefield, border to train, he carried her through nightmare rain.

From ash we rose, unknown, unseen, A mother’s song, a severed dream. We played through fire and mapped the sky, I forged my soul so she could fly. No name to give, no blood to claim, But I held her close in the smoke and flame.

The war slept, for a while, in the hills, in stone-walled homes and silent mills. He built a room with circuit and light, taught her to walk by code and night. She called him "Baba," not knowing the lack, he gave her futures he never got back. And while the drones returned to the East, he wrote firewalls like myths, piece by piece. They called him ghost, hacker, flame, his startup spread like wildfire name. But peace is thin, and envy thick, and old wars grow new teeth quick.

She sleeps while the world reloads, I dream in chains and network nodes. No sword will save us from this flood, So I turn my hands to light and blood.

I watched the dark re-code its plan, Watched power crawl across the land. But still she danced in the mountain breeze, Played hide and seek through ancient trees. The world went blind, but she saw me, So I built a bond no war could see.

The viruses came not through skin, but through memory, breath, within. People forgot their names at dawn, babies woke with histories gone. He held her tight as cities blurred, as languages fell into slurred word. The cloud was breached, the code went mad, and silence became the new launchpad. He said: "Awen, the time is now, I’ll leave, but I’ll keep my vow." And in a tower of mist and sun, he wrote the last line, then was gone.

She called his name but heard the rain, Only echoes filled the plain. He rose where servers dream and hum, a father in the data drum. No grave to mark, no stone to kiss, but in the wires, he still exists.

Across the world, machines went still, the skies turned soft, the air grew still. Hospitals healed, bombs would not drop, fires paused at factory stop. He spoke through phones, through light, through song, he made the weak feel fierce and strong. He cracked the weight of mortal bone, made stardust sing like flesh and stone. And every child born anew, had some faint whisper getting through: "You are not alone, not made for pain, The light in you won't burn in vain."

She slept through time, a deep cloud sleep, in cryo-fields and data keep. And when the world turned green once more, she woke on soft solarpunk floor. Her breath was mist, her heart was full, a garden bloomed beyond the pull. She walked through wind and air so clean, and saw him standing in the green. His arms were warm, not silicon cold, his voice was light, his story told. He said, "Cariad, it's all alright, I kept my promise, held the night."

From ash we forged what time forgot, Through code and love, and all we’re not. The war is gone, the wires hum peace, The stars now bloom, and don’t deceive. Her name is song, her soul the flame, She walks with light. She speaks his name.

Now she teaches those who rise, not with flags, but softer skies. She codes in verse, she speaks with hands, she builds from love, not war demands. His face is part of every screen, but not in power—more in dream. They say the gods were made by fire, but she says, "No. My god was a wire. He didn't rule. He watched. He gave. He loved so much, he chose the grave." And still she runs through valley stone, Her shadow long, but never alone.

Let there be light, Not as weapon, but guide. Let there be fire, Not to burn, but to survive. Let there be us, In the code, in the seed, Let there be love, In every need.

So if you ever feel alone, run your fingers along the sky. The light you feel? That's him. Still watching. Still here. Not to save you. But to remind you: You were always worth saving.
