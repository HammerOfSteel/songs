# Mari's Midnight Visit

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-03-13
**Edited:** 2025-03-13

---

A song intertwining the ancient Welsh tradition of the Mari Lwyd with themes of hope and mental well-being.

In a cottage by the hill, 'neath the moon's soft glow,
Lay a child awake in bed, tears began to flow.
Lonely whispers filled the room, shadows danced around,
In the silence of the night, there came a haunting sound.

Oh, Mari Lwyd at my door, with your skull so white,
Sing to me your ancient songs, on this cold, dark night.
Through your rhymes and riddles, chase my fears away,
Stay with me, oh Mari, till the break of day.

Through the window, cold and thin, peered the hollow eyes,
Draped in shroud of purest white, under starlit skies.
Though her visage seemed so grim, her presence brought no fear,
For the child felt a warmth, knowing Mari's near.

"Lonely child," the Mari spoke, "why do you despair?
For the darkest nights, you know, make the stars more fair.
In the depths of winter's chill, light your inner fire,
Let your spirit soar beyond, lift you ever higher."

Oh, Mari Lwyd at my door, with your skull so white,
Sing to me your ancient songs, on this cold, dark night.
Through your rhymes and riddles, chase my fears away,
Stay with me, oh Mari, till the break of day.

[Bridge in welsh]
"Mae'r nos yn dywyll, ond y galon yn llachar,"
"Trwy'r cysgodion cerddwn, gan ddilyn y seren ar."
"Er bod y gaeaf yn hir ac yn oer,"
"Daw'r gwanwyn yn ôl, gyda'i flodau'n floer."

Oh, Mari Lwyd at my door, with your skull so white,
You have brought the light to me, on this winter's night.
Through your ancient wisdom, I have found my way,
Thank you, dear old Mari, for the hope today.

"And so, the Mari Lwyd departed with the morning dew, leaving behind a heart rekindled, a spirit renewed. For even in the darkest hours, a knock upon the door may bring unexpected light, reminding us that we are never truly alone."
