# OOP (Object Oriented Philosophy)

**Labels:** chalice and cauldron
**Created:** 2025-04-16
**Edited:** 2025-04-16

---

Slide one says: WeDance.ai – Relationship Rhythm Through Choreographic AI. 
The Tagline says: Learn the steps before you lose the rhythm.

We coded our love like product-market fit.
Danced through bootstrap funds and midnight commits.
She called me “brilliant,” I called her “agile.”
We scaled. We surged. We forgot to rest.

Now she sleeps in the guest room. And I debug dreams I don’t remember.

We met where fog wears heels, Plymouth coast and low-lit meals.
Jazz bar bleeding out the frame, she said my name like slow champagne.
I showed her slides, she showed me skin—
Beneath the code, we let love in.
Started with jokes, tech meets tulle—
She danced while I mapped out rules.
We built the pitch deck over toast,
First round closed while she danced most.
WeDance.ai, born from wine—
Therapy through motion line.
Upload your fights, sync the beat,
Watch your pain learn graceful feet.
But we forgot to learn our part,
And danced the app, not the heart.

We learned the steps, forgot the song.
You mapped me clean, but loved me wrong.
You said I flowed like perfect code—
But I was mess, not neat node.
I danced in rhythm, you timed me still—
You saw the chart, but missed the thrill.

You stopped hearing—started parsing,
I’d say “I'm scared,” you'd start tasking.
Trello board with “Fix Enid’s mood,”
I cried, you checked if Wi-Fi’s good.
You loved me best as a diagram—
In theory, pure. In practice, damn.
I cooked while you deployed regret,
Every kiss a pull request.
We launched on Monday, broke by noon,
I danced alone in empty rooms.
The AI said “step, spin, sway”—
But we hadn’t touched in days.

You weren’t a product, not a task—
I just got lost in every ask.
I built a world, forgot to feel—
Now you're the code I can’t unseal.

You said “love,” but meant design.
You pressed repeat, I danced decline.
You watched my spark as debug flame,
And called it growth—then changed my name.
We scaled too fast, forgot to see—
You loved the build, but not the me.

I packed the plants, left the lamp,
Left your world of startup camp.
You stayed in meetings, slept in Slack,
Asked “ETA on coming back?”
But I was never sprint or goal,
I was heart. You cached control.
Every hug turned KPI,
Every silence: “Can we A/B why?”
I’m not your loop, your UX test,
Not a sprint you can just finesse.
I wanted wind. You wrote me cage.
You built the app, but lost the page.

You used to riff, now read the chart.
You made me parts, I gave you art.
I danced to break, not to impress—
You saw the code, not the mess.
And now I move with none to please—
Still barefoot in the Plymouth breeze.

Found your scarf behind the screens,
Still smells like coffee, old routines.
I scroll through logs, your old voice notes,
You said, “We’re not code. We’re softest hopes.”
I told the AI to write a poem—
It quoted you. Said, “I’m not home.”
Now the app’s a beast we both outgrew,
The dance is gone, but I see you.
I see the girl with jazz in bone,
Who danced through nights we used to own.
And if you find this—hell, if you care—
I left our rhythm in the air.

You weren’t a project, weren’t a pitch—
You were my glitch, my perfect glitch.
You danced in code, I wrote in light—
But couldn’t hold you when it got tight.
And now I sway with just your ghost—
Still debugging what I miss most.

WeDance shut down last winter.
The servers sleep in silence now.
But sometimes, when the street’s gone still,
I hear her footsteps in the echo.
And for a breath, I remember:
we almost had it.
We almost danced it right.
