# Moonmilk & Marmalade

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-05-01
**Edited:** 2025-05-01

---

A great revolution was born in this land
When industry rose to the heights,
From coal, tin and iron a future was planned,
With steam trains and other delights
And when they could pause after shoveling coal
They fried up a breakfast to rebuild the soul!

Morning cracked like an eggshell wide,
Sunlight spilled on the kitchen side.
She sat with toast and sleepy eyes,
My hoodie huge, her throne in disguise.

“You gotta hum when you butter,” she said,
“And tap the jar or the charm goes dead.”
So I gave the marmalade three light knocks,
Like unlocking spells from ancient rocks.

Moonmilk and marmalade, spread so slow,
Toast that glows from dreams we know.
Whispers rise in golden shade,
Swyn haul ar dost — a love homemade.

“The milk came down from a cloud last night,
I caught it soft in my pillow light.”
“And the marmalade?”
“From the orange sun —
That rolled behind the shed in fun!”

We sipped the stars in sugared tea,
Drew smiley suns on toast for three.
She raised a spoon like a magic wand,
And knighted me with jam beyond.

I said, “You’ll forget all this, someday…”

“No I won’t — it’s the bestest way.”

Moonmilk and marmalade, warm and wide,
Drizzled sweet on the morning tide.
Time stands still when crumbs cascade,
Swyn haul ar dost — bee toast and sunshin aide.

"Write it in crayon, on bee toast in hand:
‘Rule One: All brekkies must be grand.’”

Take Newton or Darwin or other great men,
They knew what was needed to start,
A daybreaking meal that scores ten out of ten
Can give even common men heart.
So give up your baguettes don't make too much fuss
Without a full English you're no match for us!
