# Three sheets to the wind - ALBUM

**Created:** 2025-03-25
**Edited:** 2025-04-04

"Hearth, Home, Hiraeth" - Waking up after years, starting to face the truth; the nature of things, missing her, the hills, wales, home.("Gwell fy mwthyn fy hun na phlas arall" - Better my own cottage than the palace of another)

Rhisgl - "Teg yw edrych tuag adref." - It is good to look homewards

“fools gold” – not all that glimmers is gold, wasabi - imperfection - impermanence.

“Sea Glass Gospel” – worn down, made softer by time. ("Dyfal donc a dyr y garreg" - Tapping persistently breaks the stone)

“A Kettle’s to care” – the start of morning, a metaphor for grief returning before the world wakes.

"Hir yw pob ymaros" - All waiting is long

“May, may moon” – a song about an off-kilter spring, metaphor for numbness. The name is a play on the swedish "Maj, maj måne, jag kan lura dig till Skån" on April fools but holding not the same meaning of "fool". Like in "The fool is wise" or "ignorance is bliss", not whising this path on anyone etc.(Never mind April Fools' Day - Welsh folklore tells us to beware April Fools' Month, which could become April Fools' Year:
"If you are made a fool in April, you will be a fool or get befooled all through the year." 
(Folklore of Wales 1909))

“Not Quite Llanfair” – a journey toward peace, but never quite arriving.

“Threadbare Hymn” – lullaby for something worn, but not broken."


Three sheets to the wind - ("Dod yn ôl at fy nghoed") - To return to my trees - to relax and unwind, to calm your mind

“Oes y Bywyd” (The Age of Life) – a poetic take on marking time in seasons, not dates. ("Yr hen a ŵyr a'r ieuanc a dybia" -The old know and the young suspect)

"Don't forget cariad, don't forget"
