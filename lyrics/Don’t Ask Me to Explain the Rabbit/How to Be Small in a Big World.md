# How to Be Small in a Big World

**Labels:** Don’t Ask Me to Explain the Rabbit
**Created:** 2025-04-22
**Edited:** 2025-04-22

---

There’s a box, down the warren.
Old paper. Fox stamp. Bit of dust.
Letters never sent —
all labeled: “To the ones I miss.”

Dear Mam,
I made tea wrong again — no sugar, two bags, way too strong.
Still sipped it like a ritual. Still felt like you all along.
Your coat still smells like kindness and petrol.
And I still whisper when I fold the laundry slow.

I burned the carrots, forgot the socks,
But I sang your lullaby while feeding the fox.
I miss your sigh when things got loud —
And the way you never let me down, even when you should’ve.

You were the sky I drew inside —
I try to be brave, but some days I just hide.
If love is a patch, I sew your thread.
Even now. Even when I forget what I said.

How do you stay small in a world this wide,
Where grief wears boots and joy can hide?
I keep your words like pixel light —
Still blinking soft in nintendo night.

Too big for shoes, too small for pride,
I write to you when I’m terrified.
If love’s a loop, then this is mine —
Your tea, your voice, your steady spine.

Tad,
Found your screwdriver. Rusty pride. Still works. Still lies.
Used it to fix the lamp. Broke it twice.
Laughed like you — through my own advice.

You weren’t one for poems or hugs,
but your toolbox said things your voice never touched.
You taught me silence ain’t empty — it’s loaded.
And grief is like WD-40 — better when exploded.

You used to say, “Crack on, boy.”
So I cracked. Then carried on. No choice.
I still try to get the light to hum,
Like that winter when the power was gone and we made up a sun.

The world grew loud. I learned to hum.
The winter pressed — but I stayed numb.
And in that numbness, you appeared —
A voice I missed. A love I feared.

How do you be small in a world this tall,
When rooms get dark and hearts feel small?
I trace your name on steamed-up glass,
Still hoping love like yours can last.

Still glitched. Still here. Still half a mess —
Still holding space, and second guess.
I talk to stars like they’re a door —
Because you taught me less is more.

Nain,
I keep your mug by the jam. Even if the cupboard’s changed hands.
I still hear your “shh” when I move too fast —
And your voice in the butter that I meant to pass.

You were bedtime stories in cardigan threads,
The crossword queen of everything said.
You smelled like soap and recipe hope —
and your arms were the world's most reliable coat.

I miss your hymns, your quiet grace —
Your hands in the dough, your slow soft pace.
I plant things now. Not always right —
But you’d say, “Try again, bach. It might take light.”

How do you be small when the world moved on,
When everyone singing your songs is gone?
I keep your seeds. I keep your pie —
I even saved your lullaby.

You called the rain your morning tea,
You made the mundane holy to me.
Now I stir slow and speak your name —
Knowing snow and grief are not the same.

Tima,
If I could sing louder than the stars, I would.
But you always heard me, didn’t you?
Even when the world said no.
Even when your voice was light and low.

Your fox is still on the shelf.
Still smiling like you left me part of yourself.
I write these letters like bedtime prayers —
hoping one reaches through the upstairs air.

You were my glitch in the timeline’s heart,
The part I’d restart if I had the chart.
I miss your hands. Your breath. Your laugh.
You taught me what I never knew I had.

How do you be small in a world so wide,
When your love’s still here, but you’re not inside?
I write, I walk, I hold the thread —
Even if the fox says you’ve fled.

I’m small, I’m soft, I’m still your song —
Still humming where you used to belong.
If love’s a loop, then let it spin —
I’ll keep the letters. You keep the wind.

You were never small to me.
