# thread & thimble gloria

**Labels:** Pockets Of Light
**Created:** 2025-08-22
**Edited:** 2025-08-22

---

If you were only one inch tall, you'd ride a worm to school.
The teardrop of a crying ant would be your swimming pool.
A crumb of cake would be a feast
And last you seven days at least,
A flea would be a frightening beast
If you were one inch tall.

Thread through the fabric, a silver-white line,
a hymn in the hem where the daylight will shine.
Thimble on finger, a guardian small,
Amen in the prick when the needle says call.

Little stitches, little prayers, running up the seams.

Thread makes a ladder the sparrows might climb,
thimble taps softly, a bell keeping time.
Gloria whispered in patches and fray,
every loose thread is a psalm on its way.

Thread & thimble, Gloria bright,
sew me a song in the quiet of night.
Thread & thimble, Gloria clear,
stitch me a hymn that will hold me near.

Thread through the patches of aprons and sleeves,
needle a pilgrim who never deceives.
Thimble a chalice that shields from the sting,
Gloria, Gloria, the cloth wants to sing.

Every stitch a secret, every seam a psalm.

Needle prays in silver arcs,
thimble hums when daylight darks.
Thread pulls tight but never tears,
Gloria lives in hands that care.

Thread & thimble, Gloria bright,
sew me a song in the quiet of night.
Thread & thimble, Gloria clear,
stitch me a hymn that will hold me near.

You'd surf across the kitchen sink upon a stick of gum.
You couldn't hug your mama, you'd just have to hug her thumb.
You'd run from people's feet in fright,
To move a pen would take all night,

This poem took fourteen years to write,
'Cause I'm just one inch tall.
