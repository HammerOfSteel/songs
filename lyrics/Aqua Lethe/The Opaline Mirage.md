# The Opaline Mirage

**Labels:** Aqua Lethe
**Created:** 2025-07-06
**Edited:** 2025-07-06

---

Ladies and gentleman skinny and scout
I'll tell you a tale I know nothing about
The admission is free so pay at the door
Now pull out a chair and sit on the floor

On one bright day in the middle of the night
Two dead boys got up to fight
Back to back they faced each other
Drew their swords and shot each other 

Mum, you dropped the mug again, the blue one wiv the moon inside. It cracked like glass that knew too much, then held its breath and tried to hide. Dad, your arms still ache some nights— you hold the air like it’s still me. I saw you trace the window steam, like you were writin’ memory.
I remember Cornwall, summer’s grin, the sand got stuck in everythin’. You said, “that’s love—gets in your bones,” and smiled like stones could dream alone.

I’m right here, past the glass, in the shimmer that don’t last. You can’t touch me, I know that part— but I still fit inside your heart. Like flame in wax or fog in jar, I’m just a whisper… from not-too-far.

I saw the photos on the stair, the ones where joy looked unaware. I watched you look at them and pause, like souls remember without cause. You lit a candle, blinked too long— the flame bent left, but stayed on strong.
You always said I shined too loud, a star in boots, head in a cloud. But stars ain’t stars 'til they explode— they burn to mark the proper road. And love, it’s like a mirrored maze— the deeper in, the more it stays.

The blind man came to see fair play
The mute man came to shout hooray
The deaf policeman heard the noise
And came to stop those two dead boys

He lived on the corner in the middle of the block
In a two story house on a vacant lot
A man with no legs came walking by
And kicked the lawman in his thigh 

I’m still here, hid in folds, in half-told tales and coats gone old. In cereal crumbs and gravel hands, in “wait for green” and “hold my hand.” I breathe where dreams begin to sway, where night forgets the shape of day.

I walked through stars like daisy chains, saw other mes in other lanes. Some were brave and some were small, but all of us still heard you call. One held paint, one climbed trees, one just danced in kitchen breeze. And all of us remember still— the mirage road down Twmbarlwm hill.
I learned that time ain’t straight nor round— it’s more like music, not like ground. And love? It’s salt, it’s spark, it’s song— it don't need now to keep it strong.

I’ll be here when the road bends, when the days don’t make much sense. In skipped songs on rainy drives, in all the “nearlys” of your lives. You built me from a kiss, a spark, I’ll echo through the softest dark.

He crashed through a wall without making a sound
Into a dry creek bed and suddenly drowned
A long black hearse came to cart him away
But he ran for his life and is still gone today

I watched from the corner of the table
The only eyewitness to facts of my fable
If you doubt my lies are true
Just ask the blind man, he saw it too.
