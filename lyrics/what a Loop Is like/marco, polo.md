# marco, polo

**Edited:** 2025-09-08

polo
marco
po—lo

I unlace bone from name, slip spine into a shoreline,
lungs trade air for spray, a brine-thin silver lifeline.
Skin salts, seams solve, stitches solve into a seamoss,
heartbeat loosens to a buoy, rises once, drifts, gloss.
Voice thins, then widens, a siren wire through gray,
mouth learns vowel of ocean, the long round O of sway.
I pocket the moon for later, tuck a gull into my sleeve,
leave what cannot float to sink, keep what learns to leave.
Compass is a collarbone, pointing where the cold goes,
pulse is just a tide note, low note no one holds knows.
I am not a walker now, I am a walking swell,
shore writes its cursive on my chest, foam signs its salt farewell.

Call goes out like a flare under rain,
answer comes back as a stranger with my name.

over here, where I am, over there where you stand,
olive branch held in hand.
land, I see land, countless grains, shifting sands.
marco, say it, throw it, let it roll low
polo, take it, shape it, send it back slow
over here, over there, one line, one band
land, I see land, I open my hand.

Sound wobbles over water, breaks and makes another,
what you heard as lighthouse was the throat of a brother.
I peel the street from my feet, heels learn reef,
knees learn kneel to grief, then learn relief.
Haze lifts, a rib of cliff, a faith of cliffs in queue,
weather stitches weather into one long blue.
I pass my history to the tide, it edits without pity,
leaves the joke, leaves the little, leaves the pretty gritty.
Hands row nothing, still I go, muscle becomes meaning,
current writes my underlines, erases all the preening.
If I am making sense, it’s sense that moves and mends,
if I am making self, it’s self that curves and bends.

Call is a lantern held under a coat,
answer is a harbor inside a note.

over here, where I am, where the salt remakes my plan,
over there, where you stand with an open olive hand.
land, I see land, sifting, shifting, soft commands.
marco, louder, let it travel, let it span
polo, closer, like a rope, like a hand
over fear, over foam, over narrow bands
land, I see land, the smallest strand.

Salt speaks simple: in, out, in, out.
Body repeats it: doubt, doubt, doubt, doubt.
I let the drum be lung and rung,
I let the tongue be tide and sung.
If I am less of man and more of motion,
let that be hymn enough for ocean.

I keep a list of islands in the hollow of my wrist,
names like pebbles: trust, adjust, be patient, risk.
Olive, anchor, arrow, all the A’s that aim,
aim me through the narrows where the water says my name.
Over here I’m current-shaped, over there you’re shore,
somewhere in the middle we are something more.
Call again, not because you know the way,
call again because the calling lets me stay.
If I return to flesh, it’s with the tide’s design,
a sine of salt across the fault that once was mine.

over here, where I am, tide inside my hand,
over there, where you stand, harbor in your span.
land, I see land, and I will land when I can.
marco, softer, I am nearer than I planned
polo, answered, like a footprint in the sand
over here becomes there, when we understand
land, I see land, we stand.
