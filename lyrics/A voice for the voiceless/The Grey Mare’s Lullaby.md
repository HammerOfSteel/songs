# The Grey Mare’s Lullaby

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-03-18
**Edited:** 2025-03-18

---

"There are many who whisper of the Ceffyl Dŵr, the phantom mare of the waters. Some say she is a trickster, luring the lost into the depths. Others say she is a guide, a spirit that carries those in need across rivers unseen. But the old ones knew the truth—that to those who carried great longing in their hearts, the Ceffyl Dŵr would offer a path beyond the waking world. And so, on a night of sorrow, beneath the silvered sky, a minister walked alone to the water’s edge, clutching a letter worn by time. He had lost too much, and prayed for a passage no man could grant him…"

Footsteps fall on shifting sand,
A prayer held tight in trembling hands.
The war has passed, but time stands still,
For love lies far beyond the hill.

A silver shape, a ghostly gleam,
Rises from the flowing stream.
A mare of mist, with eyes so wide,
She bows her head and speaks inside:

"Pa ofn sydd yn dy galon di?" (What fear lies within your heart?)

Oh, Grey Mare, guide me home,
Through restless waves, through bracken stone.
Let me ride where no road lies,
Through hollow hills and starlit skies.

Oh, Grey Mare, take me far,
Where love still lingers, past the stars.
The path I seek, I cannot find,
Unless you lend your wings of tide.

She takes the weight, she lifts him high,
The tide below, the endless sky.
But not across the sea they go,
Through silent hills where cold winds blow.

A valley lost to name or time,
Where echoes fade but do not die.
The minister, with fearful breath,
Sees fields beyond the reach of death.

"Nid môr ond ffordd yw'r hyn sy'n dod," (Not sea but road is what now comes,)
"Dy gariad yw’r llais sy’n arwain dŷ." (Your love’s the voice that carries you.)

Oh, Grey Mare, what is this land,
Where shadows move like shifting sand?
These hills, these stones, they know my name,
Yet nothing here remains the same.

Oh, Grey Mare, what path is this?
I came for love, not dreams and mist.
If this is fate, if this is true,
Then let me see what I once knew.

Through winding streams of silver light,
Through forests bathed in endless night,
The minister clings, his fingers cold,
As memories rise like ghosts of old.

A child once laughed beneath these trees,
A woman sang upon this breeze.
The world he knew was lost to war,
But here it stood, untouched once more.

"Beth yw colled ond atgof byw?" (What is loss but memory alive?)
"Mae pob enaid yn dal i fyw." (Every soul still breathes and thrives.)

The night is long, the river deep,
But love is more than what we keep.
The road we walk is never gone,
It only changes, carries on.

Oh, Grey Mare, I see it now,
The way beyond, the endless boughs.
Not lost, not gone, just turned away,
Like waves returning home one day.

Oh, Grey Mare, you've shown me clear,
That love is not a thing to fear.
For where she waits, the dawn still glows,
And love will find the way it knows.

At last they reach the other shore,
A door once closed, now locked no more.
And in the hush, the minister sees,
His love who stands among the trees.

She does not age, she does not fade,
She holds the vow their hearts once made.
And as he weeps, she smiles and sighs,
"You found the road. You heard the tide."

"Ni all yr angof ddal y rhai," (Oblivion cannot hold the lost,)
"Mae cariad yn lwybr heb derfyn na thragwyddol fodd." (For love’s a path without an end, forever bound.)

Oh, Grey Mare, my journey’s done,
The morning breaks, the night is gone.
Through hill and tide, through death and dream,
You've carried love, still strong, unseen.

Oh, Grey Mare, let me go free,
For now I know she waits for me.
And when the waves call out my name,
I’ll ride once more, but not the same.

"And so, the minister understood—not all roads are walked with feet upon the earth. Some are carried by wind, by tide, by memory. And love, the truest guide of all, will always find its way home."
