Let's get more ideas about a modernized version of this

Original:

"Mors lilla Olle
Mors lilla Olle i skogen gick,
rosor på kind och solsken i blick.
Läpparna små utav bär äro blå.
– Bara jag slapp att så ensam här gå.

Brummelibrum, vem lufsar där?
Buskarna knaka’, en hund visst det är.
Lurvig är pälsen, men Olle blir glad.
– Å, en kamrat, det var bra, se god dag!

Klappar så björnen med händer små,
räcker fram korgen, – Se där, smaka på!
Nalle han slukar mest allt vad där är.
– Hör du, jag tror att du tycker om bär.

Mor fick nu se dem, gav till ett skri.
Björnen sprang bort, nu är leken förbi.
– Å, varför skrämde du undan min vän?
Mor lilla, be honom komma igen."

I'm thinking again a UK style rap, sweet and upbeat, same acoustic instruments as original song in the beat, maybe some silver flute beat like thing too.

We keep the original lyrics as choruses while the longer verses has a narrative that much more is about mothers silence, families silence. Giving all her heart to another grandchild but not the first or second. The way my mother gave her heart and all to my nieces but not Tima nor Rasmus (but we don't use names in the song).

I want it to reflect on the struggle to love her, to help her in the fight with her demons yet never even as much as speaking Timas name.

Anger that ferments into rage, rage that crystalizes into hate, hate that subsides into the very genes when you let go. This circle that repeats over years and becomes a real part of your DNA. How this cannot be understood or even seen outside only lived.
Maybe with subtle poetic woven alchemical tones and prose but not obviously so more obscure.

How the life, but a dream maybe chosen by the soul to live doesn't ring with the clear voice of love always, some truths doesn't bring peace only, they sip into a void farther and further then can be known, a dream of a singularity seeping into infinity filling the cracks of the tree of life.

How words and intent like spells hold a sway over fate, how the threads spun weave together into strands of a tapestry binding everything to it.

At last how the egg is everything, everyone. An all encompassing everything, the paradox.
