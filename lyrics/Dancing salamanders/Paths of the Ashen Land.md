# Paths of the Ashen Land

**Labels:** Dancing salamanders
**Created:** 2024-08-18
**Edited:** 2024-08-18

---

Verse 1
In the shadow of the Spire, where the winds lament,
I walked with the nomads, where the earth is spent.
They whispered of trials, in voices low and wise,
Of fire, earth, air, and water—where the spirit lies.

"The obstacle is the path," said the Sage, with eyes of grey,
"The flame does not consume, but lights the way."
In the ashes of the past, where the roots run deep,
I began to learn the secrets that the desert keeps.

Chorus
In the crucible of earth, where mountains bow low,
I found the strength to rise, where the sages go.
In the breath of the air, where the storm clouds churn,
I found the clarity to see, what the heart must learn.

Verse 2
The fire burns, but it also forges,
In the heat of the trial, the soul emerges.
The Sage showed me the way through the flames,
"You must lose yourself to know your name."

In the stillness of water, where reflection lies,
I saw the truth in the tearful skies.
"All rivers flow to the same sea," he said,
"But the journey shapes the spirit, where fears are shed."

Bridge
Under the weight of the world, I felt my knees bend,
But the earth whispered softly, "This is not the end."
"You are more than the stone that burdens you,"
"You are the wind, the fire, the waters too."

Chorus
In the crucible of earth, where mountains bow low,
I found the strength to rise, where the sages go.
In the breath of the air, where the storm clouds churn,
I found the clarity to see, what the heart must learn.

Outro
With the nomads, I wandered, through the barren lands,
Learning to wield the elements with steady hands.
The Spire loomed distant, yet nearer each day,
For now, I carried its wisdom, guiding my way.
