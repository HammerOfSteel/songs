# The Lost Property Office
**Album:** Okay
**Track:** 5
**Created:** 2026-03-17

---

## The Premise

A lost property counter. The kind at a train station or a bus depot — a small window, a ledger, shelves behind stacked with umbrellas and phones and coats and bags. Someone has arrived to report something lost.

The clerk is not a system. They are a person — kind, patient, doing their job. Their job is to find things. They are very good at their job. Their job cannot help here.

The questions they ask are the right questions for everything except this. Size. Colour. Weight. Where did you last have it. Each question is innocent and each question is impossible. The Person answers as honestly as they can. The honest answers don't fit the ledger.

This is the sister piece to Form 7B — but while that song was sardonic and cold and the comedy had teeth, this one is warmer and sadder. The absurdism here is the kind that makes you want to put your hand on someone's arm. The Clerk is not a system. The Clerk just doesn't know. Neither does anyone.

---

## Style
- Gorillaz slow register — "On Melancholy Hill," "Empire Ants" territory; warm, slightly floating, melancholic rather than cold
- Single voice (the Person) — British, male, close, quiet; not ragged, not anxious — patient and tired
- The Clerk's lines are sung softly, not processed; warm, close, genuinely trying
- Theremin: the emotional register that can't be spoken — enters when the Person pauses, when the answer is too large for the question; wavering, slightly uncanny, never overwhelming
- Ballad structure rather than rap cadence — slower, more melodic than Form 7B; the questions are the recurring structural anchor rather than a drum machine groove

## Instrumentation
- Acoustic guitar: fingerpicked, warm, close-mic'd — establishes before the voice arrives
- Theremin: enters on the first long pause; reappears whenever the Person's answer runs out; the instrument of the unspeakable
- Upright bass: very low, felt more than heard, walks gently underneath
- Light brushed percussion: enters mid-song, barely there — not a beat, just a pulse
- The Clerk's voice could have a very faint room reverb — just enough warmth to suggest a small office
- Outro: acoustic guitar and theremin only; no voice; the theremin holds a long note and fades

## Production Notes
- The song should feel like a quiet room — no reverb excess, no swell
- The pauses are as important as the words; the theremin fills the pauses so the silence isn't empty
- The Person never breaks down — they are very patient, very careful, very tired
- The Clerk is genuinely trying and that is the most heartbreaking thing about them
- Do not rush. The tempo is the tempo of trying to find the right word and not finding it.

## Full Lyrics

---

[Intro]
[acoustic guitar, fingerpicked — four bars alone; theremin enters on the last bar underneath, one long soft note; then fades as the voice begins]

---

[Verse 1]
[Person — quiet, close, slightly careful; explaining as clearly as possible]

I'd like to report something lost, if that's alright —
I know you're probably busy, I won't take up the night,
It's been a while since I lost it — a few years now I think —
I wasn't sure who to come to, I wasn't sure what to think.

[Clerk — soft, warm, sung gently; leaning forward slightly; genuinely helpful]

Of course, no problem, take your time — have you been here before?
Can I take your name and then we'll see what we can do?
Most things come back to us eventually, one way or another —
Let's start with the basics and we'll see what we can find for you.

---

[The Questions — Round 1]
[Clerk, sung; the recurring structural anchor — asked simply, warmly, without any idea of what they're asking]

Can you tell me what it looked like?
Roughly — size, and shape, and weight?
Was it something you were carrying,
Or did you set it down and wait?
What colour would you say it was —
If you had to say a shade?
And where exactly do you think
The last time that you had it — stayed?

---

[Verse 2]
[Person — answering as carefully and honestly as possible; the answers keep not fitting]

It was — small. But not small in the — usual —
Small like the whole thing is small, the whole — the residual —
Shape is hard — it didn't have a shape, it had a — presence,
Like warmth, you know? The way a room has warmth — not the essence,

Not the temperature exactly, more the — I know this sounds —
I'm trying to be accurate, I'm trying to use the right sounds,
Colour — it was every colour I can name and fewer,
It was the colour of a name said out loud in a room —

[Theremin rises gently under the last two lines — wavering, searching]

I know that doesn't help you. I know that doesn't fit.
I've been trying to describe it for a while now, I admit.

---

[The Questions — Round 2]
[Clerk — still warm, slightly more careful now; something in the Person's answers has registered as unusual, though they can't name it yet]

Was it yours originally,
Or something that you'd borrowed?
Had it been with you a long time —
Something recent, or from before?
Is there anyone who knew it —
Someone else who'd recognise —
Or is this something only
visible to your eyes?

[Theremin holds underneath the last two lines]

---

[Verse 3]
[Person — quieter now; the precision becomes tender]

Mine originally — well, mine and — no, mine,
Mine in the way that some things are yours not by design,
By the fact of them arriving and the fact of holding on —
Yes, a long time. Since the beginning. Since before the gone.

Someone else who knew it — yes. A few. They knew it better —
Some of them avoid the subject like it's — weather.
Some of them say her — say the — some of them still say —

[Person stops. Theremin rises into the pause, longer now, searching the space]

Sorry. I — yes. Someone else would recognise. Yes.

---

[The Questions — Round 3]
[Clerk — very gently now; they understand something has shifted without knowing what; their questions are the same but softer]

Where did you last have it —
Can you tell me what you mean?
The date, or place, or moment —
Anything in between?
And is there any chance
that someone else has picked it up —
That someone's holding onto it
and just — not given up?

---

[Bridge]
[Person — close, very quiet; the theremin underneath; this is the one moment of full exposure before the composure returns]

Where I last had it.

In a room. Small room. With lights on —
The kind of lights you string up round a window.
I had it in my arms. It was sleeping.
And then it wasn't. And then the room was just a room.

Where I last had it was a Tuesday.
Not a special Tuesday. Just a Tuesday.
And the answer to the question of where —
is that I have not had it since. I've been looking. It's not there.

[Theremin holds one long, wavering note — then the acoustic guitar gently returns]

---

[Verse 4]
[Person — composed again; quiet dignity; the conversation finishing]

I know this isn't really — what you're here for,
I know the ledger isn't built for what I'm looking for,
I've tried the forms already — I've done the other floors —
I ended up here because I'd tried the other doors.

I don't need you to find it — I know you can't find it,
I just needed somewhere to — I needed to remind it —
Remind something — someone — that it's real, that it was there,
That the thing that I've lost isn't nothing — that it was the air —

[Clerk — very soft now; completely inadequate and completely kind]

I'm so sorry. I can see this isn't —
I don't have a form that covers —
Let me give you a reference number.
Just so it's — logged. Somewhere.

---

[Outro Verse]
[Person — almost spoken; taking the reference number; folding it]

I take the small piece of paper.
Six digits and a dash.
Nothing is going to come back to me —
we both know that.

But the number exists now.
Something in the ledger.
A reference under "Other."
Pending. Open. Somewhere.

I say thank you. They say anytime.
The door opens. The door closes.

---

[Outro]
[acoustic guitar and theremin only — no voice; the theremin holds one searching note, wavering, warm, not resolving; the guitar slows; the theremin holds; fades]
