Seven zero four.
Light’s hitting the plaster.
Dust motes dancing faster than I can think.
The body is a heavy thing today.
Solid lead.
Gravity’s got a personal vendetta.

I’m analyzing the ceiling, mapping out the cracks in the paintwork,
Trying to find the logic in the way that the brain works.
Synapses firing blanks, the engine is flooded with petrol,
I’m trying to grasp the intangible, make the ephemeral metal.
See, the movement is theory, the stillness is law,
I’m pinned by the weight of the silence right down to the core.
It’s not sadness, precisely, it’s physics, it’s mass,
It’s the feeling of watching the world through a layer of glass.
My legs are distinct from my will, they don’t speak the same language,
I’m trying to pivot the frame but I can’t find the leverage.
A simple rotation, a swing of the feet to the floor,
Becomes a crusade, a quiet internalised war.

They talk about strides.
They talk about heads held high.
I’m talking about knees.
Elbows.
Friction burns on the carpet.

So I drop to the deck, 'cause the altitude’s making me dizzy,
The world is rushing outside and everyone’s frantically busy.
I’m down in the grain of the wood, I’m learning the layout of dust,
Moving on instinct alone, a biological trust.
One hand forward. Drag. The shoulder complains at the motion,
I’m crossing a rug like I’m navigating an ocean.
It’s ungraceful. It’s ugly. It’s brutally real,
Stripping the ego away to see what we actually feel.
I’m not looking up at the finish, I’m looking at inches,
Fighting the muscle that spasms, the stomach that flinches.
This isn’t glory. It’s survival. It’s basic mechanics,
Keeping the breath in the chest to stave off the panic.

Rhaid cropian.
Rhaid cropian.

Don't worry about walking.
Just.
Move.

And maybe that’s it.
Maybe the upright position is overrated for now,
I’m wiping the sweat/salt water off of my brow.
I made it to the doorway.
That’s ten feet of distance.
A heavy, monumental, specific resistance.
I’m bruised on the shins.
But the location has changed.
The furniture looks different.
The view is rearranged.

Yeah.
Crawl before you walk.
It’s not a proverb,
It’s an instruction manual.
