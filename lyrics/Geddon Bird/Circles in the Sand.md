# Circles in the Sand

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-27
**Edited:** 2024-10-27

---

(Verse 1)
We sit by the shore as the tide rolls away,
Raj draws a circle, then lets it decay.
"The patterns we follow, they rise and fall—
We trace them again, without knowing at all."

I watch the waves wipe the circle out clean,
And wonder what patterns lie unseen.
How much of myself comes from the past,
How much is mine, and how long will it last?

(Chorus)
Circles in the sand, washed by the sea,
Tracing old lines in the lives we can’t see.
Round and round, the stories unwind,
The past never leaves; it lingers behind.

(Verse 2)
Raj says, "Family’s a thread that won’t break,
The choices we make or don’t dare to take.
Each line that’s drawn leaves a shadow below,
A story unfinished, still learning to grow."

I think of the paths I’ve tried to repair,
Of hands I’ve let go, of burdens we share.
Are these my circles, or ones passed along?
And how do I break from what feels so strong?

(Chorus)
Circles in the sand, washed by the sea,
Tracing old lines in the lives we can’t see.
Round and round, the stories unwind,
The past never leaves; it lingers behind.

(Bridge)
The tide pulls away, but it always returns,
Lessons unlearned in the patterns that burn.
Each wave is a moment, each line is a thread,
Tied to the voices of those long dead.

(Chorus)
Circles in the sand, washed by the sea,
Tracing old lines in the lives we can’t see.
Round and round, the stories unwind,
The past never leaves; it lingers behind.

(Outro)
We draw one more circle, then let it go,
Raj says, "We change, though the tides still flow."
And as the water sweeps over the land,
We rise from the echoes of circles in sand.
