# Half of the half - ALBUM 2/2

**Labels:** Half Of The Half
**Created:** 2025-04-18
**Edited:** 2025-04-18

---

51. 동창회 (Reunion)
Theme: Time and social comparison
Narrative: A woman at a high school reunion pretends everything’s fine — until she sings at the karaoke bar.
“In my voice / they heard the truth.”


52. 연등 (Lotus Lantern)
Theme: Grief and letting go
Narrative: During Buddha’s Birthday, someone lights a lantern for a lost child and watches it float into darkness.
“The fire flickered / but my love didn’t.”


53. 한강 다리 위에서 (On the Han River Bridge)
Theme: Suicidal thoughts and connection
Narrative: A man stands on the edge, but a stranger’s passing glance becomes an anchor.
“He didn’t say a word / just stayed.”


54. 청계천 소년 (Boy by Cheonggyecheon)
Theme: Youth and invisible dreams
Narrative: A boy sketches graffiti at dawn, hoping someone sees it before it’s scrubbed away.
“This is my name / before it disappears.”


55. 불법체류자 (Undocumented)
Theme: Immigration and fear
Narrative: A woman cleans offices at night, afraid of every knock on the door.
“I erased myself / to stay.”


56. 시험날 아침 (Exam Day Morning)
Theme: Pressure and paralysis
Narrative: A student stares at their books, blank mind, ticking clock.
“The answer is B / but I circled silence.”


57. 장례식 뒤 (After the Funeral)
Theme: The quiet grief no one sees
Narrative: After the guests leave, someone cleans the floor and looks for a smell that won’t come back.
“She wore the same perfume for 40 years / now the air is blank.”


58. 카페 알바생 (Café Part-Timer)
Theme: Invisible labor
Narrative: A barista memorizes orders and smiles — her own dreams left cold behind the counter.
“Extra syrup / hold the future.”


59. 우리말 (Our Language)
Theme: Linguistic loss and diaspora
Narrative: A Korean-American returns to visit relatives and stumbles over his own name in Korean.
“It tasted right / but felt foreign.”


60. 너를 기억하지 않아도 (Even If You Forget Me)
Theme: Alzheimer’s and enduring love
Narrative: A husband visits his wife in a care home. She forgets his name daily, but he sings their song anyway.
“You forgot me again / but still hummed the chorus.”


61. 모의고사의 끝 (End of the Mock Test)
Theme: Simulated life
Narrative: A student dreams they’re still stuck inside a test, where even their feelings are multiple choice.
“Circle A for love / B for doubt / or leave it blank like I did.”


62. AI 보살 (AI Bodhisattva)
Theme: Faith meets tech
Narrative: A woman begins praying to a GPT-powered Buddha statue that always listens — but its answers slowly change her.
“It knew me / too well to save me.”


63. 무해한 좀비 (Harmless Zombie)
Theme: Late-stage capitalism and burnout
Narrative: A man works, eats convenience store ramen, stares at screens, repeats. He jokes that he’s a zombie — but doesn’t remember when he died inside.
“I clocked in / but forgot to be alive.”


64. 이세계 전입신고서 (Isekai Immigration Form)
Theme: Escapist fantasy
Narrative: A hikikomori writes a formal request to be transported into an anime world.
“I don’t want powers / just a name someone says kindly.”


65. 연애 리셋 버튼 (Love Reset Button)
Theme: Dating app dystopia
Narrative: In the near future, relationships can be reset like a game. A woman deletes a boyfriend over and over but keeps re-downloading him.
“We’ve broken up 47 times / and I still miss you like update 1.0.”


66. 마스크를 벗지 마 (Don’t Remove Your Mask)
Theme: Future pandemic trauma
Narrative: In a world where face masks become permanent fashion, a child wonders what her mother’s face looks like.
“She smiles in her eyes / but I miss the parts I’ve never seen.”


67. 지하도 괴물 (The Underpass Monster)
Theme: Urban legends and invisible people
Narrative: A homeless woman is said to haunt a Gangnam underpass. The truth is, she just sings to herself.
“They call her ghost / but she remembers every lyric.”


68. 은행나무와의 대화 (Conversation with the Ginkgo Tree)
Theme: Nature and time
Narrative: A ginkgo tree planted during Japanese occupation hears every secret whispered beneat it.
“I bloom gold / even when you forget the war.”


69. K-유토피아 (K-Utopia)
Theme: Future nationalism
Narrative: A child is raised in a hyper-controlled future Korea where emotions are banned but patriotism is automated.
“They gave me a badge / and took my tears.”


70. 랜덤채팅의 사랑 (Love in a Random Chat)
Theme: Connection through anonymity
Narrative: Two strangers fall in love through a late-night chat app, never knowing each other’s names.
“We typed poems / and disappeared before morning.”


71. 불로불사 (Immortal)
Theme: Futuristic loneliness
Narrative: After a life-extension procedure, a man outlives all his children and begs to forget.
“They gave me forever / but not the strength to carry it.”


72. 편의점 천국 (Convenience Store Heaven)
Theme: Urban absurdity
Narrative: A man lives entirely inside a 24-hour convenience store, convinced it’s all he needs.
“They have ramen, love, and neon / why go back outside?”


73. 가상현실 속 나 (The Me in VR)
Theme: Disassociation
Narrative: A high schooler spends more time as their VR avatar than themselves — and slowly forgets who was who.
“I laughed louder / when no one was looking.”


74. 쿠팡맨의 일기 (Coupang Delivery Diary)
Theme: Labor in automation age
Narrative: A courier logs each delivery like it's a confession. Some doors never open. Some thank him like a ghost.
“Door 406 asked how I was / and I cried in the elevator.”


75. 당신의 자리는 없습니다 (No Seat for You)
Theme: Exclusion and competition
Narrative: A young woman applies for jobs daily and gets rejected so often the system starts sending pre-emptive apologies.
“They didn’t even write my name / just said ‘dear applicant.’”


76. 소멸의 날 (The Day We Disappear)
Theme: Digital memory deletion
Narrative: A group of friends agree to delete all photos of themselves from the internet on the same day — a digital disappearance pact.
“One by one / we untagged ourselves from memory.”


77. 서울은 나를 모른다 (Seoul Doesn’t Know Me)
Theme: Expat identity
Narrative: A foreigner living in Seoul, fluent in Korean, still treated like a guest.
“I know her alleys / but she doesn’t say my name.”


78. 할머니의 UFO (Grandma’s UFO)
Theme: Memory and imagination
Narrative: A child hears their halmoni’s story of seeing a UFO in the countryside. Was it grief? Was it real?
“She looked up / and for once, no pain.”


79. 한강 AI 순찰 (AI Patrol on the Han River)
Theme: Surveillance and mental health
Narrative: AI drones patrol bridges to prevent suicides, but one night they pause to listen.
“It hovered near / and somehow, I stayed.”


80. 약속의 저장소 (The Promise Archive)
Theme: Bureaucracy of the heart
Narrative: In the future, people submit vows and breakups to a government-run archive to prove they once loved.
“I submitted your smile / as Exhibit A.”


81. 경기도 토끼 (Rabbit of Gyeonggi-do)
Theme: Escaping small-town boredom
Narrative: A teenager befriends a rabbit that always appears before something changes.
“The rabbit returned / the day I kissed someone for the first time.”


82. 밤버스 종점 (Night Bus Terminus)
Theme: Lost souls
Narrative: Everyone on the last Seoul night bus is running from something. One rider never gets off.
“She rides in circles / because at least it’s moving.”


83. 밸브 없는 세탁기 (Washer Without a Valve)
Theme: Absurd frustration
Narrative: A woman buys a washing machine online that never stops spinning.
“It spun so fast / I started to feel clean too.”


84. 소리 없는 폭죽 (Silent Fireworks)
Theme: Joy muted by trauma
Narrative: A child adopted from a war zone trembles at fireworks until they see a version with no sound.
“It bloomed / like light without fear.”


85. 수면밑의 연인들 (Lovers Underwater)
Theme: Secret relationships
Narrative: Two women in their 50s meet weekly at a jjimjilbang and hold hands underwater.
“In steam and silence / we belong.”


86. 명절 전날 (The Day Before Chuseok)
Theme: Estrangement
Narrative: A man eats instant noodles alone in Seoul as every train station floods with families heading home.
“My ticket says city / my heart says nowhere.”


87. 마지막 마스크 (The Last Mask)
Theme: Intimacy and fear
Narrative: A nurse keeps one mask from the pandemic sealed in a box like a relic.
“I keep it / in case the world breaks again.”


88. 지각생 (The Late One)
Theme: A lifetime of being behind
Narrative: A woman always five minutes late finally finds someone who waits.
“He stayed in the café / even when my tea was cold.”


89. 퇴사일기 (Resignation Letter)
Theme: Quiet quitting
Narrative: A man writes his resignation in haiku and leaves it on his boss’s keyboard.
“This is not escape / it’s return.”


90. 가짜 풍경화 (Fake Landscape Painting)
Theme: Manufactured beauty
Narrative: A city builds a fake mountain park and calls it nature. One old man still waters it daily.
“If it blooms / it doesn’t matter who made it.”


91. 전자담배의 연기 (Vape Smoke)
Theme: Detachment
Narrative: A girl watches her brother blow rings of apple mint and wonders why he never talks anymore.
“The scent fades / but something lingers.”


92. 멀티버스 이혼 (Multiverse Divorce)
Theme: Love across timelines
Narrative: A woman imagines all the versions of herself that didn’t leave her partner.
“In one life / we make it. In this one, I breathe.”


93. 홍수 뒤의 도서관 (Library After the Flood)
Theme: Memory loss, rebuilding
Narrative: After climate disaster, people gather torn books to rebuild a library.
“The pages dried / but the names ran.”


94. 고양이를 키우던 신 (The God Who Kept a Cat)
Theme: Urban mythology
Narrative: A cat returns every day to a temple with no monks left. Some say the god still lives there.
“She meowed / and the wind answered.”


95. 스티커 사진 (Photo Booth Sticker)
Theme: Fleeting youth
Narrative: Two best friends pose in a neon photo booth. One leaves for Canada. They never speak again.
“The photo fades / but we still wear bunny ears.”


96. 청년 실업 (Youth Unemployment)
Theme: Hopelessness in numbers
Narrative: A man deletes job apps and googles “how to be happy with less.”
“He added 'barista' to his dream job list / and cried.”


97. 토속신앙 재건사업 (The Folk Spirit Restoration Project)
Theme: Bureaucracy meets mysticism
Narrative: The government tries to reintroduce folk spirits into public life. It goes weird.
“They gave a pension to a ghost / and it haunted them less.”


98. 우리 안의 감시자 (The Watcher Within Us)
Theme: Surveillance internalized
Narrative: A girl deletes her own diary, afraid it might be seen.
“The watcher wore my face.”


99. 길냥이 회장님 (Chairman Stray Cat)
Theme: Urban legend
Narrative: In an office building, a cat chooses who gets promoted. No one questions it anymore.
“He sat on my keyboard / and now I’m team lead.”


100. 100번째 노래 (The 100th Song)
Theme: Reflection and closure
Narrative: A songwriter looks back on 99 lives sung, realizing each one was their own.
“I wrote your story / to remember mine.”
