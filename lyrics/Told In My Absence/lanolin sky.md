# lanolin sky

**Labels:** Told In My Absence
**Created:** 2025-10-25
**Edited:** 2025-10-25

---

What is this life if, full of care,
We have no time to stand and stare.

No time to stand beneath the boughs
And stare as long as sheep or cows.

No time to see, when woods we pass,
Where squirrels hide their nuts in grass.

No time to see, in broad daylight,
Streams full of stars, like skies at night.

Lanolin sky, wool-soft high,
 smell of the rain and the hills go by.
 Hand in mine, we’re fine, no try,
 we just are, little star, that’s why.

Valley like velvet, quilted plain,
 clouds knit cables, purl that rain.
 Cheeky bit o’ wind puts curl in the mane,
 sheep say “baaa,” I say “same.”
 “Dad, look, mud’s got shine,”
 sun peeks out like, “alright, fine.”
 We share one flapjack, halves, not mine,
 rules of the road: us first, time.
 Boot prints rhyme with a brookside beat,
 fiddle does a grin, bass nods feet.
 Storm in the chest went click and delete,
 now it’s tea-leaves calm and a seat.

Cuppa in a flask, hush in the air,
 cost of the view? just turning up there.

Lanolin sky, wool-soft high,
 smell of the rain and the hills go by.
 Hand in mine, we’re fine, no try,
 we just are, little star, that’s why.

Sheep stare, deadpan, real comitment committee,
 judge my hat, still let us be pretty.
“Biscuit, Mittens, Kitty,”
 daft little list, daft little lambs.

 “What’s lanolin?” “Sheep skin balm,
 keeps out weather, rubs in calm.”

 “So love’s like that?”
“Right, stay warm.”
 We oil our hours against the storm.

 Gate latch clack, and the footpath bends,
 hedge says “shhh,” and the silence mends.

 Inner thunder’s out of friends,
 valley’s the letter the weather sends.

 Count four drops on your sleeve, leave.
 Count four breaths we can keep, one more.
 If the mind kicks up old leaves, sweep.
 when the world shouts, jumpin jacks with the sheep, sleep.

Lanolin sky, wool-soft high,
 smell of the rain and the hills go by.
 Hand in mine, we’re fine, no try,
 we just are, little star, that’s why.
 Lanolin sky, low tide sigh,
 brolly on the pack and the clouds roll by.
 Right as rain, plain as pie,
 home in a field with you nearby.

Pebbles in a pocket, charms of slate,
 luck in a puddle we couldn’t hate.
 You skip, I slip, “steady, mate,”
 two fools laughing at the gate.
 All that grief, that grit, that grind,
 gets rinsed like wool, comes out kind.
 We leave what hurt, keep what shined,
 sheep bell time resets the mind.

No time to turn at Beauty's glance,
And watch her feet, how they can dance.

No time to wait till her mouth can
Enrich that smile her eyes began.

A poor life this if, full of care,
We have no time to stand and stare.
