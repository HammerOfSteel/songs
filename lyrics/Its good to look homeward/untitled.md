1. "A Meeting of Myths"
Themes: New beginnings, connection, shared wonder, identity.
Narrative: The song recounts the weekend workshop where Nicole and Tima’s father first meet at age 17. Nicole reflects on the instant bond they form through shared love of culture, art, and myth. Her DID subtly weaves into the song, expressed through her introspective dialogue and the way she navigates the layered aspects of herself while connecting with someone new.
Symbolism: Folklore and myths mirror Nicole’s complex inner world, as she begins to trust and share parts of herself. The workshop becomes a symbolic “sacred space” where their connection begins to grow.
Mood: Dreamlike and intimate, with an undertone of self-discovery.
Key Moments:
Nicole’s fascination with the workshop’s themes and her cautious curiosity toward the boy across the room.
Their first conversation, blending art, stories, and a shared spark.
Nicole’s reflection on how this meeting feels both real and like a story unfolding.

2. "Camden Lanes and London Lore"
Themes: Exploration, connection, art, wonder.
Narrative: Nicole recalls her first walk through Camden with Tima’s father, the two of them marveling at the eclectic culture and vibrant art of the area. Their dialogue meanders from the street art they see to stories and myths of London, blending whimsy and depth as they get to know each other further. Her DID adds a subtle layer to her perspective, shaping how she views the world and engages with him.
Symbolism: The colorful chaos of Camden represents Nicole’s layered identity, a place where contrasts coexist harmoniously. The streets and stories become a canvas for their growing connection.
Mood: Playful and vivid, with a sense of exploration and budding love.
Key Moments:
Discovering a quirky shop or mural that sparks a deeper conversation.
Nicole weaving London’s folklore into their dialogue, sharing pieces of herself.
A quiet moment where Nicole wonders if she can let her walls down completely.

3. "Polaroid"
Themes: Young love, nostalgia, impermanence.
Narrative: A snapshot of Nicole and Tima’s father sharing a rainy afternoon in London, taking impromptu Polaroids under a café awning. The song reflects the fleeting, magical moments of early love and the joy they found in each other.

4. "Through the Garden’s Gate"

Themes: Togetherness, love, fleeting moments, connection.

Narrative: Nicole and Tima’s father spend an evening at a small concert with friends, enjoying music and the warmth of connection. The song weaves Nicole’s quiet moments of joy with her deeper introspection, blending the fleeting magic of the evening with her struggles. The title plays on the lyrics of "Magnolia," adding a poetic layer of connection.

Symbolism: The garden gate represents a transition between the inner world and shared experiences, a place where Nicole feels safe and connected.

Mood: Nostalgic and warm, with a bittersweet undertone.

5. "Fractal"

Themes: Identity, pain, resilience, longing.

Narrative: A hauntingly introspective song from Nicole’s perspective, exploring her inner struggle with DID. She reflects on the fragmented parts of herself, trying to piece them together through the love she feels for her family. The song blends moments of despair with hope, as she finds solace in fleeting connections.

Symbolism: The fractal represents Nicole’s mind—endlessly complex, beautiful in its brokenness, and a reflection of her resilience.

Mood: Poignant and haunting, with an ethereal tone that balances vulnerability and strength.

The narrative unfolds as he watches her paint, steeped in symbolic and alchemical imagery inspired by George Ripley’s work. Their dialogue weaves between reflections on the process of creation, the meaning behind her work, and the deeper connection they share.

17. "Tima’s Song"
Themes: Anticipation, love, connection, creation.
Narrative: Nicole sings from her perspective as she and Tima’s father spend a weekend outside London while she is pregnant with Tima. The song captures their quiet conversations about their unborn daughter—her name, her future, and the love they already feel for her. The DID plays into Nicole’s inner dialogue, shaping how she processes the idea of becoming a mother and the complexities it brings.
Symbolism: Tima’s name becomes a focal point, representing the bridge between Nicole and her daughter, a symbol of hope and legacy. The countryside around them mirrors the sense of creation and nurturing.
Mood: Reflective and tender, with a tone of quiet anticipation.
Key Moments:
Nicole and Tima’s father discussing Tima’s name and its meaning.
A quiet moment where Nicole reflects on her fears and hopes about motherhood.
Watching the sunset together, imagining the world Tima will grow up in.

18. "Yr Ffordd ar Ôl y Glaw" (The Road After Rain)

Themes: Healing, loss, resilience, hope.

Narrative: Years after Nicole’s passing, Tima and her father walk through a familiar path after a rainstorm. The song reflects the quiet beauty of healing, the enduring presence of Nicole’s memory, and the strength they find in moving forward.

Symbolism: The rain represents grief and sorrow, while the road afterward symbolizes clarity, resilience, and the journey of life continuing.

Mood: Reflective and tender, with an uplifting undertone.
