# Garden of Small Miracles — Overview

Concept

Garden of Small Miracles is a collection of intimate vignettes that treat ordinary domestic actions as ritual and revelation. Each song finds meaning in a small everyday object or moment (a bowl, a door, a drip of salt) and treats it as a locus for tenderness, grief, and quiet transformation.

Musical palette

- Core: warm acoustic guitar, mandolin, cello, soft fiddle
- Accent: toy piano / glockenspiel, kalimba/marimba, gentle ambient pads
- Rhythm: body-tap percussion, soft tap-beat on guitar body, occasional lo‑fi hip‑hop pocket for spoken/rap cadence
- Production: close, intimate vocal mic; tape/hiss and subtle field recordings to anchor domestic space

Lyrical & formal rules

- Sentence‑to‑sentence rhyming is the default for verses and choruses.
- Intros/outros: short haiku (3 lines) or micro‑vignettes (2–3 lines) to set/close mood.
- Verses: concrete sensory detail; one object or action per verse.
- Choruses: ritual/benediction refrains — short, repeatable, mantra‑like.
- Bridges/interludes: spoken‑word or half‑rap cadences to inject urgency/irony.
- Keep language accessible and tactile; use occasional alchemical metaphor (softly) as a motif.

Recurring symbols

- Bread / bowl / salt
- Door / gate / threshold
- Steam / breath / window condensation
- Petals / seeds / small stones
- The garden as a private container of small miracles
