# the gold between us

**Labels:** gullveig
**Created:** 2025-11-15
**Edited:** 2025-11-15

---

slow stuff,
fluff of sweet sweat,
settling slow regret.
Spun and twiddle,
on an old oak fiddle.
Burnt and burried,
tressure old,
this,
our gold.

You'll find it marked clear in big cross,
Hidden by the fern and moss.
You'll know it by its soft scent,
spent and bent of a hearts desire,
of you much this shall require.
An errand, you may share,
should you so dare.
take up your boots along with mine,
should you know its all but "fine".
We may, together track this hike,
if you so should like,
likened to a little wish.
May you then also know this life,
A sweet little lantern all to bright.
May you brave, may you be so bold
As to hear this story told.

By the conjunction of particle and prayer,
after all has settled in the dust and air,
when you find all fair,
fair and only in the afthermath,
hath thyne days grown so cold.

Only then,
May you know this gold.

When every bridge is char and coal,
and we stand small in the smoking whole,
you’ll see what ember’s left to hold,
quiet, stubborn, bright and old.
In the hush where harsh words fade,
sits a glimmer we two made.
Not from promise, not from vow,
just from staying here and now.

Let the cinders name our price,
all we’ve lost in sacrifice.
Still beneath the ashen fold,
something soft is setting gold.

By the conjunction of particle and prayer,
 after all has settled in the dust and air,
 when you find all fair,
fair and only in the afthermath,
hath thyne days grown so cold.

Only then,
May you know this gold…

And should your tired hands still shake,
 from every tremor, every quake,
may you feel, not bought or sold,
but simply held
 in this, our gold.

Hear the copper kettle’s low reply,
steel drums hum like distant sky.
Trumpets lean in, soft and slow,
telling things we barely know.

Mandolin with lantern strings,
picks the dust off broken things.
In its chords our echoes meet,
old bruised hearts with careful feet.

May you see, in this dim light,
all we failed and yet did right.
In the cracks where stories told,
love runs small, but runs in gold.

Oh, we were ore in a stranger’s fire,
 pressed by weight of want and wire.
All that smoke, all that noise,
all those half-grown, reckless joys.
Yet in the melt, in the unnamed heat,
where loss and mercy finally meet,
what survived was shy, but true
gold between my soul and you.

So when the slow beat finds its close,
and every last soft trumpet blows,
when the night folds up our names,
and forgives our little flames,
Lay your head where the fiddle’s old,
on the oak where stories told.

Burnt and burried, tressure old,
still we keep, in hands we hold,
not the lies or tales we sold,
only this,
our gold.
