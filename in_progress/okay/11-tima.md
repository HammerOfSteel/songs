# Tima
**Album:** Okay
**Track:** 11 — Final Track
**Created:** 2026-03-18

---

## The Premise

Every line of this song begins with a letter from her name, cycling T → I → M → A → T → I → M → A throughout the entire song — verse, chorus, bridge, outro, everywhere. The constraint never drops.

Every consecutive pair of lines rhymes: T rhymes with I, M rhymes with A, then T rhymes with I, M rhymes with A.

The name does not appear until the peak. When it arrives, it arrives on a T-line — because the name begins with T — and the T-line IS the name. One word. Said at full voice. Then the music holds, does not resolve, and fades.

The voice is second person — speaking to her directly — but uses only pet names throughout: little bird, little star, little daffodil, little sunshine, little light. The pet names are the whole of the love. The name is held back for the one moment that earns it.

The tone in the verses is the tone of uncles: warm, fond, slightly silly, completely specific. These are real details — the slug, the shoes, the jam, the mango. The song earns its ending through specificity, not through grand statement. You don't say someone's name because you have found the right words. You say it because it is their name and they had a slug conversation in May and that was real.

---

## Lyrical Constraint — stated plainly

Every line starts with T, I, M, or A in strict rotation:

```
T [rhymes with I]
I [rhymes with T]
M [rhymes with A]
A [rhymes with M]
T [rhymes with I]
I [rhymes with T]
...and so on, throughout the entire song, no exceptions
```

---

## Style
- Gorillaz / Rizzle Kicks — upbeat, UK rap cadence, warm and forward-moving; the feel of a group of people who loved someone completely singing her a song
- UK British male, close-mic'd — starts near-spoken, delivery loosens and lifts as the track builds; by the final chorus it is fully sung, at full voice
- The upbeat energy is not ironic; it is accurate — this is how you talk to a three-year-old who refuses to wear shoes; this is what loving someone exactly this size sounds like

## Instrumentation — Build Arc
- **Intro:** Single acoustic guitar — one repeating two-bar phrase, warm, slightly uptempo; alone
- **Verse 1:** Guitar only; vocal near-spoken
- **Verse 2:** Bass enters underneath; still close, still intimate
- **Chorus 1:** Drum kit enters — tight, live-feeling; vocal begins to be sung properly
- **Verse 3:** Electric guitar texture added; rhythm section now full
- **Chorus 2:** Synth pad enters (warm, Gorillaz-style); keys; the sound is now thick and full
- **Bridge:** Everything drops to acoustic guitar and bass only — most exposed
- **Build to Final Chorus:** One instrument at a time re-enters — guitar, then drums, then keys, then synth pad — building bar by bar
- **Final Chorus:** Everything in — full band, full energy; the song at its largest
- **T: Tima.** — beat drops; single held note; all instruments sustain on that one chord
- **Outro:** Music holds the note, does not resolve, fades slowly to silence

## Production Notes
- The upbeat energy of the Gorillaz/Rizzle Kicks feel should be completely genuine — do not score it sad; the sadness will arrive on its own
- Each instrument's entry should feel like another person joining a song already being sung — a chorus of love, building
- The bridge drop should feel like a room suddenly gone quiet — not dramatic, just: bare
- At "Tima.": the beat does not crash or swell; it simply stops, leaving one sustained chord in the air
- The final chord is not a minor chord — it is not trying to feel sad. It simply holds. It does not resolve. It fades.
- Outro lines are near-spoken under the fading sustain — barely audible, not mixed for clarity; felt more than heard
- Silence at the end is the last note of the album

## [Verse 1]

[near-spoken — single guitar underneath — the tone of telling a story to someone who already knows it]

There was this little bird who'd never once slow down,
Into every garden, every puddle, every town.
Mornings were her territory — first one through the door,
Arms out in the cold because the cold was what she woke up for.

Told the slug good morning — proper formal, very true —
In a voice for serious business: what a thing to do.
Made a house for beetles underneath the garden gate.
All the living world was hers, and she could hardly wait.

## [Verse 2]

[bass enters underneath — still intimate, slightly fuller]

There was this little star who had opinions about toast —
In charge of jam and butter, practically a host.
Made the rules up as she went and called them very fair,
And if you disagreed she simply wouldn't be there.

Till you came around. She knew you would. She had all the time.
I want to say she'd laugh at that — yeah, something of that kind.
Mango was her word for yellow — still don't know the why —
And I would get it wrong on purpose just to hear the sigh.

## [Chorus 1]

[drums enter — sung now, properly — UK rap cadence lifts in]

This one's yours, my little daffodil, lean in —
I've been saving up the words, and now I can begin.
Made of all the mornings that were ordinary and true —
And every single part of it goes straight to you.

This one's been a long time building, little light,
I have been inside the building of it, getting it right.
More than just a song — it comes from all the parts that stay —
And I will say your name before the end of today.

## [Verse 3]

[electric guitar texture added — rhythm section full now]

There was this little sunshine who refused to wear a sock —
Issued a decree and that decree had no unlock.
Made her case by looking at me — she had this look, you know —
And I would cave immediately and call it letting go.

That the sock was optional if grass was underfoot —
I took the minutes of this meeting. I was no good.
More defeats than I could count — I wasn't keeping score.
And I would take them all again. And then I'd take some more.

The mango word, the slug, the shoe, the toast, the gate —
I've got the whole inventory, mate, I've got the whole estate.
Matter of fact is what it is: I've got the lot in here.
And it's enough. It's more than enough. It's absolutely clear.

## [Chorus 2]

[synth pad and keys enter — warm, Gorillaz-style — sound now thick and full]

This one's yours, my little daffodil, still yours —
I know you're in the listening, wherever listening pours.
Made it while the world kept going and the seasons changed,
And every note of it has always had your name arranged.

The whole record has been circling back to this right here —
I've been building to this moment for a long, long year.
Maybe you already know it, maybe you can tell —
And there is one more thing to say, and then we all know well.

## [Bridge]

[everything drops to acoustic guitar and bass only — bare — most exposed]

[near-spoken again, plain — the most direct the song gets]

The truth of it is that the love has stayed the same size,
I look for you in ordinary light, in other eyes.
More years is not more distance — that's the strange thing in grief —
And I have found no better word for it than this one: relief.

*(not relief as in it's over — relief as in: at last I'm saying it)*

The love keeps going where it always went before:
Into your hand, into the morning, into every door.
My little light. My little star. My little bird. My own.

## [Build to Final Chorus]

[instruments enter one at a time — drums first, then electric guitar, then keys, then synth — bar by bar — building back to full]

I know I've taken every long road just to find the bend
My way around and back to where I knew it had to land —
And I am going to say it now, and I need you to understand:

My little sunshine. My little daffodil. My little bird. My light.
