# Don't go

**Labels:** ling-di-long
**Created:** 2025-04-15
**Edited:** 2025-10-05

---

[intro]
[Male voice british accent]
“Want jam or honey?”
You stare at your toes,
Mumble 

[Childs voice, british accent]
[Mumble]
“jam,”

[Male voice british accent]
like it holds the world together.

The kettle hums its sleepy tune,
Your hair’s a storm, your socks are gone,
But we’ve got our rhythm.

[Verse 1]
You colour with crumbs on the table,
I sip tea I don’t remember making.
I say, “Maybe we’ll go to the canal path later.”
You say, 

[Childs voice, british accent]
“Only if the ducks are awake.”


[Male voice british accent]
The sky’s a soft grey, but not unfriendly.
You build towers out of cereal and breath.
We play the same three songs on the speaker,
You hum off-beat — still better than the rest.

I wipe jam from your chin,
You tell me the toast looked like a bear.
You run to grab your red crayon,
I sit in the quiet of your chair.

[Chorus]
You build a rocket, name it "Nowhere",
Ask if stars make wishes too.
I say “maybe,” you nod like a queen,
Then yawn, and the room slows too.

Don’t go, not yet,
The sky’s still soft, your hands still set
In sleepy folds and morning light —
Don’t go, little star, not tonight.

[Verse 2]
You sleep early, curled like a shell
On your side of the bed,
The bear tucked under your chin,
My hoodie as your blanket instead.

I don’t move.
I sit by the door and just breathe.
Watch the rise and fall of your breath
Like it’s writing something I’ll never read.

[Bridge]
I hum an old tune,
Your foot kicks once.
The house doesn’t blink,
It just holds the hush.

And the light through the curtain
Feels too still.
The wind forgets how to move.
And I —
I forget what I was just thinking.

[Chorus]
Don’t go, please,
The air’s too sharp, the clock won’t tease.
The moon’s not here to hold your hand —
Don’t go. I don't yet understand.

[Verse 3]
I wake first, never ever ever never
Still in the chair, neck bent wrong.
The sun’s crept in without asking,
And the silence feels too long.

I stand up slow.
The bear’s still there.
Your fingers still curled
In yesterday’s prayer.

But you don’t move.
Not one blink.
I whisper your name.
The whole room sinks.

I sit again.
I don’t cry yet.
I trace your cheek
And whisper “rest.”

[Chorus]
Don’t go, I know you must,
But leave a laugh inside the dust.
I’ll hold your dreams, your shoes, your light —
I’ll say your name to the stars each night.

[Interlude]
I make toast anyway.
I sing your song.
I fold your socks.
I carry on.

[Verse 4]
I tell your name to trees and walls.
I keep your drawings. I play with dolls.
I talk to the bear. I set the plate.
I still walk the canal. I still run late.

I hear you in puddles. In birds. In steam.
In the way I still say “night, my queen.”
You're everywhere, and still so gone.
And I keep going.
Because you were mine.
And I’m still yours.

[Outro]
I keep the cwtsh inside your coat,
The laugh you left inside my throat.
I tell the wind what you once knew —
That love like yours is always true.

“I still set two cups sometimes.
Just in case.”

[Musical outro]
