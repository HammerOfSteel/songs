# The Little Moon’s Lullaby – Huna’r Lleuad Fach

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-02-17
**Edited:** 2025-03-05

---

"The little one sat by the window, eyes heavy with questions too big for their heart. The world outside was vast, the sky stretched far and wide, and the night felt endless. But above, a small silver moon hung in the darkness, watching, waiting. And as the wind whispered through the trees, the little moon began to sing..."

Silver glow on velvet sky,
Soft as whispers floating by.
A lonely child, a moon so small,
Yet light still reaches, after all.

Little one, why do you weep?
Why do shadows steal your sleep?
Though the night may feel so long,
Even darkness hums a song.

Huna, huna, lleuad fach, (Sleep, sleep, little moon,)
Daw y bore, daw y wawr. (Morning comes, the dawn is near.)
Though the sky is vast and wide,
Your light still shines, you need not hide.

The stars above, they watch you grow,
Brighter still than you may know.
Though you feel too small to shine,
The night was made for light like thine.

Huna, huna, lleuad fach,
Daw y bore, daw y wawr.
No shadow stays, no night is long,
Your heart still holds its quiet song.

Even when the clouds appear,
Even when the sky seems clear,
You are not lost, you are not gone,
Your glow will find its way at dawn.

The tides still rise, the winds still call,
The moon is small, yet sees it all.
And though you bend, and though you sway,
The light within won’t fade away.

Huna, huna, lleuad fach,
Hedfan hiraeth drwy’r awyr. (Drifting longing through the air.)
No night so dark, no wind so strong,
That you won’t find where you belong.

So rest, my love, and close your eyes,
The moon is here, the dawn will rise.
And though the world may turn so fast,
Your light will always, always last.

Little one, close your eyes. The moon is watching, the stars are listening. And your light… your light will always shine.
