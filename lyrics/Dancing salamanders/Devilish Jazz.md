# Devilish Jazz

**Labels:** Dancing salamanders
**Created:** 2024-08-11
**Edited:** 2024-08-11

---

Late at night on cobblestone, with a sax in hand,
Grandpa’s tunes in my soul, across this troubled land,
Playing notes in smoky bars, where shadows start to dance,
Struggling in the spotlight’s glow, hoping for a chance.

Devil’s bridge, devil’s tune, a deal that’s oh so sweet,
But I saw the tail beneath his coat, he couldn’t hide his feet,
No deal tonight, you sly old fox, I won’t be swayed by you,
I’ll play my jazz, on my own, and make my dreams come true.

Ponte del diavolo, musica che risuona,
Ma il tuo trucco l’ho visto, non mi inganni questa volta,
Niente accordo, caro mio, suonerò per la mia strada,
La mia musica è la mia anima, e il diavolo non l’avrà mai.

He tried to lure me with gold and fame, a penthouse in the sky,
But every time he made his move, I saw the devil’s lie,
He cursed and stomped, his plans a mess, but fate was on my side,
For every trick he tried to play, my star began to rise.

The lights went up, the crowds poured in, my name upon the marquee,
A theater full, a dream fulfilled, and who’s there in the sea?
The devil himself, in the back row seat, a ticket in his hand,
He paid in full, that’s right, no deal, as the music took command.

As the final note hung in the air, I saw his begrudging grin,
He tipped his hat, then disappeared, knowing he couldn’t win,
I’ll keep on playing, my soul intact, with Grandpa’s jazz in me,
The devil tried, but in the end, it’s my music that runs free.

Suonerò per sempre, con l’anima intatta,
Il diavolo ha perso, e la mia musica è salva.

The story of the young jazz musician's encounter with the devil, highlighting her cleverness and determination to succeed on her own terms.

Music Style:
Swinging jazz with sultry sax, upright bass, and playful piano, infused with bluesy guitar riffs and a hint of mischief.
