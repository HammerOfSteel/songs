# white ink

**Labels:** Fylgja
**Created:** 2025-10-17
**Edited:** 2025-10-17

---

“Talk to me about the djinn, sleepy poet,” said mama.

“Oh aye, the djinn.”
“My star, what do you want to know?"
Papa replied.

“Well, poet, tell me a poem.”
Mama chuckled.

He pushed his sleeves.
He breathed.
He began.

"Haunted, they say, believing
the soft, shifty
dunes are made up
of false promises.

Many believe
whatever happens
is the other half
of a conversation.

Many whisper
white lies
to the dead...

The girls are doing really well...

Some think
nothing is so
until it has been witnessed.

They believe
the bits are iffy;

the forces that bind them,
absolute."

White ink, night blink, lines that don’t show, 
write slow, right? still the letters all glow.
Soft hush, no rush, page like a dune,
words slip, moon-lipped, hum like a rune.
Ghost post, closed coast, posted to you, 
no stamp, no lamp, just the breath I blew.
Dead calm, head balm, nicked by a sigh,
cheap pen, deep end, truths that won’t lie.
Proper daft, innit?, scribblin’ air,
but the script comes crisp when I stop and stare.

White ink, no spill, still stains me, mate;
quiet quill, no frill, yet it writes my fate.
Unwrit ends, unbent bends, turn back round;
I don’t cross lines, I become the sound.
White ink, no eyes can spy this skein,
but it lifts my chest like a late-night train.

Djinn grin, thin skin, wind in the glass,
faint script, paint drip, moves as you pass.
Say less, stress less, let it all land,
bare page, backstage, ink in the hand.
Low key, no key, lock with a laugh,
no gate, no state, cut through the faff.
You gone, me on, talkin’ to space,
no face, no trace, still a proper place.
If “witness makes real,” then watch me breathe, 
these bars are scars that refuse to leave.

White ink, blind light, right on cue;
quiet vows, out loud, only ring true.
Unwrit ends, mended trends, fold not break;
I don’t chase dawn, I allow its wake.
White ink, soft blink, page turns clean;
I keep what’s lost in the gap between.

Cheap brew, deep blue, steam in a curl,
pen hums, then comes, letters unfurl.
No cap, no caper, paper stays bare,
still, look, the hook puts a pulse in air.
Dead ends, best friends, loop then lift,
rough cut, buffed up, grief turned gift.
New start, true chart, sketched in hush,
white fire, choir, ink without blush.
If love’s the tongue that the dead still speak,
then I’m fluent, mate, every day of the week.

Write what you can when the lights go thin,
ink in the wind still writes within.
Pages that hide are pages that heal, 
not everything seen needs eyes to be real.

White ink, still sings, still sets bones straight;
quiet script, tight-lipped, keeps open gate.
Unwrit ends, soft mends, fold, not snap;
I don’t close books, I unlearn the map.
White ink, no badge, no boast, no glare;
just a warm small light in the cold night air.
White ink, new skin on an ancient seam;
you can’t read this page, but you feel its theme.

“Many whisper white lies to the dead,” she smiled.
“But you,
you write white truths to the living.”
