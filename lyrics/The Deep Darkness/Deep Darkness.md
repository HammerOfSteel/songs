You feel a strange sense of déjà vu. The fog thickens. Your heart is full, but your pockets are empty. Something important is about to be forgotten.

I’m walking in circles but the trees don’t care, Paula’s got a light and a thousand-yard stare. Poo’s knee-deep in something spiritual and dense, And Jeff’s got a broken sensor that still makes sense.
I’ve got a bat but no plan in my pocket, Thinking of home, and the noise in the locket. We don’t talk loud in the swamp like this, But we laugh when the frogs croak with comic twist.

This is the deep dark, deeper than before, Fog in my lungs and mud at the door. We don’t talk much, but we know the score, Keep moving — there has to be more.

Jeff said, “If my phase distorter worked, I’d skip this part.” Paula said, “No skipping chapters. Even hard ones need a heart.” Poo just smiled, said this felt like Mu — Pain, patience, pause — like something true.
Jeff mumbled stats, poison resistance at 70%. Paula hummed softly, eyes on the sediment. Poo didn’t speak, but his silence rang, Like he was tuning in to the night’s own pang.

Hold the sound stone close, don’t drop it now, Even shadows dream if you let them somehow...

The enemy is... unfathomable. You cannot comprehend the form... But your friends are still with you. So you breathe. And you move. One pixel at a time.
