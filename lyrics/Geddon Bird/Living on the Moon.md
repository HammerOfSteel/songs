# Living on the Moon

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-23
**Edited:** 2024-10-23

---

(Verse 1)
I built a house on the edge of the tide,
With waves as my walls and stars as my guide.
Far from the noise, the chaos, the weight,
Living on the moon felt like a quiet escape.

No clocks ticking time, no voices to chase,
Just oceans of silence in a weightless place.
The world spins below, but I drift away,
Wondering if I’ll find the words to stay.

(Chorus)
Living on the moon, where nothing feels near,
Hiding from heartache, from pain, and from fear.
Floating in shadows, lost in the blue,
Learning that distance won’t carry me through.

(Verse 2)
I sent a message across the night sky,
A flare of hope from a place I can’t hide.
Zara wrote back with words soft and clear,
“Come back, love—it’s not so heavy here.”

The tides pull me home, though I fear what I’ll find,
A heart still in pieces, love left behind.
But healing’s a thread that you weave through the day,
Not a place you escape, but the steps you take.

(Chorus)
Living on the moon, where nothing feels near,
Hiding from heartache, from pain, and from fear.
Floating in shadows, lost in the blue,
Learning that distance won’t carry me through.

(Bridge)
It’s easy to run where no one can see,
But the heart still beats, quietly, free.
And love waits not in the stars, but the ground—
In every moment where we’re truly found.

(Chorus)
Living on the moon, where nothing feels near,
Hiding from heartache, from pain, and from fear.
Floating in shadows, lost in the blue,
Learning that distance won’t carry me through.

(Outro)
So I pack up my dreams and let the tide rise,
A light on the shore pulls me back to life.
Living on the moon taught me something true—
The only way forward is through.
