[intro]
Now from the stubble poets glean
Scant flowers of thought; the Muse would wean
Her myriad nurslings, feeding them
On petals plucked from a dry stem.
For one small plumule still adrift,
The wind-blown dandelion's gift,
The fields once blossomy we scour
Where the awen itself, plucked thyn flower. 

[verse]
For but your scent,
my time is spent.
Upon these hills,
aye, here in gwent!

They say could if I whisk you all away,
Would your form of flower - seed and all,
but heed my hearts long call.
In a breath would you so grant my fierce desire.
Would you tame my long lost fire.

Oh would you not, grant me but this.
Thyn sweet meadow kiss.

[chorus]
No one asked me;
Oak, meadowsweet and broom,
They fashioned a frock under the floral moon.
And stood me ‘fore the sun
Legs up to my elbows, quivering like a fawn.

[Musical solo]

[verse]
For but your stem,
so green and tall,
Do you little one too hear my call.
How long, how far this fall.

Brew and boil in times of trouble,
a potion as such upon your flower.
Cure the weary, break the shell,
Oh little lion tooth you know me so well.
 
Would you not face me again,
first born, scion.
Fairest of the triskelion

[bridge]
Meadowsweet for beauty and peace,
Obviously.
What else could I be?

[chorus]
No one asked me;
Oak, meadowsweet and broom,
Slipping from the banquet hall,
the lion tooths a-bloom.
yours, mine - ours these small hours,
A willowy sylph in a dress of flowers.

[Musical outro]
