# Apple of Enlightenment.exe

**Labels:** The Deep Darkness
**Created:** 2025-04-17
**Edited:** 2025-04-17

---

Apple Kid sat in his pile of cords,
Said, “Destiny faxed me at quarter to four.”
“I was eating a sandwich. It was pretty alright.
Then the printer screamed — and I forgot to bite.”

He pulled out a board with wires and dreams,
Said, “Orange Kid codes fonts. I code themes.”
“His robot sings. Mine cries in hex.
Mine tells you what happens when you push past next.”

The Apple said I’d win, it lit up green,
But it skipped the part where I lose my team.
It told the truth — just not the whole.
Success don’t fit a human soul.
I pressed start. I skipped the rest.
The log said ‘victory,’ but I failed the test.

Paula left a ribbon on my monitor stand,
Didn’t say much. She didn’t need to, man.
Ness said, “You okay?” I said, “I saw the end.”
“Want a sandwich? It’s cold, but it’s still a friend.”

Jeff rebuilt my toaster just for fun,
Then handed me goggles and said, “Don’t run.”
Poo just stared and smiled like stone.
Said, “In Mu, we count our breath, not bone.”

“boot sector unstable... parallel soul detected...”
“timeline: inconsistent...
identity: N355... BLUE...”

Apple Kid blinked.

“That wasn’t my code.”
“That wasn’t supposed to load.”
“I just wanted to build a friend.”

Then the screen lit up — bone-white grin.
The rhythm stuttered. The lights went dim.
Ness turned his head but said no words.
In the static:

“you’re gonna have a bad time.”

The Apple said I’d win. That part was true.
But it didn’t mention what I'd lose.
The future’s bright. The path is clear.
And nothing makes sense when you’re almost here.
I built the bridge. I read the map.
I left my heart inside the gap.

“Save file corrupted.
Timeline diverged.
Sandwich 67% eaten.

Outcome: correct.
Joy: not found.

Rebooting…”
