# The Long Way Home

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-26
**Edited:** 2024-10-26

---

(Verse 1)
I walk through the streets where the city sleeps,
Footsteps soft where the silence keeps.
The lights hum low, and the night feels wide,
Like I’m caught between the here and the tide.

Dad called tonight, his voice warm and clear,
Asking, "Are you finding your way, my dear?"
I said, "I’m trying," though I’m not sure where,
If home’s a place, a person, or just thin air.

(Chorus)
Is home a place I left behind,
Or something I carry through space and time?
It shifts like shadows, it drifts like night—
Maybe it’s found in what feels right.

(Verse 2)
I pass by the park where we once stood still,
Watching the stars from the edge of the hill.
Familiar landmarks come into view,
They stay the same, though I’ve changed, too.

Home used to mean the walls we knew,
The lullabies, the tea for two.
But now it feels like a distant song,
A melody I hum when the night feels long.

(Chorus)
Is home a place I left behind,
Or something I carry through space and time?
It shifts like shadows, it drifts like night—
Maybe it’s found in what feels right.

(Bridge)
Some days it’s a voice on the end of a line,
Other days it’s a road I’ll never find.
It’s the weight of a hug, the glance of a face,
The feeling you’ve landed in the right place.

(Chorus)
Is home a place I left behind,
Or something I carry through space and time?
It shifts like shadows, it drifts like night—
Maybe it’s found in what feels right.

(Outro)
The night wraps around, soft and low,
And I take the long way, no need to go.
The streets hum quietly under my feet,
And for now, that’s enough—home feels complete.
