# Pocket full of dust

**Labels:** Little Heart, Little Spark
**Created:** 2025-06-18
**Edited:** 2025-06-18

---

Round and round the garden, thumb along the seam,
Hide the bits they lost in pockets lined with dreams.

We spun like lids on bins out back,
Laughin’ till the air went black.
Scuffed-up boots and jelly bands,
Ghost of mud still on me hands.

Tag, you're it — but no one runs,
Empty swings and echo drums.
Someone's mum said "Not today,"
So we played quiet, far away.

Mum had crumbs down in her coat,
Said, “Hold this, love” — I never spoke.
Kept that pebble, soft and flat,
Like maybe you'd come lookin' back.

Hopscotch chalk in rain gets blurred,
Like grown-ups cryin’ without words.
Used to laugh till we’d collapse,
Now we spin, then sit, then nap.

Ring-a-ring o’ roses, a pocket full o’ dust,
Ashes, ashes — blowin’ past the bus.
We all fall gentle, knees in the green —
But I swear I heard you hummin’ in between.

Stuck a flower in me sock that day,
The kind you liked, then hid away.
Found it weeks on, dry and brown —
Still kept it though, didn’t put it down.

You left your coat on Peg 3A,
Still smells like sun and school hall clay.
We don't talk loud in cloakroom light,
But I still check for you each night.

One hand holds a crumpled card,
Other's full of things I guard.
A marble, string, some broken pin —
Bits of you I tuck within.

Can’t say grief, so we just play,
Spin again — push dark away.
A pocket full of dust feels light some nights,
Like you’re whisperin' beneath streetlights.

Ring-a-ring o’ roses, a pocket full o’ dust,
Ashes, ashes — we stand up ‘cause we must.
We all fall sometimes, but not for long —
And I still hum your name inside this song.

We all fall... we all fall... we all...
Ring the bell, don’t say the name...
It’s still the same, it’s still the same...
pocket full of... hush...
