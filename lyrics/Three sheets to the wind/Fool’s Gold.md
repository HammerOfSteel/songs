# Fool’s Gold

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-04-01
**Edited:** 2025-04-01

---

The growth of the soul may be compared to the growth of a plant. In both cases, no new properties are imparted by the operation of external causes, but only the inward tendencies are called into action and clothed with strength.

Sat on a stone above Kynance tide,
coat too thin, but I wear it with pride.
It’s older than me in all the right ways,
patched at the seams like these thoughts I replay.
Used to chase it—whatever it was,
something just west of whatever is.
Found scrolls, poems, riddles and rings,
chased myths that didn’t promise a thing.
Slept in cities, prayed in sand,
loved without knowing how to land.
Always thought the next shore would shine—
that gold was real if you gave it time.
But gold flakes fade in the light of truth—
and it was always her laugh
that held my youth.

Fool’s gold glimmers,
but it don’t weigh much.
All these years I searched for touch—
some perfect form, some hidden flame,
and missed the sky
saying my name.

Found a stone once shaped like a heart,
kept it three years then it fell apart.
Had a book with a language I never learned,
every page smelled of smoke and fern.
Held a ring I never gave—
thought timing would know how to behave.
All that waiting, all that ache,
for a thing that shimmered
but wouldn't break.
Thought wisdom was knowing more than the rest,
but I missed the girl who liked her knees a mess.
Who ran through rain like it called her name,
and saw magic in puddles
I thought were plain.

Some things break
before they shine.
Some things speak
without a line.

Fool’s gold flickers,
but the sea don’t lie.
It sings for no one,
just walks the sky.
All my maps led here,
but none were right—
it was her voice
that held the light.

Wrote my life in a notebook torn,
pages soft from nights I mourned.
Some for love, some for lore,
most just scribbles,
shadows on a shore.
Kissed a girl who believed in ghosts,
told her I’d be more—but I left the coast.
Swore I’d come back with something real,
but time don’t bend just ’cause you kneel.
Now it’s just me,
this stone, this sea,
an old man’s coat,
a prayer or three.
And in my hand, some pyrite dull—
but in the sun, it’s almost full.

Fool’s gold glows,
if you look just right—
not for worth,
but for the fight.
It’s not the truth
but it still can hold—
a glint,
a smile,
a piece of old.

Footsteps quick on the pebbled path,
a laugh like bells, soft and fast.
She runs, bright coat, skipping low—
voice like spring from the years ago.
“Taid!”
she calls.
“What are you looking for?”
I blink,
the wind don’t bite no more.
Reach in my coat, lift something slow,
hold it up in the saltwind glow.
“Ohhh! Pretty!”
she says, with wide-bright eyes,
and I smile like I see through time.

“Just fool’s gold, cariad,”
I say, so soft.
“An old man’s treasure, nothing lost.”

Not every glow
was made to stay—
some just flicker
to light your way.

She holds my hand, small and warm.
We walk toward the road where the lights are born.
And I don’t need a map,
or a sign,
or a flame—
just her hand,
and the fact
that she came.
