# Taciturnity

**Labels:** solvet et coagula
**Created:** 2025-04-22
**Edited:** 2025-04-22

---

I thought they saw me.
The way Mum paused by the kettle.
The way the dog stared at the corner of the room.
The way the bathroom light flickered when I whispered.
...But maybe it was just faulty wiring.
Or maybe it’s me that’s faulty.
Still here. But not heard.

I’m in the living room again,
Dad’s same chair, same creak when he leans,
Mum hums something soft in the kitchen,
Her hands never stop, but her eyes say she’s missin'.
I call out, “Mum,”—the word doesn’t land,
Just echoes like dust through the cracks in her hand.
The telly drones on, old reruns of laughs,
And I’m stuck in between—
not the present, not the past.

I said my goodbyes but no one replied,
I linger like breath on a mirror—
just signs.
You think I’m gone, but I’m brushing your sleeve,
A voice in the kettle, a tug on the leaves.

I tried the mirror trick—trace in the steam,
The alchemy sign like it came from a dream.
Mum looked up, blinked, rubbed it away,
Just condensation, she muttered—
like I never stayed.
Even the dog won’t bark when I pass,
Like he’s in on the pact—let silence outlast.
I float through the flat like a paused melody,
Like a song you hum
but never let free.

I said my goodbyes, but your ears were closed,
I bloom in the silence where sorrow grows.
You think I’m gone, but I whisper in light,
A breath on the mirror, unseen in the night.

She set out my mug like she always does,
Big handle, chipped lip, that ceramic fuzz.
Two brushes still in the cup—hers and mine,
One pink, one green,
frozen in time.
Sometimes I wonder—if I screamed instead,
Would they have noticed, would they have read
The signs in the fog, the breath on the tiles,
The space where I stood,
for miles and miles?

They said I was at peace, that I slipped away,
But I’m still at the sink, every night, same way.
Watching her sigh, shoulders drawn tight,
Tracing old rituals in flickering light.
It’s not hell, but it’s not really grace—
This in-between place, this half-remembered face.
Not denial, not grief, not quite gone—
A note half-sung,
a word withdrawn.

Silence isn’t stillness.
It’s the sound of breath held back.
Of a child’s finger tracing sulfur on glass.
And hoping someone sees.
Not the sign.
But the reaching.
