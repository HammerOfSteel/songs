# Beatle Box

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-05-02
**Edited:** 2025-07-25

---

it ain't pointless.
It’s just your soul stretchin’, crackin’ its shell,
makin’ room for the real you to breathe proper.

We chalked on the walls: names, daft claims,
“Gemma loves Rees” with a thousand flames.
Gum snapped rhythm, trainers scuffed grey,
Stole chips from the bin, best part of the day.

Kicked a can ‘til it told us a tune,
Drumline built from an old metal spoon.
We called it the Beatle box, no one knew why.
But it buzzed when we banged it, rang under sky.

We talked big talk, voices wide as the valley,
Dreamed of gigs in town, gold chains in the alley.
But mostly we hid. From what? No clue.
Just knew the echo made us sound true.

Beatle box, we banged on steel,
Every knock a vow we’d never unfeel.
Laughter like lightning under stone,
Where we spoke loud so we weren’t alone.

I told you a secret I’d never repeat,
You passed it back quiet, no need to compete.
“No one’s normal,” you said, “we’re all cracked.”
Then flipped the lid on a council bin sack.

Mum thought I was down at Rees’s for tea,
Didn’t know I was hiding my whole self with me.
We’d sit in the hum of a streetlamp tone,
Soundtracked by skate wheels and phones on drone.

There was magic there, unplanned, unposed.
Like something Welsh but barely composed.
It’s gone now, boxed in with the youth club keys,
But I carry that rhythm, like a pocket disease.

I still hear that buzz in drainpipe bends,
Your words in wind, where the railings end.

Beatle box, we banged on steel,
Each beat a truth we tried to seal.
Under grey skies, grit and moss,
We named our pain and called it gloss.

I left my voice there, next to yours.
In that box of bolts, with no locked doors.

If you listen now, it still replies.
Echo’s just memory dressed in disguise.

Pinch of mud, whisper of nettles,
one broken fence, two belly laughs settles,
stir in a dare under a half-moon,
T'is bramble's where I've grown.
