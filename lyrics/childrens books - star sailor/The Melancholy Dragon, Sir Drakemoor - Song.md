# The Melancholy Dragon, Sir Drakemoor - Song

**Labels:** childrens books - star sailor
**Created:** 2024-10-16
**Edited:** 2024-10-16

---

Verse 1:

Star Sailor sails through the red Martian skies,
Her crescent boat gliding where silence lies.
She sees a mountain, the Dragon’s Crater deep,
Where a dragon’s sorrow softly weeps.

Sir Drakemoor hides, his heart heavy with pain,
Afraid to connect, to open again.
His fiery breath flickers, weak and unsure,
But Star Sailor knocks on his hidden door.

Chorus:

It takes time to find the light inside,
To trust in a friend, to set fear aside.
“Show your softer side,” Star Sailor said,
“For strength is in the heart, not in what we dread.”

Even the fiercest need a hand,
To reach out and help them understand.
In the quiet, in the glow of night,
They find the courage to hold on tight.

Verse 2:

At first, he turns away, his eyes full of fear,
“What do you want?” he growls, “There’s no one here.”
But Star Sailor waits, with patience and grace,
Offering her light, giving him space.

Each day she returns, her heart full of care,
And Drakemoor watches, though pretending not to stare.
Slowly, he listens, to the tales she tells,
Of stars that shine through their own dark spells.

Bridge:

Through days and nights, their friendship grows,
As Drakemoor shares what only he knows.
He learns from her, how stars must fade,
To make room for new light, a new path made.

Star Sailor, too, learns from his flame,
That vulnerability carries no shame.
Together they find, in silence and song,
The strength to carry each other along.

Outro:

Now side by side, under Mars' red sky,
They watch the stars as the night drifts by.
Drakemoor’s fire burns steady and warm,
No longer bound by fear or storm.

Star Sailor smiles as she sails away,
Knowing that here, her friend will stay.
And the dragon, once lost in the dark and cold,
Now knows that even the fiercest can be bold.
