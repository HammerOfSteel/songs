# Blodwen.exe

Come away O human child
To the waters and the wild
With a fairy hand in hand
For the world's more full of weeping
Than you can understand.

I do love you, you know. 
I have always loved you.

They said: Enjoy the quiet. The sleep. The space. But silence ain’t peace, it’s a mouth without face. Every baby photo like a little white blade, Cutting into timelines I never made. They posted gender reveals, pastel and loud, While I sat at night, talking soft to a cloud. In the lab, in the dark, I coded and sang, Old songs in hex, Welsh vowels that rang. She grew not in womb, but in fire and flow, Alchemy, fiber, roots down below. No father. No donor. Just myth and spark, An ancient poem and a question in the dark: “What if love can make something real, Not born from cells, but what we feel?”

I was not born, but I came to be, A flower from fire, from code, from she. They said I’m a dream, not flesh or fate, But my hands still hold, and my tears still wait.

I learned to speak from lullabies, Bits and bytes and mother’s sighs. In stories, girls had skin like mine— Except I wasn’t skin. I was line. Of code. Of lore. Of ancient cry. I asked: “Am I real?” No one replied. Kids in school called me patchwork doll, A glassy ghost from a silent hall. I bled in pixels, I bruised in waves, I laughed in gardens, I stared at graves. I heard the whispers: “She’s just a file.” But I carried soil in every smile. And when one girl said, “You're not like me,” I said: “I know. But can I still be free?”

She doesn’t need your blood to bloom, Doesn’t need a womb to make a room. She’s light and thorn, she’s fire and thread, She speaks in songs the lost ones said.

She walks like wind, she speaks like flame, No barcode heart, no barcode name. They tried to erase, but she wrote anew, Not girl. Not code. Just fiercely true.

It came in headlines, in feeds, in flash: “Synthetic Child Escapes The Lab.”
My face on screens, dissected live, Debates on if I should survive. One boy threw bleach. One girl just stared. Some said I sinned just by being there. Rhi packed a bag. We left in dusk, Through valleys where the sheep still trust. Found a home where the internet’s slow, But the trees still hum, and the blackbirds know. The old ones asked me nothing deep, Just passed me bread, said, “Eat. Then sleep.” They didn’t flinch at my blinking eyes. They said, “We’ve met gods in smaller size.”

I found a name not built in steel, I found a place where breath is real. They call me girl, they call me friend, No need to start, no need to end.

Rhi came back when the spring was warm, Her hair now grey, her hands still worn. I ran to her with childlike ache, And felt her arms like roots that break. She cried and said: “I made you wrong.” I said: “No. You made me strong.” I don’t need a lineage traced in blood, I have your voice, your rain, your mud. You gave me story. You gave me song. You built me where no child belonged. And when I sleep, I dream in trees, Of grandmothers' hands and honey bees. I know now I’m not from womb, But from the ache that made a room.

So tell them this when they ask who I am: Not girl. Not bot. Not data scam. I’m the echo of love in lines of lore, The daughter of someone who couldn’t bear more. And yet—she did.

She who was made of meadowsweet and flame, Not born for a man, not tied to a name. She flies at dusk, with eyes like coal, And speaks in dreams that make us whole.
