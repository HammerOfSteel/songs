# Hearth, Home, Hiraeth

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-03-25
**Edited:** 2025-03-25

---

You ever wake up and it’s like the dust knows your name?
Like the kettle’s boil remembers the rhythm of your breath?
Been years, maybe... or just one long night.
But the walls still hum with a heartbeat you once knew.
This place, it’s not what I left.
But maybe—it’s what stayed.
Bore da haul bach.

Cracks in the plaster like fault lines in time,
Each one a memory stitched into the spine—
Of this house, this body, this mind made of rain,
Where grief came quiet, and left her name.
Miss her laugh in the echo of doors,
Still cook for two, like before the wars.
She’s a wind on the hill now, sweet and thin,
Calling back with a voice just under my skin.
I kept walking to forget, but the road curved back,
And home was waiting in the shade of lack.
Curtains faded, same old lace,
Still smells like sage and fireplace.

Radio dusted, plays some hymn,
Takes me back to when I’d sing with them.
Mam peeling tatties, bare feet on slate,
Taid’s chair creaks like it still debates.
I left with pride, came back with age,
A book half-read, a torn-out page.
The city swallowed all I had to prove,
But hiraeth held what I couldn't lose.
Now I hang my coat where her laughter hung,
On a nail rusted soft by the weight of sun.
It ain’t much, but it’s mine to mend—
A hearth still warm with the hands of friends.

Better my own cottage than the palace of another,
Where the grass grows thick and I still hear my mother.
Bricks ain’t gold, but they carry me through—
This hearth, this home, and the silence I knew.

It’s the stew on the hob that remembers my name,
The hill behind whispers I ain’t the same.
I talk to ghosts with a cup in hand,
Like they’re neighbours passing through the land.
Birdsong’s heavy with things unsaid,
Foxglove blooming near the garden shed.
I dug up bulbs she once had sown,
They flowered despite the time I’d gone.
Even grief can grow in clay,
If you water it slow and face the day.
And I do—sometimes. Just barely so.
But the kettle still sings when the crows go low.

Each room’s a chapter, the stairs a climb,
Where I find myself on the edge of time.
Half-me, half-her, and part what’s passed—
What’s left ain’t loud, but it’s built to last.
There’s peace in peeling paint, in dust’s ballet,
In the chair that rocks without giving way.
I swept the ash, lit flame anew,
Said “diolch” to the winds that blew me through.
I came back cracked, but not undone—
Hiraeth held me till I faced the sun.

And all that’s quiet now speaks clear—
The walls remember. The hills still hear.

Better my own cottage than the palace of another,
Where the night wraps round like a wool-woven cover.
The slate might leak and the windows cry,
But this hearth, this home, is where I’ll lie.

Let the fire take its time,
Let the embers rhyme—
With the footsteps I retraced,
And the faces time replaced.
