# Awen’s Breath

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-11-04
**Edited:** 2024-11-04

---

Verse 1
From sea’s embrace to cobbled stone,
A sailor walks this town alone.
In spring’s bright bloom, the streets are gay,
But he feels naught of festal day.

The laughter floats on Cymru’s air,
Yet his heart drags like anchors there.
Lost in thoughts of seren fach,
A star he held, now faded, back.

Chorus
Oh, Awen’s breath, soft and true,
Guide me where her spirit flew.
In shadowed streets, in crowd’s cheer,
Lead me to where she lingers near.

Verse 2
A flash—a laugh—a glimpse of gold,
A girl he knew in days of old.
With wide eyes bright, she slips from view,
As if in chase of skies so blue.

Through winding lanes he’s drawn ahead,
Through narrow paths the maiden’s led.
He calls her name, mo chariad,
As dancers part, his heart is glad.

Chorus
Oh, Awen’s breath, soft and true,
Guide me where her spirit flew.
In shadowed streets, in crowd’s cheer,
Lead me to where she lingers near.

Verse 3
She darts away, a wraith, a shade,
Through alleys worn, her ghostly parade.
The laughter fades, the town grows still,
As sorrow deepens, so does his will.

At last he stands by earth so bare,
And finds her grave in silent prayer.
His voice is lost, yn ddagrau dwfn,
A well of tears beneath the moon.

Chorus
Oh, Awen’s breath, soft and true,
Guide me where her spirit flew.
In shadowed streets, in crowd’s cheer,
Lead me to where she lingers near.

Bridge
And then, upon his shoulder light,
A butterfly, in softest flight.
A whisper felt from realms unseen,
In colors bright, and pure, serene.

Verse 4
He takes a breath, cariad bach,
And feels her near though worlds apart.
In Cymru’s fields, her laughter stays,
Like springtime blooms and dawning rays.

With heavy heart yet lighter step,
He turns to join where folk have kept,
Their voices strong, their hearts alight,
And Awen’s breath to greet the night.

Final Chorus
Oh, Awen’s breath, soft and true,
You led me where her spirit flew.
In laughter, life, and gentle cheer,
I find her close, she lingers near.

Outro
So walks the sailor through Cymru’s shore,
With peace he’d lost but holds once more.
In every smile, each soft embrace,
The breath of Awen, love, and grace.
