# Bare with Me

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-25
**Edited:** 2024-10-25

---

(Verse 1)
We sit by the window at the Caffeine Club,
Ella’s talking, but I feel so numb.
Lucas says, "Time will mend what it breaks,"
Maya’s quiet, sipping tea, for both our sakes.

They try to hold me in words I can’t hear,
Their kindness feels distant, heavy to bear.
I'm caught in the cold, between guilt and relief,
Wishing my heart could find some release.

(Chorus)
Bare with me, I’m not who I should be.
I’m trying to stand, but the ground slips beneath me.
Every hand that reaches feels too far away,
Bare with me, I’ll find my way—just not today.

(Verse 2)
We walked to Devil’s Point, watched the sea sway,
The sky a dull gray, like the words I can't say.
Ella holds my arm, but I feel out of place,
Grief like a shadow I cannot erase.

A missed call from Dad, a text left unread,
London feels farther than ever instead.
I want to believe I’ll feel whole again,
But every step forward reminds me of then.

(Chorus)
Bare with me, I’m not who I should be.
I’m trying to stand, but the ground slips beneath me.
Every hand that reaches feels too far away,
Bare with me, I’ll find my way—just not today.

(Bridge)
At Saltram Park, winter trees stand bare,
Like me, they’re waiting for warmth in the air.
Lucas says, “Seasons, they turn with the time,”
But some roots dig deeper than reason or rhyme.

I watch as my friends try to stay by my side,
But it’s hard to let in what I try to hide.
I need them near, though I push them away,
Hoping they’ll stay through the long winter’s day.

(Chorus)
Bare with me, I’m not who I should be.
I’m trying to stand, but the ground slips beneath me.
Every hand that reaches feels too far away,
Bare with me, I’ll find my way—just not today.

(Outro)
One day, I’ll answer Dad’s call and say,
"I’m still here, just finding my way."
For now, I’ll sit with these empty skies,
Waiting for spring to open my eyes.
