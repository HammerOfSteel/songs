# Penelope

## The Myth — What It Carries

In the Odyssey, Penelope weaves a funeral shroud by day and unweaves it by night to delay the suitors demanding she choose a new husband. She is waiting for Odysseus to return.

In this version, she is not waiting for Odysseus.

She is waiting for the world that existed *before* Odysseus left — before the whole story started, before the shape of things changed. She unweaves not as strategy but as the only honest response to a world that keeps asking her to move forward into a future she did not choose and does not want.

The suitors = everyone who wants her to be okay, move on, find a new life
Odysseus = the future, recovery, "being okay" — she is not waiting for that
The before = the world before the loss; the only place she is trying to reach

The myth carries the grief. The lyric never names it.

## Style
- Gorillaz meets Rizzle Kicks — funky, upbeat, slightly murky UK hip-hop/indie feel
- Female lead vocal: smoky, detached, rhythmically precise — think Martina Topley-Bird on early Gorillaz tracks, or the cool remove of DARE; the devastation is in the words, not the delivery
- Sentence-to-sentence rhyme throughout, rap cadence in verses
- Hook is hypnotic and repetitive — DARE-style, the kind that loops in your head
- Variable tempo: verses have flex and flow; the hook is groove-locked; the bridge drops to half-tempo before snapping back

## Instrumentation
- Synth bass line: deep, slightly murky, Gorillaz signature — establishes before the voice enters
- Drum machine: tight, clean, hip-hop pocket
- Electric guitar: sparse, slightly psychedelic stabs on the off-beat — comes in from verse 2
- Keys: a recurring three-note motif underneath the hook, slightly haunted
- Bridge: all elements drop to bass only and half-tempo — then snaps back on the final beat before verse 3
- Outro: hook loops with the synth bass prominent, vocals fading over the top Gorillaz-style; no hard cut, just dissolves

## Production Notes
- Female vocal: close-mic'd, warm but controlled, British — accent natural, not performed
- The cool detachment in the voice is doing half the work; she is not crying, she is explaining
- The hook vocals can double or have a light harmony on the repeat — DARE-style call-and-repeat
- Do not let the production get too big; the murkiness is the point — it should feel like something just below the surface
- Bridge vocal: quieter, slightly more exposed, the one moment the mask slips — then gone
