# A Penny for the Tide – Ceiniog i’r Llanw

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-02-16
**Edited:** 2025-03-05

---

"There is a well in St Ives where the old stories say wishes drift like the tide, carried to the ones we miss. A little girl once stood there, a penny in her hand, her heart full of words she didn’t know how to speak. She tossed the coin into the water, whispering her mother’s name, watching the ripples dance under the sky. And the sea, ever faithful, carried her message far beyond the shore..."

One penny bright, one wish so small,
A silver light, a voice to call.
The tide will take, the tide will know,
And whisper back where love still flows.

Barefoot steps upon the sand,
A tiny coin in tiny hands.
"One for Mam, across the sea,
Take my love and bring her to me."

The waves rolled in, the waves rolled wide,
Her wish set sail upon the tide.
A fleeting hope, a whispered sound,
A dream now lost, yet never drowned.

She stood where sea and sky collide,
Where voices echo with the tide.
"Does she hear me, does she know,
The stories that my father told?"

The book of stars, the tales they spun,
Of golden shores and silver suns.
Her mother’s touch in whispered waves,
Still reaching past the rolling days.

Ceiniog fach i’r llanw mawr, (A small penny for the mighty tide,)
Neidia’r tonnau, cario’n barhau. (Jumping waves, love still flows.)
A voice, a name, a light so bright,
Carried far on wings of white.

Years would pass, the child grew tall,
No longer by the shore so small.
The book grew worn, the pages thin,
But still her father read to him.

The tale of Gelert, brave and true,
The stars that watched, the seas so blue.
And in his voice, her mother’s tone,
As if she’d never left their home.

A wish is more than whispered air,
It lingers long, it lingers there.
The tide may turn, the years may fade,
But love is never washed away.

Ceiniog fach i’r llanw mawr,
Neidia’r tonnau, cario’n barhau.
Not lost, not gone, but waiting near,
A whispered voice, still shining clear.

And then one day, in St Ives town,
The father walked where sea winds sound.
A fountain’s song, a coin so bright,
The sun’s reflection caught his sight.

A penny worn, but still it shone,
The memory struck, the years were gone.
His daughter’s wish, his love so deep,
Came rushing back like waves in sleep.

Oh, little hands, oh, silver seas,
Still sending whispers back to me.
Not lost, not gone, just turned away,
Like waves returning home one day.

Ceiniog fach i’r llanw mawr,
Neidia’r tonnau, cario’n barhau.
Though time may take and tides may turn,
Some lights will always find their burn.

"The tide still sings, the well still waits,
For love is never lost to fate.
So sleep, my child, the waves will know,
And whisper back where love still flows."
