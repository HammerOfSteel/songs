# Ashes in the pan

**Labels:** Heartbound
**Created:** 2025-08-15
**Edited:** 2025-08-15

---

Connect,
Light, light.
One small step.
Light.

In the belly of the grate, the night has distilled
all the bright into something more patient.
The dust holds a secret warmth,
the kind you cup in your palms
as though it might tell you the truth if you listen.
I stir the grey with an iron tongue,
tracing shapes only the coals can read,
watching the air drink the last of the gold.

Ashes in the pan, whisper the art,
heat without flame, the turning of heart.
Black into silver, silver to gold,
still in the embers, the shape will hold.

Once, I carried the stone before it was stone,
soft, quickened, full of light it did not yet know.
We walked the frost path before the sun named it,
breath turning to smoke,
smoke turning to cloud,
cloud returning to rain.
And rain, in its own slow season,
feeds the wood that waits for the fire.

Ashes in the pan, turn as you stand,
lead into silver, silver to sand.
What was once burning, cool to the skin,
still I keep the fire within.

The work is never finished,
it shifts in the marrow,
in the grain of the table,
in the small hiss when water meets heat.
I tend the slow transmutation,
not for the blaze,
but for the faint thrum beneath it,
the pulse of a thing that refuses to vanish,
even in sleep.

Ashes in the pan, circle the year,
turning the hidden, drawing it near.
Night into morning, loss into plan,
I live in the ashes in the pan.

Lay down,
The gray,
today,
Ate,
it will teach you,
even the smallest ember,
remembers the sun.
