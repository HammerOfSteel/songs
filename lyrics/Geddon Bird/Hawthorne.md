# Hawthorne

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-23
**Edited:** 2025-07-19

---

We walk among stones, where time weaves its thread,
The ruins whisper the words left unsaid.
Hawthorn trees line the path we tread,
Thorns that protect, and blossoms that bled.

The professor speaks with a voice soft and low,
“Every encounter leaves marks, you know.”
I trace the scars etched deep in my skin,
Wondering what they’ve carved out within.

Each thorn, each flower, a chapter untold,
Stories of love, both tender and bold.
We grow through the pain, with petals and scars,
Leaving behind us a trail of stars.

He points to a tree grown wild by the wall,
“Even the twisted ones stand proud and tall.”
I see in its branches a memory of mine,
A moment that passed, a wound in time.

The thorns remind me of friends come and gone,
The blooms recall love that lasted till dawn.
Hawthorne, a keeper of what we endure,
A symbol that nothing stays broken or pure.

Each thorn, each flower, a chapter untold,
Stories of love, both tender and bold.
We grow through the pain, with petals and scars,
Leaving behind us a trail of stars.

He says, "It’s the way that we carry the past,
Not how we outrun it or make it last.
Lessons will find us in each twist and turn,
The branches will bend, but they never burn."

Each thorn, each flower, a chapter untold,
Stories of love, both tender and bold.
We grow through the pain, with petals and scars,
Leaving behind us a trail of stars.

We walk through the dusk, the air turning cold,
With hawthorns that whisper secrets of old.
He nods as we part, a lesson well earned,
Some paths are planted, some are still yearned.
