# Counterfactual History

**Labels:** Counterfactual History, Album
**Created:** 2025-04-05
**Edited:** 2025-08-21

---

1. The Mound at Llangybi

Theme: Beginning of the shift. He walks through grief into a different morning.
Narrative: Same as before—an old man, a wildcamp, a sound, a wisp. He enters the mound, expecting nothing, but emerges subtly wrong-footed. The trees are younger. His gear still works, but… signal’s gone. And so is the weight of age in his legs.

> A light between the larches / And the years began to bend.




---

2. A Whisper Out of Time

Theme: Realization.
Narrative: He walks back into the town. Something is off—prices are lower, posters are old, people are younger. Then he sees himself. Younger. Distracted. Haunted. His daughter is still alive. It's just before that night.


---

3. Don't Wake Her Yet

Theme: That night.
Narrative:  He sees himself in this timeline, Younger, yet haunted, The younger him sits by the fire, numb, paralyzed by grief from the loss of her mother. He knows this moment. He watches through tears. He knows he won’t wake her then in a years time, but he also realizes that there is still time left. Maybe his life wont change, he is old and has lived; but this version of himself he sees here: it could be different for his branch. How can he make a difference though, can he even to it? Sitting at the local cafe in the welsh village he ponders this, he walks by the local river and ponders this; he thinks about what he would have wanted and he understand that its risky; but a letter is the way to go.


---

4. A Letter Slipped Through Time

Theme: Trying to reach across.
Narrative: He writes a letter. The most important words of his life. He leaves it where his younger self will find it—tucked into a coat, or hidden in a songbook. It’s unsigned. But it’s his voice. His hope.


---

5. If He Listens

Theme: Uncertainty.
Narrative: He waits. Watches from shadows. Will the other him listen? Will he believe? Will he act? Time doesn’t stop. The night creeps closer. The song builds in tension and breath.


---

6. Take Her Up the Hill

Theme: The moment.
Narrative: He sees the motion. The other him rises. Opens the door. Takes the girl in his arms. Walks into the night. It’s done. She lives. He collapses with joy and agony.


---

7. Borrowed Sunlight

Theme: What could never be his.
Narrative: He walks unseen now. They are happy. She grows. But he cannot touch her. He is not part of this world. He gave it back to her, and to the man he once was. He is both savior and ghost.


---

8. The Other Me

Theme: Duality, identity.
Narrative: The young man looks older now. Wiser. He senses something. He writes songs with memories he shouldn’t have. He sketches faces he never met. Somewhere, part of him knows.


---

9. woven quilt, silent guilt

Theme: The ache of anonymity, living in separate strands, of knowing and seeing never being.
Tone: non-narative, prose's of unspoken banter - of days of futures past - loving on, living on.
Beat: UK style rap, british slang and British vocals, mandolin picking 


---

10. The Mound Again

Theme: Closure.
Narrative: He returns to the mound. The path has worn thin. He leaves his phone, his coat, his ring. Everything he was. He steps inside again, ready now.


---

11. Counterfactual History

Theme: Reflection.
Narrative: A slow, spoken outro, over music. He tells her story. Not how it was, but how it could have been. How a father walked through time to try, once more, to save her.

> “If anyone ever asks… say I was never there. Say she lived, and that was enough.”
