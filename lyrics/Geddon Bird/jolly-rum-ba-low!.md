# jolly-rum-ba-low!

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-11-08
**Edited:** 2024-11-08

---

(Verse 1)
The streets fill up, a burst of green,
Flowers and ribbons, a bright, wild scene.
They sing to the sun, to earth and tide,
Laughter rings from every side.
Look, there’s the Green Man, all garlanded bright—
Bringing spring’s warmth back to the night.
And St. George stands tall, bold as a flame,
Chasing winter by its name.

(Chorus)
Hal-an-tow, jolly-rum-ba-low!
We were up long before the day-o,
To welcome in the summer, to welcome in the May-o,
For summer is a-comin’ in, and winter’s gone away-o!

(Verse 2)
”Look, mama, flowers in the air!”
Yes, little one, they’re everywhere.
”The people sing, they clap and cheer—”
It’s joy and life, to banish fear.

But it’s more than joy, this dance we see,
It’s the strength we find in you, in me.
To feel the warmth after months of rain,
A balm for hearts that have known the strain.

(Chorus)
Hal-an-tow, jolly-rum-ba-low!
We were up long before the day-o,
To welcome in the summer, to welcome in the May-o,
For summer is a-comin’ in, and winter’s gone away-o!

(Bridge)
Life’s a circle, a wheel that spins,
Through dark and light, it always begins.
Like seasons turn, we rise and fall,
But joy returns, and that’s the call.

”Dance with me, mama, round and round!”
Alright, love, feet on the ground.
In each small step, we find the way,
Turning toward a brighter day.

(Chorus)
Hal-an-tow, jolly-rum-ba-low!
We were up long before the day-o,
To welcome in the summer, to welcome in the May-o,
For summer is a-comin’ in, and winter’s gone away-o!

(Outro)
The fire burns bright as the night grows thin,
With friends and laughter, we let life in.
In spring’s embrace, we find our cheer,
A song to carry us through the year.

”Hal-an-tow…jolly-rum-ba-low…”
