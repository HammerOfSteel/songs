# Summerland

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-04-25
**Edited:** 2025-04-25

---

Found the petal in my drawer — still pressed, still gold.
Must have been, what? - eight years old.
Litha in Pembrokeshire.
Home and hearth, midnight fire

They gave me a sunflower, she's got the breeze
Tad was handed a butterfly twice his knees
We followed the fiddle, hand in hand
Through a path that whispered: Summerland
The standing stone waited, wrapped in light
With a rose in my hand, and She held tight
The flute began singing, low and round
And the grown-ups stopped like they'd lost the ground

In Summerland, we skip the rain
We name each leaf, we light each flame
We circle ‘round with roots that hum
And call midsummer’s magic home
A rose, a laugh, a single hand —
That's all it takes to grow the land

“Why does the sky feel wide?”
“It’s ‘cause we’re standing inside.”
She picked three leaves, gave one to me
“Love, remember, and set it free.”
The priestess smiled with moss in her braid
“Nothing's forgotten, it’s just delayed.”
We laid our gifts in the flower-spun ring
And I swear I heard the stone sing.

“The wind spelled ‘cartref’ in the sky”
“And ‘teulu’ passed us, fluttering by”
“We danced till our cheeks turned rose and pine”
“And they said the world just rewrote its line”
“One pebble, one petal, one memory seed”
“To bury in winter when you’re in need”

“Litha’s light, hold us near
Bright the night, soft the year
Call the summer, turn the land —
One by one, we touch the strand”

The white rose didn’t wilt.
And neither did we.
We were children with jam on our knees,
But we knew the spell:
Hold joy with both hands.
Share it before it disappears.

In Summerland, we skip the rain
We name each leaf, we light each flame
We dance with hearts too full to plan
And bloom again in Summerland

I kept the petal.
And every midsummer, I remember:
We didn’t grow up that night —
We grew in.
