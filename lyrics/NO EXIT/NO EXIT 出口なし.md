# NO EXIT 出口なし

**Labels:** Album, NO EXIT
**Created:** 2025-04-12
**Edited:** 2025-04-12

---

“NO EXIT 出口なし” – playing on the concept of entrapment (Sartre vibes meets Japanese inner pressure)


🎵 Track Concepts
1. 「かんぺきじゃない」(Not Perfect)
Theme: Societal pressure for perfection – in school, work, image
Lyric angle: "I wore the mask until it fused to my skin"
Explores jishuku (self-restraint) and how people stop expressing their needs

2. 「息できない」(I Can’t Breathe)
Theme: Panic attacks, repression, internalized pressure
Sound: Breath-heavy production, claustrophobic beats
Half-whispered rap verses describing the spiral before and after a breakdown

3. “Tatemae / 建前”
Theme: The divide between tatemae (public face) and honne (true feelings)
Contrasting Japanese and English lyrics, like a fight between two selves
Maybe with an interlude sample of keigo (formal speech) turned unsettling

4. 「消えたい」(Wanna Disappear)
Theme: The silent epidemic of suicidal thoughts
Approach: Tender, raw, not glamorizing but giving voice to the quiet wish to vanish
Features the phrase “daijoubu” used ironically – like when someone is clearly not okay

5. “Room 302”
Theme: Hikikomori (social withdrawal)
From the point of view of someone who hasn't left their room in years
Reference to convenience store food, late night TV, blurred days
Ambient glitches, lo-fi beats, distant trains or rain outside the window

6. 「いい子」(Good Child)
Theme: Being the obedient, quiet kid – and what that erases
Could reference “adultified children” and the pressure to suppress needs for others’ sake
A spoken outro of an apology to their inner child

7. “ごめんなさい症候群” (Apology Syndrome)
Theme: Chronic self-blame and excessive apologizing (a real phenomenon)
Fast-paced rap verses spiraling through "gomen", "sumimasen", "my fault", "I shouldn’t..."
Hook: “Even my breath feels like a bother”

8. 「ふつうになりたい」(I Just Wanna Be Normal)
Theme: Neurodivergence, disability, queerness—anything outside “the norm”
Blending internal monologue with public expectations
Maybe features distorted school bell sounds or morning announcements

9. “Sick Leave / 病んでます”
Theme: Stigma of taking a mental health break in Japanese work culture
Hyper-clean office imagery, cold instrumental
Rap hook: “Clock in / clock out / cry on the floor / no one saw it”

10. 「でも生きてる」(But I’m Still Alive)
Theme: The complicated truth of surviving—without resolution
Closing track with both hope and ambiguity
Could include layered voices of past versions of the self, like a chorus of ghosts
