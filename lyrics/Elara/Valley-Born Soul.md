# Valley-Born Soul

**Labels:** Elara
**Created:** 2025-06-16
**Edited:** 2025-06-16

---

Mam says I’ve got a valley-born soul,
Valley-born soul, valley-born soul.
Like mist off the moor when the chapel bells toll,
Like stone walls that hum when the west winds moan,
My soul knows the way to bring hiraeth home.

Tad says my hair’s like the ash tree bark,
Ash tree bark, soft and dark.
Like peat by the stream through the fern and the gorse,
Or rain on the slate when the sky turns stark,
With a stubborn curl and a miner’s mark.

I’m a map of them all and the ones before,
One foot on the ridge and one by the shore.
Every path, every lambing fold,
I’m a song in the hills with a valley-born soul.

Nain says my feet were never meant still,
Never meant still, never meant still.
They skip down the scree and climb every hill,
I dance where the skylarks rise and call,
And come back when the red kites circle fall.

My sister says I’m a fire and breeze,
Fire and breeze, fire and breeze.
Like a tale told low beneath oakwood trees,
I stay just long enough to be known,
Then slip like a stream through moss and stone.

I’m a map of them all and the ones before,
One foot on the ridge and one by the shore.
Every path, every lambing fold,
I’m a song in the hills with a valley-born soul.

Mam says I’ve got a valley-born soul,
Valley-born soul, valley-born soul.
Where the foxglove sways and the blackbirds cry,
And the heart of the land is never far,
I’m a thread in the weave of the dragon’s star.
I’m a map of them all and the ones before,
One foot on the ridge and one by the shore.
Every path, every lambing fold,
I’m a song in the hills with a valley-born soul.
