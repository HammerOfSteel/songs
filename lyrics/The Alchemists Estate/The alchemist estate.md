I. THE CORE AESTHETIC

Mood

Warm indie-acoustic with gritty edges, intimate vocals, poetic realism, alchemical metaphors woven into everyday London life.

Sound

Acoustic guitar (weathered, fingerpicking + light body percussion)

Sparse electric guitar (clean, tremolo, shimmering)

Warm bass (upright or soft electric)

Occasional harmonium, glockenspiel, or toy piano for “magic” moments

Field ambience (very subtle): stairwell echo, rain on windows, a bus in the distance

Cross-rhythmic or heartbeat percussion (hand drums / stomp / brushes)


Vocal approach

Close, storytelling, almost whispered at times

Rhythmic conversational delivery in some verses

Light, drifting melodies in choruses


Overall tone

Softly transformative, street-poetic, warm, familial, reflective, sprinkled with alchemical symbolism.

TRACKS

1. Verdant close

First principles.
Here by the wide river (themes), behind curtains hiding the morning sun, you let me see you.
You show me - here is "home", boredom, cheap moments into meaning.
We find us in all this, youth astute acute, a cute little laugh.

2. Tick, tocc goes the clock

Family warmth.
The magic of simple things: chopping onions, steam on the window, warmth as protection. We do stuff together, I get to know you now - our philosophies intertwine.
Uk slang sweet and full of banter between us. Small family drama with mum etc

3. “The Tannoy Oracle”

A distorted station announcement on the Tube that feels like it speaks directly to you.
A moment of accidental prophecy, accidental claroty
Style: indie mandolin and zitar beat.

4. “A followed of wyd”

What do I mean by wydd?
(Arcane Ascension" is a fantasy book series by Andrew Rowe about the magical world of a colossal tower called the Serpent Spire. The acronym "wyd" can refer to "what you're doing," but in this context, it most likely refers to "Wyddfolk," individuals whose gender expression changes based on their feelings.)
There is some more information here:
https://arcane-ascension.fandom.com/wiki/Wydd

So this songs is still in the same themes and narrative as the earlier songs in so much that it's Tima's mother and me when we meet etc, our time in London.

This song in thinking is more on us together with her friends. Struggles with personality, dissociation but warmly homly and banter playing in, non narrative but perhaps small moments around London in the summer, festivals, Cornwall seaside trips.

A swirling, 3/4 rhythm track capturing the floating feeling of being present and absent.
I see you shift, the many in the one. Banter of us the good and the hard and the obscured

5. "Pale blue dot"
Pregnancy as a small cosmic body orbiting your life, unreal and glowing.
The uncanny idea that the unborn child is dreaming you into being responsible.

6. “Infinite argument”
Playful fight about whether infinity exists; secretly about fear of the future.
The idea that you and her exist in multiple realities — some where things work, some where they don’t.

7. "A Solipsists science"
Two people believing the world might be imaginary except for each other.
A play just for us?

8. “Ontology of Us”
A tongue-in-cheek but sincere questioning of what “we” even means.

9. “Southbound, Somehow”
A track about not knowing your destination but moving anyway.
Pregnancy lingering in the atmosphere without direct mention.

10. “Catastrophe and Cwtch”
A track about hugging to stop yourselves spiralling.
Warm, soft, a mix of Welshness and youthful fear.

11. “Chippy Tea Diplomacy”
You two negotiating life decisions over chips and curry sauce.
The taste of compromise, comfort, and cheap solutions.
A belly soft and full of kicks and conundrums 

12. "Seren Fach".
Literally meaning "little star" in Welsh.
Then she came,
our unknown little variable,
our Tima.
Welcome home daffodil.
