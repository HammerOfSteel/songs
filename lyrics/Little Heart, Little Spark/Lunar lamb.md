# Lunar lamb

**Labels:** Little Heart, Little Spark
**Created:** 2025-06-18
**Edited:** 2025-06-18

---

Mary had a little lamb,
I see her still in dreams.
She left her wool along the stars,
And slipped between the beams.

Mary had a little laugh,
Lightest thing I ever caught.
Followed me through morning streets,
Like joy forgot it could get lost.

You wore your jumper 'til it frayed,
The sleeves all chewed, the stitches played.
I never feared the dusk back then —
Just wool and stars, and home again.

Now I see you on the street,
In strangers’ curls, in tiny feet.
That kid in Lidl, lookin’ down —
My heart got caught, then fell around.

You’re in the buses, park bench swings,
In mums with messy bags of things.
I smile and ache — I change my tune,
You’re tucked among the lambs on the moon.

Mary had a little lamb,
Her fleece was white as sleep.
She wandered up the evening clouds,
And vanished in the deep.

And every place that Mary went,
The lamb would tag along —
But now she moves through pixel skies,
Inside this fading song.

Pixel sky, wool dreams,
I rewind old moon beams.
Your laugh comes soft in glitchy tones,
Between the static xylophones.

No bleat to call, no track to trace,
Just silver dust and satellite grace.
I still hum low, I sing, I swoon —
To hold you once more on the moon.

You're not gone — just far and kind,
A flicker in the twilight mind.
If grief’s a song, then I’m its tape,
Loopin’ back to when we’d scrape

Knees on paths and eat the air,
Dance in jumpers full of care.
They say don't reach — don't chase too soon,
But my lamb still plays upon the moon.

Mary had a little lamb,
I whisper her by name.
She rides the wind, she curls the night,
And answers not the same.

Yet every time the silence drops,
And all the stars are strewn —
I swear I feel a tug of wool,
My lamb upon the moon.

Pixel sky… wool dreams…
I rewind… I rewind…
Mary had… Mary had…
…a little… light…
