# Letters from the Ebbw

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-11-21
**Edited:** 2024-11-22

---

The kettle hums, the morning’s bright,
The hills of Risca wrapped in light.
Peter waits by the garden gate,
"Come along now, let’s not be late!"

"What shall we find today, my dear?"

"Secrets, Papa, where the rivers steer!"

"The Ebbw calls, its path runs wide,
With stories hidden in its tide."

The river hums, its waters glide,
A mirror of clouds on the mountainside.
She tosses a leaf, she skips ahead,
Her laughter rising where sunlight spreads.

"Papa, do rivers keep what they know?"

"They carry their secrets where they flow."

"Each bend and curve, each rock and tree,
Holds whispers of lives that used to be."

Letters from the Ebbw, soft and clear,
Its waters sing to those who hear.
Through winding paths and skies of gray,
Its voice remains where memories stay.

By the riverbank, she finds a stone,
Its surface smooth, like silver shone.
"Look, Papa, a treasure to keep!"
"A gift from the river, its stories deep."

She watches the ripples dance and play,
Her tiny hands trace where shadows sway.
"Do you think the fish can hear me call?"
"Perhaps they do—they’ve seen it all."


The park is alive with stalls and cheer,
The scent of pies and cider near.
She twirls around with her newfound prize,
A painted pebble, her joyful surprise.


"Did your mother bring you here before?"

"She loved the market, its open door."

Tima pauses, her gaze sincere,
"Papa, did she see this river here?"

Letters from the Ebbw, soft and true,
Its voice reflects the lives it knew.
Through winding streams and skies of gray,
It carries love in its gentle sway.

As the day grows soft, the river slows,
Its whispers rise where the cool breeze blows.
Peter hums an old refrain,

"She would have loved this quiet place,
The way the river mirrors her grace."

"Papa, is she here in the water’s song?"
"She’s everywhere, my love, where hearts belong."


The sun dips low, the town grows still,
The lanterns glow on the distant hill.
She holds her pebble, her treasure found,
Her tiny steps make a gentle sound.

"Papa, can we come back again?"
"Always, my love, through sun and rain."
Peter smiles as they head away,
"The river keeps the words we say."


Letters from the Ebbw, soft and kind,
Its voice connects both heart and mind.
Through rivers deep and skies of gray,
Its gentle flow will guide our way.

The fire burns low, the night grows near,
The river’s song still in her ear.
Her eyes grow heavy, her dreams take flight,
Of rivers flowing in the quiet night.


"Letters from the Ebbw, her gift to you,
A love so pure, so strong, so true."
