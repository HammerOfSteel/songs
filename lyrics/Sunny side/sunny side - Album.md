# sunny side - Album

**Labels:** Sunny side
**Created:** 2025-12-15
**Edited:** 2025-12-21

---

Sunny Side is a deeply personal, narrative-driven album about choosing to stay alive in a world that often feels unbearable. It documents moments of dissociation, trauma, belief, love, and recovery through soft, organic instrumentation and distinctly British storytelling.

The album balances darkness with warmth—heavy subject matter carried by gentle sounds, poetic language, and human connection. Across its songs, Sunny Side explores the people, memories, and beliefs that quietly keep us here. It is not an album about having answers, but about finding reasons to keep going.

Musically, the project blends UK indie, acoustic textures, spoken word, and subtle genre crossovers, guided by a female British vocal presence that feels intimate, vulnerable, and honest.

1. “All These People”
Description
“All These People” is a confessional song about standing at a breaking point and slowly realizing how many lives are connected to your own. It unfolds as a series of emotional contradictions—love, anger, regret, and longing—before arriving at a moment of clarity.
The song captures the shift from isolation to connection, from finality to hesitation. Its core is the realization that survival can come from remembering who would care, and from allowing yourself to ask for help.

2. “Natural High”
Description
“Natural High” is a coming-of-age love story told through metaphor and surreal imagery. What begins as enchantment and trust slowly reveals emotional imbalance and loss of control. The song explores the intoxication of belief—how love, imagination, and youth can feel magical, even when they are fragile or fleeting.
It leaves the listener suspended between wonder and disillusionment, holding onto the ache of something beautiful that was never fully understood.

'Not too further' he whispered, in that calm reassuring voice, 'A little be longer' he continued 'Then you can take off the blindfold.'

He never usually takes me on wild adventures, normally we just sit around in his garden chatting about, well, everything. Our last major chat was about fairies, he's convinced they exist even though i had told him many times before they are just pretend, make believe, and to believe in them was, well, just plain stupid for a 16 year old boy.

He suddenly stopped and i fell foward into his arms, he held me for a few minutes, allowing me to get ajusted to the new environment. The air tasted sweet, it didn't feel dark out anymore. He smelt gorgeous, my face was nuzzled in his neck and i didn't care about where we were, i was just so happy he wanted me there with him.

After a few moments he started pulling away from the hug. I had completely forgotten that i was wearing a blindfold, my other senses were being overloaded in this magickal place that i completely forgot that i couldn't see it.  He grabbed my hand again, then the other, and even though i was unable to see, i felt his eyes gaze into mine, his piercing blue eyes, staring directly into my soul.

Description
“All I Did” confronts the aftermath of trauma and the impossibility of forgetting. The song lives in the tension between being told it wasn’t your fault and believing deeply that it was. It reflects the loneliness of memory when everyone else has moved on.
This is a song about being changed by something irreversible, and about holding onto the truth of what happened as a way of protecting yourself from it ever happening again.

Description
This song is a promise. Written from a father to his daughter after the loss of her mother, it is about continuing forward together. Sung in Hebrew, it stands as the album’s most grounding and hopeful moment—love expressed simply, honestly, and without performance.
It reframes survival not as endurance, but as devotion.

Inspiration

Style

Raw, intimate ukulele; acoustic finger-picking, warm and unpolished. Sung in Hebrew for his daughter, Temima.

Themes
