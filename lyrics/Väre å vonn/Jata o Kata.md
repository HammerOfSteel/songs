# Jata o Kata

**Labels:** Väre å vonn
**Created:** 2025-06-05
**Edited:** 2025-06-05

---

Long, long 'go by the lake so wide,
Two trolls stood watchin’ the tide.
Jata an’ Kata, big ears, big boots,
Both stirrin’ pots full of herbs and roots.

They boiled and they bubbled, weeks on end,
Mumblin’ spells they could half pretend.
Didn’t know what they’d end up with,
Just stirred and hoped — with a trollish myth.

Then one night, sky turned odd,
Stars held breath, even owls gave nod.
From Kata’s pot came a groan, then wail —
A screechin’ howl like a twisted gale!

Then BANG! — smoke flew, all sparks and steam,
And out jumped a beast like nothin’ they’d dreamed.
Black snake body, a cat’s mad face,
Leapt from the pot and dashed into space —

Or rather, the lake — splash, gone clean,
Since then, that monster’s never been seen.
But folks still say on the right kinda night,
You’ll spot its tail in the shimmerin’ light.

Me and Mum packed bags, left town for the sun,
Storsjön glitterin’ — summer just begun.
No shoes, shorts up, hair in knots,
Chasin’ midges through nettle spots.
Sat on the jetty with a jam jar net,
Tryna catch somethin’ — ain’t caught nothin’ yet.
Then I saw 'em — two big heads pokin’ out,
Weren’t frogs, weren’t folk —
they were older than doubt.
Jata had moss in her braid like a crown,
Kata wore a coat made of roots hangin’ down.
Said “Oi, you!” and I nearly did bolt,
But they laughed like a kettle and passed me a jolt:
A drink in a thimble that tickled my lips —
“Come see the monster, and mind where it drips.”

We slid through reeds like butter on toast,
Kata said “watch out — that’s where Birger haunts most.”
Showed me where the snake of the lake goes sleepin’,
Deep down dreamin’, still breathin’, still creepin’.
Jata made jam from berries and fog,
Fed it to ducks and a grumpy old frog.
Told me stories with bits that changed —
Every retell got weirder arranged.
We sang to the moon with a fishbone harp,
Built a raft from shoestring and bark.
I asked “are you real?” Kata winked like this:
“If you gotta ask, love — you already missed.”

Fast on feet, runnin’ by the shore,
Whispers in the cattails, secrets evermore.
Laughin’ like thunder, dancin’ through the spray,
Come back next summer, we might not stay.

Days went quick, humming electric wire,
Jumpin’ logs, stealin’ time, never got tired.
They showed me where the tree frogs sleep,
Where the stones hold heat and the water runs deep.
We met the shadow with glowing teeth,
He only comes out when the wind goes east.
We made crowns from sticks and ribbon and glue,
Kata said “Queen of the Meadow, that’s you.”
Birger floated by in a fog like lace,
Jata just burped and pulled a face.
Nights were long, stars real near,
Stories stickin’ in the backs of my ears.

One dawn I woke, trolls nowhere,
Boathouse creaked, but the dock was bare.
Left a note rolled up in birch bark curls:
“Back when the ice melts, girl of this world.”
I ran to the rock with the jam jar lid,
Felt a song in the moss where I once hid.
Now when I visit, I hum real low —
And the lake gives a ripple, just so I know.
They’re still there, in the glint and the hush,
In the whoosh of wind and the cranberry brush.
Every summer’s a maybe, but I’ve got the map —
Drawn on my heart and under my cap.

Trollin ol' trolls, voices in the trees,
Skippin’ through the echoes, laughin’ on the breeze.
Swear I see their shadows, every time I play,
Come back next summer, if you find the way.
