Söndag - Första Maj

Vintern rasat ut bland våra fjällar
Drivans blommor smälta ner och dö
Himlen ler i vårens ljusa kvällar
Solen kysser liv i skog och sjö

Toast pops up like it’s preaching again,
butter melts like it knows where I’ve been.
The jam don’t judge, but it always stains —
my thoughts, my sleeves, my windowpanes.
The kettle hums hymns, soft boiler psalms,
Nan's old mug sings with shaky calm.
The telly’s low — reruns of things
that end with laughs and wedding rings.
Dad says, “That’s life.” I say, “Is it telly?”
He just smirks and stirs the welly.

Sunday hums in slippers and steam,
drags me back through honey and grief.
Quiet don’t mean peace, but I wear it still —
wrap my silence in daffodil.

Walks to nowhere, the pavement knows,
which bit sinks and where the dog rose grows.
The corner shop shut, but it still smells like toasties —
I name clouds ‘Mum,’ ‘Dad,’ ‘mostly.’
We don’t do sermons, but I whisper things
to the biscuit tin like it’s listening.
I once told the fridge about school and that —
it answered back with a hum and a crack.
And the people who say “I’m here if you need” —
never once asked, “What do you read?”

Ja, jag kommer! 
Hälsen, glada vindar
Ut till landet, ut till fåglarne
Att jag älskar dem, till björk och lindar
Sjö och berg, jag vill dem återse

Sunday hums in damp hair and toast,
grief on the steam, and tea like a ghost.
Silence ain’t empty — it's what stays behind,
like shadows of prayers the light can’t find.

Each shoe on the rack got a memory inside,
each spoon in the drawer’s seen a tear that dried.
Every sigh’s a sermon, every sniff’s a bell —
every hug is a hope we forgot how to spell.

Se dem än som i min barndoms stunder
Följa bäckens dans till klarnad sjö
Trastens sång i furuskogens lunder
Vattenfågelns lek kring fjärd och ö
