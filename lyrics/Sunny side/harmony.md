# harmony

I was sitting on the edge of the mattress,
Three A.M. on a Tuesday, in the pitch black.
The flat was silent, except for the humming of the fridge,
And I was standing on the precipice, standing on the bridge. 

Then the air shifted, the temperature changed,
Something in this story got re-arranged.

something came over me, drifting through the wall,
She didn’t look scary, not at all.
She had a bright white aura,
a shining kind of ghost,
Like that someone, you just miss the most.

And she brought a sense of harmony,
a quiet sort of sound,
A circle completing, coming back around.

another one followed,
just as bright as the first,
in the still of the room half emersed.

They looked so peaceful,
floating in the gloom,
Like they’d lived a whole life in this tiny,
rented room.

And the beauty of it hit me, Like a punch to the gut.
I was holding something sharp, I was ready for the cut.
But looking at the light,
And the kindness in her face,
I felt a strange nostalgia,
In this unfamiliar place. 

started to cry,
And the metal hit the floor.
I dropped the blade I was holding,
I didn’t want...
I didn't want this..
anymore..

I forgot I wanted to go,
before we even said hello,
I just wanted to know,
Why she had my eyes.

And she told me not to worry, 
You don’t have to speak."
She said that I’d be okay,
Even if I’m weak.

Somehow I believe her,
Though I don’t know who she was,
A trick of the light, Or a fracture in the laws.
Between the dark and the grey,
a pale substance,
a smudge,
just a try.
enought right now,
just to get by.

I didn't know what to say,
so I didn't say a word,
It felt a bit ridiculous,
it felt a bit absurd.

But I watched her very closely as she sat upon the bed,
Pulling out the tangled knots inside my messy head.
She traced the line of my jaw with her thumb,
Like she was checking exactly where she had come from.

And she had this little freckle,
just above her lip,
The same one I have, strange little freaudian slip.

She told me to forget about the lies I tell myself,
To put the heavy baggage back upon the shelf.
She looked at me with patience,
like she’d waited years to see,
The moment that I chose to stay,
and let her come to me.

I may still not yet know,
What I need to find.
I’m still a bit broken,
I’m still in a bind.

But she visited me late last night,
Proof of the promise I keep.
And she held me whilst I cried,
Until we fell asleep.

She looked just like...
like harmony.
