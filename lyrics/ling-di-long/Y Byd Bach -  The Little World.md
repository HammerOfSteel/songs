# Y Byd Bach -  The Little World

**Labels:** ling-di-long
**Created:** 2025-04-10
**Edited:** 2025-04-11

---

“You want ketchup and beans?” Mam says with a grin
“With toast in triangles,” Tiima cuts in
The radio murmurs, we sip and we share
Your sleeve’s dipped in, not a care

“Don’t run with your hashbrowns,” you whisper through tea
She hums in her seat, swings her legs, counts to three
Then off to daycare, a blur in pink
“There’s Eira!” she squeals, gone in a blink

We creak through the gate, the garden asleep
“You rest,” Mam says, “I’ll wash and sweep”
But I find her outside, bare feet on the stone
Humming to seedlings like they’re her own

“Did you see how she ran?” I ask with a smile
“She’s got your elbows,” you tease after a while
The washing’s up, your hair smells like spring
The world feels smaller, and full of everything

The door slams. “Guess what I made!”
“Is it soup again?”
“Nope — it’s glitter and tape!”
Tiima hugs Mam like she’s never let go
You kiss her curls — “My girl, you glow.”

The flat smells like garlic and cawl in the pot
Tiima spins stories that twist into knots
Uncle Peter comes by, drops fruit and a smile
“She’s still got that wild in her, been there a while”

You hand him a plate, he hands you a song
The telly’s low hum carries us along
You sit with your head tucked under my chin
And I think: if this ends, start me over again

Three of us curled on the secondhand chair
Her head on Mam’s chest, your hand in my hair
The moon paints the window, the clock ticks soft
Y byd bach — our world aloft
