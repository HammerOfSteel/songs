# Brave as a Brontosaurus

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-11-18
**Edited:** 2025-05-17

---

The morning hums with soft sunlight,
Her tiny shoes laced up just right.
Her backpack swings as she takes my hand,
A brave little roar in a brand-new land.

"Are you ready, love, for this big, bold day?"
She grips my hand, but turns away.

"What if they don’t know my name, Papa?"
"They’ll know it soon, my little star."

The doors swing wide, the room feels tall,
She steps inside, her world grows small.
With wide blue eyes, she scans the space,
The buzz of kids, the smiling face.

"Hello there, love, what’s your name?"

"I’m Tima..." She says with a small growing flame

"You’re brave as a Brontosaurus, my dear,
Take your first step—I’ll always be near."

Brave as a Brontosaurus, tall and kind,
With a gentle heart and a curious mind.
Through new frontiers, through skies unknown,
You’ll find your way, you’re not alone.

The teacher bends with a gentle grin,
"Come meet the others, let’s step right in."
She clutches her bear, her courage small,
But the laughter rises beyond the hall.

In the corner, a girl stands near,
With freckles bright and a smile sincere.

"Do you like dinosaurs? I think they’re cool!"

"Brontosaurus is my favorite, the rule!"

The blocks stack high, the crayons glide,
Her shyness fades, her joy can’t hide.
She shares her bear, her laughter grows,
A spark of friendship, a bond that shows.

"That’s my girl, so strong, so true,
She’s finding her world, she’s pushing through."

Brave as a Brontosaurus, strong and free,
With a heart so bright, for all to see.
Through gentle steps and new-found play,
Her courage grows with each new day.

The sun dips low as the day unwinds,
A first adventure, her heart aligns.
She runs to me with a beaming grin,
"Papa, I was brave, I fit right in!"

"I knew you would, my fearless roar,
You’ll conquer worlds and so much more."

She waves goodbye to her brand-new friend,
"See you tomorrow!" their voices blend.
The teacher smiles as she takes my hand,
"She’s special, you know—she’ll understand."

I hold her close as we walk back home,
Her tiny steps, her world has grown.

"Did it feel big, love, like a mountain tall?"

"It felt big at first, but not at all!"

Brave as a Brontosaurus, bold and true,
With a heart so strong in all you do.
Through every step, through skies unknown,
You’ll find your way, you’re not alone.

The stars peek out, the night feels clear,
Her tiny voice whispers near.
"Papa, will I be brave tomorrow too?"
"You’ll be braver still, it’s all in you."

She holds her bear as her eyelids close,
Dreaming of blocks and dinosaur shows.
Her courage grows as the night unwinds,
A little explorer, with a fearless mind.

The night hums soft, the stars are near,
A tiny roar I’ll always hear.
Her courage shines, her heart explores,
Forever brave, my Brontosaurus.
