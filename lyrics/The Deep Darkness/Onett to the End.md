# Onett to the End

**Labels:** The Deep Darkness
**Created:** 2025-04-18
**Edited:** 2025-04-18

---

We climbed the hill where it all began,
Flashlight shaking in my hand.
The grass was wet, the sky turned red,
Buzz Buzz said, “Go.” We ran instead.

There’s moss on the crater now —
Tiny flowers growing out somehow.
It’s quieter than it used to be.
But the stars still hide behind that same old tree.

From Onett to the end, I walked the map of me,
In every crack of sidewalk, in every broken key.
It never told the full story — but it showed me how to bend,
And I carry it, I carry it,
From Onett to the end.

The arcade’s still got the same warped glass,
And the Sharks still tagged the back wall fast.
I fought with fists, I fought with fear —
The quarter machine gave me something near.

The library smells like summer sweat,
Maps and pencils, lessons we forget.
I saw Paula once on the steps out front —
She said, “Still think about it more than I want.”

The mailbox leans, the porch light hums,
Same spaghetti, still overdone.
The bed creaks soft like it always did,
And I still sleep like I’m a scared kid.

Found a cookie in a trash can near the park,
And smiled so wide it lit up dark.
We lived on luck and leftovers then —
And I’d still eat hope like that again.

She said, “You know, it never really leaves,”
“Even when we walk on other streets.”
“It’s stitched into the places we don’t show —”
“The part that grew before we knew.”

From Onett to the end, I walked the map of me,
Through phone lines, old bikes, and family recipes.
It never got easy, it never made sense —
But every broken fence was still defense.
And I carry it, I carry it,
From Onett to the end.

Paula sat on the hill again, not saying much.
Just watched the wind carry leaves like thoughts.

I think that’s what home is.

The part you never really leave.
Even when you do.
