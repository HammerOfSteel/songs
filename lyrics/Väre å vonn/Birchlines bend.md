# Birchlines bend

**Labels:** Väre å vonn
**Created:** 2025-06-05
**Edited:** 2025-06-05

---

Oi — it’s still here, innit.
Same spot, same stone.
The bark's rougher now, but it knows.
She knows.

Planted this tree when I barely had shoes,
Mum said “dig deep” — I just wanted the views.
Wrapped that root like it were gold,
Didn’t know I was givin’ her somethin' to hold.
Wrote secrets in chalk on the flat grey stone,
Said I'd marry Jamie — then scratched it alone.
Lost teeth under her in a paper cup,
Got stung by nettles — still didn’t give up.
Birch bark peels like pages we keep,
Tree don’t sleep — she just stands there deep.

Birch up high where the meadow leans,
Seen my tears and summer dreams.
Stone below where the soft grass bends,
Knows my lies and old dead friends.

Used to dance round her in Dad’s big coat,
Pretendin' I was queen on a rowboat float.
Told that tree she’s the smartest around,
Said "if you blink once, I won’t make a sound."
Wrote poems in mud, drew swirls on bark,
Left my sandwich once, came back — full of ants.
Swore I’d run away — brought crisps and socks,
Cried in her shade when they laughed at my docs.
Tree don’t judge, she just hums real low,
Like wind got stuck and forgot where to go.

Birch on the hill with the sky so wide,
Knows where all my ghosts reside.
Stone’s still warm from years of knees,
Heard my wishes, caught my pleas.

Last spring I came with nothin’ to say,
Just sat and breathed the frost away.
Pulled my scarf tight, kicked that stone,
Said “I’m grown now — but not quite grown.”
Left a letter folded in a jar,
Buried it shallow, not too far.
I said thanks. Just like that.
For standin’ still when the world went flat.
Birch don’t cry — but she don’t forget,
Rooted deep in my alphabet.

Birch up, white and thin,
Knows where all my songs begin.
Stone below, still cold, still kind —
Place I leave what I can’t find.

Alright then…
I’ll come back next thaw.
You better be here.
(…I know you will be.)
