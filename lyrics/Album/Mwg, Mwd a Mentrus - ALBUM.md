# Mwg, Mwd a Mentrus - ALBUM

**Labels:** Album, Mwg, Mwd a Mentrus
**Created:** 2025-05-03
**Edited:** 2025-07-10

---

1. “Mae Mam yn Siŵr Bod E’n Dylluan” (Mam’s Convinced He’s an Owl)
Location: Countryside at dusk
Vibe: Folklore meets nosy parenting. Mam thinks the new boyfriend’s suspiciously quiet.
Theme: Paranoia, love, and the supernatural all in one hilarious bundle.
Acoustic guitar, quirky bassoon, subtle beatboxing, owl-call samples. Folk-hop vibe with dry humour and mystery.
DONE

2. “Cysgodion Cwm Llwch” (Shadows of Cwm Llwch)
Location: Cwm Llwch (near Pen y Fan)
Lore: The enchanted lake said to be a gateway to the Otherworld.
Theme: Lingering between grief and longing; a soul lost between worlds.
Sparse harp, droning harmonium, lo-fi ambient textures, spoken word overlay. Feels like mist and memory in sound.

3. “Pan Fues i'n Dywysoges Bws Caerdydd” (When I Was a Cardiff Bus Princess)
Location: Cardiff
Vibe: A teenage girl daydreams she's royalty while riding the Number 8.
Theme: Youthful delusions of grandeur, humor, and real pride in being local.
Synth pop meets acoustic busker loop; ukulele, cheap Casio keyboard chords, soft vocal layers with a grime beat.

4. “Y Drych yn Ffynnon Fair” (The Mirror in St. Mary’s Well)
Location: Ffynnon Fair, Llanfair
Lore: Wells that once showed visions to those seeking answers.
Theme: A woman sees glimpses of her younger self and her lost love.
Glass harmonica, cello, whispered harmony vocals, and slow trip-hop beat. Reflective, intimate, slightly haunted.

5. “Y Wiwer yn y Garden Centre” (The Squirrel at the Garden Centre)
Location: Carmarthen or anywhere semi-rural
Vibe: Spoken word banter between two pensioners debating the squirrel conspiracy.
Theme: Warmth, local color, and the fine art of small-town gossip.
Accordion, upright bass, brushed drums, and banter-style vocals like pub storytelling. Bit of klezmer bounce.

6. “Dan y Rhosyn Gwyn” (Beneath the White Rose)
Location: a secret graveyard at Llangollen
Lore: Love, said to bloom white roses.
Theme: sorrow wrapped in cute honey-sweet beauty.
Cute, sweet, scary-cute mid tempo, modern pads, Fingerpicked acoustic, muted trumpet, and bowed glass.

7. “Ffansi Ti? Ffansi Te.” (Fancy You? Fancy Tea.)
Location: Kitchen, texting back and forth
Vibe: Flirty and full of clever wordplay. Bit of S4C rom-com energy.
Theme: Courtship Welsh-style—through innuendo, tea offers, and weather commentary.
Tea-kettle samples, soft lo-fi beat, nylon guitar, vintage synth keys. Cozy romantic pop with dry wit and charm.

8. “Trwy Ddrysau Llangernyw” (Through the Doors of Llangernyw)
Location: Llangernyw Yew (over 4,000 years old)
Lore: Believed to be a meeting point between the living and the dead.
Theme: Speaking to someone gone, under the yew, not to forget—but to remember rightly.
Dark folk—crwth, slow bass pulse, whispered vocals, distant thunder loop. Feels like walking in an old churchyard.

9. “Crocs Coch ar Ddydd Sadwrn” (Red Crocs on a Saturday)
Location: B&Q car park
Vibe: Defiantly fabulous. Possibly trap beat under harp loop.
Theme: Expressing yourself loudly in places nobody asked you to.
Trap beats, harp riff, distorted harmonica, vocal layering. Wildly confident, a strut through a car park in style.

10. “Y Ddawns Olaf yn Aberffraw” (The Last Dance in Aberffraw)
Location: Aberffraw
Lore: Once a royal seat, now a quiet seaside town.
Theme: Royalty faded, stories danced into seafoam.
Melancholy piano, soft whistle, brushes on snare, subtle reverb. Feels like a ballroom dream fading into sea mist.

11. “Y Ddraig Ddim yn Tanio” (The Dragon’s Out of Puff)
Location: Pub open mic
Vibe: Acoustic + comic; sung from the POV of a dragon who’s retired to Tenby
Theme: Burnout, laughter, and finding peace in obscurity.
Pub-style singalong with banjo, detuned piano, kazoo interlude, and honest vocals. Comedy wrapped in warmth.
