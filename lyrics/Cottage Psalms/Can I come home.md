# Can I come home.

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-04-29
**Edited:** 2025-10-08

---

The days hum, hum and drone,
like the kettle left on when you're out, alone.
Ffenestri blink, pretending to see,
another Tuesday when I look at me.
World’s a waxwork, looks right, feels wrong,
plastic trees, plastic smile, plastic song.

"Life’s for the living," they say with a pint,
"Crack on, son," with a clink and a wink.
Your name, little star, lost on the shelf,
no one dares whisper it, not even to myself.
How are you today, silver script,
"Alright yeah, you?", cups half-sipped.

Maybe you knew her?
Or don't want to at all.
"That's alright" stumble and stall.
Better off, maybe, line of salt.
Chipped and cracked in nooks and fault.

So let me come home, love,
maybe not today, push come to shove.
Maybe tomorrow, maybe next week,
when the buzz of the streets doesn’t sound so bleak.

SSRI solutions on sale down the aisle,
cheers mate, chuck me a proper smile.
Pat me down with a word or two,
stitch up the cracks with a "mustn’t dwell, you."
Their kids grow up while yours stays small,
in a pocket stitched somewhere behind it all.
And I grin at their birthdays, their football games,
holding the echoes of unheard names.

Hug me, love, where the sky turns thin,
twinkle twinkle, let me in.
It's all a bit mad, isn't it?
Dancing the same two-step round the pit.
Grit in the lungs, smile on the face,
chippy tea, blue eyed, keeping pace.

So let me come home, love,
maybe not today, push come to shove.
Maybe tomorrow, maybe next week,
when the buzz of the streets doesn’t sound so bleak.

Hug me close when I lose the thread,
wrap me in the linen of the dead.
Your mam waits by the oaken door,
and Nain and Taid, and all before.
Through the silent halls of here I roam,
asking only,
can I come home?
