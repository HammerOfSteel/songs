# A Kettle’s to Care

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-04-03
**Edited:** 2025-04-03

---

Gud som haver barnen kär,
se till mig som liten är.
Vart jag mig i världen vänder,
står min lycka i Guds händer.
Lyckan kommer, lyckan går,
du förbliver, Fader vår.

She’s curled like spring in the crook of my side,
hair in her mouth, little sighs as she hides.
Dreaming, maybe, of dogs or moons,
I don’t move. Just breathe and let the room bloom.
Kettle hums in the other space,
like grief knows how to set the pace.
I blink once. The light is low,
blanket tucked like falling snow.
She stirs—smile blooming slow—
eyes half open, voice below:

“Is it morning?”

“Nearly,” I say.
She snuggles in.
And I let it stay.

A kettle’s to care,
not just to boil.
It sings for the living
and hums for the soil.
It’s grief in the quiet,
love on repeat—
something to rise to,
something to heat.

Breakfast smells like cinnamon bread,
she insists on butter to the edge instead.
Orange juice spill, we laugh, we fix—
she makes up songs with perfect mix.
Mittens by the door, boots too big,
she hops like frost is some kind of gig.
Outside the air is sharp and kind,
sky like slate with a silver line.
We race past trees that lost their coats,
she names each bird with made-up notes.
And in her voice, I hear a ghost—
not haunting—
but memory’s toast.

I carry her in the way I pause—
before I speak,
before I pour.
In each small moment,
grief is care.

A kettle’s to care,
when no one can see.
It hums before light,
for the you that is me.
It’s hands in her hair,
it’s tea in a cup—
a song without chorus,
that still shows up.

We find a café with a fogged-up pane,
she draws hearts and clouds and writes my name.
Hot cocoa, crumbs, she counts the chairs,
asks if birds still live in pairs.
I nod, and wonder what I used to ask,
before the years wore off the mask.
Later we swim in the local pool,
she splashes like joy broke all the rules.
And when her eyes catch mine mid-play,
I see both of them,
clear as day.
She swims like stars know where she’s been,
and I breathe slow,
just take it in.

"Look and me, I'll dive like a seal"

She dives in her glasses tinted teal.

A kettle’s to care,
when words don’t land.
It hums your name
when there’s no hand.
It sings through sleep,
through snow and spark—
a morning song
in a room gone dark.

She falls asleep with a hoppy tucked tight,
book still open, lamp too bright.
I turn the page, though she won’t see—
just one more for the kid in me.
Room goes hush, the heater ticks,
I clean the cups, wipe orange stick.
And in the quiet, I pour one more—
not for me,
but the life before.
I sip, I sit, the clock don’t mind,
and neither do I.
Time’s not kind—
but it’s not cruel.
It just keeps.
Like Boe,
like dreams,
like sleep.

Every whistle
has a place it returns to.
Even if
it sings alone.

Alla barn som lider nöd,
hem, där far, där mor är död,
seglare på stormigt vatten,
vandrare i mörka natten,
käre gode Fader vår,
trösta den som sorgsen går.
