# Natural High

**Labels:** Sunny side
**Created:** 2025-12-16
**Edited:** 2025-12-18

---

He said, "Do you trust me?" 

I said, "Don't be a weirdo." 

He said, "Just put the blindfold on."
Mental.

We were sitting in his garden on the plastic chairs,
Talking about the nothingness and who really cares,
About the GCSEs and the state of the nation,
Standard Friday evening dissociation.

He’s going on about the Fae and the lore,
I’m kicking up a storm on the garden floor,

"Mate, you’re sixteen, grow up a bit, 
Fairies are for kids, and your theory is...
well, it's garbage."

But he’s got this look, that intense blue stare,
Sunlight catching the dust in the air,

He says,
"I can prove it,
I can show you the gate,
But you have to be blind to the things that you hate."

So I laughed, nervous,
pulled the scarf from my neck,
Wrapped it round my eyes,
what the heck?

Let him take my hand,
warm and rough,
Thinking,

"Okay, that’s enough, that’s enough."

And suddenly the air tastes like sugar and smoke,
I’m waiting for the punchline, waiting for the joke.
But I’m floating on the pavement, feet off the ground,
It’s a natural high, a terrifying sound.

Of wings in the ether, of birds in the chest,
Put the cynicism down, put the logic to rest.
I don’t know where we are, but I don’t want to leave,
It’s the first time in ages I’ve let myself believe.

He’s guiding me forward,
"Not far, not far,"
I can hear the distant hum of a passing car,

But it sounds like an ocean, it sounds like a beast,
My senses are spiking, rising like yeast.

The smell is inhuman, damp earth and sweet rot,
Standing in a palace that the city forgot.

I stumbled, he caught me, held me so tight,
Nuzzled in his jumper, hiding from the light.

He smelt like washing powder and cheap body spray,
But in the dark, he smelt like the end of the day.
I felt his grip tighten, reassuring and strong,
Then shift into something... slightly wrong.

A panic in the fingers, a twitch in the palm,
shattering the peace, breaking the calm.

I tried to pull back, tried to tear off the band,
But he crushed the bones in my trembling hand.

"Run!" He screamed it,
dragged me down the track,
Stumbling on the concrete, nearly broke my back.
Panting like a dog, heart hitting the ribs,
Running from the monsters, running from the fibs.

Tearing through the magic,
ripping through the veil,
A breathless, frantic, teenage fairytale.

And the natural high came crashing down,
Back to the grey and the black of the town.
He let go of my hand, brushed the hair from my face,
We hadn’t even left the starting place.

My lungs were burning, my head was a mess,
Suspended in the moment of severe distress.
I ripped off the blindfold, blinking in the sun,
Trying to figure out exactly what we’d done.
I looked around.
I was expecting Narnia.
Or a forest.
Or hell.

pray tell,
tell me..
what exacly,
what?
not, anywhere
We hadn't been anywhere.
We hadn't seen anything,
anything at all.

I sat on the curb,
head spinning,
feeling sick.

there has got to be some trick,
trick to this.

"Where were we?"
"where did we go?"
"Why couldn't I see?"

He didn't look at me.
Just stood
kicked a stone into the gutter.
Absolute utter,
natural high,
high magic,
sweet.

so stand there  and I shake,
as if I'm not truely awake.

pinch my arm,
still right here.
so..
just..
take me back there.
