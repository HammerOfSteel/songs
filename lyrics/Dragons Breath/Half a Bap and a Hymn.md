# Half a Bap and a Hymn

**Labels:** Dragons Breath
**Created:** 2025-07-13
**Edited:** 2025-07-13

There's this weight I carry
It's heavy and exhausting
It's beautiful, and quite daring

It yanks me down more times than I can count
Squeezes, punches, and pushes every last nerve
But it's perfect on every account

Cawl breathes slow on the stove, mist in the garden,
Socks too big, hoodie too soft, feet on the mat just pardon,
A silence so wide it could sing, no need to explain the ache,
Half a bap in my hand, hymns in crumbs, sat still by the lake.

Old church stone’s sweatin’ stories, moss grown round the ledge,
Cuppa steams like confessionals, my thoughts perched on the edge.
Bit mental, bit brill, she left her laugh in the pan, cheeky and warm "that's our man".

Bottled rhyme, tick tock, 
slow the rise ol clock.

Step steep, sweep and swoon
Weird wollen jumper,
Steph holds the afternoon.

You gonna count, aye love you're mommies girl. Sweet little pea my perfect pearl.

Half a bap and a hymn,
Mid-morning holy within.
Breathe it in, thyme, sage, half-bit
"How ya been?"
With you, I can't even pretend to keep it in.

Woke up to toys underfoot, like shrines in technicolour,
in time for bells on change, an old school scholar.
Crows on the spire, forgettin’ their chime,
here by the hearth, “Love’s just waitin’ in line.”

Got no psalms left in pockets, just tissues and change,
Still preach in whispers to the plants like the world ain't gone strange.
Bit of mud on the buggy wheel, scribbled fears on a Tesco list,
And all I crave’s someone to say: “aye, I see the twist.”

Half a bap and a hymn,
Mid-morning holy within.
Thats the lull in the din,
ugh...
I forgot to take out the bin.

Canal path’s wild and worn and weeds and stone,
Girls sre asleep and the world’s gone quiet, the creek's in my bone.
That ache in my shoulder, it’s the weight of still tryin’,
To fold the laundry and the past without breakin’ or lyin’
Lying down, without breakin a sweat.
Aye, bet that's the best we'll ever get.

Nappies, hymn books, sweet peas and coughs,
Still argue with her voice when the light switches off.
Might not make sense but it’s sacred as sin,
This holy mess of livin’, a hymn in my tin.

Half a bap and a hymn,
Life’s sermon stitched in skin.
No prayer more thick than kin,
And silence knows where she’s been.

"I said nothin’… but she heard it all.
That’s how it is.
Theology in tea, zen in socks,
Hidden happy heart, hugging a post box.

Days spill like milk on the floor, never quite cleaned right,
But still I sing to the night, her lullabies not in sight.
Brittle breakfast barely bitten,
Cassius callous Chiton.
Worn and torn, by you.
You, little philosopher up on roof,
Gazing at wonderes, looking for proof.

Half a bap and a hymn,
Warm crumbs, the soul’s violin.
Playin a tune to the crackle within,
All the places this heart had been.

For its weight brings me great joy
And the warmth is overwhelming
So I hold tight and hold strong and enjoy

For the terrain will mellow down
And it will not always be this heavy
So this weight I hold with love, and in it I drowne.
