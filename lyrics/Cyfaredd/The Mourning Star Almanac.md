# The Mourning Star Almanac

**Labels:** Cyfaredd
**Created:** 2025-07-23
**Edited:** 2025-07-23

---

Collector’s book not to be read,
Held all heavy, woven in thread.
Scribbled script, inked and only,
In flowers of salt and antimony.

Written only, no tales for bead,
Not for the living, nor for the dead.
Dates of days that never last,
Of future, present and of past.
Only seen in the last straw of ember,
Simply, a book,
To remember.

Chapter One, Form and Sun
Snow soft on the council bins,
First year gone, father do forgive my sins.
Sleds still squeal, it's all wrong,
echo sing of songs long gone.

Scarf still smells of your blessed ghost,
Cold in my throat, miss you the most.
Built a snowman, just left off the grin,
Couldn’t have what should've been.

Tick tick “Time,” tock, clock slow and kind,
brittle grumpy, never you mind.
Mind a mind reverse rewind.
love, so much love, where can it go.
Buffers of stacks in overflow.

pages saintly stitched in ash,
Folded stars, fevered flash.
Weighted feather, bound to bone,
Helpless wholly, quartz and stone
Saturated sulphur spark,
Read it quiet, read it dark.
Mourning star, shaping sun,
Always ever only one.

Chapter Two: Salt and Thread

Once lost, not torn.
battle bruised and slightly worn 

A feather from a street-swept gull
To hear her laugh when skies are dull.

An empty crisp bag folded neat,
To catch her voice where pavements meet.

Half a stone, a button blue,
For holding tight when dreams fall through.

Biscuit edge from Sunday tin,
To guard the door she wandered in.

Chapter Three: Glass and Thread
Dreamt your shoes but you had no feet,
Voice in the hall but the house too neat.
Shouted loud but my throat just stalled,
Woke with a name I never called.

Time don’t heal, it just bends the view,
Like a spoon in glass, like a dance half-true.
Some days I see you, and some I blink,
And your face pours out in the kitchen sink.

I draw you different every time,
In charcoal, chalk, or nursery rhyme.
But memory shifts like tide and foam,
And grief’s just love that can’t go home.

These are pages stitched in ash,
Folded stars, forgotten flash.
Every name, a weight, a spark,
Read it quiet, read it dark.
Mourning star, ink runs cold,
Every chapter never told.

Chapter Four: blue as a bell

Wait all year to see them here,
Hear their chime, in this hear of mine.
Bloom and ring, is summer spring.
Does it matter, if you understand,
It doesn't seem they'll hold my hand.
Does it change over all this time,
The thought of you sweetest child of mine.
Never will it age,
Never will it fray.
These bells of blue with not decay.

Chapter Five: Tidal trail

Salt sleep under stars,
Sea don’t ask and moon don’t tell,
She breathes in my scars.

You may not read every page,
It love or fear or hate and rage.
They remain in here for you,
Always just to let you know:
Fold the light, and tuck it near,
The stars remember,
Even here.
