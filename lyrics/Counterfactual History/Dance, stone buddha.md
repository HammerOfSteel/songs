# Dance, stone buddha

**Labels:** Counterfactual History
**Created:** 2025-04-09
**Edited:** 2025-04-09

---

My tears are like the quiet drift
Of petals from some magic rose;
And all my grief flows from the rift
Of unremembered skies and snows.
A letter in my coat. A child in my care.
And a silence so loud it cracks the air.

It’s my coat, but it don’t sit right
Like it knows it’s holding a fight
Paper tucked where keys should be
No name, no date, just words and me
She’s in the tent, cuddled up small

(Welsh voice, soft) “Cwtsh, Papa,” before her fall
To sleep, I mean—not fate, not night
But still that edge don’t feel quite right
Letter says, “don’t wait too long”
But who the hell wrote this song?

If I listen / if I do
Will the world turn something new?
Or is this just / ofnadwy hope
A thread too thin for me to cope
If I move / if I wake
Will it hold, or will it break?
I don’t know
But she’s still there

We walk to town, the sky feels slow
Shadows fall where sun should go
She points up—“seren bach!”
And laughs like stars are where she walks
I buy her milk, she hums a tune
One I dreamed of under moons
The shopkeep says I look worn out

“Wedi blino,” I just mouth
Can’t explain the ache in time
Can’t forget that warning line

“If the sky begins to turn—trust it.”
I don’t trust much
But her? I must
The stone Buddha dances, then sits still
One breath is enough to break the will

If I listen / if I feel
If this moment might be real
If the wind / whispers true
If the words were meant for who
I think / then maybe she
Might not vanish silently
I don’t know
But I might try

She naps, curled like a soft soft flame
Dreams still bright, no weight, no name
I hold the letter in my lap
Creased from thought, edge torn in half
No signature, no call to arms
Just love wrapped round with quiet harm
I read again, the final line—

“Carry her, when stars align.”
She stirs and whispers, “no night, sun.”
And in her voice, the day’s not done

If I listen / if I choose
If I carry her past bruise
If I zip that tent with light
Will it keep the dark from night?
Maybe dim / but not gone
Maybe just enough to hold on
I don’t know
But I believe her

The fire’s low. My hands feel new
Like they’ve forgotten what not to do
She’s two years old. She holds the thread
Of all the things I’ve left unsaid
If love’s a loop, then let it be
Unbroken now because of me
I press my hand against the zip
Close my eyes. My throat goes thick
One breath in. One held long.
One step out... toward the wronged

Maybe I don’t fix the past
But I won’t let this moment pass
She’s still mine, and I still stand
Letter ghosted—but I hold her hand
