# Cling Clang

**Created:** 2026-02-21

---

## Style
- UK British indie feel (think Hot Hot Heat), warm and close acoustics

## Instrumentation
- Theremin, kalimba, nylon guitar with muted pluck, light upright bass
- Kitchen percussion as rhythm: tin mugs, spoons on worktop, kettle rattle — EQ'd and reverbed as musical glue

---

## Alternative Style + Instrumentation Options
*(swap any of these in for testing in Suno)*

### A — Folksy Lullaby
- **Style:** English folk lullaby, hushed and gentle, fingerpicked, like a bedtime story told by firelight
- **Instrumentation:** fingerpicked acoustic guitar, recorder or tin whistle (very soft), bowed psaltery, quiet hand drum or bodhrán brush, sparse glockenspiel for shimmer
- **Vocals:** warm British male baritone, unhurried, lullaby cadence; child's voice on intro fully unaccompanied

### B — Indie Chamber Pop
- **Style:** British indie chamber pop, think Sufjan Stevens meets Belle & Sebastian, intimate but lush
- **Instrumentation:** nylon guitar, cello (bowed, close-mic'd), glockenspiel, very quiet kick-and-brush kit, upright bass walking, light strings on chorus only
- **Vocals:** British male tenor, conversational verse delivery, opens out on chorus; child's voice intro over a single cello note

### C — Sparse Lo-Fi Singer-Songwriter
- **Style:** lo-fi British singer-songwriter, raw and close, tape warmth, like early Damien Rice or early Badly Drawn Boy
- **Instrumentation:** one acoustic guitar (slightly over-driven mic, room sound), no bass, no percussion except light finger-tap on guitar body, occasional harmonica breath between lines
- **Vocals:** British male, slightly rough-edged, no reverb — voice right in your ear; child's voice intro completely dry

### D — Post-Punk Warmth
- **Style:** warm post-punk with heart, UK indie early-2000s feel, think Elbow or early Starsailor
- **Instrumentation:** electric guitar (clean chorus pedal), upright bass plucked hard, brushed snare, organ swell on the chorus (very low), theremin hovering high in the mix on bridge
- **Vocals:** British male, resonant, full-chest delivery; child's voice intro over a single held organ note that fades in slowly

### E — Stripped Acoustic + Kitchen Sounds Only
- **Style:** domestic minimalism — the kitchen IS the band; raw, strangely joyful
- **Instrumentation:** no conventional instruments at all — all rhythm from kitchen: kettle boil, spoon-tap on mug, wooden board knock, tea-tin shake; nylon guitar one chord per bar only; kalimba for melody
- **Vocals:** British male, warm and half-spoken throughout, easy and close; child's voice intro in total silence apart from distant kettle

## Production Notes
- All vocals close-mic'd, intimate
- Haiku intro: spoken word, no music behind it — just room ambience and one kalimba note at the very end
- Verses and chorus: warm UK British male vocals, accent fully present — not ironed out, not softened
- Theremin enters under the second verse, very low in the mix, like breath
- Upright bass anchors the chorus; kalimba keeps the shimmer throughout
- Bridge: half-spoken, quieter, almost under the breath
- Outro: vocal drops away first, then instruments one by one until only kalimba remains
