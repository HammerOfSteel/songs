# Kindle for a cold god

**Labels:** Cyfaredd
**Created:** 2025-07-30
**Edited:** 2025-07-30

---

Freezing dusk is closing
Like a slow trap of steel
On trees and roads and hills and all
That can no longer feel.
But the carp is in its depth
Like a planet in its heaven.
And the badger in its bedding
Like a loaf in the oven.
And the butterfly in its mummy
Like a viol in its case.
And the owl in its feathers
Like a doll in its lace.

I lit a wick with a button and braid,
Spoke in thyme where her cradle laid.
Ash in my boots and her name on thread,
Offered to frost what I should've said.

She slept in your palm, no dreams to chase,
So I carved new stars in the fireplace.
Winter don't weep, it waits and wills,
But I pour warm milk in the cracks it fills.

Kindle, oh kindle, for the god made of snow,
Trade my breath for her dreaming glow.
Words for warmth, and rhyme for flame,
Keep her near, whisper her name.

Your chapel’s built of frostbit bones,
No hymn, no choir, just frozen stones.
But still I rap, still I rhyme,
Still I strike at the edge of time.

Gave my coat, gave my sleep,
Gave you every cry too deep.
And when your silence cracked that night,
I burned a lullaby into light.

Kindle, oh kindle, you stone-faced lord,
I braid her name in every chord.
I warm your silence, break your throne,
You held her once, she’s not alone.

Kept her mittens, stitched a spell,
Beneath the ice where the foxes dwell.
Offered dandelion, soot, and string,
To wake the god who feels no spring.

Told her tales through chimney smoke,
Cried my rage into an oaken cloak.
But you, old god, don’t blink or bow,
So I’ll love her enough for the both of us now.

Kindle, oh kindle, the frost has grown,
But her laugh still warms my bones.
Take my sleep and keep your throne,
I’ll love her loud. She’s never alone.

I walked the field where her breath once danced,
Spoke her name where the reeds advanced.
The snow don’t know what the springtime saves,
So I plant her dreams in the frozen graves.

Made my peace with the cold divine,
But I cast my words like sparks in pine.
If gods don’t answer, that’s still fine,
I’ll build a fire from every line.

Such a frost
The flimsy moon
Has lost her wits.

A star falls.

The sweating farmers
Turn in their sleep
Like oxen on spits.
