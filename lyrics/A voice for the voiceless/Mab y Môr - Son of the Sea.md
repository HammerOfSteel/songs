# Mab y Môr - Son of the Sea

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-03-06
**Edited:** 2025-03-06

---

A tale of friendship, freedom, and the rhythms of the ocean.

"In a small coastal village, where the waves kissed the shore and the salty breeze carried tales of old, there lived a fisherman's child named Ewan. Curious and brave, Ewan spent his days exploring the rocky beaches and listening to the sea's whispers. One fateful morning, as the mist hung low over the water, he discovered something—or someone—that would change his life forever..."

Ewan wandered near the tide,
Where sea and sand in dance collide.
Upon a rock, a maiden fair,
With ocean eyes and sea-kissed hair.

Oh, sing the song of waves and wind,
Of secrets deep and tales that spin.
The sea, she calls with voice so free,
To hearts that heed her mystery.

Ar lan y môr, cyfarfu'n dau,
Mab y môr a'r forforwyn ddau.
Gyda'i gilydd, dysgon nhw,
Hud y tonnau, cyfrinachau'r lli.

Oh, dance with dolphins, glide with seals,
Feel the magic that the sea reveals.
But know the tides have paths their own,
And freedom's song is theirs alone.

They laughed beneath the sun's warm glow,
And chased the tides' eternal flow.
But as the moon pulled at the sea,
She felt the call to once more be free.

"Dear friend," she whispered soft and low,
"The ocean's depths are where I must go.
But in your heart, I'll leave a light,
To guide you through the darkest night."

Oh, hearts entwined by fate's design,
Bound by love yet free to shine.
For in the ebb and flow of time,
We learn to cherish, then resign.

Gyda dagrau yn ei lygaid,
Gwelodd hi'n troi'n ôl i'r eigion llwyd.
Ond yn ei galon, cadwodd ef,
Atgofion melys, cariad heb ei le.

Mae'r môr yn rhoi, mae'r môr yn cymryd,
Ond cariad gwir byth ni fydd yn suddo.
Fel y llanw'n dod ac yn mynd,
Mae atgofion yn aros yn ein bryd.

Oh, sing the song of love and loss,
Of paths that meet and lines that cross.
For in the heart where memories dwell,
The ones we've loved still cast their spell.

"And so, Ewan learned the ocean's lore,
That to love is to set free once more.
He'd gaze upon the endless sea,
And hear her song in every breeze."
