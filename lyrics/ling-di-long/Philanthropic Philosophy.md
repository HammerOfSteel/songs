# Philanthropic Philosophy

**Labels:** ling-di-long
**Created:** 2025-04-15
**Edited:** 2025-04-15

---

The door still sticks, the same old fight,
Smells like books and beer and leftover night.

The jukebox argues — ska or swing?
A trumpet cries, the bass might sting.

Teema’s got crayons, Peter’s got views,
I’ve got one pint and existential news.

Welcome, friend, come settle in —
This is Crosskeys' Philanthropic Inn.

Where darts miss wide, and quotes land true,
And the biggest truths come pint-soaked too.

Table by the loos with the wobbly leg,
Someone’s drawn Socrates riding a keg.
Mam’s by the fire, two chips in her hand,
Teema’s under the dartboard drawing the band.

Peter’s got pint foam on his upper lip,
Quiz sheet folded, already talking sh—
Steph’s at the bar with her “serious face,”
But she’s giggling at someone’s coat disgrace.

There’s Dai with the voice like gravel and smoke,
Quotes Marcus Aurelius between dad jokes.
The fireplace crackles, the neon light hums —
A pub full of hearts and half-rolled drums.

Pint in hand, mind in flight,
Argued with Plato half the night.
What’s truth? What’s love? Who knows, but still —
We found both on the windowsill.

And it’s pints, it’s laughs, it’s found philosophy —
Sticky floors and soul honesty.

Friday’s live night, chaos in tune,
Teema danced with a tambourine under the moon.
Peter sang backup (he shouldn’t have),
Mam brought her book, still made us laugh.

Quiz night flops, every time the same —
“Blue whale” shouted for every name.
Someone spilled stout on Nietzsche’s spine,
Steph wrote “Being and Chips” as a punchline.

Teema once painted the loo door red,
“It needed colour,” she simply said.
No one minds. No one judges.
We drink, we live, we leave our grudges.

Pint and a thought, scribbled deep,
Truths we found and couldn’t keep.
Books on the shelf, songs in the air,
And stories we told like we weren’t even there.

It’s darts and chips, it’s beer and breath —
And the kind of talk that outlives death.

One night, Mam cried over nothing and tea,
Said, “It’s not the grief, it’s the echo of me.”
Peter nodded like he knew too well,
And Teema just slept through the softest hell.

We toasted to love, to the ones we had lost,
To philosophy gained at a terrible cost.
To the way this pub holds your shadow like light,
And gives it a stool and a cider at night.

We’ve said goodbye in this room before —
With jokes, with poems, with crisps on the floor.
We’ve sung too loud, and sat too still.
And somehow, both helped us deal.

There’s a corner in this place where time sits slow,
Where the ghosts lean in just to let you know —
That even if you don’t believe in forever,
The kettle once whistled for all of us together.

Pint raised high, to the nights we knew,
To the crumbs, the chips, the wisdom too.
No one leaves with answers clear —
But we all found meaning in the cider here.

So here’s to Crosskeys, this inn, this fire —
Philanthropic minds and burnt-out choirs.

It’s midnight.
Teema’s curled under my coat, dreaming.
Peter’s gone off with someone’s leftover pie.
Mam’s still humming something soft.

And I leave a quote on a coaster:
“Every truth worth finding was once a joke in a pub.”
