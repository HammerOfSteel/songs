# A Letter Slipped Through Time

**Labels:** Counterfactual History
**Created:** 2025-04-07
**Edited:** 2025-04-07

---

It starts with a blank page.
And hands that remember too much.
Not to rewrite the story—
Just to whisper…
to the man who still could.

Ink like blood, hand slow to start
Every line cut straight from heart
No "dear me," no clever disguise
Just truth with trembling in its eyes
Folded time, tucked in a coat
No name at top, just weight in the note
“You won’t know why this voice feels known
But read it slow, you’re not alone.”
The past don’t listen, not for long
But sometimes... it hums along

It ain’t signed / ain’t clear
But I hope he hears
Folded words in the quiet seams
Between lost time and might-have-beens
If he reads / if he knows
She might run, she might grow
So I’ll write
And I’ll let it go

He walks past where the coat still hangs
I’m watching close from behind the panes
He’s tired-eyed, shoulders bruised
Still wearing that same excuse
If I could say one thing, it’s this—
Don’t let fear steal what you miss
Wake her now, take that road
The storm is coming—carry hope
One letter placed. One path unsaid.
One version where she’s not dead

He won’t know where it came from
Won’t guess the hand it came through
But maybe the soul will remember
What the mind never knew

It ain’t fate / it ain’t fair
But the words still care
Tucked deep in the folds of cloth
Where maybe he’ll pause and stop
If he reads / if he sees
If he stands up with his knees
I won’t need
To sign it “me”

I wrote what I never could say out loud
What grief made heavy, what time allowed
She was light, she was fire
And he still holds that spark entire
Let him wake her, just that night
Let him carry her to the light
Let the letter burn like truth
And guide the shaking hands of youth
He won’t thank me, won’t ever know
But it ain’t about me no more

It ain’t prayer / it ain’t plan
It’s the only thing I can
A voice in paper, not in name
A ghost with nothing left to claim
If he feels / if he moves
If he breaks the loop we lose
Then she breathes
And I refuse
To call it luck

One morning soon, he’ll reach that coat
Find a folded, silent note
Unmarked, unfamed, but true in tone
And maybe it will hit the bone
Where words go when they matter most
Where silence used to play the host
She won’t know, but that’s alright
She’ll laugh again beneath the light
And me? I’ll fade. Quiet, slow—
A trace left in the undertow

Don’t need credit / don’t need fame
Don’t need to be known by name
Just a chance to shift the rain
Just a hand through time and flame

I never got to save her.
But maybe he will.
And maybe that’s enough.
A letter slipped through time.
A life folded into lines.
