# Alun the Alchemist

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-05-05
**Edited:** 2025-05-05

---

They said he kept nettles in his coat pocket,
and copper wire twined round a spoon.
Said he could bottle sorrow —
And once, he nearly did.

Alun lived up near the hill’s crooked bend,
in a caravan where the brambles pretend
to be roses. He called it his “lab” with a grin —
one kettle, five books, and a cracked violin.

He’d say, "Love is a metal, and grief is the ore —"
"Refine it right, and you won’t need more."
He’d hum to the moon with a jar in hand,
collecting dreams like beachside sand.

No wand, no cloak, no wizard's tower,
just daffodil tea and thyme for power.
He’d whisper, “alchemy’s more than gold —
it’s knowing how to warm the cold.”

Some said daft, some said witch,
one lad tried to rob him, wound up in a ditch —
Not hurt, just... changed. Came back soft.
Gave his mum a hug, took Sundays off.

Alun smiled like he knew the score.
"Not everything that works makes sense — and more:"
"I turned my brother’s coat into rain,"
"It only fits when you’re missing him again."

He had a recipe written in script and soot:
"One photo torn, one forgotten foot,"
"Three truths said too late, stir them slow..."
"If they laugh at the end, then let them go."

Grief to light, sorrow to steam,
Stir in a pinch of an old lost dream.
A teaspoon of silence, two of rain,
And a song for those who speak in pain.

He built a kindness machine out of string and sighs,
with old spoons clinking like lullabies.
It never made sound, but it calmed the air —
birds landed softer, people stared.

He mailed stones to strangers with notes inside:
“This one’s for peace, this one for pride.”
A kid once wrote: "You fixed my Nan."
Alun just smiled and fixed the fan.

He kept herbs in tins and tears in wax,
never judged, but always asked.
“If you could turn one thing to grace,”
“What would you change, and not erase?”

"The sorrow’s still there," he’d say, "but I’m softer."
"And some gold don’t shine ‘til the seventh hour."
"We hold what we lose and transmute it new,"
"That’s not magic, lad — that’s just what we do."

Grief to glow, ache to balm,
A stir of thunder, a breath of calm.
Welsh gold tears and nettle wine —
Alchemy’s a hug in rhyme.

"Combine the verdant lion with mother’s milk warm,"
"Wrap in silence, weather every storm."
"When Saturn sighs and Mercury bends,"
"Forge not an answer, but a means to mend."

Some said he never got far.
But Alun?
He wrote the sky into a jar.
And sometimes — if you’re quiet —
you can still hear it fizz.
