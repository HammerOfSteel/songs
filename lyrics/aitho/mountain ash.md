Jack the Lantern, 
Joan the Wad, 
Light me home,
the weather's bad. 

See Saw, Margery Daw, 
Left her bed and lay in the straw. 

Left the straw and lay in the hay, 
Piskey came and took her away."

Seems like this road has no end, 
no end at all in sight, 
Thinking I just might,
end up at Land’s End tonight.

No, not quite that far...
pull over and park the car.

Aye, here we are, little star. 
Back again, another little year,
Been too long this time, I fear. 

Already has the sun set? 
Let the feet find their way yet.

Nothing,
if anything at all,
to regret.

At this little place by the sea, 
I pull out my flask and pour some tea,
Just enough,
for you...
for me.

Once we had a little bird,
should she ever fly away?
Absurd.

Shall we two follow in her every stride?
Should we seek as she goes to hide?

Oh no,
no, nothing sweeter.

I look at her,
I look at you, Peter.

Salt in the night,
wonderful tiny light.
Small little,
narrow street,
set in my marrow,
The new year,
has come to Polperro.

Came...
I have come home.
Checked in,
sat alone. 

No, 
not alone. 
No,
I can feel it in my bones.

I can smell it in the breeze,
at ease,
hear it in the rustling of the trees.

Yes, little lovely daffodil,
on this hill,
could I fill...
Fill up my cup?

Just for another year or so,
Let these embers keep their glow.

Barefoot,
staring at my own two feet,
feeling my own heartbeat,
Listening for the rhythm of yours,
imagine you stumble through those doors.
The sea is cold, yet I am all warm.
Up to my knees in this storm.

Once we had a little mouse,
we built her a warm and steady house.

Shall we two be so lucky to see her dance?
Should we take every chance,
we’d get?

Oh yes,
yes, everything and more.
Both of you,
which I adore.

I look at you, Peter...
I look at her.

See a storm out there,
find the sea in here.

Another visit to this place,
steps to take,
paths to trace,
There in the house of the sparrow,
another new year,
here in Polperro.

There is more than I can say,
more than I could bear,
Unfair,
that you never will know.

Aye,
my daffodil,
I love you so.

Art of foam and salt,
warmth of hearth and ember,
Aye, daffodil...
I remember...
I remember.

Aye, 
here I stroll, 
just a ling-a-long,
her little hymn,
this sweet song.

Could you ever understand,
what it means to me to hold your hand?
Could they ever really know,
how here among the brine you grow?

Eclipse,
full on strong,
the road down here is never long.

It breathes your form,
it holds me warm,
Leads me through the shifting storm.

Airey mouse, airey mouse fly over my head,
And you shall have a crust of bread.
And when I brew, or when I bake,
You shall have a piece of my wedding cake.
