# All These People

**Labels:** Sunny side
**Created:** 2025-12-15
**Edited:** 2025-12-16

---

Right. Let’s get this right.

I’ve been sitting at this kitchen table since the sun went down,
Watching the light fade from the grey to the black of the town,
And the pile of paper is growing, a stack of white sheets,
Like a tower of surrender, or a list of defeats.

I’ve written the stories,
I’ve told them all well,
Curated the ending,
crafted the hell And each one is different,
tailored and precise,
A bespoke goodbye,
a final piece of advice.

The first one was easy, or hard in a soft sort of way.
Addressed to the one who told me to stay.

I wrote, "I have always loved you, and I always will.
Please don’t look for the logic, don’t look for the pill.
Don’t expect forgiveness for the blood on the floor,
Because it isn’t your fault, of that I am sure."
I told them I’m sorry, I absolved them of guilt,

Said.
"Don’t burn down the house that we carefully built.
There’s so much about me that you never knew,
So don’t carry the weight of what I decided to do."

The biro is running out of ink,
scratching the page,
Capturing the sadness,
missing the rage.

And I look at the names in a line on the desk,
All these people, put to the test,
I thought I was standing on an island alone,
But look at the lines,
look at the bone.

Connected to muscle, 
connected to skin.

Where do I end?
Where do they begin?

All these people I’m leaving behind,
Are the only things keeping me tethered to time.

Then there’s the second pile,
the ones written in red Metaphorically speaking,
the things better unsaid
To the one who broke me,
I wrote down your name,
And I hope when you read this,
you drown in the shame.

I told you quite clearly,
"You are to blame For the cracks in the mirror and the extinguishing flame."

I wrote,
"I can’t forgive you, I won’t make peace My silence today is the only release."
The pen dug through the paper,
tore a hole in the sheet
The anger felt solid,
the anger felt sweet.

And then there was the third one,
the gamble, the bet
To the person I haven’t quite given up on yet

I wrote,
"Maybe you could stop me,
before I go cold,
If you walked through that door,
if you were just bold,
If you confessed that you loved me,
if you made a scene.
You could wipe the slate dirty,
you could wipe the slate clean."

But I doubt you’d go to that sort of extent,
So I sealed up the envelope,
heavy with intent.

My hand is cramping,
the coffee is stone cold,
I’m nineteen years young,
and a thousand years old.

And I read back the notes,
line after line,
All these people, intertwined.
I thought I was standing in a room with no doors,
But look at the handwriting,
look at the scores,
Connected to memory,
connected to vein,
Whatever I lose,
is what they sustain.

All these people who would notice I’m gone,
Are the reason the night has to turn into dawn.

I looked at the stack.
Must be ten, maybe twelve letters there.
And it hit me like a physical blow to the chest.

The sheer volume of it.
The logistics of leaving.
I realized how many people would actually care.
All these people there are to live for.
Even the ones I hate.
Even the ones I haven't met yet.
Including myself.
Especially myself.

So I went to the shredder and I fed them all in,
The love and the anger, the virtue and sin.
Watched them turn into confetti,
a snow of regret,
And I told myself quietly... 
"Not yet.",
"Not yet."

I got out a fresh sheet,
clean and white In the very first crack of the morning light,
I didn’t write a poem,
I didn’t write a rhyme,
I didn’t try to explain the this time.

I just wrote two words to start the display

"Dad..."

"I think I need help."
