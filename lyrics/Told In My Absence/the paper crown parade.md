# the paper crown parade

**Labels:** Told In My Absence
**Created:** 2025-10-25
**Edited:** 2025-10-25

---

Toy piano tiptoes, bunting in the breeze,
 mismatched plates and knees on knees.
 Balloons say hello to the ceiling shade, 
 I crown myself first in the paper crown parade.

I’m queen of the sponge with the jam red sea,
 sprinkles like stars making maps for me.
 Bunting does zigzags, cups go cheer,
 stickers on jumpers that say “I’m here.”
 Orange squash sunset in plastic light,
 shoes in a pile and the house feels bright.
 Dad cuts slices in comet shapes,
 crumbs wear glitter like tiny capes.
 I save one seat at the end of the spread,
 a chair that listens while the jelly sets red.
 I don’t say who, but I pour them tea, 
 some birthdays are parties that wait patiently.

Make a wish, don’t tell;
 I can hum it well.

Parade, parade, paper crown, lemonade,
 I march round the table I helped Dad made.
 Hooray, hooray,  candles throw gold confetti,
 I’m bigger by inches and brave already.
 Parade, parade, footsteps in polka dots,
 we dance on the tiles in jelly spots.
 If joy is a drum then my heart’s well played, 
 boom-boom-clap in the paper crown parade.

Loot bag treasures: sticker, yo-yo, ring,
 kazoo that knows exactly how to sing.
 Colin the Caterpillar smiles side-on,
 I eat the green bit, save head till gone.
 Photos in frames do a birthday rhyme, 
 me through the years in a washing line.
 Three, then four, then five, then more,
 me at the window, me at the door.
 One slice wrapped in a napkin star,
 goes on a plate for the not-so-far.
 I clink my cup to the quiet seat, 
 good guests come softly, still get sweets.

By the ribbon, by the bow,
 by the crumbs that choose to glow,
 by the wish and whisper said,
 by the crown upon my head,
 by the fork and by the flame,
 by the song that knows my name, 
 stay, stay, stay, sweet day;
 don’t run away.

Parade, parade, paper crown, lemonade,
 I loop round the kitchen, I’m unafraid.
 Hooray, hooray, tissue rain, streamer braid,
 my age is a banner the windows made.
 Parade, parade, footprints of sugar white,
 we draw little halos in birthday light.
 If love is a drum then it never fades, 
 boom-boom-clap in the paper crown parade.

I’m learning the trick of an endless year:
 keep one chair open and call it dear.
 Keep one slice waiting, a saucer of sun,
 share what is sweet till the sweetness is done.
 Time wears a crown made of crepe and glue,
 marches in circles and starts anew.

I’m eight in the mirror with toothpaste crown,
 I’m nine on the stairs where the socks sit down.
 I’m ten in the garden where sparklers speak,
 firefly handwriting, gold on cheek.
 Every year leaves a thread in the braid,
 I stitch it back in when the colours fade.
 When candles sputter and shadows lean,
 I hum the chorus that keeps things green.

Parade, parade, paper crown, lemonade,
 I march with the moments we safely saved.
 Hooray, hooray,  laughter like marmalade,
 sticky and sunny on toast we made.
 Parade, parade, round again, softly played,
 the chair at the table is not afraid.
 If memory drums then the heart obeyed, 
 boom-boom-clap in the paper crown parade.

Toy piano twinkles; crown goes shff;
I blow the last candle, wish stays stiff.
The room says thank you in streamer shade…
I sleep in the glow of the crown I made.
