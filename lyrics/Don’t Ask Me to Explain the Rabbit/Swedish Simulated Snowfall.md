Autumn, winter, summer, spring; 
these are the four seasons that makes the world sing. 

Then everything changed when the flood gates burst (but that wasn't the worst). 

Only human ingenuity, compassion and truth could save us now. 

But when the world needed us the most - we vanished. 

The decades past, mothers rage got tired and old, the winters grew increasingly cold. 

The artificial intellect grew, where roots and bramble used to spew. 

But I still believe, I believe there is a way back.

The cows don’t graze, the birds don’t hum,
but the season towers simulate sun.

Execute the payload, Alexander,
I said the earth’s got a splintered standard.
Where’s the river? Where’s the thaw?
Just pixel petals and unspoken law.

I was raised on stories twice removed —
my mum’s mum danced where the rivers moved.
Now all I get’s the static breeze,
from the vending snow and plastic trees.

Simulated snowfall sings too slow,
like a lullaby the wires know.
Where did the color, the chaos go?
Makers rise when the thaw says no.

We were born from melted skies —
taught to dream with soldered lies.
But I still believe there’s power here,
to glitch the system and reset and clear.

Instructions meant to serve the soil,
became our scripts of silent toil.
Governments ghosted the golden pine,
gave us frost from an indoor sign.

We named the weather, sold the air,
exported grief and clicked despair.
But in the quiet — something spoke,
a crack beneath the snowflake joke.

Alexander wired the coil with care,
“Push this line and we’ll be there.”
Not back to perfect, not to clean —
just back to spring in something green.

“They dulled the world to keep us calm.
Gave us fake wind and bedtime balm.
But frost ain’t frost unless it stings —
and grief must bloom if spring still sings.”

Simulated snowfall cracks too thin,
you can feel the code break in the wind.
One more patch, one more fake freeze —
we’ll dig the roots out from the machine.

I was just a kid, but I still know —
what winter feels like in the snow.
We’ll reroute the node with midnight grace,
Kernal panic from rebel base.

Some say we’re too late.
I say frost remembers.
Some say we’re code.
But even ghosts can be embers.

So now we plan — not grand, not loud,
a cable snipped, a crowd unclouded.
We’ll tear the core from the weather node,
write “truth” on tape, let thunder load.

I’ll wear Mum’s coat with her glove inside,
hand-stitched stories we tried to hide.
If we fail — we fail with flame,
but if we win — the world gets name.

Simulated snowfall, melt in light,
let spring erupt from coded night.
If love’s a glitch, then let it spark —
real rain on earth, not server dark.

I still believe, I always did —
even back when I was a kid.
The system’s cold — but so are we.
And ice can crack from poetry.

“I saw it once. Real frost, real sky.
It was only for a second. But it was enough.”
