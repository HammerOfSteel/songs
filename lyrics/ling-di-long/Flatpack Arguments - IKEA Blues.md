# Flatpack Arguments - IKEA Blues

**Labels:** ling-di-long
**Created:** 2025-04-12
**Edited:** 2025-04-12

---

"You burnt the toast again."
“Well the kettle’s got attitude.”
“You used the good mug.”
“It’s clean. That’s not a crime.”
The dog sighs like a judge,
the radio’s still playing Sian Cothi too loud.
Mam leans back, hand on belly like it’s a full moon.
“She’s kicking in Morse code,” she grins.
“Says she wants pancakes.”
“Of course she does. She's yours.”

And we’re dancing 'round the crumbs again —
love in Welsh and toast in ten.

The box explodes in screws and shame,
“Is this a cot or a Viking game?”
One Allen key and a paper guide,
with diagrams that mock our pride.

“This bit’s the leg?” “No, that’s the top.”
“We need that bolt you made me drop.”
I kneel, you stretch, the boards all lean,
the future smells of sawdust and caffeine.

Flatpack blues and blistered thumbs —
building love where silence hums.

You rest the side rail on your knee,
say, “What if she doesn’t get the best of me?”
“What if my light don’t ever come back?”
I drop the wrench. My throat goes slack.

“What if I fall?” you whisper slow.
“What if she sees the parts I won’t show?”
“What if I’m storm and not enough sun?”
I say, “Then I’ll be the roof you lean on.”

If your sky turns grey or your stars come loose —
I’ll hold the frame through every bruise.

You laugh, half-wet, with paint on your sleeve,
“This crib is crooked, and so are we.”
I kiss your hand, you kiss my frown,
we sit on the floor with the screws upside-down.

You say, “We’ve got no clue, no plan,”
I say, “We’ve got toast crumbs and your cardigan.”
The light breaks in like a soft reveal,
and I know right then this love is real.

Flatpack souls with wonky screws —
but hearts like this don’t come with clues.

“She’ll love the moonlight in this room.”
“And she’ll have your laugh — full and soon.”
