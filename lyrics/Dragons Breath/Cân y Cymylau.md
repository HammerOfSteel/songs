# Cân y Cymylau

**Labels:** Dragons Breath
**Created:** 2025-07-16
**Edited:** 2025-07-16

---

I think, that if I touched the earth,
It would crumble;
It is so sad and beautiful,
So tremulously like a dream 

I seen your name up in them cirrus swirls,
Braided soft like your mam’s old curls.
Tip of my tongue, edge of my chest,
Clouds full o’ weight that never gets rest.
This ain’t no hymn for the chapel crowd,
Just a can o’ tea on a slate that’s proud.
You ever talk to the sky when you lie on your back?
I reckon it listens, but don’t always chat.
She’s up there somewhere, your laugh in the blue,
Echo of bedtime, ‘bout quarter past two.
Your toy sheep still in my coat’s inside,
Left it for luck on the day that you died.
But I walk, I walk — yeah, still got feet,
Got the mountain path and her crooked beat.
Bach, I miss you more than I even know,
But I talk to the clouds, and they tell me so.

Cân y cymylau, clywaist ti fi?
Tat on the breeze, still singin’ by sea.
Un, dau, tri — rwy’n cofio ti,
Clouds hold the bits you left with me.

You said the sky looked like jam on toast,
That the wind was just sheep from a castle ghost.
I laughed ‘til I choked on my lukewarm brew,
Couldn’t believe I got to be dad to you.
Now the world feels hollow in odd wee ways,
Like finding your sock in a drawer for days.
I’m still bad at folding, still hum your tune,
Still dance with the mop on a Friday noon.
Your mam would’ve smiled, I know she would —
She’s up there too, with the fae folk’s hood.
Wrote her a note in the sky last week,
Signed it “yours,” but forgot to speak.
So I rhyme in the mist, and I weep in the hail,
Write your name in the clouds on a walking trail.
Ain’t no altar ‘cept the ridge and rain,
But I light you a song through the morning pain.

Cân y cymylau, lle’r wyt ti’n mynd?
Winds take whispers I didn’t send.
Daw eto haul ar ffenest fy nghalon,
Your name’s in the fog before the dawn.

Sometimes I think you come in the shape
Of a fog that hugs, or a cloud that waits.
I hear you in kettle, in rustle of fern,
In the sheep that bleats when I take a turn.
I pack you lunch just for the crack,
Ham and cheese with your crisps in the back.
Talk to the bench like we’re out again,
Two teas, no sugar, and a splash of rain.
You’re my whole damn sky, you know that, love?
Still send you stars when I’m thinkin’ of.
It’s mad how you’re gone and I’m still this full,
Of lullabies, clouds, and all things dull.
But that’s love, innit? It never goes —
Just floats in the weather where no one knows.

Cân y cymylau, don’t say goodbye,
Sing me still ‘til the rivers run dry.
Tan fydd y sêr yn chwythu’n llwyd,
You’re the hush in my every noise.

To begin, at the beginning..
Love is the last light spoken.
And now, gentlemen, like your manners, I must leave you
