# Abra-Kadabra

**Created:** 2026-02-21

---

## Instrumentation
- Japanese modern pads, Koto, soft hand percussion, mandolin — think "Walking in Kyoto" for inspiration.
- Outro: single toy-piano motif only.

## Production Notes
- Haiku intro: teenage male voice, soft Swedish accent. Bare — no pads yet.
- Main vocals: male soft voice, intimate, near-spoken in parts.
- Child's voice mid-section: small British child, slightly off-mic, bright and matter-of-fact.
- Chorus refrain: keep whispered under main vocal at all times; layer faint chorus of child voices for warmth.
- Outro: toy-piano only, vocal whispered to nothing.

---

## Haiku Intro
*(teenage male voice, soft Swedish accent — bare, no instrumentation)*

リスカでも
リスカなつかし
時鳥

*(after Matsuo Bashō — "Even here in Risca / dear Risca, beloved / a cuckoo calling far off")*

---

## Verse 1
*(pads and koto enter softly, hand percussion barely touching)*

I boil the water twice a day
with the hem of my hair i like to play,
I cut the toast into triangles
the way you like
The lights are on upstairs somewhere
I leave them, right here
The oldest spells are all like this —
full of you, of all I miss.

---

## Chorus
*(main vocal + whispered layer beneath)*

Abra-kadabra, fold the night in two
one part kamo-river, one part you
they say it low, I say it slow —
abra-kadabra,
if you're going through hell,
keep on through,

---

## Mid-Section — Call and Response
*(adult voice calls; small child's voice answers, warm and certain)*

*Who do you see?*
— A fox between the trees

*What does it know?*
— It knows me, it always knows me

*What does it say?*
— The fish can fly today

*Then fold the night in two —*
— one part hush

*One part —*
— you

*(a beat of silence; single koto note held)*

---

## Verse 2
*(fuller — mandolin enters; slower, more Japanese in feel)*

A little hush will hold the things
it flies south on brittle wings
it fills the shape of what is left
these hands no longer all that deft,
The petals fall because they must
the river takes them home
and rising after falling is
the oldest spell known

---

## Chorus
*(as before, whispered layer beneath)*

Abra-kadabra, fold the night in two
one part hush, one part you
say it low, say it slow —
abra-kadabra, carry it through

---

## Outro
*(toy-piano only; vocal whispered, then barely breath)*

Abra-kadabra...
fold the night...
one part hush...
one part you...

*(hold — fade to silence)*
