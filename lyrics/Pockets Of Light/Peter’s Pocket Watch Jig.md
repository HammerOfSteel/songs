# Peter’s Pocket Watch Jig

**Labels:** Pockets Of Light
**Created:** 2025-08-29
**Edited:** 2025-08-29

---

Alright then, little miss—ready? Count us in.
Un, dau, tri, pedwar—tick tock, Uncle, faster!

Hop-square, chalk square, skip-step, quick!
Time’s got laces and a skipping-rope tick.
Click-snap, watch cap, pewter-bright grin,
Peter’s pocket planet going round again.
Pitter-patter minute hands, tatter-tattoo,
tap-toe tattletale, “catch me if you do.”
Ping of the spring and a wink in the chain,
boing like a jaw-harp, boing again.

Oi, Uncle Pete—does time have knees?
Only when you make it knock them, bach!


---

Click-clack, nick-nack, wind that trick.
Hop, hop—skip, skip—heel, toe, swing;
pocket watch jig makes the little bells ring.

Peter pipes pewter pips perfectly (try that twice!),
Twm Siôn Cati stole a second for spice.
Gelert’s hill listening, church bells thin,
my watch caught the echo and bottled it in.
Tick writes a riddle, tock ties a bow,
seconds chase each other like sheep in snow.
Pendulum pendragon, swing me your sign;
little miss lightning, keep time with mine.

Tongue-twister time:
“Pedr’s pocket pebbles pick perfect paces.”
Done!

Pocket watch jig—flip-flap, fling!
Wind it, mind it, make it sing.
Hopscotch numbers, un to deg,
tap the square, don’t tag the edge.

Bus-stop tick, puddle-clock plink,
curry-chip steam goes think-think-think.
Cuckoo o’clock in the chip-shop queue,
Uncle Pete winks, “half past you.”
Pebble in the pocket plays metronome light,
click like a cricket in a cardigan night.
Time’s just a tangle till laughter unknots,
he pats his watch and it purrs in dots.

ba-da-lip, ba-da-lop, tikka-takka tip!
diddle-iddle-dee on a mandolin rip!

Wind it, find it, kind it, keep;
seconds in a satchel never fall asleep.
Tap it, wrap it, snap it, swing;
jaw-harp boing and the bright strings ring.

Pocket watch jig, tic-tac-tog,
fox-clock, rock-dock, jog-dog, jog.
Turn the chain, chime the cheer,
time does a cartwheel when Uncle’s near.

Minute hand minnows shimmer and dart,
second hand swallows learn every part.
Awen in a tick.
Sweet in a tock.
We keep our joy in a click-little clock.

One more lap round the numbers?
Tomorrow, bach. Time’s tucked in.
