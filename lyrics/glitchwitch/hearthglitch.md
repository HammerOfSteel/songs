# hearthglitch

**Labels:** glitchwitch
**Created:** 2025-09-21
**Edited:** 2025-09-21

---

I keep a small place by a lane and a stile,
a light in the pane and a kettle on file.
Little witch, stitch and smile, mend what I can,
thread through the while, hand over hand.

The wallpaper wavers, a wave in the wall,
pattern repeats, then repeats not at all;
kettle goes twice in the same little breath,
twin whistles kiss, then pretend to be deaf.
I don’t take fright; I set out two cups,
lay spoons like moons, fill both to the tops;
steam writes a rune on the glass for a spell,
I hum, “all’s well,” and I mean it as well.

If time gets twitchy, clicking twice,
I’ll pour it warm and pour it nice;
one cup mercy, one cup grace,
we’ll sip the glitch back into place.
If clocks go cross and minutes miss,
we’ll fix with tea and tenderness.

Chalk at the lintel, little if/then lines,
“if boots are muddy, then leave your brines.”
Hedgerow listens; a blackbird logs,
magpies tally the shine of the cogs.
I sweep up crumbs like constellations,
thread the day through gentle stations;
laugh when the latch learns to lag for fun,
count to one-and-a-half, then the door is done.

If days go jitter, slip and slide,
I’ll set two saucers side by side;
one cup courage, one cup cheer,
we’ll soothe the loop and keep it near.
If hours hiccup, nudge, and sway,
we’ll breathe them back the homely way.

Starter in a jar, old soul dough,
keeps my house from sinking low;
fold and turn, the patient art,
the loaf remembers every heart.
The spoon appears before I take it,
the mirror fogs before I make it;
I grin at fate, “you sweet old trick,”
and butter time on extra thick.

If window fog, then write our names;
else: breathe again and play kind games.
Try: light the lamp and warm the room;
catch: the gloom, and give it bloom.
Return: two cups, a steady chair,
commit: small care to open air.

If world goes wobbly, soft and odd,
we’ll nod, we’ll laugh, we’ll thank our God,
or gods of kettle, thread, and spice,
the little laws that make life nice.
Two cups poured and one shared smile:
that’s how we patch another mile.

The clock says maybe; the rug says stay;
the catlike wind kneads clouds of grey.
I pin the sun with a washing peg,
kiss the bruise on evening’s leg;
salt in the soup, star in the salt,
bless every fault for being a fault.
The paper lifts, its seam unsticks,
I smooth it flat with gentle tricks.

If life’s a sim in cottage skin,
we still choose kind, and call it win;
one cup mercy, one cup light,
we debug softly through the night.
If time stutters at the hinge,
we brew a bridge and do not flinch.

So if the house goes double, love,
don’t fret the odd or fear the shove;
I’ll set two cups, the day will mend,
we sip, we stitch, we start again.
