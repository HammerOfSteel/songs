# Lighthouse in the Fog

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-29
**Edited:** 2024-10-29

---

(Verse 1)
Fog rolls thick past the Barbican’s lights,
Hovers by Hoe Park, swallowing night.
Down Union Street, the cobbles shine wet,
Like a path laid out that I haven’t found yet.

“Proper ‘ansome,” the lads at the quay say,
Drunk on the sea, lost halfway.
But the beam from the Sound keeps callin' me on—
A shimmer of light that feels like home.

(Chorus)
Lighthouse in the fog, calling me near,
Through mist and shadow, it stays clear.
A guide through night, a path unknown,
I walk these streets, searchin’ alone.

(Verse 2)
By Drake’s Island, the tide pulls away,
Leavin’ sand like thoughts that won’t stay.
“Could go on, could stay—does it matter?” I think,
As the sea hums a song, pulling me to the brink.

The fog's as thick as me Nan’s old stew,
Every corner hides a clue or two.
But the lighthouse waits, steady and low—
Quiet as stories I’ll never know.

(Chorus)
Lighthouse in the fog, calling me near,
Through mist and shadow, it stays clear.
A guide through night, a path unknown,
I walk these streets, searchin’ alone.

(Bridge – Guitar Solo)
(A bluesy guitar solo mimics the ebb and flow of the tide and the gradual lifting of the fog.)

(Verse 3)
Over by the Sound, the fog lifts slow,
Revealing Cawsand, where small boats go.
Not every light will point you back—
Some guide through the dark when the map turns black.

Morning slips in like a whisper, soft,
Clearing the mist that held me aloft.
And in that light, it all comes true—
What I was searchin’ for’s right here too.

(Chorus – Gradual Build)
Lighthouse in the fog, calling me near,
Through mist and shadow, it stays clear.
A guide through night, a path unknown,
I walk these streets, searchin’ alone.

(Outro – Bluesy Fade)
I walk these streets, fog at my back,
Following light through the blue and black.
The lighthouse hums as I move along,
And in its silence, I find my song.

(Guitar and bass fade out, with faint echoes of seagulls and waves.)
