# No road home (so she built one)

**Created:** 2025-04-14
**Edited:** 2025-04-14

---

Some stories start with castles.
But hers?
It started with bruises.
With fabric scraps, loud music, and two wild girls asking,
“Can we wear horns to school?”
And she said:
“Yes. You can wear anything... even wings.”

She wraps her hand around the steel,
It’s not a stage, it’s something real.
The kids are sleeping down the hall,
She spins — and doesn’t touch the wall.

A grip, a bruise, a breath, a glide,
Her body burns, but doesn’t hide.
No mirrors here, no need to prove,
Just sweat and silence, grace and groove.

She built a temple from the ground,
With nothing but her name and sound.
And every flight defies the rules —
That witches melt, and mothers lose.

She didn’t fall, she flew.
She danced the dark right through.
No wand, no script, no crowd — just her,
And all the fire the world tried to blur.
She didn’t fall, she climbed.
With velvet ropes and time.
The pole, the room, the night, the sky —
That’s where her magic learned to fly.

They told her witches must behave,
She kissed her scars and didn’t cave.
She sewed a life from thread and storms,
With daughters dressed in cosplay forms.

A fox, a mage, a wingéd queen —
They walk like light, like dreams unseen.
She taught them spells made out of play,
To laugh too loud and wear capes anyway.

She told them, “Don’t fit into frames,
This world will try to call you names.
But you are thunder in disguise,
And glitter’s just rebellion in your eyes.”

So here’s to those who won’t sit still,
Who raise the roof with quiet will.
Who build a road where none exists —
And make the fall a kind of lift.

She didn’t fall, she ran.
Right through the fear and plan.
They said, “Be quiet,” she said, “Sing,”
And taught her girls to wear everything.
No yellow bricks, no throne,
Just hands and love and stone.
She walked the road that wasn’t there —
And left a trail of burning air.

She used to dream in Broadway lights,
Now it’s leggings, poles, and Monday nights.
But every spin becomes a stage,
Where she rewrites what they call “age.”

No script can hold the kind of heart
That makes a home from broken parts.
Her hands are full, her eyes are wide —
She’s lived ten lives, and still won’t hide.

The world may see a mother first,
But I have seen her when she bursts.
A song, a spell, a storm that grew —
She didn’t fall. She always flew.

She doesn’t fall — she climbs.
And carves her truth in time.
She spins the air and lifts the floor,
And writes her name on every door.
She doesn’t fall — she knows.
That even roots can grow in snow.
Her daughters walk where she once ran —
They wear her fire in their hands.

There’s no applause in laundry piles,
No trophies in the daily trials.
But still she shines, and still she spins —
A sorceress in second skins.

The world may never understand
How strong she is without a brand.
But look — the girls with glitter eyes?
They’re hers. They laugh. They rise. They rise.

And every time they leap or fall,
She’s there, unseen, but in it all.
She built this path, this life, this view —
She didn’t fall.
She brought them through.

She doesn’t fall — she soars.
Her light floods every door.
No crown, no cape — just her and flame,
And daughters carrying her name.
She didn’t fall — she flew.
And in that flight, she grew.
And now the girls who watch her climb
Will build their sky in their own time.

Some say witches ride broomsticks.
But mine?
She wears grip gloves,
paints her girls like warriors,
and dances upside-down beneath the stars.
She didn’t fall.
She taught us how to rise.
