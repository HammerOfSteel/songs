# Nightwatcher’s Log

**Labels:** Super Nova
**Created:** 2025-06-11
**Edited:** 2025-06-12

---

She’s finally down, snorin’ like a lion on the floor.
Lights off, but my mind don’t sleep,
Got bags full o’ dreams that I never keep.

She kicked off her socks like they owed her rent,
spun three times, then crashed like she’s spent.
Crumbs in the bed from toast I said “no” to,
Now I’m pickin’ out jam stars like a makeshift crew.

I’m perched like a gargoyle in this fold-up chair,
one foot numb, tangled hair, dead stare.
Tried to scroll but the screen’s too bright,
So I just sit in the hum of the night.

Flat's quiet, but not quiet-clean —
the telly’s hummin’ ghosts in the in-between.
Still smell her shampoo, still feel the heat,
Half my love’s in her blanket, half's in defeat.

Bin’s full, bills ain’t paid,
Didn’t call Mum, still ‘fraid.
But she smiled when she passed out mid-yawn,
And that — that’s the reason I’m still sworn.

Nightwatcher, holdin’ the sky,
One eye closed, the other askin’ why.
Ain’t no cape — just breath and bone,
Stayin’ put so she don’t sleep alone.

She stirred ‘round two, said somethin’ mad —
“Tell the stars I said no, yeah? They’re bein’ bad.”
Tucked her back in, wiped her face clean,
She dreams like a poet in a jellybean scene.

Cup of milk, went warm on the side —
I drink it down like I’m swallowing pride.
Legs ache, heart worse, spine’s in a spin,
Thought I’d be more, not just holdin’ it in.

Thought she’d have more — more than me,
But maybe she’s got all I ever could be.
Her fingers twitch like she’s playin’ a spell,
Makes me think there’s still time to rebel.

But the clock’s got sharp teeth and no chill,
And the moon outside’s just doin’ her will.
I still hear her mum in the echo space —
in the way she mumbles, in the shape of her face.

Nightwatcher, holdin’ the sky,
One eye closed, the other askin’ why.
Ain’t no cape — just breath and bone,
Stayin’ put so she don’t sleep alone.

You ever tell the walls things you can’t admit?
Like, “I’m good” when you're barely lit?
Like, “I’m strong,” while you crumble in your coat,
Foldin’ every “I miss you” into the note?

I’m tired of the weight that smiles back.
Of bein’ the ship, not the one who packs.
Of shushin’ ghosts so she can rest,
Of bein’ a home while forgettin’ the guest.

But I look at her — one sock, curled tight,
and know I'd do this every single night.
‘Cause love ain’t loud, and it don’t parade —
it’s in sittin’ through fear you never evade.

So I whisper dumb jokes to the sleepin’ dark,
Write new lullabies with invisible spark.
Log this entry, half dream, half vow —
to keep her safe, even if I forget how.

Nightwatcher, watchin’ her breathe,
Soft like the hush of a midnight leave.
I ain’t fixed — just stayin’ known,
A body of love she won’t face alone.

She’s asleep.
And I’m still here.
