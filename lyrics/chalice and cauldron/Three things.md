# Three things

**Labels:** chalice and cauldron
**Created:** 2025-04-06
**Edited:** 2025-04-07

---

Two brothers sit on borrowed thrones,
Heads crowned with fire, hearts made of phones.
The plagues come quiet, then loud, then gone—
But who sees the cracks before the dawn?

They call me mad.
But I remember when the air still hummed with truth.
Now it just flickers.
Whispers.
Gone.

Three things cannot be long hidden: 
the sun, 
the moon, 
and the truth

They came not with guns but with silence first,
Ghosts in wires, dressed like thirst.
Thoughts erased mid-sentence—click—reset.
Now no one questions what they forget.
I watched them steal the words from screens,
Then from mouths, then from dreams.
A language lost, not with blade but blink,
Now even poets forget how to think.
I screamed in alleys. Nobody heard.
I painted warnings in crumbling words.
They called me prophet, called me freak—
But still, they scroll. And never speak.
They said it was safety. Said it was choice.
But I saw the shadow behind the voice.
One world order, smile on the mask,
“Freedom,” they whispered. “Just don’t ask.”

Say what you’re told.
Forget what you knew.
They’ve silenced your questions
Before they reached you.
They came in the night,
Took silence for truth—
Now the only sound
Is what they choose.

The second plague screamed through every street,
But no one moved. Just clenched their teeth.
Sirens stacked on sirens, drones in clouds,
Windows cracked and babies howled.
Was it rage? Was it grief?
Or just feedback from belief?
Every night the shrieking grew,
'Til the only truth was noise-brand-new.
Lludd said, “Broadcast hope.”
Llevelys said, “Silence scope.”
But they both bowed low to the same white throne—
Hands washed clean in algorithm foam.
And I stood beneath the screaming sky,
Whispers melted from my eye,
The sound was not a scream at all—
Just all the things we didn’t call.
“Trust the stream,” they said on air.
So we drowned
With perfect care.

Who writes the script
When no one sees the page?
Who profits most
From panic staged?

Listen to the hum.
Listen to the spin.
They sell you fear
With a plastic grin.
Shriek in your sleep,
Starve in your name.
But trust the hands
That lit the flame.

Then came hunger. But the shelves were full—
Of plastic fruit, and policy bull.
Empty plates dressed up like meals,
They fed us data, numbed our feels.
Farms bought out, the soil sold thin,
The harvest lost beneath the spin.
They said, “It’s fine. There’s apps for that.”
While mothers boiled dreams in vats.
I watched a man eat cardboard slow,
Just to feel his stomach grow.
No riots. No revolt.
Just hands in prayer that no one wrote.
The kings held meetings far from view,
Spoke of hope and markets new.
And still the fool sat in the square,
Feeding birds what wasn’t there.

They took your voice.
They stole your sight.
They fed you ghosts
And called it right.
But I still walk.
And I still write.
And I still burn
Without their light.

You reading this? You still awake?
Or did the static finally take
The part of you that used to dream—
Before the codes and dopamine?
Ask yourself—who benefits
When silence tastes like safety kits?
When food is fake, and thought’s a crime,
And kings recite your lines in time?
I am not your hero here.
I’m just the mirror smeared with fear.
But if you hear me, don’t forget—
You still have breath. You still have debt.
Not to them.
To truth.
To pain.
To the fire in the question’s name.

Pwy yw’r gwir?
Quién controla el eco?
Who watches the watchers?
Who names the plague?
And who dares speak
when silence pays?

The kings are still at council.
The feast is still a show.
