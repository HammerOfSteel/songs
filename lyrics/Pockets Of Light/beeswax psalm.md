# beeswax psalm

**Labels:** Pockets Of Light
**Created:** 2025-09-01
**Edited:** 2025-09-01

---

Wax remembers flame. Flame remembers breath.

Hive-given gospel, resin of sun,
folded in honeycomb, labors undone.
Melt me to mercy, pour me to rest,
shape of a psalm in a vessel confessed.
Amber becomes river, thick as prayer,
slow as forgiveness that scents the air.
Wick waits patient in molten gold,
light is a covenant, ancient, bold.

Salt in the sweat of the waxman’s brow,
sulfur of smoke in the vow here-and-now.
Mercury gleam on the liquid’s skin,
transmute sorrow to the light within.
I carry the candle, it carries me back,
spine of devotion in resin and black.
Ash on the table, scent in the hall,
fire writes scripture across the wall.

Beeswax psalm, sung without word,
prayer in a drip no hand has heard.

Fold of the taper, curl of the flame,
wax soft chorus keeps naming my name.
Spoon-stirred, heart-whirred, poured in mold,
every small vessel turns grief to gold.
Drip on the finger, brand of the hymn,
wax burns holy and tender within.
Lanterns align in cathedral rows,
each one a silence the candle knows.

Wax remembers winter, scent of the hive,
songs made solid to keep us alive.
Body of labor, body of prayer,
melted devotion becomes repair.
Sulfur for sorrow, salt for the tears,
bees keep the measure of countless years.
I light what was labored, I breathe what was sung,
psalm of the wax in the candle’s tongue.

Wax to wick, and wick to fire,
ashes fall but flames climb higher.
Wax to word, and word to breath,
candle hums of love and death.

Melt into mercy, pour into peace,
fragrance of honey makes grief release.
Smoke is a scripture, light is a psalm,
wax holds the heat like a mother’s calm.
I kiss the silence, I name the flame,
it flickers and answers, it answers the same.

Beeswax psalm, sung without word,
prayer in the flame no hand has heard.
