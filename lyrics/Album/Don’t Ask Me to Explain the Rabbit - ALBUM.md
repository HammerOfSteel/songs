🌐 Don’t Ask Me to Explain the Rabbit
Modern retelling of Wonderland not as escape, but as a liminal processing space.

Each track = a strange place or figure in a dream landscape stitched from memories, folklore, philosophy, and digital age symbols.

It’s both healing and glitching. Fun and sharp. Funky, but also tears in your hoodie.

🌀 THEMES TO EXPLORE:
Lost & Found Childhood

Losing childhood not to age, but to overload.

“Hide and Seek.exe” – a song about how we hide behind screens, and what we forget to seek.

Siblinghood + Parental Bonds

“Little Brother Stuck in the Machine” – exploring sibling connection when your brother’s world is Roblox and yours is grief.

“Motherboard” – your mum as a mythical motherboard spirit with love as electricity.

Folklore Rewired

Näcken, Vittra, and Skogsrå as characters in AR filters, misunderstood by the algorithm.

“Vittra in the WiFi” – song where you hear whispers of the past in the hum of your router.

Grief as Game Levels

“Level: Echo” – you walk through old save files of your life. Some can’t load anymore.

“Boss Battle: Memory Loss” – beat made of VHS crackle, tears over 8-bit strings.

Philosophy for the Brokenhearted

Concepts like absurdism, eternal return, and meaning-in-chaos woven in lyrics and skits.

One interlude is just the White Rabbit reading Camus but getting distracted by a Snapchat filter.

🧠 CHARACTERS REIMAGINED
Alice / You – a glitching narrator; maybe switching identities mid-album. Sometimes a girl, sometimes a voice, sometimes a feeling.

The Mad Hatter – a street busker/beatmaker who speaks only in existential one-liners and funk samples.

The Cheshire Cat – a shapeshifting AI that leaves cryptic TikToks and edits your dreams.

The Queen of Hearts – cancel culture embodied, rules a courtroom of guilt and longing.

The White Rabbit – always late, always anxious. Maybe he’s your ADHD personified.

🎶 SOUND & STYLE:
Genre blend: Lo-fi UK rap / Funk / Glitch-pop / Chiptune / 80s synth / jazz snippets / ambient weirdness

Vocal style: Soft female vocal leads, spoken-word segments, kids’ choir textures, AI voiceovers

Production texture: Analog warmth, tape hiss, Nintendo startup sounds, detuned pianos, dreamy flutes

Signature motif: Every song ends with the same motif reversed, creating a full-circle listen if you loop it.
