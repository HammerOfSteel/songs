# Tidy taid

**Labels:** Dragons Breath
**Created:** 2025-07-16
**Edited:** 2025-07-16

---

Make gentle the life of this world
before you let the sun in, mind he wipes his shoes. 

Taid said time don’t walk, it potters,
Like the postman when he knows there’s no daughters.
Taid said, “Don’t rush grief, lad,
It’ll queue up anyhow, like buses from Rhymney bad.”
He fixed his kettle with string and gum,
Made toast on a spade when the toaster was done.
Laughed at weather, “Rain’s just sky cryin’,
Let her weep, she’ll feel better by fryin’.”
He’d sit by the hedge, roll smoke like thought,
Say, “World’s gone daft, but your heart?, it ought.”
He told the sheep things he’d never told Nan,
And winked at the clouds like they were part of the plan.

Tidy, Taid, with your boots by the door,
Jam on your thumb and your thoughts on the floor.
World turns weird, but your tea stays right,
You kept the dark back with a porch light.

Taid said, “Truth’s a goat, stubborn, odd.”
And “No one cries tidy,” which I still find mod.
He had a mug that just said “NO,”
Used it for soup, tea, and sometimes snow.
Gave me pennies he called ‘dragon’s fees’,
Told me crows carry gossip on the breeze.
He’d whisper to dogs like they paid tax,
And knew which clouds meant back pain attacks.
Always a spoon in his pocket, why?
Said “Just in case life serves you pie.”
He knew the ghosts that sat on the stile,
Waved to ‘em like, “Alright? Been a while.”

Tidy, Taid, with your biscuit tin rules,
Said, “Even sadness goes out with the mules.”
Told the fog your secrets at night,
And kissed Nan’s head like it held all light.

He said, “We’re all held together by toast crumbs and tunes,”
And “Don’t rush a sunset, it’s full of runes.”
Told me stars are like sheep in the sky,
Lost from the farm where the gods once lie.
He sat with silence like it was kin,
Didn’t rush grief, just let it in.
Said, “Don’t tell your pain to the telly, bach,”
“Tell the kettle, the chair, or the mountain track.”
He hummed hymns while fixing a chair,
Like the world was breaking but he didn’t care.
I miss that man, his muddy wit,
He’s the reason I still sit and knit,
Words into stories, crumbs into song,
Whispers to sheep who carry it on.

Tidy, Taid, with your heart on your sleeve,
Taught me to stay, taught me to grieve.
Even now, when the kettle hums slow,
I swear he’s in steam, just sayin’ “Hello.”

Time passes. Listen. Time passes.
Come closer now.
Only you can hear the houses sleeping in the streets in the slow deep salt and silent black, bandaged night
