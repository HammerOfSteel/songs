# Verdant close

**Labels:** The Alchemists Estate
**Created:** 2025-11-16
**Edited:** 2025-11-20

---

First principles:
start with what’s in front of us,
this flat, that river, your hair all crushed
against the pillow, curtains half-shut,
sun tryin’ to get in like it’s one of us.

You laugh, say,
“Don’t overthink it, babe, it’s only morning.”
Yeah but look at it, though.
Look at you in it.

Here by the wide river, flats all leaning on the bank,
cheap net curtains doing battle with the light, no thanks.
Kettle doing overtime, paint’s flaked on the sill,
you’re barefoot in my hoodie, asking if I’m real.

Ash in the tray like snow on a small-town street,
your playlist on shuffle, same four songs on repeat.
You’re rolling a cig and you’re rolling your eyes,
“Stop staring, you weirdo, go open the blinds.”

But you let me see you in the in-between glow,
mascara half-fallen, hair saying “I don’t know.”
You shrug like it’s nothing, like this is just clothes,
but this is the softest you Verdant Close knows.

And you go, “This is home, innit?
Bored out our heads but we’re still in it.”
Turn dull little minutes to something worth singin’,
turn Poundland mornings into church bells ringin’.

Youth astute, acute, I clock it when you laugh,
sharp as a thesis, still doodlin’ in the margins of the past.
You call it “nothin’, just a flat and some toast,”
but I swear there’s alchemy in Verdant Close.

Steam on the window draws halos round your face,
you’re talkin’ ‘bout the cosmos while you wipe down the plates.
You’re like, “If it’s all random, how’d we end up here?”
I say, “It’s just buses and dumb luck, my dear.”

But my chest disagrees, does a stupid little skip,
when you dance in the kitchen with a tea towel on your hip.
There’s bills on the table, there’s damp in the wall,
there’s a future in your jumper hangin’ off in the hall.

You point at the river, say, “That’s time goin’ past,
same water, new water, nothin’ ever really lasts.”
I joke, “You been watchin’ too much TikTok philosophy,”
you grin, “Yeah, and now you’re trapped in it with me.”

You say, “Call this home, even if it’s rented,
even if the skirting board’s all chipped and dented.”
We spin bus tickets into sacred texts,
make holy ground out of what comes next.

Youth astute, acute, razor-sharp small talk,
we roast each other gently on the river walk.
You’re like, “It’s dead here,” but your eyes say “close,”
and I choose to believe you in Verdant Close.

First principles:
this glass, that plant you keep forgettin’ to water,
the chipped blue mug that says “World’s Okayest Daughter.”
Your socks on the radiator, my shoes by the door,
two little histories spillin’ on the floor.

You say you’re not deep, then you’re quoting some book,
eyes on the ceiling, stirrin’ beans you never cook.
“I’m just chatting, babes,” you say, shrugging it off,
but your throwaway lines hold the stuff I pray to, soft.

We’re not sorted, not sainted, not TikTok wise,
we’re just two twitchy kids with sleep in our eyes.
But the way your shy grin cuts through my ghost,
that’s the realest thing I’ve seen
on this side of the coast.

So we call this home, even when it aches,
when the river’s in silence and the broadband breaks.
When we’re skint and snappy and the dishes won’t rinse,
and everything’s heavy but your laugh still wins.

Youth astute, acute, we’re clever, we’re a mess,
you’re school-yard divine in your oversized vest.
You say, “It’s just us, babe, it’s nothin’ grandiose,”
but you turn cheap moments into meaning
here in Verdant Close.

Yeah, you turn cheap moments into meaning
here in Verdant Close.

First principles:
you, me, this room, this road, this river.
Curtains drawn, then opened,
sun arguing its way in.

You laugh, that small, sharp,
astute, acute,
cute little laugh,
and the whole place levels up
from “nothing much”

to home.
