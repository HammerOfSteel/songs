"Three Spoon Circus" – breakfast chaos, clatter, love

“Y Byd Bach” (The Little World) - Just you three, a bubble. Language, music, rhythms of your own.

“From Risca With Love” - The idea of where you’re from shaping who you are and how you love. A local anthem with big heart.

"Fika for Three" – stillness in a messy day, banter, fun together, favorite things, a day out, south wales, snug cafe

“Flatpack Arguments (IKEA Blues)” - Assembling furniture, unraveling deeper stuff.
Cheeky vs heart-wrenching contrast.

“Cwtsh o’Clock” A mid-day cuddle break, because life’s hard. Welsh warmth, short and sweet song, almost like a jingle.

"Sidewalk Waltz" – pushing the pram, rhythms of walking and talking

"Yr Hen Gân" – lullaby memories

“BlåhimlaBussen” - Sleepy, surreal, two of you on a night ride after a long day.
Swirly guitar, lullaby chorus.

“Foxes in the Garden” - A quiet night, unexpected wonder. Maybe a bedtime story moment. Feels like a whispered poem over soft guitar.

“A Pint and a Pram” - Trying to be young and wild with a baby in tow. Could be set in a pub garden in Newport.

“philanthropic philosophy” (a real pub name of the pub just outside out cottage in crosskeys) - Nights we spent, events, live music, slow nights, darts, books, philosophy. A pub memories. Great for rowdy energy.

"Pendragon" – A play on Taliesins "The Battle of the Trees" and "The Tale of Taleisin" celebration and ache, sweet in love and lullaby like prose and narrative how grief changes shape over time (it does not get better, but it gets different) modernly told. Told through us all.

An indie rock album in the style of Hot Hot Heat, The Kooks, maybe a touch of The Wombats, Arctic Monkeys, or Peter Bjorn and John — but with your poetic, emotionally rich, and nostalgic storytelling. 

Lyricism & Style:
🎤 Flow & Sound:
Fast-paced, slightly manic delivery in places (a la Hot Hot Heat).

Some tracks built around repeated choruses with shifting meanings.

Others more like story songs — punchy verse, lyrical chorus, a big bridge.

🖋 Language and Poetic Devices:
Blend real speech with poetic flourish. Snappy one-liners, reflective refrains.

Use Swedish and Welsh words like they’re familiar to the listener — no translation needed.

Internal rhyme and bounce — think “I owe you a loaf, a laugh, and a lighter” kind of flow.
