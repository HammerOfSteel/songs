# The Devil’s Challenge - y cythraul bach

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-03-22
**Edited:** 2025-03-22

---

in a horn of sulphur and the cloven myth,
All heaven in the midnight of the sun,
A serpent fiddled in the shaping-time.
y cythraul bach, you may laugh, but we are the voice,
Rising from shadows, reclaiming our choice

In the silence of the morning, he sat beside the bed,
The mirror cracked, his eyes were dim, too heavy in his head.
The carving in the hallway smirked, a grin too wide to bear,
He whispered, "I am nothing now," but still was breathing air.

Oh devil in the wood and wall,
You know my name, you watched me fall.
But I am not your curse to keep,
I’ve walked through fire, I’ve climbed the deep.
Your smile means nothing in this light,
I’m still here—I’m still upright.

The streets of town remembered him, the days before the weight,
Before the voice behind his eyes would twist and simulate.
But someone saw him passing by, and didn’t turn away,
They said, “You don’t have to win the war, just hold on one more day.”

We speak of demons in the dark, but never in the sun,
Yet healing grows where truth is shared—alone, we’re never one.

Oh devil carved from spite and spite,
You haunt the corner of my night.
But every time I choose to speak,
You fade like ash beneath my feet.
I’ve got a name you cannot steal—
It’s not your grin, it’s how I heal.

He reached inside a drawer once locked, and found a folded note,
From someone else who’d seen the dark, and barely stayed afloat.
Their words were cracked but brave and kind, a bridge across despair,
And in that page, he found the will to finally breathe some air.

The voice from deep whispers low and slow
But the sun will rise for those who won’t let go.

So now he stands beside the flame, not scorched but born anew,
The devil’s grin still hangs there still, but now he sees right through.
He walks with others now and speaks of every stone he bore,
And each time someone dares to speak, the devil fades some more.

Oh devil carved in spite and shame,
You’ve lost the power in your name.
I speak my truth, I sing my scars,
I walk with voices, near and far.
And if I fall, I’ll rise again,
In louder steps, with wiser friends.

"We don’t erase the devil’s grin by pretending it was never there.
We outgrow it. Outlive it. Speak louder than it ever dared."
Remember the voice when you fall to the floor.
