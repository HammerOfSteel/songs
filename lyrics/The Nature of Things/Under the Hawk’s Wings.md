# Under the Hawk’s Wings

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-10-25
**Edited:** 2024-10-25

---

[Verse 1]

The hawk’s cry echoes, the children run,
“Under my wings, come all at once!”
Hey that's not fair, don't you know,
Black ain't a color so you cannot go!
So I wait my turn, no color to care,
Round after round, I stand unaware.

The hues are called—Aeron, Celyn, Dewi fly,
Colors flashing beneath the sky.
Yet never what I wear, never called to play,
Watching life drift farther away.

[Chorus]

She stands lumen, like moonlit snow,
Her stillness sings of what I don’t know.
We drift like shadows, side by side,
Flowing through where none abide.
Her feet are heavy, mine can’t stay still—
Together we break the rules they built.

[Verse 2]

Eira, Eira cold and bright,
Waiting alone at the edge of night.
Her shoes have roots; her steps forgot,
While I still run but land in knots.

She says, “You’ll wait until you see—
The game was never meant to be.”
I take her hand, her weight I’ll bear,
Through night and day, we find somewhere.

[Chorus]

She stands lumen, like moonlit snow,
Her stillness sings what I don’t know.
We drift like rivers, side by side,
Flowing through where none abide.
Her feet are heavy, mine can’t stay still—
Together we break the rules they built.

[Bridge]

The sky stayed quiet; no colors were named,
But together, we played without shame.
I’ll carry her steps where they never tread,
She’ll teach me to rest without regret.

Through storms of names—Bryn, Ffion, Sion—we stray,
Walking where no one called our way.
We learn the dance not bound by sound,
Under wings where freedom’s found.

[Outro]

So let them call their colors clear,
While we find skies that disappear.
For night and day will always see,
The hues that hide in mystery.
