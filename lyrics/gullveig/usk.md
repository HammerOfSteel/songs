---

early sun,
the fog whitens,
to an egret.

bog thaw,
the slow lift,
of frogspawn.

Up you go,
A latter of my arms.
Soft little clouds warms,
Form fleeting tiny shapes,
Makes me stutter,
Make me heart shutter.
Each second we spend here,
A snapshot that I fear,
Is all to brief.

Look look,
Lovely roaring river way.
Aye, shall we stay and play,
What do you say?

Come come,
Scoot over here,
Little daffodil dear.
I've got something,
I want you to hear.

See this river runs,
Through these valleys,
Through slate and Fern.
Twist and turns,
And then just like that
It becomes the sea.

Glittering blue eyes look up at me.

Noo way tad!
A river become a sea?

Aye, it does.
Becomes the ocean,
Slivering little river small
Becomes a part of it all.

Flow flow,
Up and throw,
Tell me of the things you know.
River river valley through,
Little star of the Ebbu.
Fox and utter play at dusk,
By the little river Usk.

Turn they red,
The dragons blood,
Rust and iron,
Mud and flood.

Turn they black,
Soot and shale,
Here in the Ebbu vale.

We sit where the bank dips low and green,
Boots half-mudded in the in-between.
Here where the reeds hum slow and thin,
The world feels wide but the moment’s in.
You kick your heels in the river light,
Try to catch a sunbeam, hold it tight.
Skimming stones in a crooked line,
Each little skip like a borrowed time.

I say, “See how the current pulls, my dove,
Like a long soft hand from somewhere above.
Takes every leaf that ever fell,
Carries each secret it’s asked not to tell.”
You say, “Tad, does it forget the way?”
I say, “No, bach, it remembers the bay.
Every bend, every bruise, every fallen tree,
It brings all it was to the lips of the sea.”

“So… it’s like me going to Nain?
I’m still me, but not the same?”

Hehe, aye, exactly that,
Little river in a bigger map.

Flow flow,
Fast then slow,
Whisper what you’ve seen below.
River river, silver through,
Bracken, briar, alder, yew.
Heron waiting, still as dusk,
At the joining with the Usk.

Turn they green,
The moss and rain,
Old stone bridges
Holding pain.

Turn they clear,
When storms are past,
Here in the Ebbu,
Nothing’s last.

Here’s the meeting of the waters,
Love,
Where the fresh and the salt
Argue softly in the mud.
Where one life ends
and another floods.

This is where the hill’s long story,
All its coal and chapel glory,
Washes out in a single sigh,
Into a mouth
that kisses the sky.

They don’t stay separate,
These bits of us, see,
Your laugh, my ache,
our little history.
They swirl, they blend,
they shift, they sway,
Till river and ocean
Can’t tell which way
They once began.

We watch a branch come riding by,
A tiny raft under a wide blue sky.
You say, “Tad, if we sat on that,
Would it take us where the mermaids chat?”
I grin and nod, “Aye, down the line,
Past the cranes and the old coal spine.
Past the docks where the big ships gleam,
Out to the edge of every dream.”

But for now we’re here, on this soft-worn stone,
Two small souls and a river grown.
You trace a heart in the muddy sand,
Print your name with a careful hand.
Then the water reaches, patient, slow,
Tugs at the edges, lets it go.
Not to erase, but to carry, love,
To write it later in the foam above.

“So if it goes, it isn’t gone?
The river sings it in a song?”

Aye cariad, that’s how it goes,
Everything taken
in the tide still flows.

Flow flow,
High and low,
Tell her what I can’t quite show.
River river, valley through,
Hold this little star for me too.
Fox and otter, dusk-soft hush,
Guide her gently to the Usk.

Turn they gold,
The evening light,
Hills lean in
To wish us goodnight.

Turn they wide,
Where fresh meets foam,
Here in the meeting,
You’ll find home.

cold sunset,
a boy hurls back,
an eel

dawn fog,
gull by gull,
the estuary.
