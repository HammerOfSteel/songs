Lemon and a pinch,
pull parsly from its stem,
frayed fingers playing with the hem.
I swear I had it,
Give it a moment it,
just needs to set.
Why do I always forget.

I swear it wasnt me, it wasnt!
Doesnt, mostly matter what you'd say.
It would matter mostly if you could just stay.
 Hey little bird, tell me today,
Tell me you are okay.
Tell me of your day in the sway,
Swoon over small things up there,
Lay on me all you now hold dear,
don't shed a single tear,
I promise you,
all this I can bear,
why else would I still be here.
If you will,
land on my sholder soft and still,
no objective to fullfil,
no words to distil.
Aye, little daffodil,
bright, true.
Come to me,
or i'll,
come to you.

 
Drenched in under the shoot,
we scoot closer, closer.

Plink and plunk,
the rain is drunk,
dunk on an endless terain,
drunk of its own refrain.
I shall obstain.
From my hearts desire,
I shall not be burnt by this fire.

Soft is the sink light, yellow and low,
shadow on the tiles like an old black crow.
 Kettle in a slow-boil, loyal and late,
whistling worries at the kitchen gate.


I stack plates quiet, one, then two,
pretend the empty chairs are full of you.
 Little ghost giggles round the cupboard door,
socked feet skidding on the lino floor.
 

And I say, it’s fine, I’m fine, of course,
as the old pulse pulls like a tidal force.
 There’s a hush in the pipes only I seem to know, that familiar, invisible, undertow.

Drenched in under the shoot,
we scoot closer, closer.
Plink and plunk,
the rain is drunk,
spilling old names down the windowpane,
 tracing each letter then gone again.
I shall obstain.

From the softest choir,
of every what-if set on fire.

Calm seas hunger for more,
They do not care for what is in store.
They crave the telling of what came before,
 the break, the fall, the closing door.

Aye, listen it’s never the wave you see that takes you, it’s the one beneath, all neat and polite, that shakes you.

The past doesn’t knock, it seeps,
through hairline cracks and midnight bleeps, through smells of lemon, iron, rain,
through someone laughing like her again.

You call it memory, love, regret,
but it’s all water, love, just wet.
And it pulls.
It always pulls.

Look at me, pretending I can float,
chin up high in a borrowed coat.
Smile stitched on like a sunday best,
heart doing cartwheels in my chest.

I talk about the weather, safe and slow,
 while the deep says, come, there’s more below.

It hums in the floorboards under my feet,
a bassline dark in a backroom beat.

And every calm i carry in my face,
has a riptide coiled, just out of place.
Still, i stand, in the doorway’s glow,
making peace with the pull of the undertow.

Drenched in under the shoot,
we scoot closer, closer.
Plink and plunk,
the night is drunk,
sunk in a glass by the kitchen sink,
thoughts line up and refuse to blink.
I shall obstain.
From the sharp desire,
to vanish whole in the quiet choir.

Calm seas hunger for more,
always have, evermore.
They don’t ask if you’re ready to go,
they just lean in with an undertow.

So if you hear me, little bird, little flame,
 know I stayed, I stayed.
I stayed in your name.
And if i go under, let it be so,
that love was the weight,
not the undertow.
