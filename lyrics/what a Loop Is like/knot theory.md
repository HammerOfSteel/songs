# knot theory

**Labels:** what a Loop Is like
**Created:** 2025-09-04
**Edited:** 2025-09-04

---

Dawn ties its laces while the kettle counts to three.
If the night came loose, let morning loop you back to be.

Curtains breathe, a seam of sun unspools along the wall,
shoelace thoughts in crossing knots, the smallest rise-and-fall.
Coffee steam, a quiet hymn, the rim of day made round,
threaded spine of little things that stitch you to the ground.
What holds a life?, a slip, a bow, a loop that doesn’t brag;
a patient pull that tucks the fray back under like a flag.
Name what stayed and name what strayed, then let the naming rest;
sometimes mercy’s just a finger pressed against your chest.

Try as you might, taking the fight, morning awake, an ache you can’t shake;
don’t worry, it’s “only” the world at stake.

Will you tie a knot, knot, not forgot?
Do you hold the string, untangling?
Thread of story, learn to see
all that lives between you and me.
Three is tied, tide in time,
undone by love and nursery rhyme.
Spooled by hands, by all demands,
by grit and grim and all of it, still stand.

How do you hold what you were told when truth has split in two?
A bow remembered from a child’s shoe can still remember you.
Left over right, then right through light, the catechism small;
practice is the sacrament of not forgetting all.
A braid of breath, a brace of names, a brace that doesn’t bind,
a brace like bracken underfoot that springs and changes mind.
If shame is thread too tightly wound, it kinks and cuts the skin;
ease the tension, let it sing, and let the soft begin.

Try as you might, taking the fight, morning remakes the shape you break;
don’t worry, it’s only everything the day will ask you stake.

Will you tie a knot, knot, not forgot?
Do you lead the line through tangling?
Story’s fiber, let it be
warp and weft of you and me.
Three is tied, tide in time,
count it like a skipping rhyme.
Spooled by hands, by all demands,
by loss, by luck, still make, still mend.

Braid what breaks, splice what stays,
loop the loose in lighter ways.
If the morning asks, say “yes”, 
not to more, but to less:
less of knot that chokes the throat,
more of knot that keeps the coat.

So come close, be brave and ask, don’t leave it in the past;
the present is a patient loom that loves the threads that last.
Teach your hands the gentle math: two ends, one kindly pull;
the bow remembers how to bow, embarrassment to lull.
Hold the string, then loosen it, let daylight through the twist;
make room for what you couldn’t say, and what you almost missed.
If a name you cannot speak keeps snagging at the seam,
hum it softly into cloth until it turns to dream.

Try as you might, taking the fight, morning will take your weight and wake;
don’t worry, it’s only the courage to rise for your own sake.

I will tie a knot, knot, not forgot,
I will hold the string, untangling.
Thread of story, let me see
every bridge from you to me.
Three is tied, tide in time,
count it with a nursery rhyme.
Spooled by hands, by all demands,
by grit, by grace, I’ll stand, expand.

If the knot slips, breathe and tie it twice, then let it lie.
Morning binds you back to life; bow once, then try.
