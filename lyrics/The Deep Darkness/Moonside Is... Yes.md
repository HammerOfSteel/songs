# Moonside Is... Yes

**Labels:** The Deep Darkness
**Created:** 2025-04-17
**Edited:** 2025-04-17

---

“Welcome to Moonside. No, yes.
The time is color. The place is... sideways.
You are here. Or are you?”

I walked into town and the streetlights blinked,
One said “yes,” the next said “think.”
The air was pink, the sun was square,
Someone whispered, “You’re already there.”

Guy at the bar wore shades at night,
Said, “I’m your cousin, but only in light.”
Ordered a coffee, got a spoon and a scream,
This ain’t a city, it’s a sideways dream.

Say yes, say yes, it’s easier.
Lie to the mirror, it sees you clearer.
Paint the truth in disco tones,
Forget the world that cracked your bones.

I met a lamp that said, “Name your fear,”
I said, “The silence.” It said, “Very near.”
Signs point left, but the roads curl right,
Shadows wear name tags. I flinch from the light.

He said, “You can leave, just find the man.”
But everyone’s the man, and no one ran.
I sat on a chair that turned into a clown —
This place’s a joke, but I can’t sit down.

There’s a shop with hearts in a discount bin,
Where “yes” costs more than original sin.
I paid in teeth, got a map of lies,
It says I’m home, but the moon’s my size.

Asked a girl, “Are we real?” She smiled,
Said, “Only at night, and only mild.”
I laughed too long, the clock rewound,
This town’s a loop — no up, no ground.

Then I saw myself in a plastic suit,
Told me, “Jump. You got no roots.”
But I stood still, let the silence speak,
And in that hush, the static leaked.

Said, “Moonside isn’t real, it’s reflection skin.
A way to pretend you didn’t let them in.”
So I said no. The lights blinked twice.
The city cracked — and let in ice.

The man in the club turned, smiled slow.
“You made it through.”
I said, “No.”
He nodded. “Yes.”

Moonside fades when you name the lie.
And you just did.

(It’s okay to say no.)
