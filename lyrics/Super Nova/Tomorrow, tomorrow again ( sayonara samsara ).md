# Tomorrow, tomorrow again ( sayonara samsara )

**Labels:** Super Nova
**Created:** 2025-06-14
**Edited:** 2025-06-14

---

Does the caterpillar know that one day it will be a butterfly?
Does the tadpole know that it will grow into a frog?
Did I know that I would become a human when I was an embryo?

From a thoughtless combination of cells
Awareness came out of the darkness
The universe experiencing itself
Witness the beauty of life's kiss
We know not where we came from
Nor do we know why
We don't know where we're going
There is no need to cry

Cawl on cool, kettle bubble,
Her hair is a storm, oh here comes trouble.
Cupboard door’s off, bin bag’s breathin’,
and I’ve got ten missed calls from a week I ain’t speakin’.

School form unsigned, sandwich still warm,
wrapped like a relic in foil and scorn.
Aye, brush ya teeth little miss, in signs of my fingers I throw her a kiss.

Mornings is mud with a halo on top,
Over and over it does not stop.
Bitten, pardon the kitten, gone awry already smitten.
save me that cwtsh, wonderful warm,
For days when the windows shake from the storm.

Slivering silver in the grout and love in the tap,
and I hold it all close in this apron wrap.

Ain’t that bad, oh that's ashame
But I remember, I keep the flame,
Steady and warm, in loves true name.
Tima Elara, littlest star.
How, I wonder, wonder where you are.

Tomorrow, tomorrow again — I rise.
A soul in socks with sleep in my eyes.
No need for why, no need for when,
I just begin — tomorrow again.

Her name ain’t mine, not on paper or page,
but I’d burn down fate just to soften her rage.
Ain’t birthed her bones but I’ve carried her breath,
held her tears through Lego and death.

I wipe jam with the same hands that shook,
when her sister stopped breathing, and I couldn’t look.
Still pack her bag like a knight packs swords,
folded notes, small snacks, invisible chords.

There’s gods in the lunchbox, lore in the sock,
her hair's a theology of twist and clock.
I got no medals. No kin to cheer.
Just bedtime stories and cheap root beer.

And still — I vow, not loud but true,
each cereal crumb is a thing I do.
Not for show. Not for ghost applause.
Just ‘cause she’s here — and that’s the cause.

Ouroboros spins in the washing machine,
a ring of days that rinse me clean.
She calls it “magic” when the bubbles rise,
and I nod — ‘cause it is, init, no lies.

The green lion sleeps in her Gwentish socks,
and calcination happens in the school drop box.
I seen our sulphur in the burnt toast line,
and her eyes hold salt like a sacred sign.

We're dragons blood boil, mid-fold and fall,
a lap full o’ plastic and love overall.
I stir the tea deosil, soft clockwise pace,
and whisper small truths through in shape of grace.

Rebirth ain’t fire — it’s her sleepy kiss,
it’s the pile of toys that smells like bliss.
The lap she climbs to name a star,
with a voice that knows just who we are.

Sayonara, samsara, no loop round the bend,
Boes bows way to lose, no need to pretend.

Super Nova constellation,
All you need for a spell is
- imagination.

Thus spoke Zarathustra, or was it me as kid.
watching the watcher before I hid.

Ain’t askin’ for heaven, impossible things, just toast and a grin.
Tomorrow, tomorrow again — we begin.

my favourite pair of genes.

If we chose our samsara
If I could bring you back
and you could bring me back,
I'd do this again.

And again.

I wouldn't change a single thing about you.

I wonder how many lives I've already spent loving you?
