# Nowhere Differentiable

**Labels:** Koch star
**Created:** 2024-12-06
**Edited:** 2024-12-11

---

“I didn’t mean it,” you softly say,
Tracing patterns in the rain today.
Your eyes search mine, but I can’t see,
The lines between your pain and me.

“It’s fine,” I say, “Just a little bruise,
We all fall down, we win, we lose.”
But you don’t answer, your gaze retreats,
A silent rhythm my words can’t meet.
I wrap a bandage around the skin,
But not the ache that lives within.

You’re a curve I can’t define,
Each turn more jagged than the line.
I trace the edge, but it won’t hold,
It splits apart, it’s infinite, cold.
Yet in the bends, there’s something true,
A map of worlds I can’t break through.

“It’s fine,” I say, “Everyone feels this way,
A passing storm, it won’t stay.”
But you don’t hear me, your fingers tight,
Draw circles in the fading light.
The words I speak are made of glass,
They break before they ever pass.

You’re a curve I can’t define,
Each turn more jagged than the line.
I trace the edge, but it won’t hold,
It splits apart, it’s infinite, cold.
Yet in the bends, there’s something true,
A map of worlds I can’t break through.

“It’s not your fault,” the silence breathes,
“This jagged path was meant for me.
Your words don’t fit; they try, they fall,
I’m a shape you can’t trace at all.”
I try to see, but I just can’t stay,
The edges cut, they drift away.

“It’s fine,” I say, though it’s not the truth,
The weight of love, the weight of youth.
I can’t repair what’s not mine to mend,
But I’ll hold the pieces, I’ll still pretend.
Even if I can’t trace the line,
I’ll be here for you, every time.

You’re a curve I can’t define,
Each turn more jagged than the line.
I trace the edge, but it won’t hold,
It splits apart, it’s infinite, cold.
Yet in the bends, there’s something true,
A map of worlds I can’t break through.

“I didn’t mean it,” you softly say,
Tracing patterns in the rain today.
Your eyes search mine, and now I see,
The shape of your pain, though it’s not for me.
