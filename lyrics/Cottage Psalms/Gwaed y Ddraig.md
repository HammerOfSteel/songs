# Gwaed y Ddraig

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-04-23
**Edited:** 2025-04-23

---

She skips by the river, boots half-tied
Mud on her cuffs, arms open wide

“Dad, did you paint the river red?”

“No, bach, it’s just the dragon’s shed —
A drop for each time we forgot to sing
And coal dust dreams from a rusted wing.”
She squints up skyward, brow all creased

“Do dragons get tired when the singing’s ceased?”

“That’s why the black runs thick below —
It’s not just sludge, it’s dragon’s woe.”
She gasps, then hums a tiny tune
Skipping past nettles, under the moon

“Should we sing him back to light?”

Her voice is cracked, but her grip is tight
I say, “Yes, love — you go first”
She roars a note, and the river bursts

Red is for remembering, black is for sleep
We walk where the dragons once dared to weep
If you hear humming, don’t be scared
It’s just a girl and her dad, singing repaired
Singing the metal back into bloom
Singing her mother out of the gloom

“Did Mum see this river too?”

I nod. “She called it ‘the old wound's blue.’”

“She should come with next time then.”

“She will, she is — in every bend.”
She kneels to touch the water’s skin
It mirrors stars and coal within
And in her hand, a dragon’s scale —
A rusted bolt with a wishing tale

“You see, love, this water remembers.
Even when we don’t.
And when it runs red — it’s not angry.
It’s just trying to say what we forgot.”

“Then I’ll remember double.”

Red is for remembering, black is for sleep
We’re the ones the dragon chose to keep
She sings, I walk, we both believe
The river loves those who dare to grieve
And when the sun spills through the mud —
We call it gold
We call it
dragon’s blood.
