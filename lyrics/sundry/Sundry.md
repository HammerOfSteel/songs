# Sundry

**Labels:** Album, sundry
**Created:** 2026-01-03
**Edited:** 2026-01-12

---

The Narrative Core: The journey of a mind trying to navigate a "saturated" world. It’s the friction between the Will to Know (which leads to burnout and fragmentation) and the Will to Forget (which leads to dissociation). The "wholeness" isn't found in solving the puzzle, but in standing comfortably in the middle of the "Sundry" mess.

Musical Aesthetic (The Arcadian Wild Style):
Intricate Layering: Fast, "math-folk" acoustic guitar patterns that feel like a clock ticking too fast.

Vocal Counterpoint: Three-part harmonies where each voice says something slightly different, mimicking the "wall of voices" in a crowded room.

The "Blur" Effect: High-energy, clean plucking that occasionally washes out into huge, shimmering reverb-heavy sections (the "fading out" moment).

Songs:
1. "Well-ness"
The Vibe: Fast, frantic, percussive acoustic guitar. Think "the arcadian wild" but dont use band names or so to dewscribe the music style, rather comma separated words.
The Story: Being in a public place (like a train station) and the sensory overload causes a "fade out." The noise becomes a protective cocoon.
The Narrative: The "Denial" phase—choosing the static because the reality is too sharp.

2. "Indeterminate Number"
The Vibe: Playful but slightly "off-kilter" mandolin and violin duels.
The Story: Trying to count or categorize everything (emotions, regrets, grocery lists) to stay grounded, but the numbers stop making sense.
The Narrative: The desperate attempt at "Understanding" through logic, grasping and clining to small structures of shopping lists, calendars, homework etc.

3. "The Static Altar"
The Vibe: Warm, resolving, Irish-indie folk. 
The Story: Looking at you looking at a TV with no signal , gray pixels (ants in an ant stack)
The Philosophy: Negative Theology—finding the "Holy" in the blank space where the signal should be.

4. "Overexposed"
The Vibe: Slow, delicate, and "hazy" strings that gradually speed up into a panic.
The Story: A conversation where the other person is talking, but their words are batched into ballons just hovering around. The feeling of "flickering" in and out of existence.
The Narrative: The peak of dissociation—the "unstable" equilibrium, not working towards integration.

5. "The Patchwork Gnosis"
The Vibe: Warm, resolving, Irish-indie folk.
The Story: Accepting that the self is just a collection of random, sundry parts. No grand design, just the beauty of the mess.
The Narrative: The "Acceptance" phase—wholeness through the surrender of the need to understand, devotional and spiritual undetones mixed in with mentalhealth.

6. "Zero-Point Prayer"
The Story: This is the Negative Theology song. It’s about praying to the absence of things. Not praying for help, but praying for the "nothing" to be enough.
Imagery: An empty flask, a white sheet of paper, a silent room after a loud party, the "statue" of a shadow.
The Vibe: Very sparse and "hollow." Lots of open-string drones and long, bowed cello notes, think apocalyptica/HIM.
Alignment: True Neutral — The point of complete surrender where neither good nor evil matters.

7. "Great Un-Knowing"
The Story: The active choice to Deny. Living at a tragedy or a complex system, not understanding barely scratching the surface, facing the chaos that ensues.
Narrative perhaps det at a UK festival, youth, teenage. Dissociation, frustration, fear and safety etc.
Imagery: Closing your eyes in a bright room, hands over ears, a black-out curtain, burning a map so you don't have to know where you are.
The Vibe: UK indie like hot hot hear, Anthemic and driving. A "don't-care" swagger. It sounds like a celebration, but the lyrics are about closing the doors of perception.
Alignment: Chaotic Good — Denying the reality to protect the "Spirit."

8. "Parallax"
The Story: How the "Manifold" shifts when you change your perspective. If you move your head, the world looks different. If you stand on a chair, the room is new.
Imagery: Looking out a train window at the foreground vs. the background, a kaleidoscope, shifting shadows on a brick wall.
The Vibe: Complex, interlocking vocal parts (like a round) where the lyrics change slightly depending on which singer you listen to.
The Narrative: Going away together as a couple for the first time. Not just friends. Daring to show me yourself and rely on me as I rely on you. Lyrics in the style of that youthfull feel and complexity but with devotional undercurrents and tones. Lyrics in the style of and influences from hot hot heat, the arcadian wilds 
Alignment: Neutral — The simple physics of perception.

9. "Dead Reckoning"
The Story: Navigation without a compass.Photons The idea of a plant needing light to grow - but how do the photons actually hit the leafs again all probability: how can they get enought light to grow - the idea of the collaps of the super position: the collaps into the only possible state, the exact right state - like running blind folded through a forest and never hitting a tree.  how to navigate by "feel" and "guesses." It’s about the terrifying freedom of being completely lost and finally stopping the search for "North. "
Imagery: Finding the way home together, despite being totally lost : also as a metaphor for not having any idea about where one is going, trusting the wind.
The Vibe: Tense and building. A constant, driving kick-drum (the heartbeat) with a "math-folk" violin picked strings melody that keeps shifting keys, never quite settling.
Alignment: True Neutral — Accepting that "direction" is an illusion in a world this diverse and sundry.

10. "The Sundry Ledger"
The Story: A desperate attempt to "account" for every minute. The character keeps a notebook of every mundane, then looking back at this later finds the tapestry: both about changing perspective and actually starting the "great work".
Imagery: Receipts fluttering in the wind, a pen running out of ink, columns of numbers that don't add up, the "white space" at the bottom of a page.
The Vibe: Ultra-fast, rhythmic acoustic guitar and mandolin that sounds like a frantic typewriter. The vocals are breathless and rapid-fire.
Alignment: Lawful Good — The belief that order and "doing the work" will eventually bring the soul back home.
