# Cân y Gwynt (The Song of the Wind)

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-02-12
**Edited:** 2025-03-05

---

"Hush now, little one. Can you hear it? The wind hums through the trees, singing the same song it has always sung. It carries whispers from far away, voices of those who once stood where you stand, who once held hands as you do now. Close your eyes, listen well. The wind remembers… and it wants you to remember too."

The wind calls soft through valley deep,
Lulling the hills as the rivers sleep.
It hums through branches, old and wise,
Brushing dreams across the skies.

Cân y gwynt, cân y galon, (Song of the wind, song of the heart)
Sibrwd hiraeth yn y nos. (Whispering longing in the night.)
Drifting through the autumn leaves,
Bringing voices on the breeze.

A child once stood where you now stay,
Laughed and ran, then slipped away.
Yet still their laughter lingers near,
Carried by the wind so clear.

Cân y gwynt, cân y galon,
Dal yr olau, dal y gobaith. (Hold the light, hold the hope.)
Whisper soft and I will hear,
All the love that brought me here.

Some are lost, yet not alone,
The wind still sings to guide them home.
Through ancient stones and restless tide,
It carries love that never dies.

Cân y gwynt, cân y galon,
Rhwng y dydd a'r nos yn bod. (Between the day and night we are.)
Soft as dawn, yet strong as sea,
Love still whispers back to me.

"Listen close, little one. The wind is always singing. And when you hear it call your name, know that you are never truly alone."
