# great un-knowing

**Labels:** sundry
**Created:** 2026-01-11
**Edited:** 2026-01-11

---

We went on the train and we sat in a row,
To the place where the cold-water salt-breezes blow.
The paper on the table had a "Tragedy" headline,
But we used it to draw a big, wobbly red line.
We’re looking for shells and we’re looking for glass,
While the "History" feelings all shiver and pass.
Mum’s got the bag and she’s got the sun-cream,
We’re living inside of a "Sundry" daydream.
We don’t know the names of the men in the news,
We’re too busy shaking the sand from our shoes.

Oh, we’re burning the map in the wind and the spray,
We don't want to know how to get home today!
We’re hands-over-ears when the arcade gets loud,
Just a hive of "Un-Knowing" in a seaside crowd.
We’re safe in the "Nothing" tonight.

[Musical Section 1 - The Arcade Glitch]

[The guitars play a series of fast, "bleeping" staccato notes that sound like a 1990s arcade game. The rhythm is frantic but joyful, capturing the sensory overload of a pier.]

[Verse 2]

[The rhythm stays driving; the vocals are even more "child-like" in their simple rhyming]
We ate greasy chips and we sat on the wall,
Watching the seagulls and watching the ball.
Someone was shouting 'bout a "Systemic Fail,"
But we’re watching a tiny and slow-moving snail.
The world is a "Manifold" mess on a screen,
But we’re licking the drips of a mint-margarine.
It’s too much to think and it’s too much to say,
So we’re closing our eyes in the bright light of day.
Mum says "Stay close" and we stay in her shadow,
While the rest of the world turns to dust in the meadow.

[Chorus 2]

[The three-part harmonies kick in—shouting and euphoric, but with that underlying "Denial" edge]

Oh, we’re burning the map in the wind and the spray,
We don't want to hear what the grown-ups all say!
We’re hands-over-ears when the speakers all blow,
In the holy sanctuary of the "Great Un-Know."
We’re safe in the "Nothing" tonight.

[Bridge]

[The music drops into a "Hot Hot Heat" style breakdown—just bass, drums, and a very jagged, syncopated guitar stab.]

We don't look at the clock!
We don't look at the time!
The "Great Un-Knowing" is a victimless crime!
Close your eyes!
Count to ten!
Don't ever look at us again!

[Verse 3]

[The full "Arcadian Wild" style intricate picking returns, but it’s played on electric guitars to keep the indie-swagger]

The pier is a temple of plastic and neon,
A "Sundry" religion we’re happy to be on.
If we barely scratch at the surface of things,
We won't feel the bite or the burn or the stings.

Dissociation is a bucket and spade,
A fortress of sand that we’ve carefully made.
It’s "Chaotic Good" to just look at the tide,
With nowhere to run and with nowhere to hide.

Mum’s calling us back as the sun starts to sink,
But we’re staying "Un-Learned" on the edge of the brink.

[Chorus 3]

[Maximum energy—the guitars are a "Wall of Sound" and the hive-vocals are a triumphant shout]

Oh, we’re burning the map in the wind and the spray,
We’re never gonna look at the "World" the same way!
We’re hands-over-ears when the sirens all blow,
In the beautiful grace of the "Great Un-Know."
Yeah, the "Nothing" is keeping us right.

[Outro]

[The song ends abruptly on a "clean" guitar chord. The sound of waves crashing and distant children laughing is heard, but it’s processed with a "hollow" reverb that slowly dissolves into white noise.]

[Music cuts out]
We don't want to know...
we don't want to know...

[Silence]
