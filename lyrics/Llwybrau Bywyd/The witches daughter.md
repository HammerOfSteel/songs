# The witches daughter

**Labels:** Llwybrau Bywyd
**Created:** 2024-12-20
**Edited:** 2024-12-20

---

In the dew-kissed dawn where the whispers lie,
Through golden fields beneath the sky,
A mother and child, in the morning’s grace,
Step lightly where the sun leaves its trace.

The witch and her daughter walk soft in the haze,
Through meadows of gold in the newborn rays,
Plucking the light from the sunflower’s crown,
Where the first sunbeam gently bends down.

The morning sings in colors wide,
Where the earth and sky in peace collide,
The world awakens, its breath runs deep,
And all that was lost begins to keep.

Beyond the hill, the wild horses run,
Through the amber glow of the rising sun,
With a hum on her lips and a song so sweet,
The mother calls them with the rhythm’s beat.

For the world bends to the truest sound,
Where the heart sings clear, and the soul is unbound.

The wind carries whispers through fields of gold,
Where secrets of freedom and stories unfold,
The wild takes shape in the song’s pure flight,
And the day is woven from shadow and light.

They gallop through fields, with the wind in their hair,
Feeling the earth as it breathes everywhere,
Until the glade, where the forest hums low,
And the witch kindles fire with the name it must know.

“Speak its name, let it rise and ignite,
For fire’s truth is bound to the light.”
The daughter whispers, the flames come alive,
With her voice, the spirits begin to thrive.

In the glade, they sip from the herbal brew,
The mother teaching what the wise always knew,
That the world will answer, in its quiet refrain,
If you walk with its song, through joy and through pain.

The wind carries whispers through fields of gold,
Where secrets of freedom and stories unfold,
The wild takes shape in the song’s pure flight,
And the day is woven from shadow and light.

As the sun sets low, they return to the hearth,
Where the son waits near with the hunt’s hard-won worth,
In the twilight’s glow, she opens the book,
And they listen to tales as the starlight is shook.

Of dreaming shores and shaping hands,
Of time’s great holes where the starlight bends,
The witch and her children, in twilight’s dome,
Weaving their magic, in the heart of their home.
