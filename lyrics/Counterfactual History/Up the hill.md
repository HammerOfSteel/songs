# Up the hill

**Labels:** Counterfactual History
**Created:** 2025-07-03
**Edited:** 2025-07-03

---

Didn’t feel brave.
Didn’t feel like fate.
Just my hands,
shakin’ under the weight.

No prophecy.
No trumpet call.
Just her, breathin’,
too small to fall.

Could’ve stayed sat, back bent to the fire,
Cold tea sippin’, throat tight with the mire.
Could’ve let the silence finish its verse,
But her breath hummed soft in that canvas yurt.
She’s two years new, I’m twice that in grief,
A man split in half by a year of belief.
But the letter, blank, bold, near ripped in the fold,
Whispered: “Tonight’s not the one to let hold.”
So I stood.
Like my bones forgot how.
Unzipped time, let the night show me how.
She stirred once, said “Papa?” with a tilt,
And the world cracked open where regret had been built.
Lifted her up, blanket and all,
Her warmth like truth pressed into the fall.

Take her up / Take her there
Don’t wait for stars to say it’s fair
One small step / past the chill
Carry love up that hill
Don’t explain / just begin
She’s your answer to the sin
You don’t know
But she’s still warm

Feet on frost, road like a hymn,
Sky wore silver like hope grown thin.
She nestled close, dreamin’ light,
Like she knew this time we’d fight the night.
I mouthed prayers in no one’s name,
Just rhythms in breath and feet in flame.
Past the stile, past the slate,
Past the hour where I sealed our fate.
Didn’t say much, lips too dry,
Only words: “We’re goin’ bye-bye.”
She laughed once, thumb in her mouth,
And I felt my knees almost give out.
Not 'cause of weight, nah, she’s small as rain,
But ‘cause every heartbeat hurt the same.
This was it.
Undo or do.
No hero moves.
Just follow through.

She whispered, “cwtsh.”
And I broke in half.
But this time, I bled a path.

Take her up / take her through
This might not fix all of you
But maybe her / maybe now
You’ll bend the story anyhow
No trumpet / no gold
Just a girl in arms you hold
You don’t know
But she still dreams

Gate creaked slow like an old man's laugh,
Fog rolled low, moon cut in half.
The hill’s not steep, but tonight it wept,
Every step a secret I never kept.
She said “Stars, Papa!” and I looked up too,
Saw myself from before in a sky too blue.
Nah, not fate, not mystic dread,
Just a bloke and his girl in a world half-dead.
I swear I felt the timeline split,
Where grief used to scream, now silence sits.
The barn light blinked, I pushed through the field,
Heart like thunder but soul unsealed.
A hand reached out, not mine, no jest,
Just kindness, warm, in a wool-clad vest.
Took her in, gave me nod, no name.
No questions. Just saved the flame.

Take her up / to where it breaks
To the cusp where choice remakes
No more waitin’ / no more rot
This is all the time you've got
If she smiles / if she wakes
If the morning finally shakes
Then you’ll know
She still belongs

I fell down after, couldn’t quite stand.
Laughed through sobs with dirt in my hand.
Felt like birth, felt like loss,
Felt like tossin' years across the moss.
But she’s safe, mate. Tucked in right.
With her star-shaped nightlight holdin' the night.
I’m still broken, don’t mistake it.
Still wake at four from dreams that fake it.
But I saw the branch bend back toward green,
And I swear on her breath, I’ve seen the scene.
A thousand endings.
One road that stayed.
And tonight,
I finally played.

We don’t get do-overs.
Just moments that rhyme.
Just flickers of mercy
between seconds of time.
But this, this was mine.
And now it’s hers.

There’s a stone Buddha, they say,
in a field that shifts with fog.
Sometimes it dances.
Only when no one looks.
Only when the child is safe.
