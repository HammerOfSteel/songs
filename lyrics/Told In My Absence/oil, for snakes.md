# oil, for snakes

**Labels:** Told In My Absence
**Created:** 2025-10-21
**Edited:** 2025-10-21

---

Friction is faithful; polish is pain,
stones learn patience by talking to rain.
Stillness is moving, watch how it aches,
quiet combusts in the coil of the snake.
Cling is a cage; palm is a door,
gold turns greedy when asking for more.
Change is a chisel; statues will cry, 
marble remembers the sound of the sky.
You want a blossom that bless the bruise;
All that takes the waters blues.
Heat is a teacher; sparks are its notes,
iron recites them in shimmering throats.
Breath is a lantern, ribs are the slats,
open a little and light finds its path.

Hush at the hinge where the moment breaks,
oil for the wound, oil for snakes.

Shed it, let it, leave what shakes;
scales fall soft like evening flakes.
Pain’s the polish, ache remakes;
bow to the rub that goodness takes.
Lean through, clean through, heart un-brakes,
turn in the turning oil for snakes.

Nothing is nothing; even the void
hums like a hive we haven’t employed.
Grief is a grit in the oyster of time,
pearls are the patience that gathers a rhyme.
Name it “becoming,” not “being at last,”
flame grows fluent by feeding on past.
Sorrow is sand in the gears of the day,
oil it with mercy and watch it give way.
Habit is harbor, also a chain;
tide writes truth on the rust of the chain.
Wanting is weather: let it arrive,
let it evacuate, leave you alive.

Breathe at the seam where the body shakes,
soften the skin with oil for snakes.

Shed it, spread it, ease the stakes;
skin sings loose and terror breaks.
Friction is scripture, mind awakes;
read with your ribs till clamor slakes.
Turn by returning; song unakes,
move in the moving oil for snakes.

All things borrowed,
let them go.

All hearts hollow,
Know their glow.

Dukkha’s the rubbing,
balm is breath.

Clutching is doubling,
loosen loosen to let.

The self is a charge the season shakes;
wear it kindly,
Weather and wake

Edges are tutors; soften, don’t shun,
blade finds benevolence under the sun.
Rupture is ritual; peel and repeat,
every relinquishing rinses your feet.
Loss is a latitude; grief is a ground,
map it in footsteps, not in a wound.
Silence is sandpaper, smoothing the plea;
wishes stop screaming when taught how to be.
the walnut learning to split,
the lock knows its grit.
Form loves falling; falling makes room,
emptiness blooms in the breath of the room.

Kneel to the now as the shell unlakes,
slick the hinge with oil for snakes.

Shed it, bless it, give what breaks;
love is the shape relinquishing takes.
Friction to fiction, self unmakes;
space becomes music the moment shakes.
Hands like rivers, mind like lakes,
move with the molting, oil for snakes.

oil… for snakes.
