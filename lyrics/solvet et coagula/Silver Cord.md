# Silver Cord

**Labels:** solvet et coagula
**Created:** 2025-04-22
**Edited:** 2025-04-22

---

“Didn’t feel like anything special, that morning.”
“Kettle whistled. Mouse clicked. My socks didn’t match.”
“And then it all just... slipped.”

Woke up late—beans on toast, lukewarm.
Slack’s full, Git’s a mess, rain on the dormer.
New hoodie, old trainers, still scuffed from Tescos.
9:37. Rushed out. Didn’t matter, though.

Door clicks shut, headphones in, world small.
Thought about my mum—her birthday text, still stalled.
Bus rounds the bend, bird lifts, sky grey.
And just like that... the world fell away.

I was halfway through a thought when the sky went still.
Left my tea on the counter, and my soul on the hill.
You don’t hear the end—it just forgets to arrive.
But part of me... is still alive.

First thing I see is... me. Bit awkward, that.
Like catching your own face on Tesco's cam.
Body’s there, still holding the phone. Slack pings.
I float like an app in background mode—no strings.

Silver thread hums. Thin. Trembling. Real.
Like spider silk stitched with all I didn’t feel.
Tried to shout—voice came out fog.
Guy on the bike just... phased through my jog.

I was halfway through a thought when the sky went still.
Left my tea on the counter, and my soul on the hill.
You don’t hear the end—it just forgets to arrive.
But part of me... is still alive.

It hums like static on an old cassette,
Like thoughts I never voiced and still regret.
It’s silver, but not like coins or rings—
It’s breath and light, a thread that sings.

It pulled from chest to spine to air,
Then flickered once, like a whispered prayer.
Didn’t snap—just sighed, just slid—
And I fell from the world I thought I did.

Like all I was peeled from skin,
But gentle—like a tide coming in.
I stood between the cracks of clocks,
One foot here. One in the not.

And Bristol? Still grey. Still wet.
Bus driver cried, bless him, full of regret.
A woman whispered something like a hymn,
And I tried to thank her. My hand passed in.

The world kept ticking, indifferent, kind.
I wandered like a ghost still wired to mind.
No fire, no gate, no robe, no trial—
Just streets I knew, tuned to a different dial.

The pigeons still strutted like lords of Rome.
The chip shop lit up like I’d made it home.
Only thing missing was the pull of skin,
And the need to explain where I’d been.

I’m still here. Just not the way you knew.
Not gone. Just… passing through.
Silver cord gone quiet. But I still feel you.
