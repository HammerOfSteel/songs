# The Oak's Secret

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-03-21
**Edited:** 2025-03-21

---

"Some say the oldest trees remember everything — wars, kings, and even dragons. Beneath their roots lie stories no book could ever hold."
O dan y dderwen mae amser yn byw,
A llais y ddaear yn sibrwd i’w rhyw.

Beneath the ancient oak's embrace, a child with eyes so wide,
Asked the tree of tales gone by, of secrets it might hide.
The oak replied with rustling leaves, a voice both deep and kind,
"Sit with me, and you shall see the stories intertwined."

Oh, mighty oak, with branches high,
Whispering truths against the sky.
Through seasons' turn and ages past,
Your wisdom and your strength steadfast.

"In days of old," the oak began, "two dragons, red and white,
Slept beneath the rolling hills, concealed from mortal sight.
Their battles shook the very ground, yet balance they restored,
A symbol that through strife and storm, peace can be reborn."

The child pondered tales of yore, of Merlin's mystic ways,
Of prophecies and ancient oaks, and how the land obeys.

Oh, ancient one, with roots so deep,
Guarding secrets that you keep.
Through centuries of joy and pain,
Your stories in our hearts remain.

"Remember, child," the oak did say, "that life is like the trees,
We face the storms, we shed our leaves, yet stand against the breeze.
For every end a new begins, as cycles reassure,
Embrace the change, for through it all, our spirits shall endure."

"Yn y coedwig hon, mae hanesion cudd,
O ddewiniaid a dreigiau, a chwedlau'r byd.
Cofia, blentyn, nerth y llawr,
A'r doethineb sy'n tyfu yn awr."

The child rose with newfound hope, a spark within their eyes,
Thanked the oak for tales and truths beneath the open skies.
With every step, they felt the earth, its pulse, its ancient song,
Carrying forward lessons learned, in heart where they belong.

"And so, beneath the canopy, where time and tales entwine,
A child discovered nature's truths, eternal and divine.
For in the whispers of the leaves and roots that deeply run,
We find the strength to face the storms, until the day is done."
