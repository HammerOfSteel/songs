# Cyfaredd - ALBUM

**Labels:** Cyfaredd, Album
**Created:** 2025-06-01
**Edited:** 2025-08-05

---

Ash of oak and breath of flame,
Thread of moon and mother’s name,
Cradle smoke and crowbone charm,
Spoken verse to keep you warm.
Step in shadow, stir the rhyme—
These are songs that bend through time.

1. The Cauldron and the Cradle
→ Bedtime spell-raps through the Wheel of the Year; Druidic rites; Ceridwen’s cauldron and oak wisdom.


2. The Salt in Her Hair
→ Sea-bound lullabies of grief and memory; selkies, tides, lost daughters, moonlit shores.
The seventh daughter of Owain Glyndwr.


3. Gwaed y Geiriau (The Blood of Words)
→ Language as sacred weaponry; bardic rap-spells, Triads and englynion; mythic beasts and ancestral echoes.


4. Midnight in Ynys Afallon (Avalon)
→ A child’s dream-journey through mythic lands; Merlin, Morgana, and healing through story.


5. Silver Branch Lullabies
→ Otherworld journeys through song; inspired by the Mabinogi; lullabies to the gone-but-listening.


6. Ashes from the Hearthstone
→ Firelight storytelling from the last druid; emotional alchemy; family, memory, and sacred flame.


7. The Grove Never Forgets
→ Tree spirits, ancestral grief, and rites of passage whispered under moon and mistletoe.


8. Tales for the Ones Who Stayed Awake
→ Lullabies for the sleepless; rap verses wrapped in surreal myth and childhood banter and magic
Beat: UK style rap, modern Irish reel inspiration but rap, sweet fun tune
Lyrics: non-native, inspiration from Rhiannon and Pwyll and gwydion

9. The Lantern’s Last Light
→ Song from the edge of death and dream — whispered by a soul carrying the last flame.
→ Inspired by psychopomps (Ankou, Gwyn ap Nudd), and the passing of ligh

10. The Bone Clock Choir
→ Time is a cathedral. Every tick is a beat.
→ Features cycles of sleep, decay, prophecy, and awakening.

11. Cloud-Eyed queen
→ The bedtime myths told by children who see beyond — eyes filled with clouds, dreams, and ghosts.
→ Surreal, cosmic, innocent but profound.
→ Tracks blend curiosity, death, magic, and playfulness.

12. Kindle for a Cold God
→ Winter-bound deity who once held your daughter in her sleep.
→ A parent rapping lullabies that are part praise, part rebellion.
→ Mythic fire vs frozen stillness. Tracks are offerings, bargains.

13. Under the Hill, Under the Skin
→ An introspective myth — grief and trauma as a landscape beneath your own self.
→ Deep metaphysical arc, thick with symbol.
Beat: UK style rap, sweet and bits of sun and lore, Irish inspired.
Lyrics: non-native, rhyming sentences, quotes

14. The Telling Tree
→ A tree that keeps stories and births them as wind-sung songs.
→ You and your child leave your grief, memories, and dreams in its bark.
→ Soft, powerful, rooted. Songs in natural rhythm — leaf, stone, stream.

15. The Mourning Star Almanac
→ A book passed down from one grieving soul to another, each chapter a different kind of grief.
→ Track titles read like journal entries: First Snow Without You, Salt on My Tongue, When I Dream I Forget.
→ Blends folktronica and spoken-word rap into ritual form.
