# Small Blessings (ft. Rain)

**Labels:** Told In My Absence
**Created:** 2025-11-02
**Edited:** 2025-11-02

---

Mild the mist upon the hill
Telling not of storms tomorrow;
No, the day has wept its fill,
Spent its store of silent sorrow.

O, I'm gone back to the days of youth,
I am a child once more,
And 'neath my father's sheltering roof
And near the old hall door 

Rain on the sill doin’ soft percussion,
 little tap-tap sermon, low-key discussion.
 Roof says “shh,” gutter hums bass,
 streetlight drips down the glass on my face.
 Love, I swear, I ain’t healed, I’m just here,
 I’m just breathin’, still, in the same old chair.
 Still got bags under both my eyes,
 still got nights where the brave bit lies.
 But listen , we made it through one more round,
 curtains closed while the whole world frowned.
 Didn’t cure grief, didn’t cure pain,
 but we held hands and we let it rain.
 Didn’t fix much, didn’t fix me,
 but we stayed soft and we made tea.
 That’s worth thanks. I mean it true.
 I’m still in love with the sight of you.
 I’m still in love with the sound of “stay,”
 low voice, hallway, end of day.
 Tiny miracles, proper small,
 ain’t that mad? We kept them all.

Small blessings, babe , let it rain, let it rain.
 Drip on the window sayin’ “stay, stay, stay.”
 Hands round a mug, forehead to mine,
 quiet little moments keep the whole thing fine.
 Small blessings, babe , not loud, not grand,
 just “I’m still here” and “I’ll still stand.”
 Let it rain, let it rain, let it rain ,
 love sounds like water on the window pane.
(zitar arpeggios brighten a little here, almost like tiny bells)

This ain’t joy with fireworks, bang, ta-da,
 it’s calm in a hoodie, socks in a draught.
 It’s “you eat first, I’ll nick what’s left,”
 it’s holding my breath when you look that wrecked.
 It’s you on the mat with your hair all wild,
 muttering slow like a sleepy child.
 It’s me not askin’, just passin’ toast,
 kiss on your temple, “you did most.”
 It’s us in the shop with a two-quid coin,
 laughin’ ‘cos we can’t afford the join.
 It’s “we’ll be fine,” and kind of mean it,
 blagging tomorrow ‘til we’ve seen it.
 It’s rain hittin’ puddles in sync with the heart,
 same bpm since the very first start.
 Same old hum in the bones, that drum,
 sayin’ “we’re knackered, love, but we’re not done.”
 And that’s grace, yeah? I’ll call it grace,
 not in church, in this place.
 In this flat, in this late night room,
 where rain makes halo in the gloom.

We’ve been through it, yeah? understatin’.
 Whole bodies made out of waiting.
 Whole weeks built on “later, later,”
 whole chests tight like radiator.
 Had nights I was half dissolved,
 whole life askin’ to be solved.
 Had mornings you couldn’t get up, no chance ,
 eyes on the floor, in a grief trance.
 Still, we’re here. That matters most.
 Two spoons. One jam. One piece of toast.
 Soft playlist playin’ on a pound-shop phone,
 two of us crowded ‘round it like home.
 Still, we’re kind. That’s not nothin’.
 Still, we’re warm. That’s not bluffin’.
 Still, we check “you good?” at three,
 half-asleep but honestly.
 Still, we cover each other in quilts,
 even with all that ache we built.
 Still, we’re sayin’ “I love you, stay,”
 not big speech, just everyday.

Thank you for not leavin’.
 Thank you for not askin’ me to be fine.
 Thank you for lookin’ wrecked and still comin’ back in the room.
 Thank you for sayin’ her name.
 Thank you for lettin’ me cry without fixin’ it.
 Thank you for makin’ tea before you could talk.
 Thank you for breathin’ slow so I’d breathe too.
 Thank you for stayin’.
 That’s all I ever wanted: stayin’.
(zitar falls into a gentler, slower arpeggio, like rain on metal railing)

And if tomorrow kicks off again,
 if sky goes black and we lose that zen,
 if bills stack tall and the chest goes tight,
 we’ll do what we’ve done every night:
 pull the curtains, boil the rain,
 call it holy, call it sane.
 Lay low, lay here, lay soft, lay true,
 “I got you, babe,” “I got you too.”
 All this life we’ve carried in bits,
 not pretty, not clean, but it fits.
 Not perfect , nah, we’re cracked, we’re sore,
 but we’re somehow us, and I’m askin’ no more.
 That’s blessing, innit? not the prize ,
 just bein’ allowed to open your eyes.
 Just wakin’ up next to breath you trust,
 even when both of us feel like dust.

I watch this cloudy evening fall
After a day of rain;
Blue mists, sweet mists of summer pall
The horizon's mountain chain.

The damp stands on the long green grass
As thick as morning's tears,
And dreamy scents of fragrance pass
That breathe of other years.
