# Midwinter morning

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-11-12
**Edited:** 2024-11-12

---

[Intro]
She wakes with a yawn, soft and slow,
Tiny feet patter across the floor.
To the window’s edge, she makes her way,
And gasps at the world wrapped in gray.

[Daughter’s Line]
"Papa, look! It’s winter’s cloak!"
[Father’s Line]
"Come, my love, the day has woke."
With wonder wide, she claps her hands,
As snowflakes drift across the land.

[Verse 1]
The days grow short, the nights grow deep,
In Risca’s quiet, footsteps keep.
Through frosted fields, a path of white,
Her eyes wide bright in winter’s light.

Up Twmbarlwm, we climb with care,
The world below in silent prayer.
Her tiny hand in mine so tight,
As fox tracks wind and blur from sight.

[Father’s Line]
"Do you see, love? The fox was here."
[Daughter’s Line]
"Can we find him, Papa dear?"
[Father’s Line]
"Let’s follow close, he’s just ahead—"
She laughs and skips with joy widespread.

[Chorus]
On a midwinter’s morning, you and me,
In Cwmcarn’s woods by the old birch trees.
Each step a treasure, each laugh a song,
In her joy, I know I belong.
With every breath, the cold and cheer,
Our hearts beat close as winter nears.

[Verse 2]
Through Sirhowy’s valley, calm and clear,
A hint of snow, the sky drawn near.
She points to birds in icy flight,
Their wings a blur in soft gray light.

A café warm on Commercial Street,
Where warmth and chatter gently meet.
We sip our drinks, her laughter rings,
As snowflakes dance in gentle swings.

[Daughter’s Line]
"Look, Papa, the snowman there!"
[Father’s Line]
"Shall we build our own somewhere?"
[Daughter’s Line]
"Make him big and make him tall!"
We laugh and play in snow’s soft thrall.

[Chorus]
On a midwinter’s morning, just her and me,
In Cwmcarn’s woods by the old birch trees.
Each step a treasure, each laugh a song,
In her joy, I know I belong.
With every breath, the cold and cheer,
Our hearts beat close as winter nears.

[Bridge]
Machen Mountain stands so still,
Its shadow stretched on rising hill.
Past St. Mary’s, candles gleam,
We stop and gaze at lights that beam.

[Daughter’s Line]
"Papa, are they stars come down?"
[Father’s Line]
"They’re stars that found our little town."
Her face aglow, her eyes so wide,
The world a wonder, far and wide.

[Verse 3]
Back to the cabin, warmth inside,
With crackling fire and peace to hide.
I tuck her in, her eyes still bright,
With dreams of snow and Yule-tide light.

A tale I read of winter cheer,
Of stars that shine this time of year.
Her tiny hand clings close to mine,
As outside frost begins to climb.

[Daughter’s Line]
"Tell it again, the tale of Yule?"
[Father’s Line]
"It’s magic’s way, the world’s own jewel."
She smiles and yawns, begins to doze,
As winter night around us grows.

[Chorus]
On a midwinter’s morning, my little sun and me,
In Cwmcarn’s woods by the old birch trees.
Each step a treasure, each laugh a song,
In her joy, I know I belong.
With every breath, the cold and cheer,
Our hearts beat close as winter nears.


[Outro]
In winter’s arms, we find our way,
Through shorter hours to longer days.
Her hand in mine, I feel the glow,
Of love that warms through frost and snow.

Together we walk, through light and shade,
Our footsteps part of the season’s parade.
For winter’s gifts are soft and true,
In quiet moments shared by two.
