# Daily Daffodil

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-05-05
**Edited:** 2025-05-05

---

“Passed Gwenwyn on the bridge again — her coat had feathers in the sleeve.
She was muttering to a puddle and offered me a toffee.
Asked if I’d had any signs lately.
I said, ‘Dunno. Might’ve blinked when it happened.’”

Saw Gwanwyn by the bins, chatting with a crow,
said the veil’s gone thin when the wind’s this low.
Gave me a nod like she knew I’d ask,
“Seen any signs?” — I took off my mask.
Told her nah, just mist and moss,
no feathers, no dreams, no coins, no loss.
She winked, said, “Some ghosts leave petals, not dents.”
And vanished behind the Greggs’ fence.

Next morn I wake, kettle not yet screamed,
grabbed my coat, the street still steamed.
And there it was — by the step, all neat:
a daffodil bright on the cold concrete.
No roots, no note, just yellow and bold,
like a secret told when your heart grows old.
“Blown in by wind,” I muttered with a smirk,
but the breeze don’t gift with such quiet work.

Daily daffodil, soft as breath,
found your way from sleep to step.
Gold and kind, no need for talk —
just standin’ proud on the morning walk.
You don’t knock loud, you don’t explain,
you bloom like hope, not beggin’ for name.

Rain kissed the bus stop glass that noon,
I sat thinkin’ of her and a jam-stained spoon.
Used to leave her notes in scribbled code —
now I get flowers from the sky and road.
Gwanwyn's words buzzed in the trees,
“Some signs are shaped like silence, please.”
I watched a robin skip the curb,
and smiled at how she’d mishear words.

Dreamt that night of a swing in mist,
she sat there proper, socks half-twist.
Tapped my head and said, “Daft you are —
I send stars too, not just flowers by car.”
She hummed a tune from way back when,
the one with spoons and toast and ten.
I woke before the tea had brewed,
but her laugh still clung like perfume.

Gwanwyn once said: “The dead don’t leave,
they learn to lean gentle, like air on a sleeve.
You won’t hear them — you’ll just feel the grin,
like a kettle warm, like a tune within.”

Daily daffodil, no roots but grace,
you grow in the space love used to place.
You never shout — just shimmer and stay,
a whisper of someone who wandered your way.
Gold and still, your silence speaks —
I water your ghost with my tear-streaked cheeks.

“I never did thank her. Not out loud.
But if she hears thought — well, then she’s proud.
One flower. One day. That’s all it takes.
To say: I’m still here, love — through all your aches.”

“You don’t have to shout, love.
A single bloom’ll do.
I’m still listenin’. Still learnin’ your language.”
