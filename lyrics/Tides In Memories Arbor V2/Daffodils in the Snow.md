# Daffodils in the Snow

**Labels:** Tides In Memories Arbor V2
**Created:** 2025-10-11
**Edited:** 2025-10-11

---

acoustic folk, cinematic ballad, ethereal indie, chamber folk, ambient pop, orchestral warmth, poetic storytelling, soft finger-picked guitar, brushed percussion, upright bass, warm piano, subtle strings, choral harmonies, wind-bell ambience, lo-fi intimacy, alchemical symbolism, winter lullaby, hymn-like progression, tender melancholy, hopeful tone

[Verse 1]
[crunch of snow under boots]
In the hush of a winter’s morn,
The world in linen white,
I follow little lamp-warmed breaths
Through fields of folded light.

[soft inhale]

Then, gold against the quiet drift,
A brave, bright flare below:
A daffodil, a pulse of sun
In the heart of falling snow.

[Pre-Chorus]
The earth keeps faith beneath the frost,
Where hidden roots still cling;
In silence, alchemy begins,
The nigredo learning spring.

[Chorus]
Daffodils in the snow, where hope takes root,
Where sleeping seeds remember what to do.
On the coldest days, your warmth remains,
A yellow hymn in winter’s veins.
Daffodils in the snow, a promise kept,
A tender vow through tears we’ve wept.
You break the grip of iron cold, and show
How love grows gold beneath the snow.

[Verse 2]
[kettle hum far off]

Each petal keeps a whisper safe,
Of mittened hands and play,
Of laughter ringing down the lane
On a brittle blue-white day.
The wind may sing its iron song,
The drifts may climb the eaves,
But strength moves quiet, sap and soul,
Up the ladder of the leaves.

[Pre-Chorus]
If sorrow is a crucible,
Then mercy is the flame;
You teach the light to live again,
And call it by its name.

[Chorus]
Daffodils in the snow, where hope takes root,
A gentle sign of what will bloom.
Through the hardest hours, your ember stays,
A sun kept warm in winter’s gaze.
Daffodils in the snow, a promise kept,
A steadfast thread through nights we’ve wept.
You loosen winter’s clenched white fist, and show
The vow that breath and brightness know.

[Bridge]
[whisper] I hold the stalk, a little flame,
A sun distilled from stone;
The red waits in green hush,
And I am not alone.
Your song, soft wool around the heart,
A lantern at my chest,
Teaches my trembling winter-lungs
The liturgy of rest.

[Breakdown (hushed)]

[Childs voice]
[quiet laugh—distant]
“Look, Papa, yellow stars!”

Bootprints stitch the field;
I mark the map from frost to bloom<
A secret path revealed.

[Final Chorus]
Daffodils in the snow< where hope takes root,
Where buried clocks keep perfect time.
Even now, your golden note resounds,
A chapel bell in drifted pines.
Daffodils in the snow, a promise kept,
The gentle law the seasons wrote.
Through every ache, your light replies,
A living warmth in winter’s throat.

[Outro]
When April loosens winter’s lace,
And meltwater threads the lane,
I’ll find you in those lanterns bright,
And hear your name again.
Daffodils in the snow, still, always,
A bright, unwavering sign:
That love endures the longest night,
And learns the art of shine.
