# Gwaed y Geiriau

**Labels:** Cyfaredd
**Created:** 2025-06-02
**Edited:** 2025-06-02

---

It seems all the world is whirling by
In the blink of a Dragon’s eye.
like fog through a cloud-tossed sky
Images like little birds, they fly
Light and Dark, Live and Die
In the blink of a Dragons eye.
Strong and weak, truth and lie
In the blink of a Dragon’s eye.
Calm and Rave, Cower and Brave
Good and Bad, Joy and Sad 

I was born with a beat in my belly, Rhymes got teeth and a flow like jelly. Mum said “shush,” but I spat that flame, Wrote my name in the steam on the windowpane.
Got ink in my veins and a jaw like a crow, Words drop spells when I spit 'em low. Y Ddraig in my throat when I speak that truth, Made a mixtape ghost outta playground youth.

Say the name, twist the sound, Blood of words in battleground. Spell it wrong, you pay the toll— Speak it true, and you're whole.

Quick with a quip and a kick drum pace, Tongue got maps of a myth-born place. Wrote cynghanedd on my school lunch tray, Miss said “Stop,” but the rhyme said “Stay.”
Flow from the crows and the monks of Brecon, Words like wells — you blink, you reckon. Gramps said, “Language carves the land,” So I spat one bar and it broke her hand.

Talked to a ghost through a café cup, Said, “Keep spittin’, ‘cause the spell wakes up.” Dad left ink and my mum left fire, So I rhyme for the ones who retire desire.
Cracked my voice on a rainy beat, Still made gold when the rhyme felt weak. Not ‘bout bars just for show or style— Every line’s stitched with a child’s exile.

Got nine old tongues on my playlist now, One's all rhyme, and the rest just vow. Call me wandless, staffless, free— But my words make doors outta dead oak trees.
I don’t spit spells — I am the line, That loops through time like a tangled spine. Sweet with slang, but my roots run red, Gwaed y geiriau — ink I bled.

Salt on the lip, charm in the beat, Say it right and the path repeats. Gwaed y geiriau, words got soul— Say it clean, and you come back whole.

Not all magic needs a staff or wand. Some’s made of syllables... and what you’ve lived through. And when I spit, I bleed a bit less. That’s enough.
