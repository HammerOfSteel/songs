A drop of dusk from mirror's breath,
Two threads of vow, both torn by death.
A grain of ash from maiden's sigh,
One thorn kissed wet beneath the sky.

A phial of ache in velvet shade,
Moon-milk stirred where truths once laid.
Three tears steeped in blooming flame,
Bound in wax, and sealed with shame.

A breath withheld, a pulse denied,
Crushed rose salt the night has cried.
Then whisper low, and drink it deep—
For love like this forgets to sleep.
