"Terminal"
The Story: A poem-like track set in a 24-hour bus terminal. It focuses on the "emptiness" of the passengers—not as a sad thing, but as a state of pure potential. They are between lives, "unfixed" and "volatile."
The Imagery: "Cauda Pavonis" (The Peacock’s Tail)—the iridescent, oily rainbow patterns found in a puddle of diesel on the wet pavement. It represents the many colors of the soul appearing just before they vanish.
Music Style: UK british indie / the doors feeling, psychedelic undertones, british female vocals, childs vocals, sweet and warm youthful 
The Lyric Vibe: "Stutter the shutter, flicker and flutter. these vessels all waiting on a steady old grating. No names, no needs, no deeds - worthy of praise. This puddle set ablaze, never seez to amaze. a million colors, a single dream."

"Mountain ash"
The Story: The closing of our first road trip to polperro - the three of us, me, Tima and Peter (Though we do not keep the names in the song). It represents the "blowing out" (Nirvana) of the self, not in the sense of letting go but rather of rediscovering the past, the story goes from the father years later after it all looking back at photos as he sits in the little bnb room in polperro (though we dont use specific names for the place, maybe only once in all the lyrics), it's new years and he is here alone. He came here for this very reason, to find some trace, some place that is as still as he is. A place that remembers, keeps its embers warm and close when for all the world, everyone else does not. He realizes that the motion of all this time - losing himself, feeding the silence only to return back here - was the point, and now that the motion stops, he can finaly "burn out" into and gnosis of how to carry this, a knowing that it is not about comming out at another end, not about finding some truth of the world - rather he finds a content subtle gnosis in that it will always be like this, it will shift and change and eat its way into oblivion over and over - its not okay, but.. it is, as it shall be until is shall be no more.

The Imagery: "The White Swan" (the stage of Albedo or whitening). In alchemy, the swan represents the volatile spirit becoming fixed and pure.

Music Style: think "the arcadian wild - rain clouds" but in an almost UK british Indie subtle undertone, youthful and glowing acoustics, the lyrics style british like, dont use artist names to drscribe the music, rather comma separated words
