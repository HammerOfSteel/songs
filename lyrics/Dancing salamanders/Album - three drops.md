# Album - three drops

**Labels:** Dancing salamanders, Three drops
**Created:** 2024-09-17
**Edited:** 2024-09-17

---

- three drops : songs with references to Arthurian lore, the awen, Celtic mythology, Scandinavian folklore, spells.

"The Mist-Laden Path"
The album opens with a journey through a fog-covered landscape, hinting at a liminal space between worlds. The Seeker stumbles upon a narrow, winding path where whispers of forgotten tongues guide their steps. The song draws on references to Y Gododdin and the lost roads of Annwn, merging them with Norse mist-beings like the huldrafolk who conceal the hidden ways.

"The Wandering Star"
A mysterious star appears in the night sky, guiding the Seeker. This song contains allusions to Taliesin’s transformations and the "blazing star" that guided the Magi, layered with references to a star that falls into the waters, invoking the Welsh myth of Elffin’s discovery of the bard Taliesin.

"A Raven’s Oath"
The Seeker encounters a raven who offers cryptic advice. This raven is not just any bird but carries a fragmented soul trapped between the worlds, echoing stories from Norse and Celtic traditions where ravens are messengers of both life and death. This raven speaks in half-truths, pointing the Seeker toward a hidden lake.

"A fluid form"
At a reflective pond, the Seeker meets a shapeshifter lamenting their curse to transform at will — a nod to Welsh and Irish mythologies, such as the story of Fionn mac Cumhaill and the swan maiden myths. This figure represents the flux of identity and the need for self-acceptance.

"Underneath the Hollow Hill"
Drawn to a strange hill that seems to breathe, the Seeker enters the Otherworld, or Annwn, through an enchanted door. The song references the subterranean realms in Arthurian and Celtic myths, where the Hill King (a figure echoing Gwyn ap Nudd) and his court dwell.

"The Forgotten Feast"
The Seeker finds themselves at an ancient feast where the food and drink seem to nourish the soul rather than the body. The feast is an allegory for inspiration and divine communion, drawing on myths of the cauldron of rebirth and the mead of poetry that drips from the mouth of the Awenyddion.

"The Dew of Three"
Central to the album, this song represents the moment of revelation. The Seeker receives three drops from a cauldron or chalice — inspired by the Welsh legend of Gwion Bach and Ceridwen’s cauldron — granting them wisdom and poetic inspiration. These drops burn the tongue but open the mind.

"The Silent Harp"
The Seeker encounters a harp that plays no music unless it touches the strings of the heart. This song draws on tales of enchanted harps in Irish lore, like the harp of Dagda, weaving in the idea of music as the bridge between worlds, where silence can reveal more than sound.

"Nine Waves Away"
A sea voyage to the Isle of the Blessed, the mysterious realm that lies nine waves away. This song blends references to the Irish Tír na nÓg and the Norse realm of Vanaheim, where time moves differently. It symbolizes a spiritual crossing, where old wounds are healed.

"The Ash Tree’s Whisper"
The Seeker meets a talking ash tree (Yggdrasil’s seed, or Esus’ tree) that offers guidance, holding both wisdom and warning. The ash tree embodies the connection between earth and sky, a bridge of knowledge that the Seeker must climb. Here, the tree is both teacher and test.

"Sea of tranquility"
The Seeker is bewitched by the Moon's daughter, a lesser-known figure in Celtic and Norse myth who was said to wander the earth unnoticed. She speaks of hidden truths and brings light to the Seeker’s darkest thoughts. Her identity is shrouded, hinting at Selene, Mani, or the Morrigan in her darkest form.

"The Crimson Thread"
The Seeker finds a crimson thread in the labyrinth, representing fate and the interconnectedness of life. The thread is woven by invisible hands — a reference to the Norns and the weaving women of Celtic and Norse myths, who spin and cut the threads of destiny.

"The Dreaming Stag"
A song about the stag who dreams of his life as a man, drawing from the myth of the White Stag in Arthurian lore and the Irish Fianna. The stag serves as both a guide and a test, representing the primal connection to nature and the hunter's quest for self-understanding.

"The Mirror of Dreams"
The Seeker gazes into a mirror made of moonlight, where dreams are reflected, but reality is distorted. The song references the Irish story of the Cauldron of Poesy and the Scandinavian Mirror of Urd, suggesting that the truth is often buried in dreams and illusions.

"Brew of Three"
The final song represents the Seeker’s realization that the journey has been both outward and inward. They find the “Three Drops” were within them all along. The song concludes with the Seeker returning to their world, carrying the light of the cauldron, hinting at the cyclical nature of life and the constant quest for wisdom.

Narrative Arc Summary:
Beginning: The Seeker embarks on a quest, moving through mists and shadows, guided by cryptic symbols and encounters that slowly reveal deeper truths.

Middle: The journey leads to the Otherworld, through trials, revelations, and mystical experiences, where the Seeker gains insights and confronts both external and internal challenges.

End: The Seeker reaches a moment of transformation and understanding, returning to their world changed, holding the “Three Drops” of wisdom — a metaphor for enlightenment, inner peace, and deeper connection to the mysteries of life.
This album weaves obscure mythological references into a story of self-discovery and spiritual awakening, providing both a narrative and symbolic journey through lesser-known corners of myth and lore.
