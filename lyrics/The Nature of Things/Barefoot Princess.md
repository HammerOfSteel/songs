# Barefoot Princess

**Labels:** The Nature of Things
**Created:** 2024-11-14
**Edited:** 2024-11-14

---

A barefoot child, a princess fair,
In St. Ives town, with salt-filled air.
The sand is soft beneath her feet,
Where sea and story gently meet.

"Come, love, today the sea will show
The tales of long ago."
Her eyes grow wide, her laughter sings,
As she runs to see what the ocean brings.

The waves lap soft, the tide pulls low,
As Tima’s feet leave prints below.
Her father smiles, with warmth and pride,
As they walk the coast with the ocean’s guide.

"Do you know, love, of Princess Ia's tale?
She sailed here on a leaf so frail."

"A leaf, Papa? Like the ones we find?"
"Just like those, but in a sea-bound bind."

Barefoot princess, wild and free,
Dancing by the Cornish sea.
With every step, she claims the sand,
As tales of old slip through her hand.
Oh, the sea, it whispers near,
Of princesses who linger here.

Through cobbled streets, they wander slow,
Past painted doors where flowers grow.
She skips and twirls, her laughter bright,
As the day drifts on toward evening light.

"See, love, the boats that bob and sway—
They’ve stories, too, of a far-off day."

"Do they know the princess, too?"
"Oh yes, she’s sailed these waters blue."

By silver rocks, they find their way,
Where seagulls watch and children play.
She gathers shells in tiny hands,
Each one a gem from ancient sands.

"Papa, do you think she danced like me?"
"I do, my love, by the Cornish sea."

"Was she brave and bold and true?"
"Brave as you, with a heart so blue."


They climb the cliffs, the wind grows high,
The sea stretched wide beneath the sky.
She lifts her arms, as if to soar,
And dreams of Ia’s myth once more.

"A princess once, with steps so light,
She danced by day and dreamed by night."

"Will she meet us here someday?"
"Perhaps, in dreams where legends stay."

Barefoot princess, wild and free,
Dancing by the Cornish sea.
With every step, she claims the sand,
As tales of old slip through her hand.
Oh, the sea, it whispers near,
Of princesses who linger here.

The sky grows soft, the day fades slow,
In golden light, their shadows grow.
Hand in hand, they trace the shore,
With stories shared forevermore.

"Tell me again of the princess bright."
"Another time, when stars are light."
Her eyes drift closed, the sand beneath,
As twilight wraps her like a wreath.

A barefoot child, a princess true,
With dreams as wide as skies so blue.
In St. Ives town, where tales arise,
Her heart is touched by the sea’s disguise.

The waves retreat, the stars appear,
And the legend of Ia feels near.
With every step, she leaves a trace,
Of wonder’s glow in this quiet place.
