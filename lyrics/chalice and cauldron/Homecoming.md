# Homecoming

**Labels:** chalice and cauldron
**Created:** 2025-04-07
**Edited:** 2025-04-07

---

He said it like gospel: She’s the one.
Ylva, white dress, tan lines, clean run.
Everyone knew—it was prophecy.
Golden boy, queen-to-be, perfect symmetry.
The warriors cheered from locker halls,
Bedwyr punched lockers, Kai scaled walls.
He posted a pic: “She doesn’t even know yet.”
Comments blew up like a Vegas roulette.
She smiled once in Biology,
So clearly, it was meant to be.
She wore silence like a dare.
He mistook it for playing fair.
He practiced the ask for a week straight,
Balloons, violins, football cleats, fate.
And when she said yes (cameras clicked),
He didn’t notice her hands stay clenched.
Didn’t hear what she didn’t say—
Didn’t care, it was his day.

They said we’d look good in photos,
But I’m not a frame you can hold.
He came with fire and painted smiles—
I wanted rain. And quiet miles.
I said yes in a borrowed voice—
But I was never the choice.
I was never the choice.

They called me the star, the prize, the girl,
But I just wanted out of this plastic world.
Dad said “You’re lucky, baby. Smile more.”
I smiled ‘til my cheeks turned war.
After school, I’d sit in the stacks—
Poetry and silence in paperback stacks.
I wrote to breathe. I wrote to not drown.
I watched them build me a cardboard crown.
And then—
Ceri.
Hair like dusk, eyes like duskier things,
Wore ash and clay like diamond rings.
Said, “You ever seen a stone Buddha dance?”
I laughed. First time in months, by chance.
Ceri saw me—not the glow.
Not the dad. Not the show.
Just the girl who wanted valleys and sky.
Ceri said, “Stay still long enough, and you'll fly.”

Not all love is conquest.
Not all silence is shyness.
And you—yeah, you listening—
Who told you love was something you earn
with flowers and a crowd?

Maybe you were sold a script.
Maybe it’s time to burn it.

He built a castle with a name he didn’t own.
I watched from windows, cold to the bone.
She danced in shadows, slipped the frame—
A girl with no interest in fame.
He said love, but meant parade.
She said no, but it got delayed.
And somewhere between the gym and sky—
A starling passed, and didn’t reply.

Homecoming: flashbulbs, confetti rain.
Culh wore pride like a bloodstained chain.
His breath was sour, grip too tight—
He said “Let’s go,” but she said “Not tonight.”
The crowd was noise, the gym was thick,
His hand on her waist—too fast, too quick.
She pulled away.
He didn’t let go.
Ceri stepped in, said “She said no.”
Laughter broke—someone filmed the scene.
Culh’s face turned black and mean.
The king unmasked, the myth undone,
And then—

he reached inside his coat and pulled a gun.

Screams. A stampede.
The music died.
Ylva froze.
Ceri moved.
The bullet
flied.

You said love.
But you meant control.
You wanted her silent,
You wanted her whole.
She said no—but the noise grew loud—
And now we kneel,
Underneath the crowd.

The gym’s a shrine, with wilted crowns.
They built a statue. They painted frowns.
They lit candles by her locker gate—
Too little. Too late.
Ylva’s poems live in my coat.
I read them soft, like prayer notes.
They called it tragedy.
I call it truth.
She said no.
And he stole her youth.
They called me strange.
I say I see.
I saw her.
She saw me.
And now I speak.
Because I must.
The world forgot her.
But I won’t let it rust.

If you're still listening—
stop saying it couldn’t happen here.
It did.
It does.
Every day.
Somewhere a girl says no.
Somewhere a boy can’t hear it.

Don’t let this be
just another verse
you forget.
