# The Moorland Maiden

**Labels:** Dancing salamanders, Drink deeply, and dream
**Created:** 2024-09-10
**Edited:** 2024-09-10

---

"The Moorland Maiden": On the windswept moors of Cornwall, the narrator recalls a maiden lost to time, her memory lingering in the wind and wildflowers. This song is a bittersweet reflection on how love continues to shape our hearts long after those we cherish are gone.

Verse 1
In the windswept moors where heather grows,
A maiden’s tale in whispers flows.
She walked these lands in days of yore,
With a heart that loved but was loved no more.
Her laughter now in the breeze does sing,
A ghostly echo, a forgotten spring.
In every bloom and thorny vine,
Her memory weaves through time’s design.

Chorus
Oh, Moorland Maiden, lost to time,
Your love endures in ancient rhyme.
Through wildflowers and windswept plains,
Your sorrow lives where the moorland reigns.
In every shadow, in every stone,
Your love remains though you’ve flown.

Verse 2
I wander now where you once tread,
Among the paths where dreams have bled.
Your story etched in the earth so deep,
Where love and loss together sleep.
The heather sways in soft lament,
A silent prayer, a love well-spent.
Though time has worn your name away,
The moor remembers your heart’s dismay.

Chorus
Oh, Moorland Maiden, lost to time,
Your love endures in ancient rhyme.
Through wildflowers and windswept plains,
Your sorrow lives where the moorland reigns.
In every shadow, in every stone,
Your love remains though you’ve flown.

Bridge
In the twilight’s glow, your face appears,
A fleeting glimpse through the veil of years.
The moor, it holds your secret well,
In every gust, your story it tells.
And though the world may not recall,
The moorland keeps you, heart and all.

Outro
As the evening falls and shadows creep,
I feel your love in the earth so deep.
For in these moors where you once stood,
Your memory lingers as it should.
The Moorland Maiden, forever near,
In every breeze, your love I hear.
