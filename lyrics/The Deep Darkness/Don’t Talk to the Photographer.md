# Don’t Talk to the Photographer

**Labels:** The Deep Darkness
**Created:** 2025-04-18
**Edited:** 2025-04-18

---

We were crying by the tunnel, holding on real tight,
Paula said, “Just five minutes without a fight.”
Poo lit incense, Jeff fixed a strap,
I said, “This moment matters.”
Then we heard the clap.

He dropped from the sky with a cheesy grin,
Said, “Say fuzz and staaand on your shin!”
The light went off, the world went blank,
He flew away.
We barely blinked.

He’s got perfect timing for emotional breaks,
A ghost with a lens and zero stakes.
Always smiling, never stays —
Leaves before we’ve said what we needed to say.

I blink, you flash — you never ask if I’m okay,
You frame my pain like it’s part of a play.
You don’t get names, just angles and light,
But I live in the shadows after you take flight.
So don’t talk to the photographer —
He don’t know what it means to stay.

Jeff once tried to map his path — said it broke the map.
Poo meditated mid-flash, gave him a slap.
Paula asked his name. He said “Doug!”
Then vanished again in a fog of bugs.

I yelled, “What film is that?!” He said “Emotion 400!”
I said, “Bro, that ISO don’t exist!”
He winked.
“Exactly.”

Caught in the act of trying to heal,
And now it’s frozen like a highlight reel.
I don’t smile, I don’t pose —
But you catch me anyway, I suppose.

I blink, you flash — you never ask if I’m okay,
You capture the hurt like it’s part of the day.
You don’t wait long, just click and go,
But I carry the weight of what you’ll never know.
So don’t talk to the photographer —
He’s not here to see me stay.

Back home, there’s a picture on my fridge,
From that day at the river near the broken bridge.
We look so alive, you’d never guess
How scared we were beneath the mess.

I laughed when I saw it — then looked again.
He caught something true.
But not the end.
