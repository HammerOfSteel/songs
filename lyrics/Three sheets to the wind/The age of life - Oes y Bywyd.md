# The age of life - Oes y Bywyd

**Labels:** Three sheets to the wind
**Created:** 2025-03-29
**Edited:** 2025-03-29

---

May they stumble, stage by stage
On an endless Pilgrimage
Dawn and dusk, mile after mile
At each and every step a stile
At each and every step withal
May they catch their feet and fall

Morning creaks on tatami floor,
sunlight slices through paper doors.
Kimono draped from last night’s plan,
hand-stitched stories from another land.
Steam curls up from the bath like grace,
I breathe in cedar, rinse off pace.
Shared house silence, a bicycle hum,
coast down Higashiyama, soft on the run.
Two rivers kiss at a temple bend,
I follow north where the rails don't end.
Fox and fire, time stands thin
a white fox darts across the shrine skin.
And I blink
just one breath too long
suddenly I’m back in Gwents old song,
her first fox,
Red wool socks
"Wedi blino", on the rocks 

This is the age of life,
not in clocks but in light.
Where seasons speak in scattered scenes
between the hush and the might.
We don’t mark time.
We just walk through.
And memory’s a river
that carries you.

Brother brother down the plym, caffeine breath,
city rust and coastal depth.
I step out the spot with hands still warm,
sun stung streets, the calm before storm.
Mai mai waits under the mural’s eyes,
we walk the Sound, where gulls rome skies.
Lighthouse on like a distant song,
the slow old path was always long
late drive past another one,
Shes sleeping, seren on her coat pinned
the dog’s head out in the wind,
her friend asleep with a smile half on.
We passed the edge where land breaks slow,
and something in me let go.
Not of her,
No big deal.
The waves in hues of blue and teal.

Yr hen a ŵyr a’r ieuanc a dybia
The old know.
The young
they suspect it in dreams.
The rest of us live somewhere in between.

This is the age of life,
In waves and roads.
In brass born gold and secret code,
Trace the tail, let to learn.
Not all things will return,
but some things stay.
In hill and bay
Keeps in the hum of yesterday.

I climbed hill, late autumn slow,
locals in flow kids bounding like wind through snow.
The path got thin but I held pace,
a soft ache where time leaves it's trace.
Crags opened wide to show the sea,
island breathing around me.
And there
the summit,
the world laid bare.
The same wind rustling through her hair
Gwent beneath me laid out bare.
her head against my chest right here
not yet even half a year.
I’ve carried her longer than anyone knows,
but the mountain never asked,
it just rose.

The age of life,
not of keeping, but letting.
Where memory slips its name,
but keeps its setting.
You can’t hold it all
you’re not meant to.
But the breath,
the step,
the shape that’s you.

a night by hearth and by home
Just two mugs, a half read tome
snowlight blue on the window clear.
A sleeping smile, a heart held near.
No lesson, no line, just breath and heat,
a ticking wall and wool-wrapped feet.
She asleep, curls on cheek,
the house not perfect, but that’s what speaks.
Because it’s not always the travel or climb,
sometimes it’s silence that marks the time.
A kettle half-full, a fridge hum low,
a name on your lips you don’t need to show.
And if that’s not life,
what is?

Let the years come round like rain,
let the soil remember each name.
We are not fixed.
We are not stone.
We bloom in pieces,
carried home.
