# Quintessence

**Created:** 2024-12-09
**Edited:** 2024-12-09

---

The alarm cuts sharp through the fog of dreams,
Another day, or so it seems.
Their plastic smiles fade as I’m out the door,
The world feels heavy, life and lore.

The cobbled streets feel cold, alive,
In the park cross the line where time divides.
The meridian whispers its silent plea,
“Past and present are tied to me.”
A ship’s shadow stretches, its mast held high,
The gray, the day, the Thameses sigh.

They say, “Let go, it’s in the past,”
But shadows cling, they’re built to last.
Every step, they’re holding tight,
A quintessence in the light.
I can’t escape, though I try,
But if I told you, it'd be a lie.

The gates swing wide, the scene unfolds,
Bullies laugh, their voices bold.
Princesses pose with practiced grace,
Their glances sharp, says "know your place".
The weight of their words builds like a storm,
A stage where I can't fit the form.

Behind me, a voice, “Let’s leave today,
This dull world isn’t where we’ll stay.”
Her hand grabs mine, the gates fall behind,
We run like thieves through space and time.
The city hums as we find our stride,
The tunnel echoes, the river’s tide.

She says, “Every turn’s a chance to dream,
Not every shadow’s as dark as it seems.”

They say, “Let go, it’s in the past,”
But shadows cling, they’re built to last.
Each step now feels a little light,
The quintessence turns to flight.
Her voice sparks dreams, a guiding cry,
The echoes shift to “Why not try?”

Off the deck into the street, 
Through plumstead station, feet by feet
We cross the river, the air feels wide,
Her laughter ripples with the changing tide.
A market appears with its lively stalls,
Stories and trinkets tucked in its walls.
“Look at this,” she says with a grin,
“A map for lost wanderers, let’s begin.”

The streets unfold, like stories untold,
She says, “Even broken things can be bold.
Not all shadows are here to fight;
Some just dance in the fading light.”

We stumble on a garden, forgotten and still,
The air hums soft, the world grows still.
She points to a flower, magnolias bent but bright,
“Even the weary can seek the light.”
Her words plant seeds, a gentle flame,
In this quiet magic, we're the same.

They say, “Let go, it’s in the past,”
But shadows cling, they’re built to last.
Now each step feels less like fright,
The quintessence turns to light.
Her voice still echoes, “Give it a try,”
The shadows fade; I learn to fly.

The gates will swing wide again someday,
But her voice will guide me along the way.
