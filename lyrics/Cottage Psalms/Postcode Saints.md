# Postcode Saints

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-04-25
**Edited:** 2025-04-25

---

People talk ‘bout saints like halos and heavens,
But here, they wear aprons... and kettle scars.
This is for the ones who bless without askin’.
For the saints of the small.

Saint Bren of the Snug Brew, steady as steam,
Knows your order by grief, not just routine.
“Two sugars today?” — she’s seen the storm in your stare.
Adds a ginger snap and a moment’s care.
Steam curls like prayer in a chipped old cup,
Bless the hush she serves when the noise ramps up.

Saint Rhys of the Breakfast Club, school bell sage,
Egg rolls in foil, wisdom twice his age.
Skips his own toast to butter yours warm,
Tells you math’s not real, but kindness forms.
Sits quiet when needed, just peels an orange,
Cracks jokes that echo like chapel organs.

Postcode saints in fleece and rain,
Haloed in chip grease, schoolyard strain.
They don’t ask, they don’t boast —
They just hold up the coast.
These holy few on common ground,
Miracles whispered, barely found.

Saint Llinos of the Corner Shop, queen of the change tin,
Knows who needs bread and who just came in
For a chat, for a “How’s your Nan?”
Knits together the street like only she can.
Sells more hope than biscuits by weight,
And tapes lost posters on the iron gate.

Saint Haf of the Lost Leads, seen in all weather,
Walking dogs not hers like souls in tether.
Knows each bark, each bootprint path,
Winks and says, “Some folk just need a bath.”
But she waits at corners when old dogs slow,
Knows when a leash is love on a low go.

Saint Dewi of the Chippy Queue, Friday night prophet,
Tells you futures in fryers, no need for a profit.
Holds babies while mums count coins,
Swaps dad jokes for chips, balance rejoined.
Bless the salt he sprinkles with flair,
Each vinegared wrapper a communion there.

Saint Nia of Pots and Play, fortress of calm,
Draws dragons from yoghurt lids, heals with a balm.
Kneels to tie laces with mythical grace,
And paints brave hearts on every face.
She hums “Sospan Fach” with the glitter stuck,
And gives every scraped knee a fairy’s luck.

These aren’t saints you see in books,
But they’re the ones who always look.
Down a high street, up a lane,
Their grace smells like puddles and sugarcane...

Postcode saints, weather-worn bright,
Cradling the dawn in the tuck of night.
Not with wings, but a tattered coat —
They carry hope in secondhand notes.
These holy few on common ground,
Miracles whispered, barely found.

“Never waste a name.”

“The bell don’t ring the same.”

“We walk ‘em ‘til they don’t need walkin’.”

“The kettle listens more than most.”

“You can play your way to brave.”

“Every queue’s a kind of church.”

Saints don’t float.
They fold prams, hold queues, hold hearts.
And when you leave… they still wave.
From the chippy, from the bus stop, from the world we almost missed.
