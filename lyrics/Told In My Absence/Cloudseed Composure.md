# Cloudseed Composure

**Labels:** Told In My Absence
**Created:** 2025-10-20
**Edited:** 2025-10-20

---

Got the rain! Quick, open the garden!

We keep jam jars on the windowsill,
Labels neat in your secret quill:
Tuesday drizzle, giggle storm,
Leftover thunder to keep us warm.
You tap the glass with a wooden spoon,
Catch a plink from the afternoon,
Hold it up like a firefly kept,
Whisper, “Hush now, weather’s slept.”

Wellies drum that puddle-beat code,
Uke chim-chings down our road;

You say

“Dad, faith is a watering can,
Tip it slow, don’t flood the plan.
If the sky forgot, we’ll lend it rain,
Plant a rainbow, harvest again.”

“How long till colours grow?”

Till the patient minutes glow.

“And what if nothing shows?”

We keep the soil of “maybe” close.

Cloudseed composure, steady hands, open air,
We pour small hopes like we know they’ll be there.
Jam jars singing on the garden rail,
Sunlight sampled in a golden exhale.
Cloudseed composure, count to ten, let it be,
Sprouts learn music from a kind mystery.
If faith’s a kettle, then we’re almost there,
Listen: tiny colours in the steam of the air.

You hand me seed packets you drew last night:
Marigold Moon, Cabbage of Light,
Strawberry Thunder, Pea-pod Blues,
Every name a spell you choose.
We press them down with sleepy thumbs,
Call the robins peer review chums.
You chalk a chart on the garden path,
Days since rain and a puddle-math;
Prove a theorem with your muddy knees:
Kindness grows quicker than weeds.

Hold your breath, don’t rush the sky,
Let the colours learn to try.
Count the beats between the drops,
Faith is when the waiting stops.

don’t chase the arc, walk with it.

share your shade, talk with it.

if it fades, wave anyway.

plant the wave, stay, anyway.

We’re gardeners of “nearly,” keepers of “soon,”
Folding up puddles to carry the noon.

“I planted a raindrop by the daisy line,
It wriggled, went pop, turned lemon-lime!”

We wake to a hush like a page about to turn,
Dew writes cursive on the fern.
Your jars glow sherbet, apricot, teal,
You grin so wide the morning kneels.
We cut the light with paper crowns,
Tie the ends to village sounds;
Neighbours laugh, the bus goes pshh,
Somewhere a window hums shhh-shhh.
You say, “We did it, look, they grew!”
I say, “We waited right with you.”
And in that pause, the colours land,
A quiet rainbow in your hand.
