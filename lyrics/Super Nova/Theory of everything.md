# Theory of everything

**Edited:** 2025-06-06

Spacetime tells matter how to move; matter tells spacetime how to curve.

The universe is under no obligation to make sense to you.

Aye, no blueprint for this love — no tips or tricks that you'll find,
Grown will bloom in heart and mind.
You come in loud, all tooth and flame,
Sweet and strong, your little name.

Scared to hold you proper, can't swim in the deep.
a heart cracked open still tryin’ to sleep.
Suger spice and all that's nice,
A million stars within your eyes.

You taught me dif'rent with your stompy boots,
askin’ questions, spillin’ juice, shakin’ truths loose.
And I clocked the fractals in your rage, your grace —
a spiral, Fibonacci traced in your face.

Our mercury in loves true name,
etched our silver all the same.
Little heart my hands will hold,
Nearly even three years old.
You showed up like sulphur saturated softly real —
A song unsung my bones could feel.

I watched you battle silence like a sword of glass,
shatterin’ rooms just tryna get past.
And I kept holdin’ my breath like that helped you cope,
like stayin’ small would give you more rope.

turns out love don’t fear collision,
it’s in the ricochet, not the perfect decision.
Many moons mear Merlin’s mead,
coldest fire can’t believe,
Calmly claimed a seat in heart,
But that was just a little start.

Aye, it ain’t some climbin’ line —
it’s a tide, it’s a loop, it’s a spiral of time.
Hawking hears her holly heart,
not an endin’, not a start.

So I let you see me. Not the polished verse.
Not the good worn mask so well rehearsed.
Just the ghost I been, still tryin’ to sing,
and you — you just called me your “everything."

Aye, our sulphur sung a supernova into night,
burnin’ pastel purple hues, warm and bright.
For you, for me, for us — my light,
no theory holds like you held me right.

mum said you don’t cry round strangers no more,
you save that thunder for me, full-core.
And it ain’t easy, bein’ the one you storm —
but I’ll wear that lightning ‘til it keeps you warm.

Some say blood’s the bond, but I’ve seen the myth —
love’s the law when the world won’t shift.
You don’t need a matchin’ face to feel,
you just need time, and a heart that's real.

And I’ve knelt, love. At altars of my own failin’,
burned the scrolls where I wrote my bailin’.
But you — you scribbled over ‘em with marker pens,
spelled “stay” in stars again and again.

You got me chantin’ backwards, twistin’ fate,
weavin’ serpent signs in my tea-stained plate.
Little mischief tucked in your stubborn frown,
Me heart dancin’ when you spin around.

Aye, the salamander dance in sleeping stone
Truths untangled in marrow and bone.
Brown of oak, golden chalice.
From wonderland, she came - this here Alice.

Laughing lynx least lost lays,
Dreaming made in honey ways.
Seen a silver sun sliver.
A frozen heart made to quiver.
Hold her hand, a happy home,
Aye, I was never truly alone.

Up the warren, the comet call,
A dash of warmth for my cawl.
Safe and sound, you come around
A heart held on steady ground.

You saw me scared, and you stayed, bold.
Said “got you, even when you fold.”,

Aye, our sulphur sung a supernova into night,
burnin’ pastel purple hues, warm and bright.
For you, for me, for us — my light,
you cracked the dark, and it made me right.

For small creatures such as we the vastness is bearable only through love.

The cosmos is within us. We are made of star-stuff. We are a way for the universe to know itself
