# The Serpent’s Knot

**Labels:** Drink deeply, and dream
**Created:** 2024-09-10
**Edited:** 2024-09-10

---

"The Serpent’s Knot": Entangled in the coils of self-loathing, the narrator struggles to untangle a serpent’s knot deep within an ancient forest. The song delves into the painful process of breaking free from destructive thoughts and the realization that the battle is both internal and external.

Verse 1
In the depths of the forest, where shadows creep,
I found a serpent in a twisted sleep.
Its body coiled, a knotted maze,
In the ancient wood, where the sunlight fades.
It whispered low, in a voice so cold,
Of secrets kept and stories untold.
“Untangle me,” it hissed with glee,
But the knot it formed was part of me.

Chorus
The serpent’s knot, tight and strong,
Holds the weight of what’s gone wrong.
In every twist, in every coil,
The self is trapped, wrapped in toil.
But through the pain, the light does seep,
A path to where the soul can weep.

Verse 2
The serpent’s eyes, a mirror’s gaze,
Reflect the fears that fill my days.
Each riddle spoke of wounds so deep,
Of nights alone, where shadows creep.
Its grip so firm, yet I knew inside,
That this was a battle I could not hide.
For in its scales, I saw my face,
A soul entrapped, seeking grace.

Bridge
With trembling hands, I start to weave,
Through knots of sorrow, the will to believe.
The serpent’s grip begins to wane,
As I confront the deepest pain.
In the untangling, there’s a breath,
A space where life defies death.

Outro
The serpent’s knot, now loose, unwinds,
Releasing the chains that bound my mind.
In the forest deep, I leave it there,
A symbol of the burden I used to bear.
For in the end, the serpent’s game,
Was never just a beast to blame.
It was the mirror in the wood,
Reflecting all I misunderstood.
