1 "A Hénon Map"
Fractal Concept: The Hénon map describes chaotic systems that appear unpredictable but have an underlying structure. This mirrors the experience of DID, where life feels scattered, yet there’s a hidden pattern in the system’s dynamics.
DID Focus: Dissociation as a form of chaos where memory lapses and energy fluctuations disrupt daily life, yet moments of clarity reveal interconnectedness.
Everyday Life Impact: Difficulty planning or trusting one’s own memory, with social anxiety stemming from fears of being "unpredictable."
Narrative:
The protagonist walks a labyrinth with shifting walls, representing their daily struggle to trust their own memory and decisions. The Labyrinth Keeper taunts them, exploiting their dissociation. As the protagonist progresses, they realize the shifting paths are not random but reflect their inner system’s attempts to adapt and protect. By embracing this dynamic, they find moments of stability in the chaos.
Symbolism: The Hénon map embodies the journey of finding patterns in dissociation, illustrating that chaos can lead to understanding and resilience.

2. "The Douady Rabbit"
Fractal Concept: The Douady rabbit, part of the Mandelbrot set, reflects self-repeating complexity, a metaphor for the inner child and layered identity in DID.
DID Focus: The whimsical Douady rabbit represents playful, creative aspects of the system, while the Hollow Hare embodies fear and self-criticism. This duality affects social interactions, where vulnerability is hidden behind masks of humor or aloofness.
Everyday Life Impact: Moments of joy clash with inner guilt or shame, and social situations feel like navigating a warren of potential triggers.
Narrative:
The protagonist follows the Douady rabbit into a labyrinth where each room contains echoes of past selves—some joyful, others burdened. The Hollow Hare warns them to avoid reconnecting with their inner child, fearing vulnerability. However, by embracing these playful, creative fragments, the protagonist discovers resilience and the ability to balance light and shadow.
Symbolism: The Douady rabbit embodies the beauty of multiplicity, where each layer of the self contributes to the whole.

3. "Wild Apollonian Nets"
Fractal Concept: Apollonian nets form intricate, infinite connections, representing the interwoven threads of a DID system and relationships.
DID Focus: The web reflects the interconnected identities within the system and the challenge of balancing these with external relationships. The Weaver symbolizes the tension between isolation and the desire for connection.
Everyday Life Impact: Relationships are affected by dissociation, where emotional walls form, and memory gaps create strain. Social exhaustion looms, yet meaningful connections provide grounding.
Narrative:
The protagonist is trapped in a shimmering web spun by the Weaver, each thread representing a part of their system or a relationship. The web tightens, making them feel isolated and overwhelmed. However, by repairing frayed threads and accepting the interwoven nature of their system and relationships, the web transforms into a supportive network rather than a trap.
Symbolism: Apollonian nets symbolize both the complexity of DID and the potential for harmony in interconnectedness.

4. "Julia Set"
Fractal Concept: The Julia set, with its intricate, connected patterns, represents the fragmented self in DID, where each piece is vital to the whole.
DID Focus: Fractured identities are explored through the Splintered Mirror, a reflection of memory gaps, emotional dissonance, and the struggle to reconcile the whole self.
Everyday Life Impact: Frequent dissociation disrupts work, relationships, and self-esteem, but moments of integration provide hope and strength.
Narrative:
The protagonist stares into the Splintered Mirror, which reflects fragmented versions of themselves. Each shard shows a piece of their life—some painful, others joyful. The mirror accuses them of being incomplete, but they begin to see that the fragments together form a Julia set, revealing the interconnected beauty of their system. They leave the mirror not “fixed” but accepting of their complexity.
Symbolism: The Julia set shows how imperfection contributes to the wholeness of identity, celebrating resilience in fragmentation.

5. "Imagine, Dragon Curves"
Fractal Concept: The dragon curve fractal, with its elegant yet chaotic twists, represents the unpredictable paths of DID and the creative potential within.
DID Focus: The Wyrm of Doubt embodies internalized fear and the protective alters that shield creativity but also restrict freedom.
Everyday Life Impact: Fluctuating confidence in creative endeavors, with fear of judgment from oneself or others. Social interactions feel like navigating unseen curves, yet imagination offers escape and solace.
Narrative:
The protagonist meets the Wyrm of Doubt along a twisting path, its scales shimmering like fractured memories. The Wyrm claims the path is dangerous, warning the protagonist to turn back. As they press on, the path’s curves reveal hidden creativity and the beauty of unpredictability. By facing the Wyrm, they realize it is not an enemy but a guardian born of fear, and together they unlock a world of imagination and self-expression.
Symbolism: Dragon curves reflect the duality of creativity and fear, showing that resilience comes from embracing the twists and turns of life.

6 "Chiaroscuro"
Themes: Light and shadow, embracing contrast, and finding balance.
Narrative: The protagonist navigates a dreamlike landscape, moving between light and shadow as they reconcile opposing parts of their identity.
Symbolism: The interplay of light and dark represents the duality of struggle and growth, with chiaroscuro (light-dark contrast) embodying the complexity of DID.
Mood: Mystical and dynamic, with shifts between soft and intense melodies.

7 "The Hyperbolic manifold"
Theme: Parenting and Family
Narrative: A single parent with DID sings a lullaby to their child, reflecting on the challenges and joys of balancing caregiving with their internal struggles. The song captures the quiet resilience found in moments of connection.
Symbolism: The lullaby becomes a bridge between the parent’s internal world and the external bond with their child.
Setting: A small, cozy bedroom illuminated by soft moonlight.
Style: Gentle and tender, with a simple, melodic structure akin to a lullaby.

8 "Strange Attractor"
Theme: Relationships and Communication
Narrative: A protagonist navigates a party, switching between alters to manage social anxiety. The song explores the tension between wanting to be authentic and the need to adapt to feel safe.
Symbolism: Masks symbolize the personas projected in social situations. Mirrors scattered throughout the space reflect different aspects of the self.
Setting: A lively party in a city loft, blending external chaos with internal conflict.
Style: Playful but bittersweet, with upbeat rhythms contrasted by introspective lyrics.

9 "Orbit trap"
Theme: Imagination and Escapism
Narrative: The protagonist creates a vivid fantasy world through art and storytelling to escape reality. The song explores the duality of imagination as both a refuge and a potential barrier to connection.
Symbolism: The paper world represents the delicate balance between creation and isolation.
Setting: A cluttered artist’s studio, filled with sketches and sculptures coming to life.
Style: Whimsical and dynamic, blending playful melodies with moments of introspection.
