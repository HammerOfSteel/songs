# Foxglove Fiddle

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-04-23
**Edited:** 2025-04-23

---

Some flowers don’t grow in gardens.  
They just… appear.  
By fences, in gravel, behind bins —  
Soft trumpets no one tuned, but still… they sing.

We found them near the broken stile  
Foxgloves leaning like nosy spies  
You cupped one close and whispered in  

“Tell Eira she’s got better eyes”

I laughed, “What’s that for?” You went all shy —  

“She drew me purple and gave me sky.”  

“This bit’s the kissy bit.”

You twirled and said, “She likes my voice.”  
I said, “That’s love, bach — not just noise.”

[Male and Girl british voices – soft harmony]  
Foxglove fiddle, broken string song  
Still she sings, still skips along  
Wind in the bloom, buzz in the stem  
Love’s just laughter you hum with a friend

I made her a crown out of dandelion heads  
“This is magic.”  is what I said

She gave you a leaf with her name in biro  
You wore it like a badge — hero spiral  

“Dad, why are friends like flowers too?”  

“They grow where you let them through.”  

“That’s for Eira.”

“If I had a foxglove violin  
I’d play her a song ‘bout where we’ve been  
About sandwich halves and skipping rope  
And how her jumper smells like soap.”

We don't fight, just argue songs  
“But it goes like — la la wrong wrong wrong!”

Still sounded right, if I’m honest, kid  
That’s why the foxgloves never hid  
Not to impress, or be found  
But just to be near without a sound

“This one's Miss Finn — she's the queen of string”  

Foxglove fiddle, dandelion crown  
Still she sings when no one’s around  
Love is just a soft pretend  
That holds your hand and calls you friend

So we don’t fix the string or bow  
We just play slow...  
And let love grow.
