# The dreaming

**Labels:** ling-di-long
**Created:** 2025-04-11
**Edited:** 2025-04-11

---

“We flew upside down,” she says with a grin,
A story of dreaming about to begin.
“clouds soft in over the yonder, 
Fell asleep in an hour, woke up down under.
He wore socks with his sandals and chased every lizard,
Called a kookaburra a seagull wizard.”
“Did the lizard talk?” Tima asks with a stare —
“Maybe,” Mam winks. “If you listened just there...”

“The wind told us stories,” Mam whispers soft,
“About Walu the sun and the moon up aloft.
We sat by a creek and a guide told us clear:
‘Everything speaks, if your heart wants to hear.’”
“Did you see the rainbow snake?” Tima beams,
“Only in the water… or maybe my dreams.

Peter burned his sausage, blamed the stars
I traced dream lines in the sky from afar
“You think too much,” he said with a grin
But I swore I saw Bunjil winging in
“Is that a bird?” Tima tugs at her sleeve
“He makes the wind, and maybe… me.”

“I didn’t bring back much,” Mam says, eyes low
“Just a feeling, a map where the rivers still flow
Where stories sleep under bark and stone
And wake in your voice when you dream alone”
Tima yawns, already halfway there,
“Tell it again?” — a soft bedtime prayer

Everything speaks, if your heart wants to hear
The dreaming’s not gone, it’s just sleeping near
Through branches, through song, through tea and toast
In the story you tell to the ones you love most
