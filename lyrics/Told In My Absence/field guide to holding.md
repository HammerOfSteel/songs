# field guide to holding

**Labels:** Told In My Absence
**Created:** 2025-10-19
**Edited:** 2025-10-20

---

Axiom: you arrived and every unit of time re-sized.
Given: two hands, a kettle, a room of weather.
To prove: love—solved for the exponents of my heart. Q.E.D.

Open to Page One: Discovery, ink bloom, coffee ring, smudge of jam.
Footnote: the morning was rain-shaped; the window wore stars from your stickers.
Your fist, a conker, I write: specimen of thunder held in velvet.
Habitat: my chest. Range: all hours.
Control notes: when you breathe in, the kettle agrees; when you laugh, the clock forgets itself.
I measure your world in silly units, seven ladybirds from wrist to window,
two sparrow-hops from bedside to brave.
[plucked fiddle echo]
On graph paper, I draw a proof,
the curve goes skyward wherever your name lands.
You yawn; I annotate: soft weather event, localised; probability of wonder = 1.
I copy the sound of your socks on the kitchen lino, shh-ssk, shh-ssk,
and the way your hair argues kindly with the wind.
Margin note: hold gently, not tight; we are custodians, not cages.

What’s fragile isn’t weak,
it’s simply telling us how to touch it.

Hold lightly, keep warm; never hard, never harm.
Hands like pockets of calm in a storm.
Write it down so the heart remembers,
feathers, freckles, rainy Decembers.
If love is a bird, then open your palm,
let it sing, let it land, let it go, stay calm.
This is my field guide to holding,
letters, maps, a lantern unfolding.

Identification: hair the colour of wet oak, eyes—bluebells after rain.
Call: a three-note “Dad?” ascending like a kettle nearly there.
Diet: toast soldiers, crumbs of moonlight, stories with the crusts left on.
Favourite words: nearly, anyway, hello again.

Distribution: windowsills, bus stops, my left shoulder at dusk.
Behaviour: picks wild names for ordinary streets;
renames fear into a pet we can train, “sit, wait, okay, off you pop.”
Control sketch: little boots on a canal path;
I notate the rhythm, tap tap rest, tap tap rest,
and underline the secret: you walk like you’re listening.
I test a theorem: if grief is rain, then love is the coat that doesn’t mind getting soaked.
The numbers behave; the page fogs slightly; a teardrop becomes a lens.
Conclusion: you make even the hard bits gentle by naming them true.

When it’s small: hold near the heartbeat; count to four.
When it’s loud: make tea; open a door.
When it’s fragile: two hands, wide breath.
When it’s heavy: ask the floor for help—sit, rest.
When it’s leaving: don’t clutch; stay near.
When it’s storming: sing its true name, not its fear.
When it’s silence: place a chair beside it.
When it’s joy: step back, don’t hide it.


Advanced Section: absent phenomena; care protocols for the not-here.
I log the shape you leave, oval of air on the pillow,
steam ghost on the mug, faint moon of a thumbprint on the pane.
I practice holding without keeping:
wrap the memory in breath, not tape;
warm it with song, don’t laminate.
I chart the routes where I still find you—
in hedgerow grammar, in puddles rehearsing the sky,
in the way the village exhales when the bus doors hiss open.
Footnote: when I say your name, the rain stands up straighter.
Lemma: love outlives its evidence.
Therefore (by mornings): the proof re-proves itself,
my heart, exponentiated, still resolves to you.

The rule holds: we never own,
we hold, we learn, we’re loaned.
