# Familiar Strangers

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-25
**Edited:** 2024-10-25

---

(Verse 1)
We crossed in the hallway, eyes sharp as knives,
Her words like stones, thrown to survive.
At first, all I saw was the edge of her face,
But pain has a way of leaving its trace.

She wore her anger like armor bright,
A shield built from battles fought out of sight.
I didn’t yet know what lived in her gaze,
A story unfolding beneath the haze.

(Chorus)
Familiar strangers, passing by,
Each one hiding the reasons why.
Behind every word is a weight they bear,
A burden unseen, held in the air.

(Verse 2)
One day, we talked by chance in the rain,
And her voice cracked beneath the strain.
She said, “Sometimes, I don’t know how to be—
It’s easier to hurt than let them see me.”

Her laughter felt thin, like a thread about to break,
I saw in her eyes the toll heartache takes.
The bully I feared was someone alone,
Fighting a battle unknown.

(Chorus)
Familiar strangers, passing by,
Each one hiding the reasons why.
Behind every word is a weight they bear,
A burden unseen, held in the air.

(Bridge)
We all wear masks, some heavy, some light,
Each one tailored to get through the night.
Her mask was sharp, her words cut deep,
But pain like that doesn’t let you sleep.

(Chorus)
Familiar strangers, passing by,
Each one hiding the reasons why.
Behind every word is a weight they bear,
A burden unseen, held in the air.

(Outro)
Now when we pass, I look past the glare,
To the silent burdens she tries to repair.
She’s still learning how to survive the fight,
And so am I—maybe that’s all right.
