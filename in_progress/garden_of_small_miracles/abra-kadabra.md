# Abra-Kadabra

**Created:** 2026-02-21

Instrumentation
- Toy piano, glockenspiel, soft hand percussion, mandolin, warm synth swells.

Focus
- Playful domestic magic and the small conjurings we do for comfort (making tea as spell, folding laundry as a charm).

Hook
- Light incantation: "Abra-kadabra, fold the night in two — one part hush, one part you." Repeated as whisper in background.

Structure
- Haiku intro (tiny conjuring image)
- Verse (small domestic 'spells')
- Chorus (incantatory refrain)
- Mid-section (call-and-response: a child's voice answers the incantation)
- Outro (a single toy-piano motif)

Lyrical seed

Haiku:
A folded hem hides
three coins for the travelling god —
a towel becomes cloak.

Verse sketch:
I boiled the tea like a promise and hid my wish within the steam,
I folded all the shirts like secret maps and stitched the seam of dream.

Production note
- Keep chorus whispered under main vocal; add a faint chorus of child voices for warmth.
