# Crumbs on the Event Horizon

**Labels:** Super Nova
**Created:** 2025-06-11
**Edited:** 2025-06-11

---

When you feel like breaking down or crashing in,
Who do you turn to, to forgive your sin?
When you cried your lonely tears,
Who will be there to fight your fears?
And when it feels like no one would understand,
Who was there to hold your hand?

Woke up in a multiverse of toast and tears,
alarm screamin’ like it’s ten lightyears.
One sock on, juice down the stairs,
and the dog's eatin’ glitter with zero cares.

Someone’s cryin’ ‘cause the jam’s the wrong shade,
the cereal box exploded mid-upgrade.
I'm stirrin’ tea with a butter knife again —
that's three spoons lost to the kitchen plane.

Tried to put her coat on — she’s wearin’ mine,
said “It’s soft and falls in libe.”
No time for logic, the bin’s on fire,
and someone’s drawn Saturn on the tumble dryer.

I whisper, "breathe," to no one in sight,
while I chase a shoe through quantum night.
I ain’t her dad by blood or law —
but I’m still the gravity on her floor.

Crumbs on the event horizon,
half-dressed and still risin’.
Chaos got laws I’ve learned to love,
stars in my socks and toast in the glove.

Lunchbox leak — fruit juice flood,
turns her backpack into an ocean of mud.
She says, “Why does cheese smell sad, no I mean bad?”
and I genuinely don’t know, that’s mad.

The school gate greets me with a look,
as if I’ve written crime scenes in her reading book.
One glove gone, one sleeve inside out,
someone’s singing Frozen at full self-doubt.

I nod, I smile, I deflect, I absorb —
I’m the shield, the snack, the metaphoric orb.
She melts down ‘cause her shoe won’t tie,
then hugs me like I’m the sky.

I say, “We’re okay,” and it ain’t a lie —
it’s just held together with sighs and pie.
Someone says, “You do so well alone,”
and I wanna say, “mate, it’s a found-thing home.”

Crumbs on the event horizon,
half-lost but still risin’.
Entropy’s real, but so’s her laugh,
and love don’t come with a clean-straight path.
Socks ain’t matchin’, hearts still beat,
burnt toast gospel under bare feet.

Evenin’ hits like a soft collapse,
she's got jam in her hair, no naps.
I’m countin’ breaths like prayer beads,
foldin’ the day's impossibles into needs.

She breaks down — full sob, mid-spill,
says, “I miss her but I don’t know what, still.”
And I — I know that ache,
the one with whos names, a wild worn shake.

So I hold her like the whole day’s worth,
like I’m the thread between her and earth.
No cape, just sleeves rolled wrong,
whisperin’, “You’re okay,” like a lullaby song.

I don’t fix it — just stay in place,
a quiet body, a softer space.
And somehow that’s enough, tonight.
In the wild swirl of wrongs, we got one right.

Crumbs on the event horizon,
full tilt but still risin’.
Toys in the sink and socks in the stew,
but this is our orbit, held by glue.
No cape, no script, just breath and tea,
Hers, mine by heart — she’s family.

The floor’s a galaxy of cereal dust,
and the stars are fingerprints on glass.
I breathe.
Not because it’s peaceful —
because this, this is home.
