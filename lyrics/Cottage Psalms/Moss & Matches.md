# Moss & Matches

**Labels:** Cottage Psalms
**Created:** 2025-04-30
**Edited:** 2025-04-30

---

The summit of Snowdon,
Here, you are,
nearer to heaven,
Here you will see,
tempests and tranquility

Boots wet, socks worse — sheep don’t care.
Mist wraps me like a nan’s old prayer.
Lichen glows green on the stones I climb,
Each step a maybe, not a line.

Got your photo in my chest zip, creased and warm —
You hold me like weather that never quite storms.
Used to think fire came fast and bright,
But you taught me how to strike it right.

With moss for patience, tinder for grace,
And matches you keep in a dry place.
Love ain’t loud — it’s slow and earned.
The kind of flame that don’t get burned.

Met a bloke near the halfway café —
Said, “Alright, love? You walkin’ or runnin’ away?”
Told him, “Bit of both, depends how it ends.”
He laughed like a song you only share with friends.

My thoughts come talkin’ like old mates drunk:
“Why climb this rock, it’s pointless bunk.”
But I hear you, Mam, soft in my grin —
Saying, “There’s always light when you look within.”

I packed too light, forgot a map —
But I know the stars, and I know your laugh.
So I walk on, knuckles in the wind,
With a whisper of you where breath begins.

You can build a fire if you know where to look,
Even moss will catch when the storm’s mistook.
She taught me to breathe in the smoke, not choke —
A match is a promise, a path she spoke.

Summit’s close, but the view ain’t loud —
It’s just the hush that follows a crowd.
I lay my hand on a stone that’s warm,
Like someone lit a prayer in the form of form.

Thought I’d cry, thought I’d break —
But grief is quieter than folks make.
It’s in the pocket fluff, the tea gone cold,
The dreams you don’t dare to hold.

Mam, they say you were wild, fierce, and sweet —
Left before I knew your feet.
But here’s me, on the top of Wales,
Writing you in mist like living tales.

So here’s the hex I cast for you:
A flame in moss, a sky turned blue,
A laugh I’ll teach my daughter too —
To love like smoke and hold like dew.

You never needed to stay long to stay deep.
You stitched your name where the ridges sleep.
And when I strike this match tonight,
I'll light it with your kind of light.

Love ain’t loud.
It don’t ask permission.
It’s the slowest kind of ignition.

You can build a fire if you know where to look,
Even moss will catch when the storm’s mistook.
She taught me to breathe in the smoke, not choke —
A match is a promise, a path she spoke.

Where the stone wears lichen robes,
And breath becomes gold in a mouth that remembers —
There, we name the spark:
Not flare, not blaze… but becoming.
