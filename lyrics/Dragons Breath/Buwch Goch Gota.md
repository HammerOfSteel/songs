# Buwch Goch Gota

**Labels:** Dragons Breath
**Created:** 2025-07-05
**Edited:** 2025-07-05

---

Ladybird, ladybird, fly with flair,
Land on my nose if you dare!
Grampa says they carry dreams,
So I sent mine off in threes.

One for love,
One for sky,
One to ask the question “Why?”

Buwch goch gota on her leaf throne,
Red and black like she's hand-sewn.
She don't speak, but she knows time,
Lives a week, still climbs like it's prime.

Tima asked, “Do they miss their mums?”
I said, “Maybe... but wings hum drums.”
See, life's not length, it's light we leave,
Like soft footprints in July's eve.

“Why do some fly, and some crawl slow?”
She said, “Maybe they just don’t know.”
And that’s the truth — from out her mouth,
Wisdom wrapped in strawberry South.

Buwch goch gota, tiny and free,
Painted like stories on a summer tree.
She don’t need a reason, she just does,
Little red wonder, just because.

They don’t care ‘bout the time that goes,
They sit on flowers, boop your nose!
And if you cry, they just stay still —
‘Til you laugh again. That’s their skill!

Mam used to say, “Small things hold the most,”
Like cwtshes, toast, or haunted posts.
I used to chase big truths, full speed,
Now I just sit — and let the weed grow seed.

Ladybird lands on the bus stop bench,
Tima goes, “That’s Nan — look, she’s French!”
Nah, love — she’s Welsh and proud,
But maybe she’s both now, up in the cloud.

Time ain’t cruel, it’s just busy, see —
It rushes past those who can’t just be.
But bugs know better than we ever will,
How to rest, and blink, and feel the thrill.

Buwch goch gota, tiny and free,
Painted like stories on a summer tree.
She don’t need a reason, she just does,
Little red wonder, just because.

She got dots like polka pants!
She don’t need no dance!
But when she flies — oof, she goes!
All zig-zag, like “BYE you nose!”

Some say time is straight — a line.
But you? You live it like a rhyme.
You say:

“I’m here, then there. Then snack time too.”

And just like that — you teach me truth.

I watched you draw wings on every stone,
Said “they’re all bugs that got too alone.”
So we lined ‘em up by the windowsill,
And now they shine when the house goes still.

And when I go, when my clock runs out,
I’ll ride a ladybird wing — no doubt.
I’ll land in your dreams, soft and bold,
Say “You still got stories left to be told.”

Buwch goch gota, tiny and free,
Painted like stories on a summer tree.
No need for reasons, just let it be —
You’re the whole wide world on a leaf with me.

Bye ladybird.
Tell the stars I said “hi.”

She said, “What if ladybirds carry the sun?”
And maybe they do.
Maybe they do.
