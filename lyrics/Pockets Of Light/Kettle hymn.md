# Kettle hymn

**Labels:** Pockets Of Light
**Created:** 2025-08-25
**Edited:** 2025-08-25

---

Click of the switch and the hymn begins,
whistle like psalms through the coppered tins.
Steam curls scripture in a silver line,
halo of kettle makes the kitchen divine.
Drip-drop gospel on the window’s glass,
echo of prayer in the pipes that pass.
Clatter of cups in a cupboard choir,
tea-time temple, spark and fire.

Amen with a teaspoon. Amen with a sigh.

Milk-pour mantra, swirl in the deep,
sugar-sweet rosary stirred in sleep.
Biscuit dunk blessing, break on the lip,
laughter liturgy in a finger-tip.
Clink-song chorus of porcelain rim,
poured-out hymn in the kitchen dim.
Hiss of the kettle, incense rise,
holy is steam where the daylight lies.

Even the spoon’s a cantor tonight.

Kettle hymn, kettle hymn, sing it again,
steam in the rafters, prayer in the den.
Kettle hymn, kettle hymn, rhyme in the cup,
pour it all gentle, let the world drink up.

Cup-rim gospel, saucer psalm,
table’s an altar of sugar and calm.
Jam-jar hymn book, buttered bread creed,
patience is faith in the boiling’s speed.
Swirl of the steam is a hymn in disguise,
turning the air into starry skies.
Teabag oracle, prophecy steep,
wisdom in water, secrets deep.

The whistle is scripture, the clatter is prayer.

Kettle hymn, kettle hymn, sing it again,
steam in the rafters, prayer in the den.
Kettle hymn, kettle hymn, rhyme in the cup,
pour it all gentle, let the world drink up.

Teaspoon swinging like a censer bright,
clink of the handle is holy night.
Cup after cup after cup, amen,
each sip a hymn we begin again.

Stir of the sugar is salt of the earth,
song of the kettle a second birth.
Whistle and echo, chant and rhyme,
tea-time temple outside of time.

The kettle sings louder than any choir.

Kettle hymn, kettle hymn, sing it again,
steam in the rafters, prayer in the den.
Kettle hymn, kettle hymn, rhyme in the cup,
pour it all gentle, let the world drink up.
… pour it all gentle, let the world drink up.
