# Cling Clang

**Created:** 2026-02-21

Instrumention
- Tinplate/kitchen-percussion (pots, teaspoons), kalimba, nylon guitar with muted pluck, light upright bass.
- Handclap rhythm, occasional toy piano hits for sparkle.

Focus
- Domestic cacophony turned into accidental music; finding melody in daily noise and the small rhythms that hold a home together.

Hook
- Short, percussive chant: "Cling clang, keep the small; cling clang, sing it all."

Structure
- Haiku intro (3 lines of image)
- Verse (list of those small noises, each line = object + verb) — sentence-to-sentence rhyming
- Chorus (mantra chant, percussive)
- Bridge (spoken micro-story about a child learning rhythm)
- Outro (soft kalimba + one pot struck once)

Lyrical seed

Haiku:
A spoon on a bowl,
a clock with no hands counts the day,
we drum what we know.

Verse sketch:
The kettle knocks like a kindly fist,
keys scatter like coins in a list.
A lid claps a joke against the pan,
and laughter keeps the meter of our plan.

Production note
- Record real kitchen sounds; treat them as instruments (EQ and reverb for musical glue).
