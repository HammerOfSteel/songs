# Väre å vonn

**Labels:** Väre å vonn
**Created:** 2025-05-27
**Edited:** 2025-05-28

---

“I made a spell, with real stuff.
Not fake wand telly rubbish.
Proper things.”

I got string from the swing that snapped in spring,
and a bell from Gran’s cat that don’t even ring.
A biscuit crumb from behind the chair,
a ghosty hairband stuck in my hair.

Found fluff from my sock and a dead leaf kiss,
plus a frog’s burp (I heard it – promise).
I mixed it all in a cup of jam,
stirred it round with a stolen crayon.

Now every time I stamp three toes,
the sun peeks out and the toaster glows.
It’s real, I swear, just try it once—
but not too loud or you’ll wake the bunks.

Väre and Vonn in a mountain jar,
Dash of thunder from blue mountains far.
Drop of mist from a bear's old sigh,
Boil it slow ‘neath a midwinter sky.

I pinched a pebble from the playground shade,
and a half-done sticker off a lemonade.
I nicked the smell of toast at school,
and a puddle laugh that made me drool.

There’s mud from my knees and chalk from the wall,
and the echo you hear when your name gets called.
I added a worm that was nearly a snake,
and a tissue I used for a cry-fake.

Then I whispered a secret, just for the brew,
about how my mum said “I’m proud of you.”
That made it fizz like pop and stars—
Now it heals your knees and sticky jars.

Väre and Vonn in a sleet-bound spoon,
Howl from the hill and a scrap of rune.
Bit of breath from the great lake at dawn,
Spell to wait when your love is gone

I put in the blink from the firefly game,
and the bit in a hug where you feel your name.
A button from Gran’s coat, shaped like a moon,
and the tick from the clock at the end of June.

A sigh from the dog when he finds his place,
and the soft warm line on a pillowcase.
I tied it up with a plait of breath,
and kept it safe in a jar marked: “YES.”

Väre and Vonn in a soup of sky,
Sun plays shy and clouds drift by.
Raindrop joke and a laugh from the storm,
Spell for days both wild and warm.

“Bits of väre... bits of vonn.
One spell a day keeps the grey all gone.”
