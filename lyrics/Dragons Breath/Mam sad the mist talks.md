# Mam sad the mist talks

**Labels:** Dragons Breath
**Created:** 2025-07-12
**Edited:** 2025-07-12

---

One spoon fog from the mountain bend,
Two drops caught where the tree roots end.
A whisper from a window just before light,
And socks that went missin’ on Tuesday night.
Stir it slow with a feather from rain,
Say nothin’ loud, or it won’t come again…

Out the door, wellies on, skip a stone to start the day,
Mist curled round my cereal breath like it had somethin’ to say.
Mam pegged socks with a wink, called it "ghost’s laundry,"
I just nodded, eyes wide, thinkin’ fog had a job, see?
By the hedge it clung like secrets on a granny’s tongue,
Talkin’ soft as chimney sighs, back when I was young.
Didn’t get the jokes then, now I laugh out loud,
Like when she said, “The mist keeps track when your head’s in the cloud.”

Mam said the mist talks, under hedgerow hush,
Spillin’ old tea in the bramble bush.
Says what you ain't ready to hold in your palm,
Whispers soft thunder dressed up as calm.

By the bus stop, thick as stew, clung to my coat,
Said, “Tell your mate she’s kind,” so I scribbled a note.
Didn’t post it, just tucked it by the mossy post box,
Felt daft, felt magic, like I’d just fed a fox.
Mam said fog’s nosy, gets in your socks,
Finds old lies folded up like paper clocks.
Now I know she meant it’s truth in disguise,
Wanders slow so it catches your sighs.

Mam said the mist talks, and it don’t shout,
Just leans on your shoulder till the words leak out.
Tells you your own tale with a rhubarb grin,
A riddle in a teacup, sugar and sin.

There was a lad once said he heard it hum,
Like a chorus of “don’t you dare forget where you’re from.”
In the sheep field, near the stile where I slipped,
Fog told me, “She loved you, even when she flipped.”
Didn’t cry then, just hummed like the fog,
Felt my chest bloom like dew in the log.
Mam said it’s cheeky, a meddlin’ sprite,
But always fair, just don’t ask it twice.

Mam said the mist talks, and aye, she’s right,
Told me “carry love light,” not just when it’s bright.
It’s the ghost of the kettle, the puff of toast smoke,
A nod from the past in a poppy cloak.

Now I walk where she did, boots in her tread,
Fog twirls round me like the things unsaid.
Wears her perfume, lavender and toast,
And sometimes, swear down, I hear her the most.
Told the mist “I’m listenin’,” and it smiled in return,
Left a dandelion poem in the fern.
Mam was fog, soft, slow, but sure,
And the mist, well, it’s her, just a little bit more.

Mam said the mist talks, and it always did,
Kept my secrets when I was just a kid.
Now I keep hers, in rain, in steam,
In every quiet moment between a dream.

Sprinkle a giggle, a hush and a hum,
Fold in the echo of what we become.
Tie it with ribbon from Mam’s old coat,
And blow it to sleep in a milk-bottle boat.
Mist don’t forget, it just waits in the dew,
So whisper your love, and it might answer you.
