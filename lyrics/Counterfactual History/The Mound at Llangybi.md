# The Mound at Llangybi

**Labels:** Counterfactual History
**Created:** 2025-04-05
**Edited:** 2025-04-05

---

Tick tock, tick tock
The rhythm of the clock
As the time of day is racing by
I shout out loud ‘please stop’

Canvas crackles, fire low,
Mist curls tight where the larches grow.
Maps worn thin, boots full of ache,
Seventy years in every step I take.
Ghost of a name in the valley mist,
Her laughter still wrapped in a cold night’s kiss.
I wasn’t dreaming—I know I heard
A hush too loud, like a whispered word.
So I stood, and the world held its breath—
Even silence can smell like death.

A light between the larches,
And the years began to bend.
I followed where the stillness led,
Like I knew just how it ends.
Not a dream, not a trick, not a hope half-spun—
I walked into the dark,
And the dark came undone.

Stones like teeth in the side of the hill,
An old barrow mouth, forever still.
Brambles clawed like they knew my name,
Like the earth remembered who I became.
Crouched and crawling, scraped and torn,
A man with bones the light had worn.
And then—a shiver. Then a breeze.
Then dawn too early through younger trees.
My heart kicked once. Then twice. Then still.
Time had cracked. I think I will.

My pack was there. My boots were dry.
But no buzz, no beep. No signal sky.
And something in my spine felt new—
Like sorrow shrugged, and let me through.

A light between the larches,
And the present slipped away.
The birds were wrong, the sky too soft,
Like it never held that day.
Not a dream, not a fluke, not a tale half-spun—
I stepped through time,
And time came undone.

Cwmcarn’s roofs in the valley wide,
But the roads were thinner, the signs all lied.
A girl at the shop with a Walkman grin,
Told me the date with a dimpled chin.
And I—I stood still in my boots and skin,
A relic in a time I shouldn’t be in.
Could it be? Did I… break through?
Or was this place always waiting too?

A light between the larches,
And the weight fell off my years.
My knees remembered younger ways,
My eyes unlearned their tears.
Not a ghost, not a god, not a war half-won—
But the moment I stepped through,
My story had begun.

I wandered down the high street slow,
Past the house I used to know.
My hands were shaking—was she near?
Could I find that night? Could I interfere?
The man I was still walked this ground,
With her small hand tucked in, safe and sound.
But one mistake would cost it all—
That night, that choice, that fatal call.
And now I stand outside my life,
Where one more breath might rewrite time.

Don’t wake me yet—if this is dream,
Let it run until the seams
Of grief and time and love align—
And let me lose myself in signs.
A flash of hair. A laugh half-heard.
A second chance stitched to a word.

What is this life if, full of care,
We have no time to stand and stare.
No time to stand beneath the boughs
And stare as long as sheep or cows.
No time to see, when woods we pass,
Where squirrels hide their nuts in grass.
No time to see, in broad daylight,
Streams full of stars, like skies at night.
No time to turn at Beauty's glance,
And watch her feet, how they can dance.
No time to wait till her mouth can
Enrich that smile her eyes began.
A poor life this if, full of care,
We have no time to stand and stare.
