# Snap, Crackle, Taid

**Labels:** Dragons Breath
**Created:** 2025-07-06
**Edited:** 2025-07-06

---

How can you get very far,
If you don't know who you are?
How can you do what you ought,
If you don't know what you've got?
And if you don't know which to do
Of all the things in front of you,
Then what you'll have when you are through
Is just a mess without a clue
Of all the best that can come true
If you know What and Which and Who.

Snap, crackle, Taid, brew steamin’ slow,
Old blue Husqvarna hummin’ in tow.
Said, “Fix what’s broke, even hearts, 
Just needs parts, patience, and proper starts.”

He tuned his life like a trumpet’s bell,
Didn’t speak loud, but when he did, it fell
like stars in your lap or a drop on glass,
Small words stick, let the big ones pass.

He once said, “Now is all you’ll get,”
So he rolled his cigs with a kind of regret.
Not for what was, but what folks miss, 
Always chasin’ clocks instead of bliss.

Taught me string theory with jam on toast,
Said, “It’s all connected, bach,  coast to ghost.”
And when I asked what God might be,
He much,  just cracked on strong. 

Taid had vinyls, real old cuts,
Bit of Chet, bit of Ella, no ifs or buts.
He’d nod to the trumpet like it knew his ache,
Said, “Some sounds fix what words can’t make.”

Had his shades on even in the gray,
Didn't miss a single day.

Missed him though, holding you,
Would haves, should haves,
Still don't quiet do.

He'd ask me things like,
“What’s heavier, time or choice?”
Then shrug and say, “”

"Don't argue with fools or clocks," he said,
"Just boil yer storm and go to bed."

He painted with yellow and Sunday air,
Knew the value of quiet, just bein’ there.
In the hiss and sizzle on fry, 
Pomadoro hours on the fly.

Snap, crackle, Taid,  rhythm in his bones,
Tea-stained fingers and weather-worn tones.
Kept the day hummin’ with wrench and song,
Didn’t say much, just cracked on strong.

“Life’s not about what you know, son...
It’s about what you notice.”
Sit, see, feel and be.
A string of stars between you and me.
breakfast rambles, joy, 
toast n' oboy.
Bet, wild wild way for a morning to start.
moments living rent free in the heart.

Now I pass on what he passed to me,
In the small things, worn old spaghetti recipe.
She asks, “Was he magic?”
I say, “aye, proper jazz magician and all that. But the kind you don’t see, just feel.”

He never preached, just lived it clean,
Laid bricks in rhythm, kept his corners green.
A trumpet call, hear it still,
In every “crack on bach”, goosebumps up, gives me chill.

So we fix bikes and we hum old tunes,
And when the toast burns, I swear he swoons.
She says, “Taid sounds cool,” and she ain’t wrong.
Wish you could've met him, hear his trumpets song.

Snap, crackle, Taid, a storm in ease,
Built a home in the wind and the honeybees.
Didn’t chase nothin’, just stood and smiled, 
Carried the world… held my warm an wild.

Easy now, bach.
Crack on.
