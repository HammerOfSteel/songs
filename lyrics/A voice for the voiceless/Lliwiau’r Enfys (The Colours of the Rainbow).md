# Lliwiau’r Enfys (The Colours of the Rainbow)

**Labels:** A voice for the voiceless
**Created:** 2025-02-15
**Edited:** 2025-03-05

---

"On a quiet spring morning, in a small valley town, a father and his daughter walked hand in hand. The early light stretched across the sky, chasing away the winter’s chill. Today was a special day—it was painting day at The Three Sisters Daycare. And the little girl had her mind set on something bright, something beautiful… something she couldn’t quite remember, but always felt in her heart."

The river hums, the branches sway,
A new sun rises, gold and grey.
Little hands reach for the sky,
Colours bright, though she knows not why.

She dips her brush in golden hue,
The morning light, the meadow’s dew.
A touch of blue—her father’s eyes,
And red, the warmth of soft goodbyes.

Her teacher kneels and softly asks,
"Who is the lady in the grass?"
The girl just smiles and shakes her head,
"She’s from the stories Dad once read."

Lliwiau’r enfys, lliwiau’r nef, (Colours of the rainbow, colours of the sky,)
Paentio cariad heb y llef. (Painting love without the cry.)
Through little strokes and shaded seams,
She paints a face she’s seen in dreams.

The evening falls, her father waits,
The teacher speaks by the open gates.
"She paints her mother by the stream,"
"She only knows her through a dream."

The river sings, the reeds reply,
The stars wake slowly in the sky.
And though the years may steal her name,
Love still paints the world the same.

Lliwiau’r enfys, lliwiau’r nef,
Paentio cariad heb y llef.
Each colour holds a story bright,
A whispered love in golden light.

Night-time comes, the pages turn,
By candle’s glow, the embers burn.
"Gelert the Gallant," soft and low,
Her mother’s tale from long ago.

The river runs, the valley keeps,
Every story, every grief.
Her mother’s love still lingers near,
Not in touch, but always here.

Lliwiau’r enfys, lliwiau’r nef,
Cariad pur heb ddim diwedd. (Pure love without an end.)
No matter how the seasons change,
Some colours never fade away.

"Goodnight, little one," her father sighs,
"Sleep beneath your mother’s sky."
A candle flickers, the pages close,
The stars still shine, the river flows.
