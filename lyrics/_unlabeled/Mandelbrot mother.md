# Mandelbrot mother

**Created:** 2025-05-25
**Edited:** 2025-05-25

---

“You always ask on this day.”

“Well, yeah... It’s my mum’s day too, innit?”

“fair point, love. Café first, then I’ll spin one up”

“And no fibs this time!”

“Wouldn’t dream of it.”

“She moved like a fight song in a silk dress
Could cook a storm, kiss your stress — and press
every button on the telly remote of your soul
Tima, she had range — full fractal, full scroll.”

Talk about her laugh — wild and cracked like a sky
Her silences — not quiet, just full of thought
Her mind sharp, like scissors in a drawer of spoons
“Could quote Auden while throwin' shade at cartoons”

"She’s the curve you don’t unsee /
The part of you that don’t ask to be /
In the swing of your steps, in the bend of your brow /
She’s here, now — just like that, somehow."

“She weren’t easy, love. Don’t mean bad — means real.
Like mountain weather — sun, sleet, and steel.
She loved loud. Argued sweet.
Could find the flaw in any beat — but still danced.”

“We fought, yeah. But we laughed like thieves.
Slept curled up like commas in tangled grief.
She’s the reason I still check the kettle three times —
Just in case love lives in the steam, not the rhyme.”

“Would she like me?”

“Like you? Love — she’d orbit you.”

“She’d say you’ve her spark — and your own whole sun.
That you don’t just echo her — you run
further, wilder, brighter still —
like the wave that climbed past the mother hill.”

“You hum when you eat — that’s hers, not mine.
And that face you pull when you’re thinkin’? Line for line.
You wear her chaos with better shoes
And love like her — no need for cues.”

"She’s the rhythm you didn’t know you knew /
The mirror cracked, but still shines through /
In every smile you share, each song you hum /
A Mandelbrot of a mum."

“Do you reckon she’d still be wild now?”

“Oh love, she’d have broken the internet and knitted it back up again.”

“Tell me one more story?”

“Aye. But only if you finish that toast.”
