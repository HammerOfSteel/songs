# Real-time Trenches

**Labels:** solvet et coagula
**Created:** 2025-04-22
**Edited:** 2025-04-22

---

“Year one. Maybe? Hard to say. 
Same cafés, same dogs barkin’. 
Heard my own laugh down on Gloucester Road once — didn’t remember laughin’. 
You’d think dying would be more… final. 
But it’s like I just logged out, forgot the password. 
Still here. Still waitin’.”

Park bench ghosts on Whiteladies Road
Bus don’t stop, still flickers "Depot Closed"
Same rain, same bakery smell in the air
Except no one sees me — I ain’t quite there
I watch her flat — lil’ shoes still by the door
Toothbrush untouched, like before she was born
Did I ask for this? A year gone soft
Not death, mate — just driftin’, like Wi-Fi lost

I walk through walls, but not my past
A city made of mirrors, holdin’ fast
And I keep waitin’ for a sign, a shift, a bend
But real-time’s just a trench I can’t transcend

So I stepped past Bristol, past the known
Was in St Ives, then suddenly Rome
Distance don’t mean what it used to mean
Paris fog, Kyoto still, a New York scream
Lookin’ for a crack in the clouds, a lift
What’s that light? Tunnel’s just a myth
Everywhere, but nowhere to land
Even ghosts get tired of standin’

I float through time, through cities asleep
Where silence keeps the dreams we keep
And though I’m gone, I still pretend
That trench is just the start, not the end

Driftin’ through London — lights all blur
Near Shoreditch High, swear I felt a stir
Turn my head — girl starin’ right through me
Proper eye contact, not that glazed-out movie
I flinch. “You see me?” — she nods. “Alright, mate?”
My whole form wobbled like a dodgy gate
She steps closer, calm as a breeze
“You been out long? You look… kinda freeze.”
“Year, give or take.” — “Dead, yeah?”
“Think so.” — She smirks, “Madness, innit?” — “Fair.”
“I’m just visitin’. Projection. Bit of sleepcraft.”
“What like… dreamin’?” — “Nah, it’s the craft.”
She walks through a bin like it ain’t even there
Says, “Most don’t linger — you must care.”
I ask, “Why me? Why can you see?”
She shrugs, “You’re loud, emotionally. Must be the grief.”
And for the first time since the lights went out
I ain’t hollow — just mapped, with a route
We sat on steps, didn’t talk for long
But she said, “You ain’t stuck, mate. You’re just not gone.”

What if this ain’t waitin’? What if this is the task?
To walk, to witness, to feel and ask
What if I’m the thread still left to sew?
My daughter’s eyes — they still glow
And maybe this journey — trench and trail —
Is where I learn what tips the scale
Not done yet. Not quite. But maybe, just maybe
There’s work to do — and she’ll guide me
