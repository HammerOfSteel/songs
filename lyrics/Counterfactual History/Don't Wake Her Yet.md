# Don't Wake Her Yet

**Labels:** Counterfactual History
**Created:** 2025-04-05
**Edited:** 2025-04-05

---

Do not go gentle into that good night,
Old age should burn and rave at close of day;
Rage, rage against the dying of the light.

He’s by the fire, jaw set like stone
Body here, but the soul long gone
I know that posture, know that stare
It’s the look of a man who don’t dare care
The tent behind him, zipped up tight
Inside the last flicker of his light
She’s sleeping, tree years soft
And he won’t move — won’t shake her off
I’m watching from the trees, fists clenched
Too many years in every breath
Screaming silently at a choice I know
But he can’t hear — not yet, not so

Don’t wake her yet / that’s what he said
Too scared to move / too full of dread
But the stars remember / and the wind still knows
What silence takes / and what grief won't show
Maybe he still can / maybe I still can

Next day I walk down roads I know
But the paint is brighter, the time moves slow
Shop signs same but dates are wrong
Familiar ghosts walk right along
I see him again — younger face
Carrying milk, moving through space
Like it don’t press down, like the grief’s still new
Like her laugh ain’t fading, like he still grew
But I know what comes, I know what cracks
I know how love folds under facts
I sip tea in a café still warm from sleep
And wonder what words cut that deep

What would I have listened to?
Not advice — I’d throw that through
But maybe a whisper, maybe a note
Something that floats when you start to choke

Don’t wake her yet / yeah, that’s the curse
The line we walked / that made it worse
But what if paper / could hold the sound
Of what we lost / before it hit the ground
Maybe he still can / maybe I still can

By the river now, sky washed pale
Following thoughts like boats that sail
What would I write? What would I say?
To make him hold her one more day
Not change it all — just shift the thread
Make him turn his haunted head
Make him see the cost ahead
Without breaking him in grief instead
It’s not for me, I’ve had my years
But he could dodge this trail of tears
A letter. Yes. Folded truth.
A second chance slipped to his youth

Don’t wake her yet / no, wake her now
Hold her hand / remember how
The smallest move / the quiet sound
Can turn the wheel back around
Maybe he still can / maybe I still can

What’s the cost of altering fate?
A life unlived, or one too late?
He’ll never know the man I am
But he might become what I never began
Let him see the light before it breaks
Let her dance before time takes
One letter slipped inside his coat
Not a plea, just a note
“If you love her, take her high.
The storm is coming. Trust the sky.”

Some things can’t be fixed
But they can be heard
Even if the message
Arrives in a blur
If the road can’t change
The footfall still can
He was me
But I can still be the hand

Lifting the slab takes our breath away
Corner to edge, edge to corner.
Its weight steps the plank
shifting from foot to foot.
