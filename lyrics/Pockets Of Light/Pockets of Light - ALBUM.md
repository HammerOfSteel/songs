# Pockets of Light - ALBUM

**Labels:** Pockets Of Light, Album
**Created:** 2025-08-22
**Edited:** 2025-09-01

---

Salt for the sorrow,
sugar for the song,
a candle for the keeping
when the night feels long.

Threads of the ordinary,
woven to the skies,
small hands turn the daylight
into holy lullabies.

Pockets of Light
• Description: A daughter’s soft devotion to the tiny holiness of home — crumbs as constellations, steam as incense, love as a pocket-lantern. Non-narrative, rhyme-rich, alchemical hush.
• Tune & Style: Welsh harp arpeggios + mandolin tremolo; airy childlike melody with hymnlike cadences; sparse two-part hums.
• Beat: Mid-slow 4/4 with brushy frame drum and fingertap heartbeat.

Kettle Hymn
• Description: non-native Turning the whistle and clatter of the kitchen into a playful psalm; the ordinary made luminous, “tea-time as temple.”
• Tune & Style: Picked ukulele lead; call-and-response hums; bright open chords; tiny bell hits.
• Beat: Mid-tempo jig-ish 6/8 with soft handclaps and wooden spoon taps.

Lantern in My Pocket
Intro starts like rhyming story, a father and daughter walking through the autumn afternoon, small banter at the chippy, curry chips and little questions about everything.

• Description: Joy kept in a coat pocket—pebbles, tickets, tiny suns—rhymes tumbling like marbles; devotion as everyday carry.
• Tune & Style: Mandolin cross-picking + zitar drones; quick, lilting vocal phrasing.
• Beat: Up-tempo 2/4 walking-jig with foot-stomps and shaker.

Firefly Rosary
• Description: Counting lights on a summer night like prayer beads; sweet, obscure metaphors for hope; non-linear images that rhyme and glow, the sacral in the mundane, the unfathomable thought of the rest of my life without you daffodil
• Tune & Style: Welsh harp harmonics + tin-whistle lines (soft); layered child chorus “mm”s.
• Beat: Gentle 3/4 waltz with brushed cajón.

Salt • Sugar • Candle
• Description: The sacred trio of home—salt tears, sugar spoons, a candle’s catechism; alchemical undertones (salt/sulfur/mercury) in kid-safe imagery.
• Tune & Style: Zitar shimmer under ukulele; close-miked whisper-sing.
• Beat: Mid-tempo 4/4 with rim-taps and soft tambourine.

Little Alembic
• Description: “We stew our giggles into gold, heartly held, boiled and bold” ; devotion in simmer and steam; rhymes bubbling, playful and light.
• Tune & Style: Mandolin + hand-pan (light, acoustic); round-style refrains.
• Beat: 6/8 rolling jig with palm-on-table percussion.

Dear daffodil
• Description: Tima and mams (Nikki's) moments (but not by name) - carrying her, those first 2 months of them together - a world full, sweet gratitude in rhyme, non narrative, just moments stitched, ends like I'm scattered across spacetime - slowly trickling back back home second by second.
• Tune & Style: Welsh harp leads; glass-rim hums; airy falsetto echoes.
• Beat: Slow 4/4 sway with brushed snare-on-cajón.

Peters Pocket Watch Jig
• Description: Time as hopscotch; tick-tock; cheeky devotion to the one who makes you laugh, non narrative banter oi little miss, welsh tales and tongue-twisters.
• Tune & Style: Mandolin melody with jaw-harp twang; playful vocal scats.
• Beat: Snappy 2/4 reel; heel-toe steps and claps.

Shoelace praise
• Description: non-narrative, Knots, loops, bows—small rituals of care; rhymes that tie and untie; devotional undertone, Tima and Papa - small routines and rituals, devotion to each other and the mundane
• Tune & Style: Ukulele + soft zitar ostinato; whispered chorus hook.
• Beat: Mid-tempo 4/4 with muted hand drum and fingertaps.

Dandelion Crucible
• Description: Making wishes in a field; breath as bellows; seeds as little prayers; alchemy of wind and hope.
• Tune & Style: Tin-whistle + harp; airy, sustained vowels; layered hum choir.
• Beat: 3/4 pastoral lilt with light bodhrán brushes.

Honeycomb Geometry
• Description: Counting hexagons, learning little sweetness by shape, why compassion is key to love, holding love and sadness in two hands, devotional math for small hands; rhyme-drunk and golden
• Tune & Style: mid tempo upbeat, Mandolin tremolo, ukulele strums; close harmonies in thirds.
• Beat: Mid-fast 6/8 jig; wooden clackers and shaker.
• as sung by a father and a little girl: use [male voice] and [female voice]

Windowsill Monastery
• Description: Plants, pebbles, toy saints; quiet vows to sunlight; seeing your room after a long long long time; Who am I, why am I here
• Tune & Style: Sparse Welsh harp; single-line vocal that blooms, cross fading between a male voice and a childs female voice.
• Beat: Very slow 4/4, almost rubato; occasional soft chime.

Teacup Oracle
• Description: Reading leaves for tiny futures; playful prophecy, tender humility; timelines strings sings, what do you see in the cup - can you know what I know.
• Tune & Style: Zitar drones + ukulele arpeggios; whispered call-and-answer.
• Beat: 7/8 (gentle, rocking odd-meter) with finger-snaps on 1 & 5.

Thread & Thimble Gloria
• Description: Sewing little hallelujahs into cuffs; prick of a needle as amen; sweet, half-sung rhymes.
• Tune & Style: Mandolin lead; humming round; soft bell tree.
• Beat: Mid 4/4 with thigh-slaps and table-taps.

Beeswax Psalm
• Description: Candle-making images—warm, resinous devotion; words melt and set; non-linear scent-poetry.
• Tune & Style: Harp harmonics, low zitar pedal; breathy close vocal.
• Beat: Slow 6/8 with brushed frame drum, distant handclap on turnarounds.

Chalk & Fern
• Description: Drawing blessings on the pavement; fern-fold secrets; child-sized benedictions that rhyme and tumble, do I kow what they feel, do I know their heart, drawing your name next to a heart - in their play what is you part?
• Tune & Style: Ukulele lead with tin-whistle echoes; light harmony stack.
• Beat: Mid-tempo 2/4 skipping rhythm with claps on 2.

Philosopher’s Pebble
• Description: “This stone is my stone of stones”—transmutation as patience; devotion in pockets; rhyme-heavy, playful.
• Tune & Style: Mandolin ostinato + gentle zitar swells; spoken-song verses.
• Beat: Moderate 4/4 with muted cajón and foot-stomp on choruses.

Apricot Epiclesis
• Description: Blessing the fruit bowl; sacred sweetness; a tiny kitchen liturgy in juicy rhymes, the body of bread the benediction but it's your favorites - orange marmeladen homemade, toast in triangles of the three - sweet summer honey bee.
• Tune & Style: Harp glissandi under ukulele; bright, bell-like vocal lines.
• Beat: Bouncy 6/8 jig; handclaps and spoon-on-mug ticks.

Under Raincoats
• Description: Storm-walk joy; puddles as chalices; devotional play; words splash and chime, blue stained ink - sink into marbled joy.
• Tune & Style: Mandolin + bodhrán (soft); call-and-response “la-la”s.
• Beat: Up-tempo 2/4 reel feel with heel clicks.

Settling star
• Description: Closing hush; breath-prayer for the ones we love; non-narrative images settle like dust-light, your star shines divines the steps to take next, beads of my heart in your last text.
• Tune & Style: Solo Welsh harp, single child voice that folds into a tiny hummed choir.
• Beat: Free-time opening, then slow 3/4 cradle-sway to close.
