# Starling cry

**Labels:** chalice and cauldron
**Created:** 2025-04-07
**Edited:** 2025-04-07

---

She left with sunlight in her suitcase,
A one-way to somewhere warmer, freer.
The ticket was cheap. The promise was bright.
No one told her how light bends before it breaks.

And the sea watched.
And the stars blinked once.
And a single starling flew the wrong way.

It was meant to be a first breath, not the last.
Out of the valley, the slate, the past.
He said, “Come with us—cheap drinks, gold coast.”
I laughed like I hadn’t been hurt like a ghost.
The air tasted sweet, too sweet, too fast.
We danced till the sun kissed broken glass.
I trusted the girl with the eyes like mine,
Didn’t see the leash beneath the wine.
The club pulsed red, like a vein exposed—
Then the blur, the drag, the door that closed.
I woke with wrists too quiet to scream,
New clothes, no phone, no dream.
I was given a number, not a name.
A room, a bed, a face in a frame.
And every smile became the same:
Lure the next, or take the blame.

Say my name in the language of wings,
Where no chains cling and no sirens sing.
I left with light in both my hands—
But came undone in stranger lands.
And no one knows, and no one tries—
But the starling flies,
The starling flies.

I came back to the slate roofed house,
Boots still wet from offshore routes.
Mum said nothing, eyes gone wide,
Just pointed at the badge outside.
Cops said “vanished,” like she ran.
Like she chose some faceless man.
But her bed was made, her books still stacked—
Her scarf still hanging on the coat rack.
I found the post she never sent,
Told her friends where she went.
An island. Blue. Some ferry’s edge.
No return. No final pledge.
I booked a ticket. Left at night.
Didn’t pack much. Just rage and fight.
If they wouldn’t look, then I would burn
Every dock 'til I made them turn.
I spoke to ghosts with names in code—
Followed feathers down the road.

She speaks in shadows.
I scream in light.
We’re stitched in silence,
but built for fight.
Starling cry, guide me through.
Brother, I’m broken—but not through.

Seven years in a cage of gold,
They broke her name, but not her soul.
A bird made of bone left a sign on the wall—
A trail of flight before the fall.
The door was locked, the world stood by—
But the starling flew,
And I heard her cry.

Time here doesn’t walk, it crawls.
Days blur behind these walls.
Each girl has a story she won’t say,
Each eye’s a scream packed away.
I learned how to breathe without sound,
How to lie still and not be found.
The man said, “Smile, this isn’t hell—
It’s business, girl. Play it well.”
But I held a secret in my skin—
A shape of feathers tucked within.
One night I etched a bird in soap,
A shape we carved back home in hope.
Left it in a mirror’s fog,
A prayer inside the prison smog.
And somehow, somehow it reached the sea—
To him. To us. To the last of me.

I came back wrong.
He held me still.
The house remembered,
The wind was ill.
But light can live
Where death once slept—
And the starling rose
Where sorrow wept.

Even home ain’t clean of the flame.
Peace is quiet—but never the same.
I slept in closets. Walked in loops.
He said “you’re safe”—but I watched the troops
March in my mind when plates would clink,
Or water ran too fast in the sink.
I tried to end it, soft and clean—
Just dissolve beneath the green.
But he found me. Pulled me back.
Left a wound in his own track.
And now he’s gone. The room’s gone dim.
I light a candle just for him.
At his wake, I didn’t cry—
I just watched a bird in the sky.
Small and dark. Just one. Just near.
And something whispered: stay right here.
Now I speak where silence grew—
And if they listen, they might too.
For every girl who doesn’t scream—
I fight. I burn. I dream.

They sold my name,
But I wrote it back.
They took my fire,
But I found the spark.
They said I’d fade,
But I became flame.
And I will not forget her name.

They thought I was gone.
But I became the storm in their sleep.
They thought I’d break.
But I learned how to bend like light.
And if you see a starling at the edge of dusk—
Follow it.
You might find her.
You might find me.
