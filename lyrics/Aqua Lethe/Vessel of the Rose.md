# Vessel of the Rose

**Labels:** Aqua Lethe
**Created:** 2025-05-21
**Edited:** 2025-05-21

---

I came down wrapped in linen light,
A whisper slippin’ through the night.
A heart in bloom, a jar of glass,
They sealed me shut and let me pass.
I dreamed of fire, but woke to skin,
To bleed me soft and break me in.
They said, “you’ll learn, but never whole.”
They stitched a lock into me soul.

I kicked the gates with bloodied feet,
Found nothin’ soft, nothin’ sweet.
I cursed the stars, I cracked the bone,
Dragged me through this life alone.
They said, “it’s rage that makes you wise,”
I burned through truth, then swallowed lies.
But salt still drips, don’t matter how—
We’re all just tryin’ to make it out somehow.

I broke slow, I broke kind…

I tore through like a landslide blind.

Thread by thread, I faced the flame—

I let it scar me, ain’t got shame.

We cracked, we bled, we bore that prose—
The soul remembers…
in the vessel of the rose.

Each thorn was ink on pages torn,
I loved in silence, stitched and worn.
You screamed, but me, I studied pain,
Both of us caught in the same rain.

You walked with grace, I limped through fire,
Still we met by the same wire.
You with a book, me with a bruise—
Two ways through, we didn’t choose.

Did you hear the hush before the fall?

Only felt the fists against the wall.

Did you see the light beneath the stone?

Just shadows starin’ through me bone.

We cracked, we split, we bore that prose—
In every scar, the rose still grows.
From wax to ash, from breath to close,
We found each other…
in the vessel of the rose.

Salt or silence, love or flame—
It all leads back to where we came.
