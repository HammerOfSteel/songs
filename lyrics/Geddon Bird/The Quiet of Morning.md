# The Quiet of Morning

**Labels:** Geddon Bird
**Created:** 2024-10-29
**Edited:** 2024-11-08

---

(Verse 1)
The boat pulls from harbor, mist on the Sound,
With dawn’s quiet hush, no other sound.
Ella sits close, a half-smile in place,
Watching the shoreline slip into space.

Past Devil’s Point and Mount Edgcumbe’s trees,
The sea hums softly with old memories.
A gull calls out, cuts through the gray,
And together we drift, carried away.

(Chorus)
In the quiet of morning, nothing’s set,
Every moment a promise, not a debt.
The waves roll soft, the path unwinds—
What stays behind is what we find.

(Verse 2)
Ella laughs as Rame Head fades,
Says, “Funny how life so quickly sways.”
Cawsand’s rooftops, small and low,
Hold echoes of paths we used to know.

“Life’s not a map,” she murmurs light,
“It curves like fate, dips out of sight.”
The lighthouse blinks from a far-off shore,
Guiding us forward, but nothing more.

(Chorus)
In the quiet of morning, nothing’s set,
Every moment a promise, not a debt.
The waves roll soft, the path unwinds—
What stays behind is what we find.

(Bridge – Conversational Reflection)
Ella leans in, says, “What do you see?”
“Not sure,” I smile, “just you and me.”
The sea whispers secrets as tides take hold,
In waters silvered, dark, and old.

What’s ahead won’t wait, what’s gone won’t stay,
But we’ll meet it all, each in our way.

(Chorus – Gentle Build)
In the quiet of morning, nothing’s set,
Every moment a promise, not a debt.
The waves roll soft, the path unwinds—
What stays behind is what we find.

(Outro)
The coast lies ahead, a harbor in sight,
Ella hums softly, her gaze warm and bright.
Whatever comes, whatever remains,
We’ll hold it gently as morning gains.
