ACT I: Light Begins

---

1. First Light
Narrative: The soul choses, it watches before it reincarnates, it sees what was before — her small saying of what she's seen before when I talk about what was before she arrived, banter fun sweet
Theme: mix in some quotes from Rumi that fits, on the journey of the soul and love


---

2. The Universe in a Lunchbox
Narrative: A day in life, framed through her imaginative retelling of school, snacks, and secrets, banter and fun.
Theme: Wonder in the mundane, how children turn the ordinary into entire galaxies.


---

3. Tiny Philosopher
Narrative: A montage of big, bizarre, beautiful questions she asks — and your struggle to answer without breaking the magic.
Theme: Childhood curiosity, the limits of adult understanding, everyday existentialism.


---

❖ ACT II: Orbit & Gravity

---

4. Super Nova (Title Track)
Narrative: love so vast in shines across space, across time, in all its multitudes of colors, materials and ways - seen and felt not only in the moment but in all possible moments like particles in super position, a wave function collapsing into the only possible perfect state like fate. The real meaning of love with no demands, the impossibility to carry her love still  and yet be here with this little star, wanting to go home but not wanting to leave this little star behind.
Theme: Intensity of love, destruction and rebirth, identity loss and devotion.


---

5. Threads Between Stars
Narrative: A dreamy meditation on connection across time — shared dreams, memories, and feelings that pass between you invisibly.
Theme: Memory, love’s persistence, unseen bonds that tether us.


---

6. The Other Side of Silence
Narrative: The loneliness that can come even when you're doing everything “right” as a parent — especially in the quiet moments.
Theme: Emotional exhaustion, unseen effort, silent devotion.


---

❖ ACT III: Cosmic Mess

---

7. Crumbs on the Event Horizon
Narrative: A wild ride through a day of parenting chaos — everything going wrong in hilarious, surreal ways.
Theme: Domestic disorder as cosmic law, entropy through love, absurdity.


---

8. Nightwatcher’s Log
Narrative: The voice of a sleepless parent observing their child through the night, recording love in stillness.
Theme: Quiet vigil, tiredness as ritual, protection through presence.


---

9. Toybox Entanglement
Narrative: toys shared by two stars, passed in as she arrived from wonderland, a hoppy hippo - very curious said the rabbit, the strange beauty of that shared things passed on, to know the holding (plushies not just a thing, like a heart held)
Theme: Chaos and connection, the surreal domestic realm, playful disorder.

Music style description still UK british slang Rap. Perhaps the style of the writing though heavy UK british slang, it can be written in that story telling flowing way like Alice in wonderland is, but rhyming deeply etc. Longer verses.

4 verses, 2 choruses in-between them, an intro and an outro and a musical solo after the 3rd verse.

❖ ACT IV: Refrain & Return


---

10. Theory of Everything
Narrative: Reflecting on everything learned through raising her as her bonus dad, through watching her struggles, family struggles, through being afraid to be close to her when she was young (because of my own heart and feeling as though Tima would see me etc)— realizing love is the only unifying law, seeing what she needs from me, letting her in to see my heart without fear.
Theme: Love as a fundamental truth, philosophy through unbreakable love even not by blood, clarity through experience, understanding grief is so much more and not a process.


---

11. Tomorrow, Tomorrow Again (sayonara samsara)
Narrative: A calm look forward — accepting that the routines, the mess, and the effort all continue, and that’s okay, even without the family by blood, even in their silence - we go our way for us not for anyone else.
Theme: Continuation, peace, daily love as timeless ritual.
