# lantern in my pocket

**Labels:** Pockets Of Light
**Created:** 2025-08-25
**Edited:** 2025-08-27

---

Autumn afternoon, leaves in a race,
hands in my pockets, scarf round my face.
Dad says, “hurry, the chippy will close,”
I laugh at the pigeons that chase after crows.

Curry chips steaming, vinegar bite,
salt on my fingers, napkin’s too light.
“Why’s the sky orange, why’s the sea brown,
why do the buses all smile through town?”

Lantern in my pocket where the pebbles go to sleep,
tickets folded neatly like a promise I can keep.
Tiny sun of toffee wrap, silver in its glow,
button from a coat I wore a hundred days ago.

Lantern in my pocket when the lamp-posts start to hum,
sticky note of scribble, half a riddle, half a sum.
Chewing-gum cathedral stamped beneath my shoe,
every crack in pavement sings a hymn or two.

Listen, the curbstone’s got secrets.

Lantern in my pocket, glow in the seams,
carry little bonfires, stitch me some dreams.
Lantern in my pocket, shine when I roam,
everyday devotion in the things I call home.

Lantern in my pocket with the pebble from the shore,
smooth like a prayer I forgot I had before.
Stub of a pencil, rubber worn thin,
scratches of a spelling test, places I’ve been.
Half a sweet wrapper, curled into gold,
lantern in my pocket keeps the small things bold.

Even the bus ticket’s magic if you fold it twice.

Lantern in my pocket, glow in the seams,
carry little bonfires, stitch me some dreams.
Lantern in my pocket, shine when I roam,
everyday devotion in the things I call home.

Stamp, stomp, step, keep the ember lit,
coin in the pocket’s got a psalm to it.
Stone in the pocket’s got a sky inside,
lantern in the pocket is a hand to guide.

Lantern in my pocket when the rain begins to speak,
bus stop halo shining gold across my cheek.
Dad hums a song and I rhyme him back,
lantern in my pocket glows green in the black.

“Why do the stars always hide in the day?”
… “So they can listen to the things that we say.”

Lantern in my pocket, glow in the seams,
carry little bonfires, stitch me some dreams.
Lantern in my pocket, shine when I roam,
everyday devotion in the things I call home.

Pebbles, tickets, tiny suns,
lantern in my pocket, the day never ends.
